St Andrew Goldfields' (STADF) CEO Duncan Middlemiss on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 1:32 PM ET  |  About: St Andrew Goldfields Ltd (STADF) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Linda Weinzettl - Corporate Secretary

Duncan Middlemiss - President and CEO

Ben Au - CFO and VP, Finance and Administration

Doug Cater - VP, Exploration

Keyvan Salehi - VP, Corporate Development & Technical Services

Analysts

St Andrew Goldfields Ltd. (OTCQX:STADF) Q2 2015 Results Earnings Conference Call August 13, 2015 10:00 AM ET

Operator

Welcome to the Q2 Conference Call. At this time, all the lines are in listen-only mode. Later, we will conduct a question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions] As a reminder, this conference is being recorded, August 13, 2015.

I’d now like to pass the conference over to your host, Linda Weinzettl. Please go ahead

Linda Weinzettl

Thank you, operator, and thank you to all the participants for joining us this morning. Today’s call will take approximately 15 minutes. We will allow an additional five minutes for question-and-answer.

Before we begin, please make note that certain statements made today and within the financial reports and MD&A are considered to be forward-looking information and carry certain risks and uncertainties with them. I refer everyone to the forward-looking section of the presentation for more details. Please also note that the presentation slides and the recording of this call will be available on the SAS website within 24 hours of the call.

On the call with us this morning, we have Duncan Middlemiss, President and CEO.

Duncan Middlemiss

Good morning. This is Duncan Middlemiss.

Linda Weinzettl

Ben Au, CFO and VP Finance and Administration.

Ben Au

Good morning. This is Ben Au.

Linda Weinzettl

Doug Cater, VP Exploration.

Doug Cater

Good morning. Doug Cater speaking.

Linda Weinzettl

And Keyvan Salehi, VP, Corporate Development & Technical Services.

Keyvan Salehi

Good morning. Keyvan Salehi speaking.

Linda Weinzettl

The agenda for today will be as follows: Q2 highlights and operational review, financial results review, discussion of exploration programs, summary and closing, followed by a question-and-answer session.

I’ll now pass the call on to Duncan for a general overview of our second quarter results and operations.

Duncan Middlemiss

Thank you, Linda. Good morning to everyone joining us today. I am happy to provide you with an overview of our second quarter results for 2015. For the second quarter of 2015, SAS realized a net cash inflow of $1.8 million. Cash and cash equivalents remained strong at $26.8 million at the end of the quarter. We achieved a 16th consecutive quarter of positive cash flow from operations of $9.2 million. During the quarter, we sold 23,319 ounces of gold at an average realized price of US$1,201 per ounce.

In Canadian dollar terms, we realized -- our realized price for the quarter was $1,475 per ounce. In 2014 and in Canadian dollar terms, our realized average price was $1,425 per ounce. This resulted in revenue of just over $34.4 million during the quarter. Our average mine cash cost for the quarter of US$641 per ounce came in below our guidance of between $750 to $800 per ounce. With royalties of US$98 per ounce, our total cash cost for the second quarter was US$739 per ounce. We have been able to reduce our cost on a Canadian dollar basis to around CAD$900 per ounce and we’ll keep the pressures on cost containment and improvements.

Production at Holt during the second quarter was 15,951 ounces of gold. The head grade of 4.91 grams per ton was above the mine reserve grade due to stope sequencing. Mill recovery of approximately 95% was as expected. Mine site costs achieved during the quarter were $108 per ton. We expect cost per ton for 2015 to remain at these levels. Total cash cost per ounce of gold was $698 per ounce in U.S. dollars. At the Holloway Mine, we produced 7,582 ounces of gold, with 75% coming from the Smoke Deep Zone and 25% from the Blacktop Zone. Recovery of approximately 92% was in line with expectations. Mine site costs during the quarter were $132 per ton. There is no capital development budgeted at Holloway due to the current level of reserves, so all mining costs are expensed. Current reserves, resources and mineralization at the Holloway Mine will now extend the life of mine well into 2017. We expect further continued exploration success within our targets at Holloway and believe there is more room for upside.

At Taylor, we are currently permitting the property in the commercial production which we expect to be ready for the fourth quarter this year. The contractor has resumed development late in the first quarter, and we are currently completing the required infrastructure for commercial mining. Throughout the course of 2015, we’ll be conducting property wide exploration on this underexplored asset. We will keep the market apprised of our progress and timing of commercial production and will adjust our production guidance accordingly.

During the second quarter of 2015, the Holt Mill processed approximately 160,000 tons of ore from Holt, Holloway and Taylor. Additionally 26,640 tons of toll milling was completed for a total quarterly mill throughput of 186,000 tons. This represents an average milling rate of 2,050 tons per day. Availability was 84% which was in line with expectations as work was concentrated on the mills in preparation for increased ore delivery expected as Taylor commences commercial production. The processing of the 26,000 tons of custom ore from two local operators as part of our Custom Milling agreements generated $1 million in revenues for the company.

Our 2015 plans consist of total gold production of approximately 106,000 ounces from Holt, Holloway and Taylor. However, a portion of the Taylor production will be pre-production and is not regarded as commercial. Once the timing of commercial production is determined, we will be happy to adjust our production guidance.

Ben Au will now give you an overview of our financials. Ben?

Ben Au

Thanks, Duncan, and good morning.

Net income for the quarter was 3.5 million or $0.01 a share as compared to $600,000 in 2014. The increase in net earnings was the result of our strong U.S. dollar and $3.1 million decrease in depreciation expense. Revenue for the quarter increased by $2.7 million from 2014, despite a 7% lower gold price, due to the favorable U.S. to Canadian exchange rate.

Holloway continued to perform well this quarter with 22% increase in throughput and 26% increase in ore grade. Cash margin increased by $2.1 million from 2014 to $3.2 million in the quarter. Operating performance at Holt in the quarter was as expected and delivered $10 million in cash margin in the quarter, this relates to $3.3 million increasing cash margin during the quarter. Toll milling in the quarter also provided $1 million in additional revenue.

Operating profit Holt and Holloway this quarter increased by $2.8 million from 2014 and are consistent with the expenditure levels in the previous quarter. The overall mining operating cost increased marginally from 2014.

On the cash flow, we generated $9.2 million in operating cash flow and $1.8 million in net cash flow this quarter. Operating cash flow increased by $3.7 million from last year which is the result of the increase in cash margin from mine operations, while cash deployed in mine capital reduced slightly when compared to 2014. The investment Taylor this quarter amounted to $3.9 million. Sustaining mine capital in the quarter was also $3.9 million and was in line with our expectations.

Capital investment forecast for 2015 remains unchanged at $26 million. However, as Doug will note later, we will adjust our capital programs later this year, depending on the exploration success. Growth in development capital for Taylor for the second half of 2015 is expected to be $4.4 million. We also plan for $8.5 million increase in mine equipment at Taylor. All mines during pre-production will be stockpiled and processed upon the completion of production closure plan which is expected later in the year.

Working capital at the end of the quarter was $17.1 million and cash stood at $26.8 million. The $10 in U.S. revolver remains undrawn and has able to provide additional liquidity. Also we have increased leasing facility from $3 million to $7 million for the procurement mining equipment at Taylor. At the end of the quarter orders for equipment to the extent of $7 million were placed.

I’ll now hand over to Doug Cater, our VP Exploration to review our exploration program.

Doug Cater

Thanks, Ben.

Exploration and drilling in Q2 totaled 11,000 meters of surface and 10,000 meters of underground drilling which was conducted adjacent to the operations. On the Holt property, drilling targeted the Westerly strike and plunge extension of Zone 4, and at Holloway targeted the Easterly strike extension of Smoke Deep and the depth extension of Blacktop from surface.

The Holt, Holloway plan provides a reference to the location of the mineralized zones, shown in red, with the exploration targets, in the yellow boxes, relative to the operations. A total of three surface drills are active on the Holt, Holloway properties.

On August 5th, SAS reported recent Phase 2 drill results which extended Zone 4 mineralization and additional 265 meters west of the year-end resource shape and indicates that the zone remains open, both along strike and at depth towards the McKenna Fault zone. On the Holloway property, three targets are being actively explored by SAS at this time. On the left, surface exploration, consisting of mapping and sampling is well advanced at the Holloway West, Seagar Hill target. In the middle, drilling continues to trace Smoke Deep along strike and plunge to the east on the 945 meter sub-level and surface drilling is testing the depth extension at Blacktop.

At Holloway, underground drilling on the 945 meter sub-level continues to extend Smoke Deep mineralization to the east and down-plunge. Significant assay results from the program are found summarized on the table on the right.

This longitudinal view at Blacktop shows how surface drilling demonstrates that mineralization continues to depth. Drilling continues to assess the mineral potential of the zone along strike and at depth.

At Taylor, surface exploration drilling has commenced which will both target the strike and depth extensions of 1004 mineralization. In addition, a number of underground drill holes are being extended from the 390 meter sub-level and are being extended to test for mineralization situated to the north or in the footwall of the Porcupine Destor Fault Zone.

On surface at Holloway, the exploration group has commenced with prospecting, mechanical stripping, mapping and sampling, one kilometer west along strike from the Lighting Zone, which has historically produced more than 1 million ounces of gold.

Trenching has uncovered strong shear zones which stratigraphically contained the same lithologies as those found at the mine. Drilling is planned to follow up on these targets, once the sampling and field work program is complete.

In closing, our 2015 exploration programs are well-advanced with drilling being accelerated in Q3. We continue to focus our exploration efforts on, both testing both mine extensions and reputations but also on a number of more generative targets. And I look forward to updating you with results from our programs as they become available.

I will now return you to Duncan for our summary.

Duncan Middlemiss

Thanks, Doug.

The quarter was challenging due to gold price volatility offset by the weak Canadian dollar. The operating mines are performing well and development of the Taylor mine into commercial production is on track for the fourth quarter of this year. Positive exploration result on the western portion of the Holt mine continued throughout the quarter, as is a successful mine exploration program at the Holloway mine. The development of the Taylor mine into commercial production is on track and the company can soon realize a production growth which will result. Couple these developments with the continuing focus on cost reductions and a strong team and the company’s well poised for the future.

Additionally, we continue to forge ahead with our other near mine and grassroots exploration programs. The high potential of our land package is recognized and the exploration team is focused on returning results. The operating team remains determined to mine more ounces, cut costs, increase margins, and maintain the culture responsiveness. Strategically, St Andrew Goldfields will maximize organic growth opportunities from our properties.

Moving forward, the company is well-positioned to realize the growth potential we all recognize and to sustain at gold price just lower.

We’ll open up the call to questions-and-answers.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

A - Linda Weinzettl

We actually have one question from the web.

Can you expand on the exploration target at Taylor, more specifically the targets north of footwall zone?

Duncan Middlemiss

Doug, would you like to take that?

Doug Cater

Yes, thank you. The Taylor deposit consists of three known mineralized zones. We have the Shaft Zone on the east; we have the West Porphyry Zone, which includes the 1004 lens and that has been our focus; and then we have the Shoot Zone on the west end. Mineralization is associated with the series of core [ph] strains which our shallow dipping en-echelon and peel off [ph] the Porcupine Destor Fault Zone. What we’ve been doing is that we have been following up on the 1004 lens with definition drilling but then we’ve been extending any of those definition holes to depth and intersecting the Porcupine Destor Fault Zone and then drilling to the north. And it’s important to note that in the Timmins map scenario [ph] many of the mines and historical deposits are situated in the footwall of the Porcupine Destor and our sediment hosted, contains sediment hosted gold. So, we just want to note that there has been very little historic exploration drilling in the footwall for mineralized structures at Taylor.

Linda Weinzettl

If anyone else has question, please let the operator know.

Operator

Our first question is from Peter Campbell. [Ph] Please go ahead.

Unidentified Analyst

Just a couple of questions, the first question on Taylor and with your reference into your disclosure that commercial production could commence in Q4 and which you would -- which you are sort of foreseeing depending upon that time could contribute 10,000 to 12,000 ounces of production. That rate at Q4 seems to be higher than the potential output from Taylor. Does that include mailing of like stockpiles or something?

Duncan Middlemiss

Peter, it’s Duncan. We’re continuing with our pre-production development. Of course some of that is this development of ore sills and preparation for commercial mining. So, yes, we’ll have a fairly substantial stockpile on surface, probably in the -- might say range of 25,000 to 30,000 tons by September 30th. So that would be milled as commercial production once we get our final permits.

Unidentified Analyst

I see and I know that this is forward-looking, but you’re expecting this prior to the end of Q3 or will it slip into Q4 do you think?

Duncan Middlemiss

Forward-looking for sure, Peter. I don’t know, I think we’re comfortable saying sort of October 1st seems to be the most likely time, but it may slip a week either way by the looks of it. So now, we’re on track and that’s what we expected. So, we’re quite pleased with our progress so far.

Unidentified Analyst

Second question on Holloway, you mentioned Duncan that all the development is being expensed at Holloway, given the resource or reserve situation there. But it’s also been extended into 2017, as I understand. Does that imply that you’re going to require some capitalized development later on?

Duncan Middlemiss

Quite likely, I mean our real -- essentially you need to have at least 18 months of mine reserves in the mine plan in order to start deferring development. So, we’re just at that point where obviously recent developments on the Smoke Deep Zone have added some mineralized material that we’re looking to put into reserves. And Doug is doing a good job below Blacktop there and regarding some interesting results also. So yes, certainly, we are just in the process of doing the 2016 plan and sort of amalgamating all these good results at Holloway. And so, we’ll certainly be I would say deferring some developments in the near future.

Unidentified Analyst

One final question which you’ve kind of led me to here, which is when do you typically prepare and release your year-end reserve resource statement? Is that something done in like January or do you do it conjunction with like quarterly or annual reports?

Duncan Middlemiss

Typically, I believe it’s a February in terms of with the release of our Q4 results. So, around February 13th to 14th is when we typically do a statement.

Unidentified Analyst

And that update, I am assuming just based on the success that you had, I guess primarily at Holt but also interestingly Holloway that this year’s exploration results ought to have a positive impact on that resource statement?

Duncan Middlemiss

Yes, I would think so. And I certainly would add Taylor Mine into that also Peter, just because of the potential we’re seeing, now that we’re underground ad especially into the 1004, it’s really exciting ore body for us. So I think that we’ve got a lot of potential there to expand on.

Linda Weinzettl

We have another question from the web. The picture of the west of Holloway on surface appears to be [indiscernible] for mid values, how does this tie in on strike to the east? Thank you.

Doug Cater

What we have doing is that, that picture and trenching is situated 1 kilometer west of the Holloway head frame. Stratigraphically, we are exposing core strains along the mafic -- volcanic ultramafic contact. We’ve taken some prospector samples and graph samples at this point in time. We are just going back and we are doing systematic channel samples. So, we are continuing to watch that sample and take channel samples. The other thing we’ve done, we’ve done an IP line survey immediately east of that area. So, there is more information to come. Thank you.

Duncan Middlemiss

Alright, I guess that concludes the questions for today. I’d like to thank everybody for joining us for our Q2 results. We look forward again to apprising everybody of the progress at Taylor, the progress in our exploration programs and our progress at all the operations of St Andrew Goldfields. So, thank you for joining us.
