Aug. 13, 2015 4:36 PM ET  |  About: Claude Resources, Inc (CGR) by: SA Transcripts

Claude Resources, Inc. (NYSEMKT:CGR)

Q2 2015 Results Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015, 11:00 AM ET

Executives

Marc Lepage - Manager, Investor Relations

Brian Skanderbeg - Interim President and CEO

Rick Johnson - CFO

Analysts

Derek Macpherson - M Partners

Operator

Good morning. My name is Stephanie, and I will be your conference operator today. At this time, I would like to welcome everyone to the Claude Resources’ Incorporated Second Quarter 2015 Earnings Conference Call. [Operator instructions]

Thank you. Marc Lepage, Manager of Investor Relations, you may begin your conference.

Marc Lepage

Thanks, Stephanie. Good morning and thank you for joining us on our second quarter of 2015 earnings call. We’d like to welcome all analysts, current and prospective shareholders and the media. On the conference call today, we have Brian Skanderbeg, President and CEO and Rick Johnson, CFO.

During today’s call, the company may use forward-looking statements which are based upon current expectations and involve risks and uncertainties. For further information regarding forward-looking statements, please refer to our cautionary note located on Page 2, in today’s presentation.

Please note that today’s presentation is located on our home page of our website within the corporate presentation icon on the left-hand side. Also you can download and view our second quarter of 2015 management’s discussion and analysis and financial statements located under the investor’s page under financial reporting.

I’ll now turn the call over to Brian Skanderbeg, President and CEO.

Brian Skanderbeg

Thank you, Marc. Hello everyone and thank you for joining us as we review our second quarter operating and financial results, discuss our outlook for the remainder of 2015 and at the end answer any questions that you may have.

Before we begin the formal presentation, I would like to take this opportunity to acknowledge our operations team for an excellent second quarter and for their ongoing focus towards safe production. In addition, I would like to acknowledge dedicated staff that remained onsite during their recent evacuation due to local force fires, their dedication and tireless commitment to ensure safety, and employees, the facilities and to facilitate a timely return back to normal operation were tremendous. Thank you for your efforts and keep up the great work.

I will now start the presentation on Slide 3 with the second quarter highlights. The second quarter continued to build on the company’s trend of strong operating and financial performance and during the quarter we achieved record net earnings of $10.2 million or $0.05 per share. This was an improvement of $7 million, $6.9 million or a 208% improvement versus the second quarter in 2014. Along with the strong earnings we continued to see solid operating results with gold production of 20,619 ounces which was a 10% increase versus the second quarter of 2014.

We continued to mine higher margin ounces in the second quarter as mill head grades increased 15% to 8.88 grams per ton. As expected, our total cash cost per ounce decreased to $6.3 or U.S. $507 a 17% decrease from the second quarter in 2014 and our all-in sustaining cost per ounce decrease to $954 Canadian or U.S. $776 representing a 10% decrease from last year’s second quarter, both well below our 2015 guidance.

Our strong operating and financial performance strengthened their financial position as we increased cash and bullion to $20.9 million which now exceeds our long term debt at $20.8 million.

Moving onto slide 4, the first six months of 2014 have certainly been record breaking for Claude. We have three record earnings of $15.4 million or $0.08 per share a $17.2 million improvement during the same period of 2014. The main factors driving record earnings have been record production and higher mining high grades.

For the first six months of 2015, we had record gold production of 41,686 ounces nearly a 40% increase versus the first half of 2014. Grades have been very strong averaging 9.49 grams per ton, a 40% improvement over the same period of 2014. And our peer leading cost performance year-to-date continues to improve.

Year-over-year our total cash cost per ounce decreased 23% to $647, U.S. $524 and our all in sustaining cost decreased 18% to $1,146 or U.S. $928 per ounce. Based on our record production, we have increased our annual gold production guidance and lowered our cost guidance for 2015.Our annual gold production guidance is increased to 68,000 to 72,000 ounces, cash cost per ounce to range from $730 to $830 or U.S. $580 to U.S. $635 and our all in sustaining cost per ounce to range from $1,100 to $1,200 or U.S. $975 to U.S. $950.

Our expected fully loaded cost in 2015 are well below today’s gold price and this reflects our ability to continue to generate material free cash flow. I will now turn the call over to Rick Johnson, CFO to discuss the financials.

Rick Johnson

Thanks, Brian. Before I begin on slide 5 with the financial highlights, please note that all figures presented in the table are in Canadian dollars unless otherwise stated. Gold revenue generated during the second quarter was 29.7 million, was 20% higher than the $24.7 million reported in the comparable period of 2014.

Year-to-date gold revenue of $55.9 million increased 39% from the first half of 2014, a reflection of a 33% increase in gold sales volume and a 5% increase in Canadian dollar gold prices realized.

Net earnings for the quarter were $10.2 million or $0.05 per share, a significant improvement from the second quarter of 2014s net earnings of $3.3 million or $0.02 per share. Year-to-date net earnings of $15.4 million or $0.08 per share were $17.2 million improvement over the net loss of $1.8 million or $0.01 per share reported during the first half of 2014.

The improvement in financial performance period-over-period and year-to-date was due to increased ounces produced and sold along with higher realized Canadian dollar gold prices.

Cash flow from operations before net changes in non-cash operating working capital was $15.6 million or $0.08 per share was up from the $9.9 million or $0.05 per share reported in the second quarter of 2014. Year-to-date cash flow from operations of $24.9 million or $0.13 per share more than doubled the $11.6 million or $0.06 per share reported during the first half of 2014.

Our strong operating performance mining higher grades and a successful utilization of the Alimak mining method decreased both total cash costs and all-in sustaining costs per ounce.

Total cash cost per ounce decreased by 17% to $623 or U.S. $507 per ounce for the quarter and by 23% to $647 or U.S. $524 per ounce for the first six months of 2015. All in sustaining cost per ounce of gold decreased 10% to $954 or U.S. $776 per ounce during the quarter and by 18% to $1,146 or U.S. $928 for the first six months of 2015.

Now on to slide 6. In 2015, we remained focused on improving the strength of our balance sheet with further debt reduction and increasing our cash on hand. As you can see we have made significant progress over the past year of lowering our total debt to $20.8 million, $31.7 million a year ago.

Our total debt servicing cost in 2015 will be about $5.6 million consisting of $2 million in interest cost and $3.6 million of principal payments. The strong operating performance year-to-date have allowed us to reduce long term debt and to increase our cash and bullion position to $20.9 million. This compares to the $5.6 million we’ve withdrawn on our line of credit last year at this time.

As Brian had noted previously, at the end of the second quarter our cash and bullion amount exceeds our long term debt of $20.8 million. At quarter end, our working capital position was $33.9 million an improvement over the $23.7 million in working capital we had at the end of the second quarter in 2014. With continued strong operating performance and our ability to generate free cash flow, we expect our cash balance to increase for the balance of the year.

Brian will now discuss our operating performance.

Brian Skanderbeg

Thanks, Rick. We will now move onto page 7 of the presentation to discuss in further detail our operating performance. We continued to see strong operating results in the second quarter and are now on pace to set a new annual production record.

Second quarter gold production of 20,619 ounces were 10% higher than the same period of 2014, a product of higher ore grades averaging 8.88 grams gold per ton and strong recovery rates of 96.5%.

Year-to-date total gold production of 41,686 ounces was a new six month production record. The 39% increase in gold production grade year-over-year is driven by positive reconciliation in grade and ounces versus our mine plant at both the L62 and Santoy Gap deposits and the replacements of a lower grade Santoy 8 ore with the higher grade Santoy Gap ore.

If we look back the last four quarters, we produced 74,500 ounces of gold. Our ability to achieve consistent results demonstrates that our production profile is sustainable.

Moving onto slide 8, Santoy Gap deposit has been a key contributor in mining higher grade ore and increasing stope availability to increase margin and decrease our production risk. During the first six months of 2015 the Santoy Gap deposit had increased overall head grades of the Santoy mine complex by 86% and combined with the 47% increase in mill throughput has improved gold ounces produced by 177%. This demonstrates the high grade nature and significance to our future production profile.

Since we started mining at Santoy Gap in May of 2014 its produced approximately 25,000 tonnes at 8.44 grams per ton for a total of 33,695 ounces. Throughout this time development and production rates have been well ahead of pre feasibility schedule and grades have been reconciling above reserve or ounces.

For the remainder of the year plans are to move from one mining front to three driving increased production and stope availability while reducing production risk. Santoy Gap production is expected to account for approximately 60% of overall production in 2015 and will play an even larger role in 2016 and beyond. By the end of 2015 we expect to have approximately 3 years of production developed at Santoy Gap.

During the second quarter, we continued to work on infrastructure upgrades to increase production profile to 600 to 700 tons per day in 2016 and one of the advantages that we have is that our capital requirements to fund the upgrades and production growth are minimal and we can fund this organic growth via internal gas flow.

Moving onto slide nine. The Alimak mining method along with the L-62 deposit continued to be a key driver in our production and cost performance. One of the most important aspects of the Alimak mining technique is that it has significantly decreased our development intensity and time to produce making it a far more productive mining method. Also, it continued to achieve better than budgeted dilution rates from effective grounds for techniques, the decrease in development capital required better than budget dilution rates and positive reconciliation in grade have contributed to an 18% reduction in our all-in sustaining cost from 2015.

In 2015, the Seabee mine is expected to make up approximately 40% of annual production tonnage. To date, the Alimak mining method was done primarily by a contractor while Claude simultaneously trained its own personnel. Training has advanced very well and on June 30, we elected to bring the Alimak mining technique in-house and during the second quarter we initiated development of Alimak Long-hole stope on certain of the Seabee deep lenses [ph].

Now onto slide 10, there is significant exploration potential to find more gold in the under explored 19,950 hectare land package at Seabee. We feel the best opportunity to find more gold is within or in close proximity to our current mine infrastructure. At the Santoy mine complex, we have an excellent opportunity to go resources as most of the mill reserve and resources are above the 500 meter level.

In 2013, we had two exploration holes that tested the depth extension and these were among the highest gram-metre product in the camp’s history. Now in the second half of 2015 we will have, we will follow up on one of these holes, hole 692. This program is budgeted to be 6000 meters and will begin near the end of the third quarter once we have established the [Indiscernible] drill chamber. We are confident that the system continues the depth and are confident that the drilling results will translate into a larger resource of longer mine life at the Santoy mine complex.

On slide 15, the 2015 underground exploration at the Seabee mine are outlined in red. Drilling at the Seabee mine will consist of 10,000 meters of exploration targeting seven high priority underground near-mine targets. These near-mine targets are analogous [ph] structure and down planned extension to the Seabee and L-62 deposits. There has been limited drilling in these areas and we believe that targets are highly perspective for all L-62 style lenses. Results from the Seabee gold mine underground exploration are expected in the third and fourth quarters while results from the Santoy mine complex are expected later in the year.

In addition to the two exploration programs, we will also complete approximately 55,000 meters of underground and infill definition drilling at the Seabee and Santoy mine complex to expand current reserves and resources. It’s important to note that we are able to conduct all of the drilling ourselves as we own five drill rigs and we will complete the underground drill program for approximately $1.3 million or $20 per meter. Our ability to drill at a very low cost is a competitive advantage and translates to a very low discovery cost per ounce.

Now on to slide 12, I would like to reiterate that our 2015 outlook and operating strategy remains focused on grade, cost controls, productivity improvements and ramping up production at Santoy Gap. With the strong start to the year, we had increased our annual production guidance to 68,000 to 72,000 ounces from our original guidance of 60, 000 ounces to 65,000 ounces and with the increase in production guidance our ability to remain on budget unit cash cost and all-in sustaining cost are now expected to be 6% lower than original guidance. Our cash cost are now expected to be $730 to $800, U.S. $580 to U.S. $635 and our all-in sustaining cost to be $1,100 to $1,200 or U.S. $875 to U.S. $950 clearly well below current gold prices.

Our revised outlook for 2015 demonstrates that we have the potential to set new production records increase margins and lower unit cost to strengthen our balance sheet. By achieving the midpoint of production guidance remaining on budget with our cost and realizing similar gold prices for the remainder of the year we expect to yield annual free cash flow margins of approximately 15% of revenues. Our cost structure and free cash flow yields position the company as leaders in our sector and we’re confident that it will continue to generate shareholder value.

That concludes our presentation for today and we’d be happy to entertain any questions that you may have. Thank you.
