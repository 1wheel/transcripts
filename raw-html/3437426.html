Klondex Mines' (KLNDF) CEO Paul Huet on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 1:53 PM ET  |  About: Klondex Mines Ltd. (KLNDF) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Alison Dwoskin – Manager, Investor Relations

Paul Huet – President and Chief Executive Officer

Brent Kristof – Chief Operating Officer

Barry Dahl – Chief Financial Officer

Analysts

Rahul Paul – Canaccord Genuity

Derek Macpherson – M Partners

Tara Hassan – Haywood Securities

Oliver Turner – GMP Securities

Samuel Peleg – UBS

Klondex Mines Ltd. (OTCQX:KLNDF) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 13, 2015 10:30 AM ET

Operator

Thank you for standing by. This is the Chorus Call conference operator. Welcome to the Klondex Mines' Second Quarter 2015 Conference Call. As a reminder, all participants are in listen-only mode and the conference is being recorded. After the presentation, there will be an opportunity to ask questions. [Operator Instructions]

At this time, I would like to turn the conference over to Alison Dwoskin, Manager, Investor Relations. Please go ahead.

Alison Dwoskin

Thank you, Zuwan, and good morning, everyone. Welcome to the Klondex Mines' second 2015 earnings conference call. On the call today we have Mr. Paul Huet, our President and Chief Executive Officer; Mr. Brent Kristof, Chief Operating Officer; and Mr. Barry Dahl, Chief Financial Officer.

Other members of our executive team will be available to answer questions at the end of the call. Before we begin, I want to remind listeners that some of today’s commentary may contain forward-looking information for Klondex Mines under applicable securities laws. You are cautioned that actual results and future events could differ materially from the respective conclusions for tax or projections. I'll refer everyone to the section on the forward-looking statements in the company's latest MD&A and other filings available on SEDAR, we’ve set out the material factors that would cause these results to differ.

As a reminder, we report our financials in Canadian dollars, unless otherwise stated. And please note that the recording of the call will be available for replay, the details of which are posted on the corporate website.

And with that, it gives me great please to introduce Paul Huet, Chief Executive Officer.

Paul Huet

Thanks Alison, good morning everyone. Thank you for joining us here today. We had yet another great quarter. Our team continues to deliver, and I'm so proud of their accomplishments. Within the quarter I am pleased to report that our team grew with the addition of Mark Daniel as Director and Charles Oliver as Special Advisor to the Board.

Subsequent to the quarter, we also added John Seaberg team. John joins as Senior Vice President of Investor Relations. He will be based in Reno at the corporate office with the executive team. Our team operates in an environmentally responsible manner, while protecting the health and safety of our employees and contractors. We believe that safety and production go hand in hand, and I'm pleased to announce that our Fire Creek operation has gone 1,033 day without a lost-time accident.

During my 28 years in underground mining, I’ve personally never seen this milestone achieved at any mine or operation that I've been part of. Many of you at Fire Creek are listening on this call today. Congratulations to all of you for always putting safety first. Also this quarter, we received two permits that are critical to our long-term growth plan. Brent will have an opportunity to discuss them in detail.

As previously announced, we are on track to produce 125,000 to 130,000 gold equivalent ounces, up from our original guidance of 120,000 to 125,000. These additional ounces are being produced from our Midas operation. Congratulations to the Midas mine and mill group for their continued effort, we are all very proud of you.

Our second quarter performance was one of the best yet. We lowered both our production and all-in sustaining cost per ounce from Q1. By concentrating on and profitable production, we’ve actually reduced our all-in sustaining guidance for the year to a range of US$750 to US$800 per gold ounce, down from the previous range of US$800 to US$850 per gold ounce.

Based on the exploration successes in the first half of the year, I'm very pleased to announce we received approval from the Board of Directors to increase the capital exploration expenditures for the remainder of the year by 10 million. These additional funds will be spent equally between Fire Creek and Midas. This redirection of funds will not have a negative impact on the company’s free cash flow, as the dollars were originally set aside to prepay the debt. After careful evaluation, we believe investing the dollars in our mines rather than prepaying the debt at this time increases value for our shareholders, while reducing the risks for future years of production.

As mentioned in our Q1 call, our aim is to have two years of waste development ahead of ourselves at each operation, providing sufficient access to multiple working bases and giving us the flexibility to deliver on our mine plans in a more challenging metal price environment. We currently have approximately 9 to 12 month of development ahead of ourselves at each operation.

Klondex’s focus and strategy remains simple; we want to continue increasing shareholder value, you will see with the Q2 results that we are well on our way. The details for the operations and financials in the quarter will be discussed by both Brent and Barry.

Our number one objective is still organic growth from Fire Creek and Midas to fill our mill. As always we will continue to evaluate all capital allocation opportunities. For the quarter, we’ve made excellent progress on many fronts, including safety, permitting, construction, exploration, sustainable growth and most of all delivering gold at a profit. Additionally, we have made significant progress on the US listing. We anticipate it to be completed in the third quarter achieving yet another milestone.

At this point I will turn the call over to our Chief Operating Officer, Brent Kristof to discuss the progress of the operations. Brent?

Brent Kristof

Thank you, Paul. Starting with Health, Safety, and Environment; our employees and contractors continue to perform very well. It’s a pleasure in reporting our second quarter this year no-lost time incidents in our operations. And our 12 month rolling average medical reportable injury frequency continues to fall, it’s now 44% lower than the industry average for metal and non-metal mines in the United States. Regarding the environment, there were no environmental issues or spills at Midas. While at Fire Creek, we had one minor benign spill.

Moving to production; our second quarter was as expected. The Midas mill processed approximately 19,600 tons of Fire Creek ore creating an average of 1 ounce per ton of gold and 1.31 ounces per ton of silver, resulting in production on track with 18,558 ounces of gold and 23,852 ounces of silver. Processing of Midas ore in the second quarter resulted in approximately 43,400 tons milled, creating an average of 0.2 ounces per ton gold and 11 ounce per ton of silver, resulting in production of approximately 8,000 ounces of gold and 449,000 ounces of silver.

Record silver production of 472,473 ounces in the second quarter was due to higher productivity, better silver grades than expected and control of dilution at Midas. The focus of our other activities at Midas was exploration drilling from the new platform at the south end of the mine. As well as development to set up mining of the 505 main extension that was discovered in the first quarter.

At Fire Creek, development in the North and South ends of the mine continues in order to generate diamond drilling platforms and new mining bases. We look forward long-haul mining trials in the third quarter. In addition, mid-year mineral resource updates were started at both of our operations and these will be used for our 2016 and life of mine updates during this year's budgeting and planning cycle.

In summary our mill processed approximately 63,000 tons, recovering 26,552 ounces of gold and a record 472,473 ounces of silver. Our second quarter gold and silver recoveries averaged approximately 94%.

Regarding our operating and capital costs; in the second quarter production cash costs per gold equivalent ounce sold were reduced to US$605 per ounce from US$689 per ounce in the first quarter, as calculated using realized metal prices for both quarters.

Our operating costs in the second quarter benefited from the additional work completed during the first quarter, specifically bringing forward our planned mobile maintenance, our accelerated development at Midas, and settlement, and water treatment and removal costs are Fire Creek.

Capital work was focused in two main areas in the second quarter. At Midas we continue the accelerated development to take advantage of our exploration successes in the vicinity of the underground workings. At Fire Creek, we brought forward drilling to update the resource model from mine life planning.

Finally with respect to permitting; at Fire Creek the water pollution control permit was approved removing the tonnage cap. And also at Fire Creek, our environmental assessment, we’ve agreed upon a schedule with the lead regulatory agency and completion is expected in the fourth quarter of this year. At Midas, a permanent application to increase tailings and storage capacity to approximately five years by building an engineered reinforced concrete wall has been approved. The construction is underway and is scheduled to be completed during the fourth quarter of 2015.

I will now pass over to Barry Dahl, our Chief Financial Officer to discuss our financial results in more detail.

Barry Dahl

Thank you, Brent. As a reminder, we have an objective to convert our financial statement presentation to US dollars by the end of the year. However today, we continue to report our financials in Canadian dollars. Unless otherwise stated, my discussion will be in Canadian dollars.

In summary, we had another great quarter, and I will discuss the financial highlights. Beginning with the statement of income, in Q2 our revenue was $51 million from the sale of 34,200 gold equivalent ounces. Our average realized price per ounce for gold and silver were $1,492 and $20.37 respectively or in US$1,213 and US$16.57 respectively. Production cash cost for the second quarter of 2015 were $25.4 million or $728 per gold equivalent ounce sold. Our non-IFRS cost for gold equivalent ounce was US$605 as Brent had mentioned.

Depreciation and depletions in the quarter were $8.7 million, which equates to an average depreciation costs per gold equivalent ounce of $249. Revenues and cost of sales resulted in gross profit of $16.9 million, which provided us a book margins of 33% of revenues. Our G&A expenses were $3.7 million, which includes share based compensation.

Our income from operations was $12.7 million, which represents approximately 25% of revenue. Finance charges for the quarter were $2.6 million, which approximated Q1 as our only borrowing costs involved scheduled repayment. Finance charges are primarily attributed to the gold purchase agreement and senior notes.

Our net income totaled $4.8 million or $0.04 per share. Our cash balance at June 30, was $68 million, approximately $15.2 million higher than year end. Second quarter operating cash flow totaled $23.1 million, which includes $16.5 million of net income, net of non-cash adjustment and $6.6 million from the change in non-cash working capital.

During the second quarter, we invested $14.7 million in our mines, consisting $6.2 million at Fire Creek and $8.5 million at Midas, principally from mine development and exploration. We recovered $4.2 million from cash collateral on reclamation bonds, which brings our restricted cash on bonds down to $18.8 million, approximately 50% of the bond amount.

We received $2.8 million from the exercise of options and paid $1.7 million of principal and interest on our senior notes. As Paul stated our guidance increased by 5,000 gold equivalent ounces. The 5,000 additional ounces are from Midas, which has a higher gold equivalent ounce cost than Fire Creek. We revised our guidance for production cost for gold equivalent ounce to US$575 to US$625 from US$550 to US$600.

I'll now turn the call over to Paul.

Paul Huet

Thanks, Barry. Again, I want to congratulate the team. And thank both our shareholders and the Board of Directors for their ongoing support as Klondex continues to evolve quarter-over-quarter. We are truly excited for the future of our company.

Now, I'd like to open the call for questions.

Barry Dahl

Thank you, Paul. So yes, you can expect that that cost will increase during Q3 and Q4. So on a quarterly basis, we assess the activities which occur at the mine. For the capital expenditures, we need to determine sustaining and non-sustaining, and we look at the current mine plans to do that.

Rahul Paul

Okay. And then just quickly, I'm trying to get a break down. So I mean of the $1.4 million, how much of the spend that Fire Creek?

It’s going to be more in Q3 and Q4. The increase in silver grades at Midas was all from our long-haul stoping in the existing Veins that we’re mining, the 805 and 905.

Brent Kristof

Yes.

Paul Huet

Thanks, Rahul.

The next question is from Derek Macpherson from M Partners. Please go ahead.

Derek Macpherson

Good morning guys. Just a couple of questions on the financial side. First on G&A spending on a cash basis has come up sort of over the last few quarters, like which is what we would expect with the team growing. Do you guys have an idea where you think that might stabilize?

Barry Dahl

We’re not providing guidance on the G&A expense. But as you mentioned, there have been an increase in G&A. We’ve mentioned in the MD&A that we’ve hired a director consultant to the board, we just hired John as Executive Vice President, we’re listed on the New York Stock Exchange. There is going to be some increases in the next couple of quarters related to G&A expense.

Derek Macpherson

And then I guess my next question, and Barry probably it’s for you. We’ve seen a little bit of a, some positive return capital adjustments flow through the capital statement in the last couple of quarters, is that something that’s just part – is that something that we should expect to see a negative adjustment, or what’s that directly related to or mostly related to?

Barry Dahl

We’ll see changes within working capital category every quarter. So we’ve maintained – our working capital today is about CAD$60.5 million and it’s a very strong working capital position, we’re about 2.4 current assets over current liabilities.

Derek Macpherson

Right. But is there something particular, this building, I know in – is there something particular there that’s building and is it, if we’re going to see come out or is it a – or is it just the normal adjustments?

Barry Dahl

Just the normal adjustments.

Okay. Thanks. That’s all my questions.

Thanks, Derek.

The next question is from Tara Hassan from Haywood Securities. Please go ahead.

Tara Hassan

Good morning everybody. Just touching on Fire Creek and the EA, can you maybe give a little more detail on the agree to schedule and how long that process is for the review and the sort of expected timing for approval?

Paul Huet

Thanks Tara, its Paul. The agreed schedule that we have with the lead agency which is the BLM is actually to complete the EA within the fourth quarter of 2015. You may have seen I the MD&A that we wrote that it could potentially slip into first quarter and that is conservative, however I would like to point out that the Gantt chart and the schedule that we do have with them is completed by the year-end. If there is any slippage in it and I really want to stress if there is, it will not have any impact on our production plans for 2016.

Tara Hassan

Okay. So when you're saying agree to schedule like if that’s the final approval by the EPA or that is submittal and review period after that process.

Paul Huet

It’s the final approval by the BLM, Tara.

Tara Hassan

Okay. And then just touching on the additional capital spend, I believe the original CapEx guidance is given in Canadian dollars, but it was reference in MD&A in US dollars, so what should we be modelling on total capital spending, is it US$42 million or Canadian dollars?

Paul Huet

Yeah. Look, we apologize if we’ve created a bit of confusion. Sometimes with this Canadian and US reporting, we do miss some of the numbers and the dollars, the original dollars, the $33 million was always expected to be in US dollars and the $43 million is in US dollars as well, that’s one of our mandate – and very opened up is to make sure that all our reporting is in US dollars on a go forward basis. And I think there is quite a bit of work because we got to go backwards and restate our old financial. So we are working towards that. And we apologize, if we’ve created any confusion over that, but it is US dollars in both.

Tara Hassan

Okay. That’s great. And just touching on the listing, is there still a goal to be through that process like some time this year or is that looking like into next year with weakness here?

Paul Huet

Well actually at this point, it looks like we will have the US listing in the third quarter of this year Tara.

Tara Hassan

And then in regards to the debt in place, now with the senior secured. In other words, some discussion of considering, replacing that with a lower rate instrument, is that something that’s still on the table and could that be done this year as well to lower your interest rate payment?

Paul Huet

Well based on several quarters now of demonstrating consistent revenues and profits, we are seeing a lot more interest in potential revolving line of credit. So we will always be assessing the best opportunity for that debt. And we are definitely looking at different opportunities.

Tara Hassan

Can you touch on per ton mining cost and sort of the trend that you’re seeing at both Midas and Fire Creek quarter-over-quarter I guess.

Paul Huet

Yeah. So based on the efforts and work done from Brent and his team, we are actually seeing a reduction quarter-over-quarter, I’m not going to give exact amounts, but quarter-over-quarter we are seeing a unit cost, reduction on cost grounds, as you can see and our unit cost and the cost per ton is diminishing.

The increase in tonnage is going through the mill you can appreciate, all we have to pay is a variable cost, but fixed cost remain the same, so we are seeing a reduction in the milling cost as well because of the additional throughput.

Tara Hassan

That’s great. And just a final question on the increased capital budget, what’s the split between developments and exploration really and I guess you could call it, and what was the sort of factor that drove you guys to accelerate the work that you’re doing, is it just encouragement in sort of what you’re seeing from acceleration drilling or is it sort of a plan to get yourself to that two years of development at a more accelerated pace?

Paul Huet

Yeah. So there is a lot of moving parts with that one. As far as the exact split between drilling and development, we are actually working through the details. Because I will say we had to get board approved, while it was a recommendation from management, it wasn’t approved. So we are working through the details on exactly how many dollars will be allocated to the drilling versus the development and you're correct in your assessment in saying that what we are trying to do is get that development ahead of ourselves and the drilling to prepare ourselves for any metal prices. So always in our best interest to prepare ourselves and get that two years of waste development ahead of ourselves.

And earlier on in the year, we saw some very strong results from the expiration on both sites. We need to push some additional development and prepare ourselves to 2016 and 2017 to sustain these production rates and the growth that we want in the company.

Tara Hassan

Okay. Thanks. And I might sneak in one more quickly. We’re seeing some interesting assets potentially come up through both weaker markets on the junior development side and also company like Barrick they’re looking to divest in Nevada. I know you guys are always on the hunt, but has that changed, has the market sort of changed that approach and the focus that you’re putting on those efforts?

Paul Huet

We’re always going to look at where the best opportunity is to allocate our capital. So there are several things coming up on the market. We never want to lose focus of what we are doing. We’re building something within these two mines that we already have. We don’t want to be distracted, but we’ll always see evaluating one of the best use of our capital will be for our shareholders.

Tara Hassan

Okay. That’s great. Thank you so much.

The next question is from Oliver Turner from GMP Securities. Please go ahead.

Oliver Turner

Actually that’s a good question Oliver, and we do have some penalties on prepaying that debt, there is a 4% penalty. So we're going to be evaluating and accessing what the best thing to do is, we want to see some of the results from the drilling before we make any decision like that and see where the market is going to be. But it is not something that we have omitted or forgot about, it is something that we can consider. We just really want to see some of the results on some of this drilling. We don't ever want to find ourselves short of cash that’s for sure.

Thanks, Oliver.
