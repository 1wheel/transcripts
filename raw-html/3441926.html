Imperial Metals Corp. (IPMLF) Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 14, 2015 7:22 PM ET  |  About: Imperial Metals Corp. (IPMLF) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Brian Kynoch - President

Andre Deepwell - Chief Financial Officer

Analysts

Orest Wowkodaw - Scotiabank

Derek MacPherson - M Partners

Craig Hutchison - TD Securities

Brett Levy - Credit Research and Trading

Imperial Metals Corp. (OTCPK:IPMLF) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 14, 2015 1:00 PM ET

Operator

Good morning, ladies and gentlemen, and welcome to Imperial Metals Corporation Second Quarter Results Conference Call. At this time, all lines are in a listen-only mode. [Operator Instructions]. This call is being recorded on Friday, August 14, 2015.

I would now like to turn the conference over to Mr. Brian Kynoch. Please go ahead.

Brian Kynoch

Thank you. Welcome to the Imperial Metals conference call to review our second quarter 2015 results. First, I’d like to note that our comments may contain forward-looking statements, which by their nature is subject to risk of uncertainty and actual results may differ materially from those expressed today. For further information you can check out the risks and uncertainties in our cautionary note that is attached to our news release.

I’ll start the call with brief updates on our major projects and then I’ll have Andre Deepwell, our CFO, go through the highlights of the financial statements. At Huckleberry, the mills running well and the tonnage in the second quarter was 1.7 million tons, up from 1.5 million tons in the first quarter. The grade was also up a little bit, so in the first quarter we made 11.5 million pounds of copper, up from the 10.6 million that we made in the first quarter.

Mount Polley operations started on August 5th following receipt of a permit amendment on July 5th which allowed us to recommence the operation of the mine using a modified operation plan.

And the key part of that modified operation plan is that we’re using the Springer pit to contain the tailings we’re producing right now. Operations commence the week ago, on one week on and one week off basis and I was up there for the first couple of days and lot of smiling faces in the mill building.

The ore is coming from the Cariboo pit and the boundary zone, and underground mine we have up there. And the restart progress very smoothly and during the first week restarted operations. We treated 140,000 tons of ore and produced about 1400 tons of concentrate containing over 830,000 pounds of copper and 1300 ounces of gold. With the recommenced operations the mine currently has about a 183 people employed at the site, that’s pretty close to half of what we had before.

Portion of the funds that we’re raising and the rights offering will be use the fund that working capital that we required to restart the Mount Polley mine. Permitting work on both the short term and long term water discharge permit is still underway and obtaining these permits is required to maintain the water balance in the site.

We’re also working on permitting and a designing of system to curtailing for the long term which would allow us to restart the mine at full scale operations. Rehabilitation at Mount Polley of the areas affected by the breach in the tailings and bank are continuing.

Imperial remains committed to working with the Ministry of Environment, the first than the local communities to medicate the effects for the breach. We’ve established a rock [ph] line channel for the entire length of Hazeltine Creek and Hazeltine Creek water runs clear.

The turbidity levels in Quesnel Lake have now return to normal level. And at the moment we are working on the lower portion of Edney Creek placing gravels in the Creek and into the repair channel and shaping along the Foreshore to return that Creek to Fish Habitat [ph].

As most of you know the repair of the breach and rehabilitation work on the Creek was a tremendous task that was accomplish in a very short time and during the winter and I’m personally very proud of the work that we’ve done at Mount Polley and [Indiscernible] the contribution of the staff, our consultants and our friends in the area, so thanks to everyone up there.

At Red Chris production for the second quarter was 12. – Almost 12.7 pounds, up significantly from the 4.7 we produced in the first quarter. Mill throughput in the June quarter average 20,000, just over 20,000 tons a day with water supply being our biggest single cause of down time.

Early in July we have that issue resolved. Then during the month of July 730,000 tons were milled producing 7.23 million pounds of copper and over 3600 of gold both record monthly productions for us so far up at Red Chris also in July with the steady operations our copper recovery increased to 80.9% and gold recovery was up to 53.2%.

And the gold recovery in particular is actually meeting our targeted, our predicted gold recovery for the ore that was delivered, still have some work to go on the copper recovery.

The current tailings dam constructions well over way and by the end of June we had more than half of our plan construction for the year completed. On April 11th, we loaded our first shipload of concentrate for sale to a smelter out of the port of Surrey, and now we’ve ship the total of four shiploads and expect to do another load next week. And so far in August we’ve average the mill throughput of over 28,000 tons a day and we’re headed towards another months with over 7 million pounds of copper production.

Based on our July performance Red Chris achieved accounting criteria that we set for commercial production. So effective July 1st, the revenue and expenses from the operating – of operating the mine would be reported through of statement of income.

Just to be clear thought the Red Chris mine we haven’t achieved our mine completion test under a senior credit facility, we have till December 1st to do that and we’re targeting to meet these tests during this fall.

With this brief summary, we will have Andre now to go through the financials and then we’ll have a question period after.

Andre Deepwell

Thank you, Brian. Imperial recorded net income of $1.6 million in the second quarter of 2015, compared to net income of $15.2 million in the second quarter of 2014. This variation is largely the result of two items. The suspension of Mount Polley operations since August 2014 and the related to Idle mine cost offsetting impart by the receipt of $11 million insurance recovery related to the Mount Polley tailings dam breach which more than offset all the other expenses in the June 2015 quarter.

These other expenses included higher interest expense flowing through the income statement, lower mine operating margins exclusive of the Mount Polley mine and lower unrealized losses on derivative instruments.

The foreign currency swap that the company entered into at the time of the senior notes were issued in March of 2014 covers about a third of the value of the notes and mitigates the impact of the increase or decrease on the Canadian dollar.

During the June, 2015 quarter, the company recorded loss of $2.4 million related to the foreign currency swap partially offsetting the $6.6 million foreign exchange gain on the senior notes.

The company recorded $700,000 loss on gold derivatives for this quarter, versus losses of $2.1 million on copper and gold derivatives in the same quarter last year. The company has no derivative instruments for copper at June 30, 2015 or today.

But at June 30, 2015 company had hedged 48,600 ounces of gold for the balance of 2015, via min/max zero cost collars. With gold that, U.S. 1,100 oz per ounce, these hedges will generated about Canadian $925,000 per month till the end of 2015.

The company recorded about $600,000 as it share of Huckleberry’s income for the quarter compared to equity income of about $1.4 million in the same period as last year. Although Huckleberry had three shipments of concentrate in this latest quarter compared to one shipment in 2014, the lower net income reflect reduced operating margins.

Imperial’s capital expenditures including capitalized interest of $14.4 million were $48.9 million in the current quarter, down from the $141.8 million in the 2014 quarter when construction expenditures at Red Chris were in full swing.

The 2015 additions for Red Chris are comprised of $57.6 million related to preproduction operating expenses, $13.2 million of capital primarily tailings dam and all that is offset by preproduction revenues of $52.5 million.

The revenue was booked as an offset to mineral property, capital expenditures, because Red Chris was not in commercial production during the June 2015 quarter. In addition the company incurred $17.3 million in capital expenditures at Mount Polley primarily tailings dam related.

The company reports four non-IFRS measures. Adjusted net income, adjusted EBITDA, cash flow, and cost per pound of copper produced. The adjusted net loss was $9.4 million in the June, 2014 quarter primarily as a result of excluding the insurance recovery received in the quarter and to a lesser extent the net unrealized foreign loss on debt after adjusting for the foreign currency swap.

The major impacts on adjusted EBITDA were the same two items with the resulting adjusted EBITDA loss of $7.8 million in the current quarter. Cash flow was $2.3 million in the quarter primarily due to the insurance recovery. The cash cost per pound of copper produced was calculated only for Huckleberry at US$2.20 per pound, compared to US$2.06 per pound in the June 2014 period.

The cash cost per pound of copper produced for Red Chris and Mount Polley will be included in the September quarter as Red Chris reach commercial production in July and operations from Mount Polley were restarted in August.

At June 31, 2015 Imperial has cash of $17.5 million and working capital deficiency of $73.4. In July the company filed the rights offering circular which provided the details of three financing to raise the total gross proceeds of $80 million.

The private placement of shares closed earlier this week providing $6 million to the company’s treasury. The full back stopped $44 million rights offering and $30 million convertible debenture financings are expected to close on or about August 24th.

Net proceeds from three financing trenches will be use to complete their ramp up at Red Chris sits with the restart of operations at Mount Polley repay this $30 million short term loan facility that company received in May 2015 and provide fund for general working capital purposes.

The funds from the financings projected cash flow from the Red Chris and Mount Polley mines and other additional sources of financing are expected it to be sufficient which allow the company to meet its obligations in the normal course, inclusive of debt servicing costs and principle repayments. Other than principle repayments of about a $1 million per month on equipment loans the company has no long debt coming due within the next 12 months.

The majority of the company’s long term debt is due in 2019 and beyond. Only the senior credit facility and second lien credit facility are due in the intervening period. Financial covenants on those facilities are not applicable until the quarter ending March 31, 2016.

Brian Kynoch

Okay. Thank you, Andre. So operator we’re ready for the question period now.
