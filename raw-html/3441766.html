Astea International's (ATEA) CEO Zack Bergreen on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 14, 2015 6:04 PM ET  |  About: Astea International, Inc. (ATEA) by: SA Transcripts

Astea International, Inc. (NASDAQ:ATEA)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 14, 2015 04:30 PM ET

Executives

Zack Bergreen - Chairman and Chief Executive Officer

Rick Etskovitz - Chief Financial Officer

John Tobin - President

Analysts

Sam Robotsky - SCR Management

Operator

Welcome to the Astea International Inc. Second Quarter 2015 Financial Results Conference Call. You are currently in listen-only mode. Later you will have the opportunity to ask questions during the question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions]

With us today from Astea International Incorporated are Zack Bergreen, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer; Rick Etskovitz, Chief Financial Officer; and John Tobin, President.

With this statement concluded, I will turn the call over to Mr. Bergreen. Please go ahead. The floor is yours.

Zack Bergreen

Thank you, Elisa. Good afternoon and thank you for joining us today to discuss second quarter and year-to-date 2015 results. Following my overview, I’ll turn the call over to Rick Etskovitz, our Chief Financial Officer who will cover details of our financial results. We will then open the call for Q&A.

I’m pleased to start off today’s call highlighting the positive momentum that we’re experiencing today. For the second quarter, we achieved a 22% increase in overall revenue, and 83% increase in software license revenues. For the first half of 2015, we had a 20% increase in total revenue and 10% increase in license revenue.

Our license sales during the second quarter have increased our existing backlog for professional services, which was derived higher level of professional services throughout the remainder of 2015 and beyond. As said in the prior earning conference calls, we had many large scale multi-national projects which we will increase this total time as we expand our new account and geographic deployment. Some of those projects have already has a positive impact on our second quarter results.

This customer met certain demands from objective in the deployment plan, we were not purchasing additional licenses for expanding uses of our software to incorporate other operating regions and division. Given this visibility, we fully recognize the vast opportunities that lies ahead of us. We are excited to be in the platform of choice to facilitate the sales confirmation initiative.

When it comes to product development we are very excited about new product release we will be announcing shortly. This release contains significant number of innovations to deliver substantial operational and financial improvement for our customers.

Looking ahead we are already walking on our next major product release with innovative functionalities that will focus on expanding further our leadership position winning new business and driving several of the revenue growth.

Innovation is the hallmark of our company and are especially part of our ongoing commitment to our customers and the key driver of value creation for our shareholders. With that I will turn the call over to Rick Etskovitz, to provide a financial review. We will then catch some additional closing comments after he finishes the review. Go ahead Rick.

Rick Etskovitz

Thank you, Zack. For the quarter ended June 30, 2015, Astea reported revenues of $6.5 million compared to revenues of $5.3 million for the same period in 2014. Net loss for the second quarter was $0.3 million or $0.11 per share compared to a net loss of $0.8 million or $0.23 per share for the same period in 2014. Software license revenues were $2.2 million, compared to $1.2 million for the same period in 2014. Service and maintenance revenues were $4.1 million compared to revenues of $3.9 million for the same period in 2014.

Revenues for the six months ended June 30, 2015 increased 22% to $12.4 million compared to $10.1 million in the same six-month period in 2014. Software license revenues increased 101% to $3.3 million compared to $1.7 million for the same period in 2014. Service and maintenance revenues were $8.6 million, a 5% increase compared to revenues of $8.2 million for the same period in 2014. Net loss was $1.1 million or $0.37 per share compared to a net loss of $2.0 million or $0.60 per share for the same six-month period last year.

Adjusted EBITDA was $1.4 million for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 compared to $0.2 million for the same period in 2014. The first half of 2015 adjusted EBITDA was $1.8 million compared to $0.1 million for the same period in 2014. This measure eliminates the variation in operating results that occur from accounting rules related to the deferral of hosting and implementation fees on hosted customers, as well as the rules related to the capitalization and amortization of development costs.

This concludes my remarks and I will turn the call back over to Zack.

Zack Bergreen

Thank you Rick. With our product and partner initiatives Astea is a much stronger company in the marketplace today and heading into the future. We are encouraged by our recent performance. Our customers continue to see tremendous value in our software and services which reflect in the expansion of the aligned solution footprint in main customers throughout the world.

Our professional services teams have substantial backlog of the work, which will continue well in 2016 worth experiencing some nice uptick and the demand of our product and our customer excitement is high for our next wave of product innovation. We continue to execute against our plan and remain laser focused on growing the company while continuing transition to a fast business model.

Our differentiated solution suite, domain expertise, and ongoing commitment to innovation will enable us to extend the competitive advantages built-up over the years, while driving our future market success. Thank you for taking the time to attend today’s call. I would like to now to open the call for Q&A.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

[Operator Instructions] Our first question comes from Sam Robotsky with SCR Management. Please go ahead.

Sam Robotsky

Good afternoon Zack, Rick, and John.

Zack Bergreen

Good afternoon Sam.

Rick Etskovitz

Hello Sam.

Sam Robotsky

It seems to be - is this a transition or change and let me understand the revenue that you produced from these licenses. Does this change anything with the Cloud, do you have the number on what the Cloud produced?

Rick Etskovitz

You asked a bunch of questions Sam.

Sam Robotsky

Okay.

Rick Etskovitz

So, I will give one at a time.

Sam Robotsky

Yes. That’s good.

Rick Etskovitz

Okay. Software license revenues were 2.79 million, 2.1 million [ph], up from 1.2 for the same period last year. Subscription revenues were 234,000 compared to 194,000 last year and service and maintenance was 4.1 compared to 3.9 last year for the quarter.

Sam Robotsky

Yes. So, in other words, I don’t hear a number what you produced in the Cloud. Is this the license revenue from the Cloud or is this license revenue where you are not going to get any additional revenue in the Cloud.

Rick Etskovitz

The license revenue is strictly from perpetual license sales - the license revenue itself has no Cloud’s revenue connected to it. Most of our Cloud revenues up to this point is still entered in our differed accounts, not yet recognized. The differed accounts has grown significantly since last quarter and they continue to grow. So, once these projects go live we will start to recognize that revenue.

Sam Robotsky

And the release talks about significant improving going forward with licensing revenue. So, has the customer sort of gotten more of - more willing to license the revenue, which will produce significant profit sooner and is that happening?

Zack Bergreen

What happens is that a number of our larger customers start off with a limited deployment and once they go live and they’ve proven it successful, they will then add different regions, they may add at different department and then once they proven, they are happy with what they saw for the purchase and getting the returns and better than they expected, they start buying more and more significant larger number of licenses. But those are for existing customers, it is not necessarily an indication that the buying public has changed and there is an significant interest in the hosted opportunity. It just said, existing customers have already made the commitment to jump in and purchase perpetual licenses as they expand, we’ll continue to purchase perpetual licenses.

Sam Robotsky

Okay. Does this mean that your pipeline has increased significantly, Zack? Are you getting more momentum from your pipeline?

Zack Bergreen

The pipeline, Sam, is really a function of inevitably the buying appetite of the customers and our deep prospects and frankly the customers that we have. So as we engage with these prospects typically not aged, we typically being asked to offer two alternatives for purchase, one is an on-premise and the other one is the perpetual. Basically these vessels will like to held the option to analyze these two alternatives and make the financial decision and operational decision which way to go. We typically have a limited availability to influence the decision one way or the other, we simply try to be as informative and as complete as we can with the pricing model for each of those deployments.

At a conclusion of that proposal stage, we fundamentally see two things taking place. They either select an on-premise or an SaaS model and but typically nowadays the customers have become sophisticated and they tend to basically commence the deployment in one particular locale, it could be a country, it could be a division, it could be a branch whatever the particular decision made, the operational decision. Once that deployment, that initial deployment concluded in one of those two models, they will then expand and then broaden - then it’s just a matter of roll out. But remember under the so-called [ph], we have revenue possibilities in the backlog but vessels based on the already existing deployments that we have started in addition to the so-called deferred revenues that we already projecting in what’s called the [indiscernible] or the deployment phase as well as we have new projects that we are working on that we have a very clear visibility as to when they will likely conclude and therefore we will also add the additional revenues that we anticipate.

Sam Robotsky

This is the most exciting thing that has happened in a long time, Zack. And based on this press release, it appears that things should improve. Is it fair to say, is there a trend that has started that will increase the sales revenue, license revenue going forward that by the end of this year we might be profitable?

Zack Bergreen

Certainly. We have quite a few projects in fact we are very stretched I think is probably a fair comment on the professional services to deploy these mission projects across the globe. The demand on our services is very, very high. We’re also utilizing some of our partners to help us in some of the ones. So clearly the sooner we can deploy, the quicker we can recognize the revenue and so on. And we also obviously working on a number of new opportunities which we believe will happen between now and end of the year, and which will certainly build not only the license possibilities for us but also professional service possibilities for us well into 2016.

But again, Sam, I want to remind you that on the SaaS revenue, even if we would have cloned a single deal, so this is hypothetical for $1 billion today, it would not have made an ounce of impact on our imported results because that really – part of that would not have been influenced by absolutely, completely – there is no possible likelihood that would be impacting anyway because we have the SaaS model with the project – the customers have to go live before we can possibly recognize any revenue as such. And even after that time, the revenue is prorated and spread across the remaining portion of the contract which is typically three years. So it really complicates the whole revenue delivery for all SaaS companies but certainly for Astea as well.

Sam Robotsky

Okay. I have a couple of more questions. I’ll come back into queue and if there aren’t any questions, I’ll be back shortly but I’ll let somebody ask a question. But it seems much better, good luck, but I’m going to come back into the queue.

Operator

And Sam, you can actually go right ahead.

Sam Robotsky

Okay. Thank you. Do we have a number of a comparison of what the SaaS backlog might be that have been produced? Is there a way of knowing what the numbers might be?

Zack Bergreen

Deferred revenues relating to SaaS as of June 30 was $4.4 million.

Sam Robotsky

And compared to - the previous quarter margin compared to the same quarter last year?

Zack Bergreen

Right. So as for same quarter and as of – I don’t have that number handy.

Sam Robotsky

Okay.

Zack Bergreen

But I can assure you that in every quarter that deferred number has grown. The deferred revenue increases by services we perform or by hosting revenue that we received and then it goes down by hosting revenue we recognized and hosting services we recognized. So as it increases, it’s showing a net growth in that business.

Sam Robotsky

Okay. That’s good. Now, as far as this new product that you expect to bring it out, is there a timeframe when you expect to do it or you prepared to tell the market when you’re going to bring this new product. And I don’t want to steal it under about what the product is going to do, but whatever you can say I would appreciate.

Zack Bergreen

Yes, Sam, it’s not a new product. It’s a new version. It’s a newer version, technology has improved more functionality but it’s not a new product and it’s going to during this third quarter.

Sam Robotsky

Okay. Good. And as far as your balance sheet, I think at the end of the last quarter, your net equity deficit was about $700,000, you lost another $300,000, is there a need for any capital at this point?

Zack Bergreen

The reason that the equity decline is because of the funky accounting for the SaaS revenue that’s causing us to recognize expenses in the current period but not the revenue. The reality is that our cash flow is positive, so it’s not causing any need for any digital capital.

Sam Robotsky

I guess one of the things, I wanted to sort of comment Zack, it’s Friday, its 4:30 most people have gone. I would think based on what you’ve accomplished today, I think it would be good, I think there are people would like to know about it sooner and to participate in a conference call. So to the extent that you can do it where there is more of a possibility, it would appear it would be beneficial to the company. What do you think of that?

Zack Bergreen

Certainly, Friday afternoon on a summer day, I think it is not a ideal time to have a earnings call. I fully appreciate it and I think everybody understand that. We as a company have traditionally throughout the years always done the conference call at the end – the last day of the quarter, the filing stage as well. And to that end, we tend to not deviate or some of it is driven by auditors and their schedule and their ability to conclude the audit process and so on. So it’s unfortunate that today this quarter, it fell on Friday afternoon. It’s certainly not by our own doing and we shall need to strive to do this as soon as we can but I do appreciate the comment, these are valid time.

Sam Robotsky

I mean at this point in time, you have to realize that this revenue, the license revenue is a phenomenal increase relative to where we’ve been and to the extent you can continue this improvement to the extent that the clients that you’ve introduced your product too and they want to signed up for additional license revenue. At some point, you should become profitable sooner than later if you could continue to improve on this.

Zack Bergreen

Appreciate the comment, Sam, but certainly our objective as well.

Sam Robotsky

Do we think we could be profitable by the fourth quarter? I mean Rick, if you look at when we have a visibility based on our pipeline and what we’re doing, when we decide that it’s going to turnaround so to say.

Rick Etskovitz

The possibility definitely exists as we stated the new pipeline is strong but the pipeline is still pipeline and we have to close these deals and then become a reality, and that unfortunately it tends to always bunch up towards the end of the quarter. So I really can’t make any strong projections and I can tell you that certainly what was driving for us.
