Cleantech Solutions' (CLNT) Management on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 14, 2015 10:56 AM ET  |  About: Cleantech Solutions International, Inc. (CLNT) by: SA Transcripts

Cleantech Solutions International, Inc. (NASDAQ:CLNT)

Q2 2015 Results Earnings Conference Call

August 14, 2015, 9:00 AM ET

Executives

Adam Wasserman – Chief Financial Officer and Principal Accounting Officer

Ryan Hua – Vice President of Operations

Elaine Ketchmere – Compass IR

Analysts

Matthew Larson – Morgan Stanley

Operator

Good day and welcome to the Cleantech Solutions International Second Quarter 2015 Earnings Conference Call. All participants will be in listen-only mode. [Operator Instructions] After today's presentation there will be an opportunity to ask questions. [Operator Instructions] Please note this event is being recorded.

I would now like to turn the conference over to Elaine Ketchmere. Please go ahead.

Elaine Ketchmere

Thank you, operator and good morning, ladies and gentlemen and good evening to those of you joining in from China. I'd like to welcome all of you to Cleantech Solutions' earnings conference call for the second quarter of 2015.

With me today on the call are Cleantech Solutions' CFO, Mr. Adam Wasserman and Vice President of Operations, Mr. Ryan Hua. Also on the call is Maple Jong from Compass Investor Relations, who will provide translation for Mr. Hua.

At this time, I'll remind our listeners that on this call management's prepared remarks contain forward-looking statements, which are subject to risks and uncertainties and that management may make additional forward-looking statements in response to your question. Therefore, the company claims the protection of the Safe Harbor in its forward-looking statements that is contained in the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995.

Actual results may differ from those discussed today and therefore we refer you to a more detailed discussion of the risks and uncertainties in the Company's filings with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, including factors described in Risk Factors and Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations in our Form 10-K for the year ended December 31, 2014 and management's discussion and analysis of financial condition and results of operations in our Form 10-Q for the quarter ended June 30, 2015. In addition, any projections as to the Company's future performance represent management's estimates and are made as of today August 14, 2015. Cleantech Solutions International Inc. assumes no obligation to update these projections in the future as conditions change.

At this point, I would also like to state that on this call, we will be discussing a non-GAAP financial measure, EBITDA. We present this financial measure as a supplement to our GAAP results, because we believe it provides useful information in analysing and benchmarking the performance of our operations and assists investors in analysing our year-over-year financial performance. Please visit our earnings press release, a copy of which is on our website and has been filed with the SEC as an exhibit to our Form 8-K for a complete reconciliation of EBITDA to the nearest GAAP equivalent.

And now, it is my pleasure to welcome Cleantech Solutions' CFO, Mr. Adam Wasserman who will deliver management's prepared remarks, covering operations and financial performance today. Adam, please proceed.

Adam Wasserman

Okay, thank you Elaine. Good morning everyone and thank you for joining us on our call today. We appreciate your continued support and interest in Cleantech Solutions. In the second quarter of 2015 our total revenue declined by 13% to $15.2 million despite a savings measured by the Chinese government to lift spending and cut interest rate, credit conditions remain tight and fixed asset spending continues to slow in the second quarter. This economic climate adversely affected our forged rolled rings and related components business in particular which saw a 62% decline in revenue during the quarter to $3 million. We believe that there is a degree of market saturation for forged rolled rings and related components for wind power and other industries, and anticipate that our revenue for the segment will continue to decrease in the near future.

Sales in our dyeing equipment segment also declined as concerns regarding the ability of some of our customers to make timely payments caused us a delay in shipment of certain orders. In addition, we experienced softer demand for our low-emission airflow dyeing machines, as many of our customers upgraded to new models last year. As a result, sales from our dyeing equipment segment declined 11% year-over-year in the second quarter to $8.6 million.

We're encouraged by the interest shown in some of our new styles of textile equipment, including environmentally friendly air-fluid, dual-use dyeing machine and our garment washing machine. At the 17th International Exhibition on Textile Industry held in Shanghai, in June, we received orders from several domestic customers and enquiries from a number of international textile manufacturers. We expect these new orders to help offset softer demand for our low-emission airflow dyeing machines in the second half of the year. We also are hopeful that we will receive payment for the temporarily delayed shipment of our low emissions airflow dyeing machines in the near future.

On balance, we believe our dyeing equipment segment will continue to be our largest revenue driver going forward, although sales will remain at or near the second quarter level for the balance of the year. Strong sales for our newest business segment, the petroleum and chemical equipment market helped to partially offset the sales decline in our other segments. We generated $3.6 million in revenue from petroleum and chemical equipment sales in the second quarter of 2015, primarily from the major contract we received from a large state owned enterprise for parts and equipment to be used on a major chemical project in Xinjiang, which generated revenue of approximately $3.1 million or 87.7% of the petroleum and chemical segment revenue for the second quarter of 2015.

We have made good progress executing on this $13 million contract which covers parts and equipment, including washing, distillation, purifying, dehydration and refining other columns along with storage containers, seat exchangers and carbonation reactors that will be used to transform glycol calcium carbide furnace tail gas into ethylene glycol. We believe this contract is helping us establish our reputation as a high quality manufacturer of equipment and system through the petroleum and chemical industry and we plan to utilize it to expand into other areas such as power generation in large infrastructure projects.

In sum, despite the challenging economic condition, we remained profitable, generated positive cash flow from operating activities and closed the quarter with a stronger balance sheet.

A closer look at our financial performance. I would encourage you to refer to our Form 10-Q filed with the SEC and our earnings press release issued earlier today. Our revenue for the second quarter of 2015 decreased 13.3% to $15.2 million, compared to $17.5 million for the same quarter of 2014. The decrease was the result of a lower sales of forged rolled rings and related components to customers in the wind power and other industries and to dyeing and finishing equipment customers compared to the comparative quarter last year. These decreases were partially offset by sales of equipment to customers in the petroleum and chemical industries.

The gross profit for the second quarter decreased by 34.1% to $2.7 million, compared to $4.1 million for the same period in 2014. Gross margin was 17.7%, during the second quarter of 2015, compared to 23.3% for the same period a year ago. The decrease in gross margin for the second quarter of 2015, was primarily attributable to our smaller scale of operations resulting from lower revenues, including the allocation of fixed costs, mainly consisting of depreciation, to cost of revenues in the forged product and components and dyeing and finishing equipment segments, combined with a slightly increase in labour costs; the production of new styles of dyeing machines; and the lower gross margin from the sales of equipment to customers in the petroleum and chemical industries. This segment currently has a low gross margin because the Company is a new entrant into the market and is offering low prices to attract customers and we cannot assure you that we will be able to improve our gross margin in the segment.

Operating expenses decreased 6% to $1 million, compared to $1.1 million in the comparable period last year. The decrease was primarily due to our lower selling, general and administrative expenses, partially offset by an increase in depreciation expenses related to new office equipment, furniture and other improvements which the Company started depreciating.

Operating income was $1.7 million, compared to $3 million in the same period of 2014. Income tax was $0.4 million compared to $0.8 million in the same period of 2014. EBITDA, a non-GAAP, which adds to net income interest expense, income tax, depreciation and amortization, was $3.8 million, compared to $5.2 million in the second quarter of last year. Net income was $1.2 million, or $0.31 per basic and diluted share, compared to $2.2 million, or $0.61 per basic and diluted share, in the same quarter of 2014.

Now, let's turn to the balance sheet. As of June 30, 2015, we had cash and cash equivalents of $16.8 million, compared to $7.8 million at December 31, 2014. Accounts receivable were $18.5 million, compared to $20.3 million at December 31, 2014. Inventory was $4.6 million compared to $4.2 million at December 31, 2014. We had $3.3 million in short-term bank loans payable and stockholders' equity of $100.9 million. In the first half of 2015, we generated $8.7 million in cash from operations compared to $5.3 million in the first half of 2014.

In conclusion, although the Chinese economy is flowing, showing some kinds of an improvement, we expect capital spending by our customers to remain low, particularly in the forged rolled rings and related components business. We are hopeful our dyeing equipment business will remain relatively stable supported by newer models of textile equipment, such as our air-fluid, dual-use dyeing machine and garment washing machine. We expect our petroleum and chemical equipment segment to make a large contribution to revenue as we continue to make good progress in fulfilling the orders we had on hand. We are excited by the growth opportunities available to these industries and are also exploiting opportunities to expand to other industries.

We want to thank all of our investors for your continued support. With that, we now open the call for Q&A from the audience.
