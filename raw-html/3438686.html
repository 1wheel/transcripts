Roka Bioscience's (ROKA) CEO Paul Thomas on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 6:16 PM ET  |  About: Roka Bioscience (ROKA) by: SA Transcripts

Roka Bioscience (NASDAQ:ROKA)

Q2 2015 Results Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015, 04:30 PM ET

Executives

Paul Thomas - President and CEO

Steven Sobieski - CFO

Analysts

Mike Sarcone - Leerink

Derek de Bruin - Bank of America Merrill Lynch

Operator

Good day, ladies and gentlemen. Thank you for standing by and welcome to the Roka Bioscience Second Quarter 2015 Conference Call.

At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. Later, we will conduct a question-and-answer session and instructions will follow at that time. As a reminder, this conference call is being recorded.

I would now like to turn the conference to our host Mr. Steven Sobieski, Chief Financial Officer. Please go ahead sir.

Steven Sobieski

Thank you. Good afternoon, and thank you for joining us on our second quarter 2015 earnings call.

During this call, Paul Thomas, ROKA's CEO and I will discuss the financial results we released earlier today. Our press release discussing second quarter results is available in the Investor Section of our website. A replay of this call will also be archived on our website.

Before we get started, I’d like to remind everyone that certain statements made during our call today maybe forward-looking statements made pursuant to the Safe Harbor provisions of the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995, the Securities Act of 1933, and the Securities Exchange Act of 1934. These forward-looking statements speak only as of the date hereof and are based on our current expectations, estimates, forecasts and projections about the industry and markets in which we operate and management’s current beliefs and assumptions.

There are number of important risk factors that could cause actual results to differ materially from those indicated by such forward looking statements and such risk factors include those set forth in the company’s filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission. We disclaim any obligation to update any forward looking statements except as maybe required by law.

I will now turn the call over to Paul.

Paul Thomas

Thanks Steve. As we discussed on prior conference calls, near the end of last year, we commenced the development of a more user friendly version of our Listeria assay that is designed to deliver the same rapid time to results as our first generation Listeria assay, but with a simplified work flow.

Recently we announced that we received certification from the AOAC research institute for our new Atlas Listeria Environmental Detection Assay. This new assay is approved for the detection of Listeria from environmental services after a 24-hour enrichment and was specifically designed to meet the unique challenges of Listeria environmental testing in the food industry.

With recent events reported in the media involving Listeria outbreaks, we believe that Listeria environmental testing plays an important role for food safety. We're currently preparing for our full commercial launch later on this year.

Our installed base of Atlas instruments did not change in the quarter. As of June 31, we have 41 commercial instruments placed with customers. However, during the second quarter, we did place an additional instrument with one of our existing contract testing lab customers, but we also removed an instrument from an underperforming customer account.

Our revenues have been flat for the last few quarters as of the results of being continuum negative impact from the challenges related to the timing of closing new accounts and the adoption of our new Listeria assay.

We're optimistic that the launch of our new Listeria Environmental Assay will allow us to reignite customer adoption of our Atlas system. Our commercialization strategy remains very focused on targeting customers that perform high volume pathogen testing in key market segments such beef, poultry, produce and contract testing labs.

Let me turn to call back over to Steve to discuss financial results.

Steven Sobieski

Okay. Turning now to the details of our financial results for the second quarter, our revenue for the quarter was $1.5 million compared with $1.4 million for the second quarter of 2014 and $1.5 million for the first quarter of this year.

During the quarter we sold approximately 169,000 assays which was flat with the same quarter in 2014. Our average revenue per instrument was consistent with the level you experienced in the first quarter of this year and approximately 50% of our revenue in the current quarter was derived from three large customers who each account for more than 10% of our total revenue.

As Paul noted in his opening remarks during the quarter we installed one additional Atlas instrument and also removed one underperforming instrument. Revenue has been flat for several quarters due to the challenges related to the timing of closing new accounts and adoption of our Listeria assay that Paul talk about in his remarks.

Our revenue growth in the future is dependent upon our ability to place additional instruments with customers and to increase demand for our Atlas detection assays. Our cost of revenue increased by $182,000 to $1.8 million for the quarter and that's compared to $1.6 million for the same period in 2014. The increase was primarily due to an increase in our inventory provisions.

Research and development expense was $1.9 million for the quarter compared to $2.2 million for the same period in 2014. We expect R&D expense to remain at approximately the current level throughout the remainder of the year.

Selling, general and administrative expense was $5.4 million for the quarter compared to $4.5 million for the same period in 2014. Part of the increase was due to higher expenses associated with being a public company such as insurance and other professional fees. We expect our SG&A expense to remain at approximately throughout the remainder of the year.

Amortization of intangibles increased to $937,000 for the quarter compared to $42,000 for the same period in 2014. The higher expense was due to the increase in our intangible asset in July of 2014, which resulted from the amendment to our license agreement with GenPro. Amortization expense is expected to remain at approximately the current level throughout the remainder of the year.

Our net loss for the second quarter was $9.2 million compared to a net loss of $7.4 million for the same period in 2014 and the higher loss was primarily due to the increase in SG&A expense and intangible amortization.

We ended the quarter with cash and investments of $43.8 million compared to $57.1 million at the end of 2014 and our cash burn during the quarter was approximately $6 million. We expect our quarterly cash burn to remain at approximately the current level throughout the remainder of this year.

We're not providing quarterly or full year financial guidance at this time due to the challenges related to forecasting the timing of placing additional instruments and the adoption rate for our new Listeria Assay.

With that I'll now turn the call back to Paul for closing remarks.

Paul Thomas

Thanks Steve and before we take questions, I just wanted to comment on an announcement we made today that Nick Valeriani has joined the Roka Board of Directors. Mr. Valeriani is currently serving as the CEO of the Gary and Mary West Health Institute and prior to joining West Health, Nick spent 34 years at J&J and served as a member of its Executive Committee.

Nick brings a unique perspective to our Board through his vast diagnostic expertise and track record of driving achievement of strategic and operating objectives. Myself and the other members of the Roka Board look forward to working with Nick as we continue to make progress towards our key initiatives and position Roka for accelerated long term growth.

At this time, let me ask Denise to open up the call to questions.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

Thank you, sir. We will now begin the question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions] The first question will come from Dan Leonard of Leerink. Please go ahead.

Mike Sarcone

Hi, this is Mike Sarcone in for Dan Leonard.

Paul Thomas

Hi Mike.

Mike Sarcone

Hi, do you think you can give us an update on the sales funnel for the new Listeria assay or any color on early customer interactions?

Paul Thomas

I can't get into the details of the sales funnel. As we mentioned on the call, have just received approval from AOAC for our new Listeria assay. We have a number of customers who have been evaluating all Listeria assay in real world samples and we are in the process of gearing up or manufacturing processes so that we can have commercial product in inventory and ready for sale.

That scale of process will take about six to eight weeks. So we expect that we'll be launching our assay in the latter part of this year.

Mike Sarcone

Okay. Thanks, and do you think, I know on the last conference call, you had mentioned you may give an update on the mini Atlas platform? Can you provide any color there?

Paul Thomas

You know Mike, we thought we would be prepared to talk about the mini Atlas at this call, but I think we're going to differ that till the next call. We are just completing some voids of the customer work that we conducted at the Food Safety Meeting in Portland several weeks ago and we want to complete the compilation and analysis of that work before we give further updates on mini Atlas.

Mike Sarcone

Okay. Thank you, guys.

Paul Thomas

Okay. Thank you.

Operator

The next question will come from Derek de Bruin of Bank of America Merrill Lynch. Please go ahead.

Unidentified Analyst

Hi, hello. Good afternoon. This is [Won] [ph] filling in for Derek. I was wondering now that the revised Listeria assay received AOAC Certification. What are some of the requirements by potential customers that maybe outstanding in order to facilitate the commercial launch of this assay?

Just want to know what are some of the requisites or steps required to actually close potential accounts that maybe in the works?

Paul Thomas

Sure, what typically happened Won, and it depends on the customer, but they go through either a verification or a bit more involved validation set of studies that really just confirm that our assay works on real world samples in the same manner as what we went through for the AOAC Certification as well as the Beta studies we have also conducted in parallel with customers.

So that's a process we're actually engaged in and it's part of the adoption process. So we expect that as we complete that work and as we gear up commercial manufacturing processes and put inventory on the shelf that we'll begin to realize benefit from the launch of the new Listeria assay.

Unidentified Analyst

Good and I also was wondering if you had any updates at all regarding these customers that you're working with since a couple of quarters ago?

Paul Thomas

Sure, so over the last couple of quarters we have conducted a number of comparative studies on real world samples with several different beef customers. We followed up with several of those customers at the Food Safety Meeting in Portland several weeks ago.

We feel like we're continuing to make good progress with those customers in the sense that there are some additional work that we need to complete, but I think we're encouraged by the performance of our assay and the benefits that it can deliver to customers with respect to accuracy and automation in particular in the food safety arena.

Unidentified Analyst

Okay. Good and one last question if I may, wondering if you had any -- I know the focus has been in time to get the revised Listeria assay launched, but overall just curious if there has been any development work on the non-amplified assays.

Paul Thomas

Yes, so -- and I think we probably discussed this on the last conference call if I recall correct, we really put all hands of deck to get the revised Listeria assay through AOAC approval. So that pushed back a bit some of the work that we were doing on a number of new product initiatives including non-amplified assays and our mini Atlas work and several other initiatives within the company.

Now that we have the AOAC Certification on Listeria behind us, we can now turn our attention back towards mini Atlas and non-amplified assays and some of the other initiatives that we have ongoing and certainly by the next conference call I'll be able to update you on the mini Atlas program in particular because we think that's a very important and interesting addition and compliment actually to the work we're doing with Atlas.

Unidentified Analyst

Okay. Thank you.

Paul Thomas

Thanks Won.

Operator

And ladies and gentlemen, this will conclude our question-and-answer session. I would like to turn the conference back over to Steven Sobieski for any his closing remarks.

Steven Sobieski

Thank you and I want to thank everyone for participating in our call today and we look forward to updating you on our next conference call in connection with our third quarter results. Thank you.

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, the conference has now concluded. We thank you for attending today’s presentation. You may now disconnect.
