InterCloud Systems' (ICLD) CEO Mark Munro on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 12, 2015 12:30 PM ET  |  About: InterCloud Systems, Inc. (ICLD) by: SA Transcripts

InterCloud Systems Inc (NASDAQ:ICLD)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 12, 2015 10:00 AM ET

Executives

Tim Larkin - CFO

Mark Munro - Chairman & CEO

Analysts

Andrew Nowinski - Piper Jaffray

Operator

Good day and welcome to the InterCloud Systems Inc Second Quarter 2015 Earnings Call. Today's conference is being recorded. At this time, I would like to turn the conference over to Tim Larkin, Chief Financial Officer. Please go ahead sir.

Tim Larkin

Thank you, Anna. Thank you for joining us for InterCloud Systems second quarter earnings conference call. I am here with our Chairman and CEO, Mark Munro.

Before we get underway, I'd like to ask everyone to take note of the Safe Harbor paragraph that appears at the end of this morning's news release. This paragraph states that any forward-looking statements that we make, speaks only as of the date made and are subject to inherent risks and uncertainties including those described in the company's most recently filed reports with the SEC.

Except as otherwise required by federal securities law, we disclaim any obligation or undertaking to publicly release any updates or revisions to any forward-looking statements contained herein or elsewhere to reflect any change in our expectations with regard to any changes in events, conditions or circumstances on which any such statement is made.

And now, I would like to introduce InterCloud's Chairman and CEO, Mark Munro. Mark?

Mark Munro

Thank you Tim, good morning everyone and before I turn the call back over to Tim our CFO, Tim Larkin our CFO to review second quarter 2015 results I wanted to take a little time to review some of our accomplishments in the second quarter of 2015 and the first half.

First off we beat second quarter estimates on both revenue and earnings this quarter, second quarter 2015 revenue growth of 25% over 2014, first half year revenue growth grew over 35% over 2014 first half. Another important note will be first half year adjusted EBITDA grew to over 1 million and during the second quarter we made the final payments and fully repaid during Q2 2015 our December 2013, $11.7 million debenture. So that's fully repaid and been extinguished. These are just a few of the highlights and significant accomplishments in the first half of 2015.

Overall InterCloud continues to make financial improvements over 2014 and we're pleased with our year date results. In the first half of this year we increased revenue over 2014 by 35% and did this by adding both new customers in enterprise and service provider markets as well as a higher sell through in existing customers. The ongoing success within our sales group as well as our new product roll out gives us confidence in our continued success for the remainder of 2015 and beyond. In addition to strong revenue growth InterCloud increased gross margins to 32% for the first half of 2015 an increase of 2% over the first half of 2014.

Gross margins were lower in Q2 2015 compared to Q1 of 2015 and this reduction was primarily affected by one major sale at a lower than normal gross margin so it's not, what we're getting at is the margins are sustainable and in the mid 30 range and we're very excited about that. Our goal is to continue to build up gross margins by selling higher margin cloud and managed services revenues. The first half of 2015 adjusted EBITDA was over 1 million and is also a very critical and important achievement.

This was accomplished in increased revenue and margins over 2014 as well as cost containment at the corporate level. The company has continued to scale while maintaining corporate expense level. Corporate costs will become a smaller percentage of expenses as the company achieves new revenue milestones in the future. I'm now going to spend a few, some time reviewing some of our business highlights in the second quarter. In a recent announcement we discussed briefly a sale to Russell Reynolds a global organization with over 50 offices.

The sale's a particularly important example as it involved the new cloud based solution that was only officially introduced in May of 2015 by InterCloud. It proves the flexibility and scalability of our cloud platform and the ability of our software engineering team to quickly rollout a proof of concept services in a matter of months to live our production grade solution to our partner Aruba Networks and our client Russell Reynolds.

This opportunity will produce over 300,000 in revenue over a five year period. Of more importantly than its scale is InterCloud now has a solution for all multi-site Aruba Networks Wi-Fi opportunities. InterCloud has introduced the managed service solution for the customer which allows for the elimination of service at each and every location for the management of the network. InterCloud is now positioned extremely well against all competition as we have a centralized cloud based orchestration platform for all Wi-Fi based networks worldwide. Enterprise Wi-Fi management and deployment is a fast growing segment of IT, as CIOs must gain control of the growth of mobility and the security risk that mobility poses to the overall IT platform.

Another key milestone during the quarter was our unveiling of software defined networking proof of concept of POC as a service, as we call it. We did this in May at the OpenStack Conference in Vancouver, Canada. The idea of proof of concept as a service has turned out to be extremely valuable for our Company and our clients. One of the difficulties for CIOs of enterprises and service providers today is, is how do they determine how they can use a software defined network and NFV or network function virtualization to rebuild our existing hardware based IT environment.

SDN and NFV are new enough that it’s difficult for CIOs to get budgets that include the flexibility to actually demo these next generation networks, why they’re in the environment. InterCloud offers existing and new client the ability to test and learn what a software defined network based IT environment actually operates like and looks like to the end user. This allow our sales teams to introduce our net product dPod or Dedicated Private Cloud with specific compute storage and virtualized networking functions in a preconfigured box like package. The preconfigured package as a software defined cloud platform with OpenStack at the core and InterCloud for proprietary unified orchestration platform NFV grid included.

This can save our channel partners’ and customers’ one to two years of testing and expenses to deploy. According to software SDxCentral an analyst group, the 2015 software defined networking and network function virtualization market size report predicts the combined revenue impact of SDN and NFV network virtualization and other next generation networking initiatives will exceed 105 billion per year in 2020. This is important because the core of InterCloud strategy is to be a major competitor in this market space over the next five years.

InterCloud can deploy a software defined network and NFV or virtualized networks today along with our easy to use drag and drop orchestration portal NFV grid. The platform enables our network service provider and enterprise customers to quickly launch network functions. Imagine being able to rollout your entire virtual network, virtual compute and virtual storage infrastructure from a single intuitive and consistent orchestration system. The projects that took months to finish can now be completed in hours with readily available skills in a simple repeatable workflow.

Software defined networking makes it possible to deploy, configure and modify networking on the fly and Juniper Networks OpenContrail software provides the best underpinning cloud network automation platform upon which to base such a capability. We built this because we envision a world where the creation and consumption of virtual IT is a dynamic and fully automated experience. Our products were born in the cloud for the cloud.

Now I’d like to turn the call back over to Tim Larkin, our CFO. Tim?

Tim Larkin

Thanks Mark. Revenue for the second quarter ended June 30, 2015 increased by $4.4 million or 25% to $22.2 million. This compares to $17.8 million for the second quarter of 2014. Organically, we had a growth rate of 10%, primarily from our managed services and professional services segment and the balance of our growth was due to acquisitions in our cloud segment.

Gross profit percent is 30% for the second quarter of 2015 compared to 32% for the comparable period in 2014. The overall decrease in gross profit percentage was due a decrease in our managed services segment in applications an infrastructure segment. This decrease was offset by an increase in gross profit from our cloud segment. Our gross profit percentage of our cloud segment was 83%.

The Company had a net loss attributable to common stockholders of $14.4 million for the second quarter of 2015 as compared to a loss of $10 million for 2014. The increase in net loss was due to an increase in salary and wages of $4.8 million, a change in the fair value of derivatives of $3 million and a loss of exchange of shares of $2.3 million. These increases were offset by an increase in gross profit of $1 million and a decrease in the loss on the extinguishment of debt of $2.8 million.

Salaries and wages included a non-cash stock compensation expense of $5.2 million for the second quarter of 2015 compared to $1.1 million in 2014. The company reported four segments. During the second quarter of 2015 the cloud segment had revenue of $2.7 million and a gross profit of $2.2 million compared to no revenue or gross profit 2014. Managed services had revenue of $8.5 million and a gross profit of $1.7 million compared to $7.9 million in revenue and a gross profit of $2.7 million in 2014. Professional services had revenue of $6.3 million and a gross profit of $1.2 million compared to revenue of $5.4 million and a gross profit of $1.1 million in the same period in 2014. Lastly applications and infrastructure had revenue of $4.8 million and a gross profit of $1.5 million compared to revenue of $4.6 million and a gross profit of $1.9 million in 2014.

Revenue for the six months ended June 30th, 2015 increased by $11.3 million or 35% to $43.2 million. This compared to $31.9 million for 2014. Organically we had a growth rate of 18%, primarily from our managed services and professional services segments and the balance of our growth was due to acquisitions in our cloud segment.

Gross profit percentage was 32% for the six months ended June 30th, 2015 compared to 30% for the comparable period of 2014. The overall increase in gross profit percentage was due to an increase in our gross profit percentage from our cloud segment. The gross profit of our cloud segment was 84% during the six months ended June 30th, 2015. The segment had no revenue in 2014.

Company had a net loss attributable to common stockholders of $24.8 million for the six months ended June 30th, 2015 compared to net income of $456,000 in 2014. The increase in net loss is due to a change in the fair value of derivative of $23 million, an increase in salary and wages of $6.9 million, a loss on exchange of shares of $2.3 million and a decrease in the benefit from the provision for income taxes of $2.2 million. These increases were offset by an increase in gross profit of $4.4 million and a decrease in the loss on extinguishing the debt of $6.9 million. Salary and wages include non-cash stock compensation expense of $6.5 million for the six months ended June 30th, 2015, compared to $1.3 million for 2014.

During the second quarter, during the six months ended 2015 the cloud segment had revenue of $5.4 million and a gross profit of $4.6 million compared to no revenue or gross profit 2014. Managed services had revenue of $15.1 million and a gross profit of $4.1 million compared to $13.1 million in revenue and a gross profit of $4 million 2014. Professional services had revenue of $13.2 million and a gross profit of $2.7 million compared to revenue of $10.3 million and a gross profit of $2.1 million in the same period of 2014.

Lastly applications and infrastructure had revenue of $9.5 million and gross profit of $2.7 million compared to revenue of $8.5 million and a gross profit of $3.5 million in 2014. For the six months of 2015 company had cash flow from operating activities of $752,000.

And now I'll turn the call back to Mark. Mark?

Mark Munro

Thank you, Tim. I'd now like to take a few minutes to talk more about the robust markets that we serve. I've spoken quite a bit about our products in more details in the past, because it's important for all of our investors to have a chance of seeing more detail about the value we believe we are creating here at InterCloud. It's never easy to blaze a new trail in any industry but we believe in our strategy to deliver a fully automated IT experience.

I noted earlier, one analyst's predictions around SDN and NFV, the IT markets at a 105 billion by 2020. Over the past 24 months we've seen the market forecast increase dramatically in this arena as CIOs of enterprise and service provider markets have begun to focus on transitioning legacy, hardware based networks to the fully virtualized SDN networks that we offer. InterCloud began building its solutions two years ago to meet a demand that we knew was about to grow exponentially.

Last year as an example AT&T announced it was no longer interested in buying hardware based solutions from its vendors. They sent them away and said bring it back virtualized software defined networking solutions. This triggered a major market reaction and even more focus on SDN and NFV. InterCloud at the time was already well on our way to delivering fully automated virtualized solutions to this market. We continue to focus our capital on R&D for our uniquely competitive cloud solutions.

We continue to build on existing partnership, focus on building proof of concepts for our largest customers to validate and test our solutions and have been building out an industry leading software development team past two years that can continue to develop leading edge features in customized as our clients request. We look forward to continuing to grow our strategic partnerships, customer relationships and organization that meet the demands of the next generation IT.

Thank you. This concludes management’s review and I will now move on to questions from our investors.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

[Operator Instructions] And we’ll go ahead and take Andrew Nowinski with Piper Jaffray.

Andrew Nowinski

Maybe to starting off -- maybe if you just give us your thoughts in the cloud segment. And how it performed relatively to your expectations? And then with regards to the gross margin of the cloud segment, do you think 84% level is where that segment should be operating at, or is there any of that that go higher as you get more sale on that?

Mark Munro

So the first question is, were we happy with the growth in our cloud segment, and then the second question was around the margins, is that correct?

Andrew Nowinski

That’s correct, yes.

Mark Munro

I think two things, I think the thing that we’re probably the most excited about is overall traction in the cloud segment. Clearly we know and you know the market is maturing around SDN and NFV and we were probably a little bit early to the market starting two years ago with building our platform. But we truly think that that’s going to reap huge rewards for us throughout the rest of 2015, ’16, ’17, and beyond. As the market has begun to mature, as you know, SDN was a little slower because to be a -- because you don’t have all the network functions virtualized yet so the real power behind SDN is also having virtualized routing and switching in firewalls that you can deploy within that software defined network.

So I think we’re finally there. It has give us time to get our product to a much more mature state and I think that overall we will garner greater growth going forward in that segment as we sell through to both the enterprise and the carriers that we’re doing POCs for today as those turn into significant revenue streams and opportunities. So, overall I think we’re happy with where we are but we also think there is a long way to go and that group and that division of organization is where our focus is and the attention will be, and hopefully much greater growth as we extend out over the next year to two years.

In terms of the margins, I think overall it will be probably difficult to maintain 83% margins in the cloud but I think especially as you scale and the market matures. But we do believe that we can certainly be north of 50% within that group as we move forward. Does that help?

Andrew Nowinski

Yes, it does. And then maybe if you could just give us at a high level can you call around where you saw the most traction in Q2 whether it’s from service providers or enterprises? And then in terms of looking to the back half of 2015, where do you think you will see the most traction in your customers, whether it’s service provider or enterprise?

Mark Munro

Well, it’s a great question. I think that we’re seeing actually for InterCloud we’re seeing similar traction in both enterprise and in carrier, or in service provider. I’ll touch on service provider for a minute. It’s clear that all service providers really truly have to move to an SDN and NFV environment, we know that to manage their cost, drive flexibility in how they manage their networks, how they provision, et cetera. The second part of it is, obviously the attraction to the service provider segment for our product is the back a date and then also sell more products through the pipes that they’re selling to their customers and their end users, they can sell the same products, virtual firewall, virtual routing as a service, et cetera.

So we see that as an incredible opportunity but right now I think we’re seeing both, we’re seeing traction in both. I think the scale of our opportunities within the service provider segment is larger than in enterprise. And I think that overall, as a whole, enterprise will lag a little bit behind service provider which it typically does when major market shifts like this occur in the IT sector. So I think that we’re happy with both. We have significant proof of concepts in the works with both segments and we expect big things from both of them. But hopefully, that gives you an idea of our thoughts around what’s happening in both of those segments at the moment.

Andrew Nowinski

That certainly does it. And just a quick follow up on that. Is there any sort of margin differential between two separate different stones one or the other are into those segments that’s meaningful?

Mark Munro

What do you mean by marketing differential?

Andrew Nowinski

Margin differential, different gross margins…

Mark Munro

No, I don’t think so. I think it’s early enough in the adoption stage that there is such a lack of capacity of OpenStack engineers on the consulting side and on the planning and strategic development side. And there is such a lack of product actual completed product to deploy in this type of an environment. Rather than hey, we can deploy SDN but then we got to customize all these pieces for you because they're not built yet. I think that that's where we see our advantage we actually have a platform that can be delivered today. But I think that, I don't think you're going to see major differences in margins for quite some time in either segment.

Andrew Nowinski

All right, thanks guys, keep up the good work.

Mark Munro

Thank you, Andrew.

Operator

[Operator Instructions] And there are no further phone questions in queue.

Mark Munro

Okay. Thank you everyone, appreciate you joining our second quarter earnings call and look forward to speaking with you again soon.

Operator

This concludes today's conference. Thank you for your participation.
