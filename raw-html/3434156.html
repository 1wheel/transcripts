EZchip Semiconductor's (EZCH) CEO Eli Fruchter on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 12, 2015 4:44 PM ET  |  About: EZchip Semiconductor Limited (EZCH) by: SA Transcripts

EZchip Semiconductor Ltd. (NASDAQ:EZCH)

Q2 2015 Results Earnings Conference Call

August 11, 2015 10:00 AM ET

Daureen Green - Marketing Communications Manager

Eli Fruchter - CEO

Dror Israel - CFO

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, thank you for standing by. Welcome to the EZchip Semiconductor Second Quarter 2015 Conference Call. All participants are present in listen-only mode. Following management’s formal presentation, instructions will be given for the question-and-answer session.

The remarks of management during their presentations and in the question-and-answer session may contain projections or other forward-looking statements regarding future events or the future performance of the company or the industry.

Please note that the safe harbor statement in today’s press release also covers the contents of this conference call. By now, you should have all received the company’s press release. If you have not received it, you may view it in the News section of our company’s website, www.ezchip.com, or you may call Jeffrey Schreiner, Director, Investor Relations at 408-520-3676.

I would now like to hand over the call to Mrs. Daureen Green of EZchip. Mrs. Green, please go ahead.

Daureen Green

Thank you, operator, and good day, everybody. I would like to welcome all of you to EZchip’s second quarter 2015 earnings conference call. With us on the line today are Mr. Eli Fruchter, CEO; and Mr. Dror Israel, CFO.

Before we begin, I would like to point out that during this call, certain non-GAAP financial measures will be discussed. These non-GAAP measures are used by management to make strategic decisions and forecast future results, and the company believes that these figures provide a better method of evaluating the company’s current performance. A full reconciliation of the company’s non-GAAP financial measures to GAAP financial measures is included in the earnings release.

I will now hand the call over to EZchip’s CEO, Eli Fruchter. Eli?

Eli Fruchter

Thank you, Daureen. Good day everyone and welcome to our second quarter 2015 earnings conference call. First, I would like to discuss our main achievements during the second quarter being one, our strong financial results; two, our Tier-1 NPS design wins; three, the improved customer diversification; and four, the renewed partnership with Marvell.

One, strong financial results: Overall, we are very pleased with our reported second quarter results. Revenues increased 28% year-over-year and 5% sequentially during the second quarter of 2015 to $28.3 million, above the mid-point of our prior guidance range of $27.5 million to $28.5 million.

Gross margin of 76.7% in the second quarter was higher than anticipated driven mainly by favorable mix and combined with lower than expected OpEx spending drove robust second quarter non-GAAP earnings per share of $0.34. Revenues for the first half of 2015 reached $55.2 million, a 30% year-over-year growth compared to $42.4 million during the first half of 2014.

Successful integration of Tilera contributed to top-line growth while achieving better than expected cost synergies, reducing expected 2015 annual OpEx to $48 million from the $55 million estimated at the beginning of the year and the $52 million estimated last quarter.

Two, Tier-1 NPS design wins: NPS remains on track to tape-out this month with expectations for customer samples in the fourth quarter of 2015. Interest in NPS remains elevated, and we are very pleased to report five Tier-1 design wins in our new target markets for white boxes and data centers as well as in our traditional carrier router markets, to commence following sample availability. Three of these reported design wins are in our new target markets, a white box with a Tier-1 carrier; a white box with a hyper-scale data center operator; and a third win with a Tier-1 data center switch vendor. Within our core networking end market, we already reported a win with ZTE and we now have another Tier-1 switch/router vendor, which brings the total to five Tier-1 wins.

We believe it is important for investors to note that the NPS enables EZchip to capture significantly more customers with more use cases than previously achieved with the NP-4 or NP-5 products. We are now targeting both the carrier and data center markets, where we sell to networking equipment vendors that serve these markets, and we are also pursuing the white box model in which we sell directly to the carrier and data center operators that utilize the white box model. The reported design wins of Tier-1 customers demonstrates all these types of opportunities, carriers and data centers served through equipment vendors and white boxes.

Three, improved customer diversification: During the first half of 2015, we recorded strong growth within the others customers segment which includes all customers other than Cisco and ZTE. For this others category, we saw the first half of 2015 revenues up more than 90% versus the first half of 2014, suggesting a broader customer base and revenue diversification which lessens the company’s dependence on the Cisco ASR9K platform. Total sales to Cisco including Cisco’s other platforms served by our network processors and multicore processors represent 35% of our sales, but roughly 25% is attributed to the ASR9K. While we expect continued revenue growth from this platform, we believe prior investor expectations for 35% to 40% of our revenues to be at risk due to the decision by Cisco for next generation line cards in its ASR9K platform, may be overstated.

We believe there is ample time to develop our NPS-1000, which offers a 2.5x performance increase over the current NPS-400 offering, and to change their decision on future line cards for their edge routing platform in 2018. Cisco will continue to be a successful and growing customer for years to come with ASR9K being the main platform.

Fourth, renewed partnership with Marvell: We believe our market leading position in merchant NPUs saw further gains due to changes in the competitive landscape. Marvell Semiconductor, EZchip’s ASIC partner for Cisco for the NP-3, NP-4 and NP-5 product lines, has recently reorganized its networking business and has chosen to partner with EZchip for merchant NPU design needs and recommend EZchip’s NPUs within Marvell’s current and future networking customers. This expanded relationship will provide Marvell’s networking customers the best-in-breed merchant NPU product portfolio, and can open up new revenue opportunities for EZchip. We believe the expanded partnership with Marvell provides further validation of EZchip’s market leading merchant NPU portfolio. With the joint partnership between Marvell and EZchip, we believe there are no remaining high-speed merchant NPU competitors.

Other companies that have previously made public statements suggesting a more meaningful entrance into the merchant NPU market, as of today to the best of our knowledge, have no product roadmap for a next generation merchant NPU product. Given recent developments within Marvell, and no future high-end NPU-based roadmap within other companies, we believe EZchip is the only remaining high-speed merchant NPU company actively developing new products to serve the market. It is important to note that NPS is targeting a much broader market than NPUs have traditionally been targeting owing to its applicability to data centers and cloud networks. Other merchant NPU vendors saw limited success in the traditional NPU market and now that there are expansion opportunities to other markets their architecture is lacking functionality and flexibility, specifically C programmability, Layer 4-7 processing and support of a standard operating system.

These NPS features are a must have for a wide range of products and applications beyond switches and routers, including network virtualization, SDN, NFV, security, network monitoring, load balancing and more. This is demonstrated through the reported NPS design wins.

Looking at our legacy products, late last year we announced the production of NP-5 which boosts design wins in our customers’ leading platforms as well as with new customers. It is important to remind investors that substantially all NP-4 customers adopted the NP-5, and we continue to gain new design wins with NP-5 and expect that NP-5 will continue to generate new opportunities in 2015 and beyond. The NP-5 provides unprecedented integration and programmability which enables our customers to build leading routers and switches for carrier networks as well as data center appliances, especially customer platforms which require exceptional port density and high-level functionality. We also see new design win opportunities for our multicore TILE-Gx products, marked by the growing need of offloading networking functions to high performance and programmable silicon in data center networking appliances and servers.

Looking at our ongoing multicore development: The second quarter of 2015 our multi-core revenues continued to be comprised primarily of the TILE-Gx product family. And over the next two years we anticipate the mix of our multi-core revenues will remain weighted towards TILE-Gx. However, to become a multicore market leader we are leveraging technologies and expertise from EZchip and Tilera to build what we believe will be one of the most powerful next generation family of multicore processors, the TILE-Mx product line.

We view the TILE-Mx as a leading multicore solution for networking and SDN/NFV opportunities. We estimate potential growth opportunities for the TILE-Mx may allow for expansion of our current multicore TAM, and could be a key driver of future EZchip growth in coming years. We see the multicore markets as very strategic for our growth; in particular those related to NFV and virtualization, in carrier, data center and cloud networks.

Like the NPU market, we believe EZchip’s multicore products offer strongly differentiated technology, which may allow for increasing market share in the coming years and potentially becoming a leader in the multicore CPU market. The TILE-Mx remains on track to deliver customer samples in the second half of 2016.

Looking at our second quarter revenues in further detail, quarterly revenues from Cisco reached a record $9.8 million, equaling 35% of revenues in the second quarter, up 4% sequentially and up 15% from the second quarter of 2014.

We expect revenues from Cisco to continue to grow in 2015, and anticipate Cisco may represent roughly a 35% customer for us. NP-5 has moved to production and we expect all of Cisco’s NP-3 and NP-4 based platforms to migrate to NP-5 overtime. We believe the potential still exists to add new NP-5 and NPS platforms within Cisco. It is our expectation that NP-3 will continue to sell for approximately one more year and NP-4 will continue to sell for several more years.

We believe that NP-5 will ramp up over the next three years and continue to sell for several years beyond that timeframe. Although the NPS-400 lost the initial bake-off for next generation ASR-9000 line cards to an internally developed ASIC, we will continue to try and win it back with the NPS-1000. We believe that the NP-5 replacement will not start to sell before 2018.

Quarterly revenues from ZTE totaled $2.5 million, reaching 9% of revenues, up 15% sequentially and down 49% from the second quarter of 2014. We have said repeatedly in the past that revenues from ZTE are not spread evenly throughout the year and thus we believe investors should measure ZTE on an annual basis.

During the second quarter of 2015, ZTE selected the NPS-400 for its next generation carrier router line cards. We believe ZTE’s endorsement of the NPS-400, prior to tape-out, reflects the continued trust, and support between the two companies, which started with EZchip’s NP-1 and has spanned thru several generations of EZchip’s NPU products.

Quarterly revenues from all other customers totaled $16 million, reaching 56% of revenues in the second quarter, up 4% sequentially and up 85% from the second quarter of 2014.

During the first half of 2015, we delivered strong year-over-year revenue growth from our group of other customers. Total other customer revenues during the first half of 2015 equaled $31.2 million, implying first half year-over-year growth of 90%. Continued year-over-year growth within our other customer segment is consistent with our long term goal of increasing customer and market diversification.

For the full year 2015, we estimate other customer revenues continued growth. This group of other customers includes many routing and non-routing customers, some of them with a potential of becoming 10% customers in future years.

With regards to the coming years, we expect the significant investments we have undertaken related to NPS and the TILE-Mx to pay off, with NPS starting to contribute revenues potentially as early as 2016 and TILE-Mx revenue contribution currently expected to begin in 2017. Given the majority of investment in NPS and TILE-Mx are already included in current OpEx levels. We do not expect OpEx to increase substantially in the coming years resulting in improved operating margins. We believe emerging trends in service provider markets, such as SDN/NFV, may shift the future sourcing of networking equipment. Service providers, and large data center operators, could become leading merchant silicon customers as they utilize white box designs, which are forecasted to incorporate both third-party software and merchant silicon. We remain confident our unique NPS architecture will allow EZchip to remain a leading merchant silicon provider for many years to come.

We anticipate near-term growth will be driven by the combination of opportunities within both NP-4 and TILE-Gx products, with NP-5 shipments continuing to see rapid customer adoption during the second half of 2015. Growth in 2015 will come from a mix of both legacy and current products, as well as the first full year of multicore contribution to EZchip results. It is our belief we will likely see continued benefit from our NPU market leadership, which should allow for year over year growth in our 2015 revenues, non-GAAP EPS, and NPU market share.

With regards to guidance for the upcoming quarter, we expect revenues to be at the range of $30 million to $31 million with gross margins in a range of 75%.

I would now like to turn over the call to our CFO Dror Israel for a more detailed financial review. Dror?

The non-GAAP financial measures mainly exclude the effects of stock-based compensation and amortization of intangible assets.

Now to the results, revenues for the second quarter of 2015 totaled $28.3 million, up 28% from the $22.1 million in the second quarter of 2014, and up 5% from the $26.9 million in the prior quarter. Cisco accounted for $9.8 million or 35% of revenues; ZTE accounted for $2.5 million or 9% of revenues for the quarter and our other customers, as a group, including all Tilera customers and including Juniper, accounted for $16.0 million or 56% of revenues.

Non-GAAP gross margin for the quarter totaled 76.7%, down from 80.9% in the second quarter of 2014, and up from the 74% last quarter. The decrease in gross margins compared to last year resulted mainly from the full consolidation of Tilera, representing lower product gross margins. We expect 2015 gross margins to be at the range of 75%.

Non-GAAP R&D expenses net for the quarter totaled $7.5 million. This amount included $2.2 million in R&D grants received from the Israeli Office of the Chief Scientist. On a gross basis, our R&D expenses for the quarter totaled $9.7 million. Non-GAAP operating expenses for the quarter totaled $11.3 million, compared to $7.1 million in the second quarter last year, and compared to $12.7 million in the prior quarter. We expect that our annual OpEx level in 2015 will be in the range of $48 million, down from the $52 million projected last quarter, driven mainly by the successful synergies achieved through the Tilera integration as well as higher than forecasted Chief Scientist contribution.

Non-GAAP operating income for the quarter was $10.4 million, a decrease of 3% from the $10.7 million operating income in the second quarter last year, and an increase of 44% compared to $7.2 million in the prior quarter. Non-GAAP net income for the quarter totaled $10.9 million, 1% down from the record $11 million net income last year and an increase of 45% compared to $7.5 million in the previous quarter.

Fully diluted EPS on non GAAP basis was $0.34 for the quarter, down from $0.36 in the second quarter last year and up from $0.24 in the previous quarter.

Moving over to the balance sheet, cash, cash equivalents, marketable securities and deposits totaled $189 million as of June 30, 2015, compared to $184.1 million at the end of the previous quarter. Cash generated from operations during the quarter was $10.4 million. Cash used in investing activities was $0.8 million. Cash provided by financing activities was $4.4 million, of which $4.7 million was used to repurchase shares as part of our share buyback program. And $0.3 million was generated from the exercise of stock options. An additional $0.3 million decrease resulted from unrealized loss in marketable securities.

Last quarter, we announced a 20 million share buyback program. During the second quarter, we have repurchased 294,000 shares at an average price of $16.15, totaling 4.7 million. Following these purchases, we have $15.3 million remaining in our current buyback program. We expect to be opportunistic with buybacks during the second half of 2015 with future purchases subject to our share price, trading volume and other factors.
