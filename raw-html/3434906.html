Sizmek's (SZMK) CEO Neil Nguyen on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 12, 2015 9:41 PM ET  |  About: Sizmek Inc. (SZMK) by: SA Transcripts

Sizmek Inc. (NASDAQ:SZMK)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 12, 2015 05:00 PM ET

Executives

Ken Saunders - CFO

Neil Nguyen - CEO

Analysts

Darren Aftahi - Northland Securities

Operator

Good day, ladies and gentlemen, and welcome to the Sizmek, Inc. Q2 2015 Earnings Call. At this time all participants are in a listen-only mode. Later we will conduct a question-and-answer session and instructions will follow at that time. [Operator Instructions]. As a reminder today's conference call is being recorded. I would now like to turn the conference over to Mr. Ken Saunders, Chief Financial Officer. Please go ahead sir.

Ken Saunders

Thank you and good afternoon. As the operator said, this is Ken Saunders, the company's Chief Financial Officer. Leading the call today is Neil Nguyen, our CEO and President. During our call today Neil will discuss some of the highlights of the quarter and the trends we're seeing, and I will follow up with the financial details and our review of full year guidance.

Earlier this afternoon, Sizmek issued a press release announcing its second quarter 2015 results, which is available on the company's website. This call is being broadcast live over the Internet and the audio of the call will be available on the Investor Relations page of the Sizmek website.

I'd like to remind everyone that statements made in this conference call regarding our current expectations, estimates, outlook, guidance and projections about our operations, industry, financial condition, performance, results of operations and liquidity constitute forward-looking statements within the meaning of the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995. In addition, any forward-looking statements represent our estimates only as of the date hereof and should not be relied upon as representing our estimates as of any subsequent date.

We disclaim any intention or obligation to update the forward-looking statements to reflect subsequent events or circumstances or update the reasons that actual results could differ materially from those anticipated in the forward-looking statements, except as required by law. Please refer to the company's SEC filings for detailed information.

In addition, non-GAAP financial measures will be discussed during the call. Reconciliation to the most directly comparable GAAP financial measures are included in the earnings release and on the website. Now, I'll turn the call over to Neil.

Neil Nguyen

Thank you, Ken. Good afternoon and thank you for joining us on the call today. We closed the second quarter with some headwinds. But on balance I think this was more than offset with positive momentum in some key areas that point to a stronger second half of the year. As we had discussed in our Q1 earnings call we knew that our results would be challenged by negative FX and the worldwide sales reorganization to align with the company's global account strategy. In addition we saw budget cuts from several major clients which I'll discuss in a minute.

First FX accounted for close to $5 million of our revenue decline year-over-year for the first half of 2015 and we expect an additional $4 million to $5 million negative impact in the back half of 2015. Second, while we entered the quarter cautiously optimistic that our sales reorganization would not be overly disruptive the change in personnel roles and some attrition definitely slowed our sales performance globally. And third, we had a number of counts where clients cut and/or shifted their budgets, similar to what I discussed last quarter in video. We see opportunities to mitigate this or recapture the revenue in other ways. I'll discuss this shortly.

But more importantly there were a number of positive developments in the second quarter that reaffirm Sizmek is on the right track strategically and technologically. And while the near term results do not demonstrate the potential of the company’s position and opportunity we have several proof points that support a stronger outlook.

Let me highlight a few examples. In the second quarter Sizmek won a global RP from one of the largest global automotive companies to be their ad management and server across all territories. We won because they wanted to rationalize the multitude of ad servers being used worldwide. The client’s emphasis on taking control of their data, streamlining their work flow and finally the ability to activate their data with Sizmek’s open ad management suite in conjunction with a number of other technology partners.

This multi-million dollar win is a strong validation of our overall product suite and strategy. Due to the size and complexity of engagement we anticipate it will take upwards of four to six months to fully on-board in all markets. A second multi-million dollar win was with a major global technology services company anchored on our new data management solution, Data Hub combined with our existing dynamic creative optimization solution.

Our solution won in large part because of our ability to help the client activate their first party data to maximize the value of their digital media investments. We have already launched new campaigns under the agreement and are exploring opportunities to expand the relationship into other areas and other markets.

Lastly, let’s not overlook Sizmek’s core competency as one of the industry’s best creative solutions, which was key in winning the leading consumer electronics company, where they are leveraging our innovative creative formats and world class service to launch highly sensitive new advertising around the world. The deal is worth multi-millions of dollars in 2015. These wins reflect the success of our new customer acquisition strategy as part of the sales reorganization.

These recent selected wins hopefully give you a sense of how our investments over the past 24 months has strengthened and expanded our technology strategy, from the complete re-architecture of our platform and the product suite to extend beyond our core competency as just an ad server to include a greater emphasis on data collection, tracking and ultimately a data activation platform is resonating with large multi-national clients who are increasingly demanding transparency and control of their marketing technologies suite.

Another element in the evolution of our value proposition is the expansion of our programmatic offerings. Let me provide a brief update on our newly formed programmatic business unit, a.k.a., PBU, now comprise of the company’s managed service offering, Call Vantage [ph] and our newly acquired Mobile DSP StrikeAd. The PBU will account for roughly 16% to 18% of the company’s total revenue by year end.

In the first half our programmatic business Vantage, a full service offering was under pressure as a few large clients either reduced budgets or in some cases shifted their business to a self-service model. Essentially they decided to in-source their programmatic display trading business and recapture some of the service margin. Clearly without a self-service platform we were not considered as an option. But now with StrikeAd we have an opportunity to compete for both the mobile display and video programmatic budgets as both a service provider and a self-service technology platform.

While large clients will most likely look to move to a self-service model in the long run we still see numerous opportunities with small and mid-tier agencies requiring our expertise in programmatic trading. This along with our global footprint we see our programmatic offering as a significant growth area for Sizmek and customer feedback has been positive, both from StrikeAd clients and for Sizmek’s product extension. The StrikeAd integration is on track and we are now aggressively moving forward on our plan to build the industry’s best pure play mobile DSP. This will be a key driver to our second half results and especially in 2016.

Leveraging our mobile DSP we are refocusing our efforts within the APAC region where we see our programmatic suite in a position of neutrality [ph] to be a key value proposition due to dominance of mobile in Asia. Another area of progress in the second quarter was the completion of our sales reorganization and we are beginning to see the benefits. Our first priority had been to strengthen the emphasis on multi-national marketers which includes a shift in our selling process and a longer sale cycle.

Second, we've built out a dedicated sales team to focus on video, mobile, publisher and data driven products. These teams have become product sales specialists allowing us to compete much more effectively with point solutions as the industry continues to evolve from silos to a multi-channel approach.

Finally, regarding our technology platform, today we announce our global cross device solution further strengthening our tracking and attribution capability. This addresses our advertisers’ need to be able track users across multiple channels, devices and screens. As you can see we have made significant progress with our technology. But we still have much to do in a very dynamic marketplace. So what should you look for in the coming months to show we are on track. First, that our sales organization is winning large global platform deals. Please keep in mind that these are more complex wins, requiring a longer on-boarding process.

Second, look for investments in key growth areas, particularly in video and data. In video we have already added more dedicated product sales specialist to sell one of the leading video solutions in the market. During the next few months we will officially launch our data, the next step is building out our data activation offerings. Data Hub will have a significant impact on our overall business shifting our value proposition from a logistics platform to one that helps marketers and agencies activate their data while connected to the world’s best ad management platform.

By talking to customers around the globe confirms that our strategy is the right one to create an end-to-end ad management platform. I firmly believe the value of an independent advertising technology platform for marketers and agencies is becoming increasingly apparent as the major publishers build their wall guard [ph]. Sizmek is uniquely positioned to provide an alternative to this industry shift. And I look for our efforts to more closely match our results in the second half of the year but even more so in 2016.

Now I return the call to Ken to review the financials in greater detail. Ken?

Ken Saunders

Thank you, Neil. As a reminder we closed StrikeAd acquisition at the end of May, so Q2 results have approximately one month of StrikeAd’s revenue and expenses. Going forward StrikeAd will be fully integrated into our existing programmatic service business unit and not reported separately.

Let’s begin with our view of Q2 results; for the second quarter of 2015 Sizmek’s revenue was $40.2 million, representing 9% year-over-year decrease from the same period in 2014. On a constant currency basis, revenues declined 2% year-over-year to $43 million versus $44 million for Q2 of 2014.

When calculating the effects of foreign currency translation to a dollar for both revenue and earnings, we looked at the monthly exchange rates for the second quarter of 2015 when compared with the same period in 2014. The major currencies driving the translation adjustment to the dollars are the euro, Australian dollar, Sterling Pound, and to a lesser extent, the Brazilian real and the Japanese yen. On a regional level EMEA and APAC, which together represent about 40% of revenue were the most impacted by FX.

With that being said let me provide a little more color on revenue. As we discussed on the year-end call we anticipated that revenue for the first half of 2015 would represent a lower percentage of total revenue when compared to the first half of 2014 and they were. Revenues for the first half of 2015, excluding StrikeAd for comparison, were expected to represent 44% of total revenues versus 48% for the first half of 2014. That being said we were not satisfied with these results.

Our core business grew 8% on a constant currency basis, with particular strength in our mobile business, as Neil said. Our rich media flash business continued its run-off at 35% year-over-year, now representing 19% of our total revenues in the quarter.

Pricing remained strong with eCPM rates fairly consistent with the last six quarters and we continue to see the adoption of our platform strategy as the average number of products per customer continues to increase. For example looking at the last 12 months of June 2015 we saw a 6% increase over the same period of 2014. We announced last call that we were acquiring StrikeAd, a mobile DSP. I am happy to report that the acquisition of StrikeAd is complete. The back office integration is complete and the efforts to enhance the offering for additional channels are well underway.

StrikeAd’s revenues and expenses represent one month in Q2, 2015 and were in line with expectations. StrikeAd when combined with our existing programmatic managed services offering represented 11% of our revenue for this quarter and the take rate remains consistent with the past six quarters.

The company’s adjusted EBITDA was $2.9 million for the second quarter of 2015 when compared to $6.9 million in the second quarter of 2014. Lower revenue is the main driver for the EBITDA decline as our costs were essentially flat to the prior year despite investments made in sales and product. On a sequential basis though total costs were 8% higher than Q1 2015. The primarily drivers for this were investments in building out a sales organization as well as the increased cost of revenues specifically related to our operations team in support of customer migrations to the MDX mix platform.

For the second half of the year our costs are expected to increase 20% from the first half, driven in large part by the addition of StrikeAd. Without StrikeAd total cost as a percent of revenue would have actually declined 14% year-over-year demonstrating the company’s ability to drive leverage.

Gross margins remained in line with guidance which was low to mid-60s at 64% for the second quarter of 2015 which is slightly lower than 65% for the same period in 2014. As we mentioned when we provided guidance for the year we will likely see additional cost of revenue to driven by spend to support customer migrations to our new MBX platform and the amortized [ph] cost associated with moving our platform to the cloud. So I continue to expect that gross margins will remain in low to mid-60% range for the full year. For the three months ended June 30, 2015 Sizmek had a net loss of $7.9 million or $0.27 per share, compared to a net loss of $1.6 million or $0.05 per share for Q2 of 2014.

Now turning to the balance sheet, capital expenditures for the second quarter of 2015 were $4.9 million, including capitalized software of $4.1 million. DSOs in Q2, 2015 were 108 days, which was up slightly from the 105 DSO in Q1 of 2015, approximately five days of which were driven by the acquired receivables from StrikeAd. At the end of the second quarter we had $66.9 million of cash and no debt, down from the prior quarter due to closing the acquisition of StrikeAd.

For the second quarter of 2015, cash flow from operations showed a net cash use of $3.7 million compared to the second quarter of 2014’s cash flow from operations of $1.6 million due largely in part to payments of acquired payables and liabilities related to StrikeAd’s acquisition. For the six months ended June 30, 2015 cash flow from operations was $2.7 million or 22% higher than $2.2 million for the same period of 2014 after adjusting for the StrikeAd purchased liability.

We did not repurchase stock as part of our buyback program in the second quarter, leaving $23.5 million available to repurchase under the plan. Following acquisition of StrikeAd we decided to allocate cash towards building out our mobile DSP offering and thus did not act on our buyback program in Q2. We will likely be conservative with our use of cash during the remainder of the year as we focus on investments to our tech stack and sales execution.

Turning to guidance for the full year, the company is updating its full year guidance to reflect the first half results and the projected impact of $9 million to $10 million of negative foreign currency. Revenues are now expected to be between $180 million to $184 million representing a 5% to 8% growth over the full year 2014 or 10% to 13% growth after giving effect to the negative impact of foreign currency changes.

Let me provide some insight into the drivers we see for the second half growth. We anticipate our core business will grow over 35% for the second half of 2015, reflecting the adoption of our programmatic mobile offering and revenue from larger deals closed in the first half. For example of the top 75 deals closed in the first six months of 2015, which represents about $25 million total bookings value, only 30% was recognized in the first half and a great deal is expected to hit in Q4 beyond.

Let me drill down a little further in to the factors behind our outlook for the second half. We project between $4 million and $5 million of negative foreign currency to impact the second half of the year with more impact in Q3 than Q4. Mobile momentum was building exiting Q2 with mobile sales products especially in full force, and a strong pipeline of deals which was being helped by the shift away from Flash. We’re expecting to see second half growth of about 50% when compared to the prior year.

Video, as Neil mentioned we have one larger customer that reduced their spend throughout the first half of the year leading [ph] the growth for the first six months. We expect this client to, at a minimum, maintain their current spending levels for the balance of the year. And additionally we’ve expanded our product, video product specialist sales team as we entered Q3 and we believe we’ll see similar successes that we have with our mobile sales team. Therefore we’re expecting to see second half for us at least 20% higher than our first half.

Our programmatic business unit is expected to contribute over 100% year-over-year growth for the second half of the year versus same period in the prior year. Adjusted in the guidance for 2015 associated with revenue ranges I just provided of between $23 million and $25 million. We continue to manage company for both growth and profit targeting 13% to 14% EBITDA margins and positive cash flow from operations.

With that I’ll turn it back over to operator for your questions.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

Thank you. [Operator Instructions]. And our first question comes from Darren Aftahi of Northland Securities. Your line is now open.

Darren Aftahi

Hey guys, good afternoon. Got a bunch of questions here. So Neil some of the wins, I think you just called out three of them, some of these multi-million dollar wins, does that, comments that Ken made, did that reflect that kind of literally 30% of revenue of those were booked or has any of that revenue been booked and most of that is sort of assumed to be booked in the second half of the year.

Neil Nguyen

So I think with two of the announced, two of the ones - examples that I cited, some of the revenues have been booked in the first half with the balance we expect in the second half. With one of the specific account we expected it to be later in the fourth quarter and really getting to, I’ll call it a normalized run-rate into 2016. It's the long onboarding process.

Darren Aftahi

Okay. And then in terms of StrikeAd, Ken what was the revenue in the quarter?

Ken Saunders

Yeah it was about $800,000ish, plus a couple $100,000, I guess that is going to be nominal to the quarter.

Darren Aftahi

Okay. So I guess when you kind of look at your growth for the first half of the year on the core business, if my math is right, was about 4%. That's taking out FX [ph] and your guidance, I think implies 35% decline in rich media for the rest of the year, about 18% roughly maybe at the low end for your core business. So like what gives you the confidence outside of adding kind of the StrikeAd in the comps year-over-year that you can grow that fast, just kind of given what you've seen or is just one of these things where you feel a lot more confident about what you build in terms of the sales pipeline and the sales reorganization is completely done.

Neil Nguyen

I would say probably both of those factors are key contributors to our confidence that the back half will be better than the first half. So specifically with the sales organization our programmatic business we expect to have a much better performance specifically around our full service offering. We're seeing better deal traction on that side. The addition of, I’ll call it, more sales personnel into the video product will drive, we believe will drive additional results for us in the back half of the year and then some of the bookings that we have won in the first half begin to materialize in the back half of the year.

Darren Aftahi

Okay, and then in terms of StrikeAd, I know you said it's fully integrated. In terms of marketing, is your client base kind of aware of the capability and what sort of traction specifically are you seeing in terms of kind of cross selling this into your kind of existing let's say sort of pre-StrikeAd client base. Thanks?

Neil Nguyen

So I think the back office integration is complete. The technical integrations are still underway. There is a deep and robust product build plan for us over the next two to four quarters with some really critical milestones along the way and they have a high level of confidence that our team will execute on the product development side to get it to a highly competitive offering at a marketplace.

From a marketing standpoint, I think we're still in the early days of that. I think the reception by our customer base has been positive, more so around the fact that they see that Sizmek continues to be laser focused and follow through on its commitment to build out an end-to-end mobile fleet. So overall, I would say that we're still cross-pollinating our opportunities. The exposure continues to grow each day. We formalized the business into a separate operating team to give it greater focus. And the team will have the resources to really drive the business into the back half of the year and into '16.

Darren Aftahi

Great, just one last one. Ken, you said expectations with StrikeAd 20% operating cost growth. Is that versus the second half of last year or versus the first half of this year?

Ken Saunders

Yeah, that's a sequential statement. So it's 20% inclusive of StrikeAd.

Darren Aftahi

Got you. Okay thanks.

Ken Saunders

Yeah, no problem.

Operator

Thank you. And I'm showing no further questions at this time. I'd like to turn the conference back over to Mr. Nguyen for closing remarks.

Neil Nguyen

Thank you everyone for joining us today. We continue to be focused on our customer acquisition strategy. As we have shared with you today our technology and product development is begin to yield some level of traction and gives us a level of confidence that our strategy is on point, based on the wins that we've had in the second quarter. So I look forward to updating all of you at the third quarter call. And thank you for your time today.

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen thank you for participating in today's conference. This does conclude the program and you may all disconnect. Have a great day everyone.
