Lattice Inc.' (LTTC) CEO Paul Burgess onQ3 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 14, 2015 3:27 PM ET  |  About: Lattice, Inc. (LTTC) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Jon Cunningham - Investor Relations

Paul Burgess - Chief Executive Officer

Joe Noto - Chief Financial Officer

Analysts

Thomas Wiester - RedChip Companies

Lattice, Inc. (OTCQB:LTTC) Q3 2015 Results Earnings Conference August 14, 2015 12:00 PM ET

Operator

Good day everyone, and welcome to the Lattice Incorporated Second Quarter 2015 Earnings Call. As a reminder, today’s conference is being recorded. And at this time, I’d like to turn the call over to Jon Cunningham, Vice President, RedChip Companies. Please go ahead, sir.

Jon Cunningham

Thank you, Erin. Good afternoon everyone, and welcome to Lattice Incorporated second quarter 2015 earnings conference call. With me today is Lattice’s Chief Executive Officer, Mr. Paul Burgess; and Chief Financial Officer, Mr. Joe Noto.

Before I turn over the call to Mr. Burgess, may I remind all listeners that in this call, management’s prepared remarks contain forward-looking statements which are subject to risks and uncertainties and management may make additional forward-looking statements in response to your questions.

Information regarding forward-looking statements except for historical information contained herein are made pursuant to the Safe Harbor provisions of the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995. Forward-looking statements of our known and unknown risks and uncertainties which may cause our actual results in future periods to differ materially from forecasted results. Actual results may differ from those discussed today August 14, 2015 and therefore we refer you to a more detailed discussion of the risks and the uncertainties in the company’s filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

It’s now my pleasure to turn the call over to Lattice’s Chief Executive Officer, Paul Burgess. Paul, please go ahead.

Paul Burgess

Thanks, Jon. And welcome to everyone joining us on the call today. I’ll make a few opening remarks and then pass the call over to our Chief Financial Officer, Joe Noto for a more in-depth discussion of our financials. In the second quarter of 2015, we continue to expand our product portfolio and expand our direct services and technology revenue base.

We also completed testing of two new products our Video Arraignment solution and our Wireless Handheld Technology. As part of the new product release, we have rebranded our core technology and plan a commercial roll out of the new technologies later this year. We continue to expand with our domestic and international business strategy that focused on replicating our success in Oklahoma, where we now have over 50% of our target market.

We wanted to ensure we have the most effective strategy for capturing market share amongst the small and mid-sized correctional facilities. This included geographic focus, product portfolio and sales and marketing strategy to expand the company with the goal of becoming the predominant provider of services to small and mid-sized correctional facilities in our target market.

Before I give a more detailed review of our operations, the new product launch and the progress we’ve made against some of our strategic objectives, I’ll pass the call over to Joe Noto for a more in-depth discussion of our financials. Joe?

Joe Noto

Thanks, Paul. I’ll start by running down the P&L for the quarter. Starting with revenues, our total revenue increased 7% from prior year to $2.4 million from $2.3 million and breaking down our revenues into technology and services, our technology revenue increased 82% to $1,205,000 to $661,000. The increase include a large order to a strategic partner during the quarter.

Technology revenue fluctuates quarter-to-quarter and is not indicative of the trend and we expect to be at planned levels for full fiscal year 2015. Turning to service revenue, our service revenue accounted 50% of total revenue in the quarter, service revenue decreased 24% to $1,210,000 from $1,592,000 prior year. Decrease was due to termination of large wholesale service contract which ended in 2014, as we move our business closer to a direct services model in line with our strategic objectives.

Revenue attributable to this contract accounted for approximately $500,000 in year ago quarter. After factoring out this revenue, service revenue on the direct basis increased approximately 11%. Our direct call provisioning revenue as a component of total service revenue was up 23%, a key metric for a business which moves proportionately with the growth in customer base.

And turning to gross profit, total gross profit percentage increased to 48% for the quarter compared to 40% prior year. Gross profit percentage in both revenue streams without technology and services are in line with expectations and the increase is driven by relative mix in revenues as we had a strong quarter in technology shipments which is primarily time related.

We expect to track to prior guidance of blended gross margin for fiscal 2015 in high 30% to low 40% range. And turning to operating loss, we had an operating loss in the quarter of $269,000 which compared to a loss of $358,000 in the prior year quarter. The reported operating loss $269,000 adjust to approximately breakeven after adding back $250,000 non-cash expenses.

With the proper level of capital formation, we expect to continue to invest in our sales, marketing and engineering to support delivery of our pipeline and growth objectives into 2015 and 2016 and turning to cash flows, our cash position increased $268,000 from $256,000 last December 31, 2014.

For the quarter, our operating cash flows or OCF are negative $520,000 mainly due to an increase in accounts receivable resulting from the strong quarter and technology shipments. We borrowed against certain of these receivables with our line of credit to actual capital into Q3. As discussed on prior calls, we’re actively involved with raising capital in the range of $4 million to improve working capital and liquidity to support our cash requirements. As part of these activities, during the second quarter we closed on the portion of the $4 million financing objectives totalling $908,000 from the issuance of convertible notes and we received net cash proceeds of $454,000 after paying $363,200 of the $500,000 bridge note we closed during Q1 and cash placement fees $90,800.

Our working capital deficit moved to $3.3 million from $3.8 million at March 31, 2015 mainly due to the partial repayment in the short-term bridge note. We continue to solicit investors for the remaining financing targeted and expect to complete the range of the remainder of this financing this year. We continue to be in good standing with two asset backed lending facilities which are available to us to finance larger technology orders as we have done so today.

With that, I’ll turn the call back to Paul.

Paul Burgess

Thanks, Joe. As I mentioned in my opening remarks, we continue to expand our product portfolio and rebrand our core technology in anticipation of a complete commercial rollout later this year. Our new video technology completed testing in the second quarter and we currently have it operating in three facilities.

The new technology enables arraignment hearings to be conducted remotely with HD quality video if required. This enables a judge to arraign an inmate without having to move the inmate from correctional facility to a court house reducing the security risk and cost of transportation for inmates.

The new system enables remote visitation with friends and family increasing accessibility to the inmates and reducing the need for friends and family and in some cases having to travel long distance for visits. There is a secured consultation module that provides us secure consultation between an Attorney and inmate. This provides the inmate with more readily available legal assistance and reduces the need for face-to-face consultation with inmates and their attorney.

The remote visitation module is also capable of interacting with a Smartphone enabling the inmate have a video visitation session with a commercial Smartphone or tablet such as an iPhone or Android device. We also anticipate upgrading our video visitation security to include facial recognition that will identify the individual on the call to ensure only those people with scheduled visitation sessions are actually on the call.

Our new CellMate product testing was completed in the second quarter and we are preparing for our commercial rollout in the fourth quarter of this year. Our first 1,000 units will be shipped by the end of Q3, although there are other tablet based technologies being introduced to the market, Lattice has the only completely wireless handheld technology in the market and is the only device that has the capability to become a fully functioning Smartphone upon the inmates release.

Well in the correctional facility, CellMate provides the inmate with the same experience as a commercially available Smartphone in a completely secure environment. Capabilities of CellMate mobile include phone and voice mail, secure access to account information, access to family connect, a low rate communications plan, managed commissary and phone accounts, email with keyword search, text messaging with keyword search, entertainment including music, books, and games, access to the lot of library that provides free public domain books, educational services, broadcast, legal library and services.

Based on the initial market feedback, we believe this technology to be industry leading and provides inmates with a technology at a fair price points and provides inmates and friends and family with access to services currently not available. In addition, the device enables facilities to provide services reduce the cost burden on their operations.

We will continue to provide updates as we rollout new apps for the CellMate mobile platform. In evaluating the company’s strategic initiatives, the company has performed favorably compared to industry metrics. During our evaluation, it should be noted that the company’s focus on small and medium size domestic facilities has led to an industry leading position in technology and customer satisfaction.

Based on our findings, the company has remained focused on our sales and marketing efforts on specific geographic sectors and product mix that will maximize our growth potential over the next three years. It was noted that Lattice has been recognized by our customers having one of the most reliable and innovative product portfolios and is known for very responsive customer service leading to minimal downtime. Customers describe our products is highly reliable and key need for their service.

In addition, since moving to a direct service model over four years ago, the company has approximately100% customer retention rate from our direct service customers. It should also be noted that Lattice’s average revenue per inmate in our target market is approximately 45% higher than the industry average and facilities that incorporate our Kiosk technology the revenue increases by an additional 30%.

Identifying these key factors has positioned the company to implement the sales and marketing strategy specifically focused on our target market that has enabled the advancement of our growth strategy over the next three years. Since we implement that our new sales and marketing strategy as we noted in some of our recent press releases, we have been awarded a number of new contracts which we will be implementing in the coming months. We have also seen our sales pipeline grow which we anticipate enabling us to continue to build on our growing market share.

As demonstrated by growth in our direct service revenue, the model has changed a little bit for the company and that our new mobile technology will allow us to go into new market and new facilities without waiting for the exploration of an existing phone contract. As a result of the international evaluation, we conducted last year, we identified key partners, products, and geographical territories that are best suited to facilitate the growth of the company. We are currently working with key partners, we’ve identified in a number of phone markets and are actively pursuing new opportunities in a number of new markets.

We continue to do revenues from a Canadian partnership that will be recognizing additional project revenue from the market this year. We completed the turn off of our new U.S. base customer service centre and related service in 2013 and the centre started to turn a small profit this year. We anticipate profit from the centre to increase proportionately with our direct service revenue.

In 2013 the [FCC] (Ph) issued a decision that would cap interest rate call rates, this may have an adverse effect on some of the larger competitors that utilize high connection fees to subsidize very large payments back to state facilities. At this point, we see little impact to our business model based on this decision and actually welcome the FCC’s focus on utilizing technology to better facilitate inmates integration back into society. This is precisely what our next generation technology enables specifically our CellMate product platform and we are glad to see the industry is moving in this direction.

We will continue to evaluate the regulatory environment and the potential to impact that will have on our industry. We do anticipate a decision from the SEC this year to regulate intra-state rates in addition to inter-state rates. We have been working with our industry and correctional facilities and FCC to develop an environment that works well for correctional facilities, inmates and their friends and families and the inmate communication providers let service the industry.

With that I’ll open the call to questions. Operator?

Thank you. [Operator Instructions]. And we’ll go first to Thomas Wiester with RedChip Companies.

Hi, Paul, Hi Joe. How are you both you doing today?

Well Thomas.

Paul Burgess

Yes, don’t get sales and marketing certification confused because we actually entered into a market and start selling into a market when we’re awarded that first contract that we get the certification and the certification doesn’t really take that long, if you feel little different but what we usually do is wait until we’re about to be awarded the first contract before we get the certification.

So what you’ll see is a certification is actually flat in the markets we’re going into throughout the markets as well as eight markets that we identified, we’re actually selling in six of eight of them and should selling in seven of eight of them before the end of Q3 and all eight of them by the end of the year.

Thomas Wiester

Okay, great thanks for clarifying that, I appreciate that and then my next question here is can you kind of give us an idea of how we should expect may be the sales and marketing ramp for some of these new markets and how much of that is attached to when you will be able to raise the finished raising the additional capital you’ve been working on?

Paul Burgess

Yes, the additional eight markets that we’ve targeted without the financing we’re already perhaps to go into start selling seven of those eight markets it’s just how I guess how much we concentrate efforts on each of those states but we will be selling into - into each of those and a big part of the ramp is also the new technology roll out. The new filming mobile platform has garnered a lot more interest than we originally perspective, when we actually went in and got developing this new technology, we expected that only a fewest facilities would adopt it but the initial market test that we did putting the technology in front of these correctional facilities showing them the capabilities, it’s garnered a lot more interest than we anticipated.

So what we’re expecting is that we will be able to penetrate these markets faster based on this new technology because it may not be completely reliant on a phone contracting to expire, so for example we could go into a facility not actually have the phone contract but able to provide the service just limit the phone capabilities and when the phone contract comes up we could also go after the phone contract but with the new CellMate device we will be generating revenue outside of phone calls, which is a whole new revenue stream that we didn’t even anticipate.

Thomas Wiester

Yes. Great. I think, Paul, I think we discuss this a little bit on previous calls but can you just kind of remind me, what’s the general range of some of these phone contracts when - or the length of these contracts.

Paul Burgess

They typically run one to five years. So traditionally in the past what would happen until that contract was about to expire unless there is a reason for the facility to cancel it, you would really have to wait for that contract to expire. What happens with the new CellMate mobile product is now we don’t need to wait for that contract to expire to be able to provide service through the friends and family in the correctional facility.

We are limited on what we could provide, because we wouldn’t provide the phone service, we just provide all the other features of the device, but what we’ve seen from some of the facilities out there, they are very interested in bringing it in prior to a phone contract expire. So that’s why we think we may have a quicker market penetration strategy than we thought would have in the past.

Thomas Wiester

That definitely sounds really great. And then just my last question here, could you maybe give us an update on some of your progress on international fronts?

Paul Burgess

International fronts we’re currently working on a couple of RFPs over in the UK, we’re still working with Telus Canada and we’re working with a smaller provider WiMAX although we expect to get an award from later this year. International markets are little hard for us to predict just because we’re not the ones managing the sales process, that’s usually another partner that we’re working with. So quite often, when we’re working on these bids, we are not really in privilege to the timelines that the end customer is working on. And we also have some work with the project that we’re are working on in Singapore as well, but it’s really Canada and the United Kingdom that are the two markets we are working closely with right now.

Thomas Wiester

Okay. Thanks for the color here and you guys are making some great progress all the new technology come out is very exciting and I’m really looking forward to your feature results.

Paul Burgess

Thanks, John.

No. It’s a much different metric, think of it more like a software licensing fee where your license is a piece of software for a certain period of time. So in other words let’s say it’s a three year license agreement.

What you would, did you do a three year license or what

Yes. The final agreement hasn’t been signed, but it was contemplated on being a three year agreement and then what tends to having with those agreements is the licensing agreement for that and then there are additional modules that could be build on in license. So the core licensing agreements we’re talking about with a core piece of telecommunication switching technology and then if the customer wants other modules and they can snap those on and license them as well or develop them and interface with the course of it.

Unidentified Analyst

Paul Burgess

No. Our philosophy has always been that if we are not going to sell in a specific market, or to a specific target demographic, then what we wanted to do is make sure that those customers have potential access or technology. So in other words, if we are not servicing a customer I would still like those customers be serviced by Lattice technology. And that’s why we are looking at licensing some of our core technology to other providers.

Unidentified Analyst

Can you give me a little perspective as to this educational service that you are considering because this is really one of the hottest topics out there, because education is really being downsized, because of state budgets and if you could penetrate educational services they know that some of these prisoners who have repeat offensive, they become more responsible individuals. And the [indiscernible] actually has been declining for those people who have been exposed to proper education.

Now I don’t know if you know with but McGraw-Hill just set up an electronics education division, they just spoke about it recently and its designed to guide each student individually, it’s not just to read and reader of text material, but it kind of handholds each student individually on their iPad or whatever the devices by constantly testing them and making sure that the understand what they are reading so that they can go on to the next module and build up the confidence that there is minimal dropout, because even in the colleges its known that its about 20% dropout rate and in the smaller colleges to three year colleges it’s close to 34 to 40% dropout rates. I was wondering if you kind of spoke in or familiar with the McGraw-Hill the electronic education new methodology.

Paul Burgess

Not necessarily McGraw-Hill but where we’re working but let me just kind take a step back and give you higher view of what we had planned and our vision for CellMate product so when we started the industry four years ago that the challenge I really put in front of the company was I want that inmate to have a device that were mainly they can still effectively use in a commercial fashion and it helps them not only while they are in the facility but helps them when they are out of the facility as well.

And that does a couple of things first off it provides us when that because the inmate is one of our customers. When that inmate leave he is still customer of Lattice and for every inmate there are three friends and family that also provides us to access to a whole market that’s actually three times the size of our inmate market outside of the correctional facility. So in order to do that we came up with suite of services we thought would be to be available on this mobile device and one of them being education.

Now on the education side, specifically in facilities, most states have a preset education plan that they can give inmates. So we’re looking at a number of different platforms also looking at different platforms on the entertainment and gaming side of that as well.
