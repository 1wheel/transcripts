The concept of the reorganization was the right one, now it needs to be completed in a way that instills basic element of clarity and prove communication to employees regarding expectations, alignment for the people in groups who are working toward common goals and accountability.

We are changing the way we consider investments, to be clear investments to us means addition of people outside of the ordinary course of our normal business cycle, expansion of offerings, geographic expansion and any other discrete deployment of capital. When we invested we do so in a planned sought out way with a focus on our long term strategy and returns. Let's turn to our financial results for the second quarter end of July 4, 2015. Revenues for the second quarter of 2015 were 16.9 million, a decrease of 3% from the same year ago period primarily driven by the impact of approximately a $1 million unfavorable change in foreign currency translation along with reductions in total volume of projects in our North American segment. These reductions were partially offset by modest growth in our EMEA segment.

On a non-GAAP constant currency basis, our revenues in the second quarter increased 2% 17.8 million from the same year ago period. Looking at Q2 revenues by geographic region and the portion of each revenues contribution to total revenue on a reported basis, Europe, Middle East and Africa or EMEA accounted for 9.2 million or 55% and North America accounted for 7.7 million or 45%. Going forward we will provide additional breakdown by category of offering to improve the transparency in our reporting. Breaking down Q2 total revenues by source and their percent of total revenue strategy consulting accounted for 1.6 million or 9%, management consulting accounted for 1.1 million or 7%, managed solutions accounted for 2.2 million or 13% and execution accounted for 12 million or 71%.

Our gross profit was 6 million or 32.9% of revenue compared to 6.3 million or 36.4% of revenue and the second quarter of 2014. The decrease in gross profit was primarily due to higher percentage of lower margin services work relative to our higher margin advisory or solutions business. We had a $300,000 inventory reserve included in our cost of goods. This relates to our Elutions partnership and I will elaborate on this later.

There is a natural tradeoff between certainty of revenue from long term and higher volume contracts and margin elevation across the business. To be sure we are focused on expanding margins which will be driven by increasing our mix towards managed solutions and other higher margin offerings. We need to capitalize on our ability to serve the needs of our clients in order to expand our position within the organizations, extend our brand and give us entry into future high margin opportunities. Along those lines one of my key initiatives is to accelerate our transition from a service centric lower margin business model to a higher margin model driven by technology enabled managed solutions and other higher margin offerings. We’re working on our strategy to effect this transition which involves analyzing our go to market approach including future organizational requirements that may enable acceleration.

In Q2 we started to see a favorable mix of higher margin strategy and managed solutions projects particularly in North America. Our EMEA business is showing positive signs of steps taken to accelerate the growth of higher margin offerings on that side of the pond. SG&A expenses in the second quarter of 2015 were 8.2 million compared to 7.8 million in the second quarter of 2014. The increase was primarily due to higher non-cash expenses and certain other unusual charges in the quarter of approximately $1 million. The $1 million included a crude executive severance of just under a $0.5 million, $250,000 in expenses related to our strategic acquisition of Farncombe that closed last month and a charge of $256,000 related to our decision to vacate one of our leased office spaces.

I'm committed to making every attempt to align our total cost with the current revenue size and we will be looking at ways to maximize the efficiency of our SG&A dollars. Our GAAP net loss for Q2 2015 totaled 2.8 million or $0.34 per share. This compares to GAAP net income of $87,000 or a penny per share in the second quarter of last year. Our non-GAAP adjusted net loss for the 1.5 million or $0.20 per diluted share for the second quarter of 2015, this compares to non-GAAP adjusted net income of 1.8 million or $0.23 per share in Q2 2014.
