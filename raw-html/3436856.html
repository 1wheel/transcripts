National Holdings' (NHLD) CEO, Robert Fagenson on Q3 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 12:22 PM ET  |  About: National Holdings Corp. (NHLD) by: SA Transcripts

National Holdings Corporation (NASDAQ:NHLD)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015 9:00 am ET

Executives

Robert Fagenson - Chairman, Chief Executive Officer

Mark Goldwasser - President

Glen Worman - Executive Vice President, Finance

Ed McGregor - LHA Investor Relations

Analysts

Gary Reib - Marco Consulting

Robert Strougo - RIS Investments

Artem Fokin - Caro-Kann Capital

Operator

Good day and welcome the National Holdings Corporation Third Quarter 2015 conference call. Today’s conference is being recorded. At this time, I would like to turn the conference over to Ed McGregor from LHA. Please go ahead.

Ed McGregor

Thank you, Michelle, and good morning everyone. Thank you for joining us for National Holding Corporation’s third quarter 2015 earnings conference call. With me this morning are Robert Fagenson, National Holdings Corporation’s Chairman and Chief Executive Officer; Mark Goldwasser, President, and Glen Worman, Executive Vice President, Finance. Alan Levin, Chief Financial Officer is also on the line and will be available to answer your questions during the Q&A session.

National Holdings Corporation issued a press release this morning with details of the company’s quarterly financial results. A copy of the press release is available on the Investor Relations page of the company’s website at nhldcorp.com. I’d like to remind everyone that today’s call is being recorded. A replay of today’s call will be available by using the telephone numbers and conference ID provided in the earnings press release. In addition, an archived webcast replay will be available on the Investor Relations page of the company’s website at nhldcorp.com following conclusion of this conference call.

I’d also like to call your attention to the customary Safe Harbor disclosure regarding forward-looking information included in the earnings release. The conference call today may contain certain forward-looking statements, including statements regarding the goals, strategies, beliefs, future potential operating results and cash flows of National Holdings Corporation. While management believes these statements are reasonable based on estimated, assumptions and projections as of today, August 13, 2015, these statements are not guarantees of future performance. Time sensitive information may no longer be accurate at the time of any telephonic or webcast replay. Actual results may differ materially as a result of risks, uncertainties and other factors, including but not limited to the factors set forth in the company’s filings with the SEC. National Holdings undertakes no obligation to update or revise any of these forward-looking statements.

I would now like to turn the call over to Robert Fagenson. Robert?

Robert Fagenson

Thanks, Ed. Welcome and good morning everyone. I hope you’re all enjoying the same magnificent weather we are here in the northeastern United States and that that incredible market reversal we saw yesterday afternoon continues for all of you who are long; and for those of you who are short, I’m sorry.

On today’s call, I’ll start with some brief opening remarks about our financial and operational performance during the quarter and then turn the call over to my partner, Mark Goldwasser, who will give you a review of our business segments. Following Goldie’s remarks, we’ll hear from our new Executive Vice President of Finance, Glen Worman, who will provide some brief comments about operational initiatives that we have underway and discuss our financials in the third fiscal quarter of 2015 in greater detail. Once Glen is finished, I’ll wrap up and then we’ll open the call to your questions.

Despite challenging market conditions, we’ve built on our track record of profitability and on a quarter-over-quarter basis generated higher EBITDA after adjusting for some third quarter non-recurring expenses. We’re very encouraged by our third quarter results, and while the reported number might seem to suggest that we haven’t made significant progress on revenue and profits, we’re going to explain to you why that’s not the case and why we have cause for optimism looking forward.

On the revenue side, we continue to feel the weight of a choppy market environment for trade execution. This generally has a depressing effect on commissions as well as transfer fees, clearing services, and other fee-driven components of our revenue stream. Market volatility, energy and commodity prices, China, uncertainty about what the Fed may or may not do have all contributed to the types of markets that give retail investors the jitters and lead to lower volumes. These factors have created some headwinds for us in our principal transaction business as well, particularly the persistently interest rate environment.

Nonetheless, because of our diversified business model, we’re able to offset much of this weakness in the quarter with record investment banking results and a continued recovery in alternative investment product sales. In fact, our alternative investment sales have rebounded to the best levels since the interruption in that business in October of 2014.

While we’ve been adding registered representatives and advisors at a steady pace in recent quarters, the incremental revenue uplift and profit contribution from these additional team members are not always immediately apparent. That’s because it can take time for these registered reps and advisors to complete the account and asset transfer process and to ramp up their business to normalized production levels once they’ve joined National. We expect the full benefit of these additions of new advisors and reps will become evident in both top and bottom line numbers in subsequent quarters.

On the profit side of the ledger, continued expense control kept our net income and adjusted EBITDA positive for the third quarter. We did record some non-recurring expenses which had a depressive effect on the bottom line. These one-time expenses were related to compensation, legal fees, consulting fees and other provisions, and added up to $800,000 in the quarter. Adjusting for these items, pre-tax income would have been around $1.2 million and adjusted EBITDA would have been approximately $1.8 million. By comparison for the second quarter of fiscal 2015, we generated pre-tax income of a little over $500,000 and adjusted EBITDA of close to $1 million on essentially the same revenue.

With that, overview, I will turn over the call to my partner, Mark Goldwasser. Goldie, over to you.

Mark Goldwasser

Thank you, Robert, and good morning to everyone. Let me start by giving you my perspective on the quarter. On one hand, it is frustrating to be successfully executing on our strategy to see some real operating improvement in the quarter, and not have that improvement flow to the bottom line because of non-recurring expenses. On the other hand, we know our true earnings potential is well above our reported numbers and we have a business model in place to drive profitability even higher.

I will now run through our business segments. Our core retail brokerage operations and extensive network of registered employee and independent brokers and advisors offers a broad range of financial products and services to retail and institutional clients. In the third quarter of 2015, revenues from commissions, transfer fees and clearing services totaled $26 million versus $30 million in the same period last year. Of the $4 million decline, commissions accounted for roughly $3.6 million with part of the drop related to lower year-over-year sales of alternative investments, and the remainder to weaker industry conditions versus the prior year. That said, alternative investment products continued to see improving sales as a result of the actions taken in our second quarter and are now at the strongest levels since last October.

In terms of our broker network expansion, the continuing dislocation amongst independent and small broker dealers is creating unprecedented opportunities for us to accelerate our plans to enlarge and grow our distribution network and drive top line growth and improve margins. Year-to-date, we have enjoyed a record recruiting season and our forward pipeline remains as robust as we’ve ever seen. It is an ideal environment for us and we’re proud to become known as destination of choice for so many high caliber brokers and advisors looking for a new home, particularly in the tri-state area. Year-to-date, we have added 51 registered reps and advisors with $19 million in trailing 12-month production who have expanded our assets under management by $956 million. These figures represent only strategic additions we’ve made at the corporate level.

Recruiting at our local branches has also been robust. As we move into the final quarter of our fiscal year, we now have an expanded and more productive distribution network through which to offer a larger set of [indiscernible] products. We’re very pleased with the high caliber of brokers and advisors we continue to attract and hire.

In our investment banking operations, a business that addresses the underserved capital needs of emerging growth companies, the strong deal pipeline we’ve built has produced solid offerings and execution. Revenue for the third quarter increased 21% to approximately $6.4 million, which was our strongest banking quarter ever. We participated in 19 transactions comprised of a mix of private, lead and co-managed deals. Of the public deals, two were in the healthcare space, one in technology, two in energy, two in industrials, three in service and five in diversified funds. During the quarter, we also raised capital for three private companies. I’m pleased to report that this business is off to a strong start in the fourth quarter. We were the sole lead manager of Aqua Metals’ successful IPO in July, a pivotal milestone for national securities, and our future banking pipeline remains robust and strong.

Moving on to trading, our principal transaction revenues in the third quarter of 2015 continued to contract, declining almost 40% to $2.4 million from the prior year’s level. Our low interest rate environment, reduced profile of large institutional principal investment business, and shrinkage of proprietary trading throughout the industry continued to negatively impact trading volumes for equities, municipals, and corporate debt and treasury bonds where our trading is highly concentrated. In our asset management business, we saw investment advisory fees decline slightly to $3.8 million versus the prior year period.

Lastly, our tax preparation and accounting business saw revenues climb 6.5% to $2.7 million versus the prior year’s level as we continue to steadily grow our client base and fees. Throughout the organization, we are actively taking steps to cut operating expenses further wherever possible and narrowly refine our necessary or excessive costs. Evidence of such efforts was seen in our communications, occupancy and other admin expenses, all of which declined in third quarter from prior levels. The bottom line is that we have a sound strategy in place and we’ll continue to execute against it. We’ll continue to expand our retail broker and advisor network, as well as our offerings of diverse products and services we distribute through that network. We’ll also drive improved profitability by reducing our overall cost base as we scale this platform.

I’ll now turn the call over to Glen Worman, who will provide some overview comments on our operations and financials. Glen?

Glen Worman

Thanks Goldie. Good morning everyone. I’ve been on board as Executive Vice President of Finance now for three months at this point, and I’d like to take a moment to share with you my observations since joining the company, what initiatives I’ll be working on, and the intended outcomes of these initiatives.

I joined the firm to support Goldie specifically to enhance our operations, streamline the production and reporting of our financials, and strengthen our corporate governance. Over the course of my career, I’ve been involved in the management at one time or another of nearly all of National’s business lines, and I’m bringing that wealth of experience and best practices knowledge to the firm.

For a financial services company of our size, National offers a broad set of businesses, employs a diverse business model, and has a talented executive management team. A good cost control mindset is already in place at the company, which should become more apparent as we move forward. Finally, National is well capitalized for its current business model and size, and these are impressive attributes upon which we can scale the business and we are well positioned for growth. The initiatives I plan to devote my efforts include business line growth and profitability, corporate governance, operating controls, and improved financial reporting and analysis, all with the goal of driving profitable growth for the company as a whole.

I’ll now take you through the financial results of our earnings, which will be filed on Form 10-Q with the SEC. For the June 30, 2015 quarter, we reported total revenues of $42.3 million, which represented a decline of 9% versus revenue of $46.6 million in the prior year’s quarter. The principal components of revenue in the current quarter is as follows: commissions and related fees of $26 million, investment banking $6.4 million, investment advisory $3.8 million, tax prep and accounting $2.7 million, principal transactions of $2.4 million, and interest and dividends and other income totaling $1 million.

On the expense side, costs declined in the quarter by 7% versus the prior year, with the most meaningful decline coming in the commissions, compensation and fees category which was down commensurate with revenue 7% to $35.8 million. We were also able to realize double-digit declines in our costs related to clearing fees and other administrative expenses and single-digit declines in our communications and occupancy expenses. Offsetting these declines was a 61% increase in professional fees to $1.7 million and a 15% increase in our license and registration expenses. As Robert stated, $800,000 of our expenses in the quarter were non-recurring.

Despite the declining year-over-year revenue and the inclusion of one-time expenses, we still reported operating income of roughly $400,000 for the current quarter. While this fell short of the $1.7 million reported in the quarter ended June 30, 2014, good expense management helped maintain profitability.

Net income was $149,000 or $0.01 per basic and diluted share for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 compared to net income of $1.7 million or $0.13 per basic and diluted share for the prior year quarter. Income in the third quarter of 2014 benefited from the recognition of tax credits which resulted in an effective tax rate of 1.7% in that period. Adjusted EBITDA, which is a non-GAAP measure, was $1 million for the third fiscal quarter of 2015 versus $2.3 million in the prior year’s quarter and $3 million for the first six months of 2015 versus $8.2 million in the previous year’s period.

Turning now to our balance sheet, as of June 30, 2015 we had cash and cash equivalents, which included receivables from our clearing firm and marketable securities of approximately $30.1 million or roughly 46% of our total assets as compared to $30.5 million or 47% of our total assets for the fiscal year ended September 30, 2014. The company’s total stockholders equity was $45.8 million or $3.68 per share, and represented a 3% increase when compared to the $44.5 million as of September 30, 2014. Lastly, our balance sheet remains debt-free.

With that, I’ll turn the call back to Robert.

Robert Fagenson

Thank you, Glen. Obviously we would have liked to report stronger profit numbers for the quarter, but we know our underlying profitability was much more robust than that shown in our reported numbers and that we continue to be pointed in the right direction. Our business has remained steady throughout the quarter and continues to track that way as we move into the fourth quarter. Despite industry-wide commission and transaction headwinds, our core retail brokerage business remains robust, our distribution network continues to expand, our investment banking business continues to enjoy a steady and diversified deal flow, and our investment advisory and tax and accounting business remains solid.

In short, we find ourselves well positioned to capitalize on our strengths and deal with whatever uncertainties the economy and capital markets may have in store for us. We’re going to continue to explore opportunities to add scale to our business, opportunities which seem to repeatedly arise when firms combine and talented individuals and groups become available through either dissatisfaction or engagement, something we’re seeing in record numbers at this point in time. Because we’re known in the market for giving brokers and advisors a growing platform, both individuals and groups are attracted to National Holdings.

With that, I’m going to conclude my prepared remarks and turn it back to the operator for any questions you might have. Operator, back to you.
