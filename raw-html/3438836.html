diaDexus' (CPA) CEO Lori Rafield on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 7:32 PM ET  |  About: diaDexus, Inc. (DDXS) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Leone D. Patterson - Chief Financial Officer

Lori F. Rafield - Chairman and Chief Executive Officer.

William H. Hammond - Vice President of Sales

Analysts

diaDexus, Inc. (OTCQB:DDXS) Q2 2015 Results Earnings Conference August 13, 2015 4:30 PM ET

Operator

Good day ladies and gentlemen, and welcome to the diaDexus, Incorporated Second Quarter 2015 Financial Release Conference Call. At this time, all presentation in a listen-only mode. Later, we will conduct a question-and-answer session and instructions will follow at that time [Operator Instructions] As a reminder this call is being recorded.

I would now like to introduce your host for today's conference Leone Patterson, Chief Financial Officer. You may begin.

Leone D. Patterson

Thank you Kat and good afternoon everyone. We just issued a press release with our financial results for the second quarter 2015, which is available on our new corporate website at www.diadexus.com. With me today are Dr. Lori Rafield, Chairman and CEO and Hodi Hammond, Vice President of Sales. Following our prepared remarks, we will open the lines for questions.

Before I begin our formal remarks, let me remind you that we will be making forward-looking statements including plans and expectations for our products and pipeline, plans for listing to NASDAQ, market opportunities, financial guidance, regulatory submissions, and execution by a new leadership team all of which involves certain assumptions risks and uncertainties and could cause our actual results to differ materially from these statements. A description of these risks can be found in our latest SEC disclosure documents including our Form 10-K, From 10-Q and today's press release.

With that, I’ll turn the call over to Lori.

Lori F. Rafield

Good afternoon and thank you for joining us for our second quarter 2015 financial results conference call. On today's call, we will review our financial performance for the second quarter of 2015 and provide an overview of recent corporate achievements and plans. We will also be providing an update on our ongoing commercial initiatives.

We recently achieved several key milestones and corporate objectives that we believe establish a path towards accelerated revenue growth for the company. First, Hodi Hammond was recently appointed VP of Sales. Hodi brings to us extensive experience in commercial strategy in sales within the diagnostic market, and he has the deep understanding of the commercial channels that are important to diaDexus.

His expertise in developing relationships with accountable care organizations and integrated delivery networks will be invaluable and we welcome him to the team. I would be turning the call over to Hodi shortly and he will be providing a detailed commercial update, including an overview of our new focus on hospitals and hospital networks as a source of untapped growth for our PLAC franchise.

We also recently announced a new collaboration with the Mayo Clinic surrounding our new PLAC activity test. Under this partnership diaDexus and the Mayo Clinic will work together to properly integrate Lp-PLA2 activity testing inter-clinical practice at Mayo. Once fully integrated, the test will be available to patients and providers worldwide through Mayo Clinic’s reference laboratory, which offers advanced laboratory testing in pathology services to more than 5000 healthcare organizations. Mayo has been strong supporter of the value of the information generated from the PLAC activity test and we look forward to working with them on this important initiative.

In addition to the Mayo, we have signed an additional PLAC activity agreement with the University of Oklahoma. This partnership was the result of our relationship with key opinion leader and biomarker researcher Dr. Kenneth Glic, who is also a strong advocate for the importance of the information that PLAC activity provides. And finally on the corporate front, we recently completed one for 15 reverse stock splits. We believe this reverse split is a critical step toward our NASDAQ up listing and is part of our larger strategic initiative to increase awareness and visibility for diaDexus.

For those of you who might be knew the story, I would like to provide a brief overview of our business. PLAC ELISA is our first commercial product, which has been on the market for approximately 10 years. It measures the concentration of circulating Lp-PLA2 in the glide and aids physicians in accessing risk for both coronary heart diseases in patients who have not had prior coronary event and in ischemic stroke in patients with atherosclerosis. PLAC ELISA is an important product for diaDexus as it provides a solid foundation to top line revenue growth and important cash flow.

PLAC Activity is our newest Lp-PLA2 test. It was cleared for market by the FDA in December 2014 and recently launched by diaDexus. New thinking junction with clinical evaluation and patient risk assessment, PLAC Activity aids the physician and predicting risk of CHD in patients with no prior history of cardiovascular events. This is an important distinction, because approximately 75% of CHD patients the vast majority have no history of cardiovascular events. Our launch activities to-date have been largely focused around introducing the test to KLLs at academic hospitals working to secure partnership with these academic hospitals and generally educating and driving awareness about this new test.

In our heart failure franchise, we continue to advance proADM the lead by our market from our pre commercial pipeline. ProADM has entered development on the ELISA platform, which is the fastest establish path to FDA regulatory clearance for novel heart failure biomarkers. We are currently preparing to initiate discussions with the FDA in the fall of 2015 and we remain on track to file a 510(NYSE:K) regulatory submission in early 2016.

In addition proADM on the ELISA platform, we are also developing a proADM point-of-care product. Feasibility testing is complete and we are currently assembling development plans to commercialized this test. In our clinical services business, we continue to seek potential partners that are developing therapies for indications for cardiovascular risk information would provide valuable insights.

I will now turn the call over to Hodi to review our commercial business in more detail. Following his and Leone’s update, I will provide summary remarks and then open the line for Q&A. Hodi.

William H. Hammond

Thank you, Lori. diaDexus products are targeted towards providing physicians with information to help better manage of their patients with a broad spectrum of cardiovascular disease conditions. Over last several months, there have been multiple product approvals for new cardiovascular drugs, which underscore the continued interest in this therapeutic area.

First I will provide an overview of the PLAC ELISA business. diaDexus has been successfully selling PLAC ELISA to physicians through our specialty lab reference and national partners for over a decade. PLAC ELISA is the only blood test cleared by the FDA to aid in accessing the risk for both coronary heart disease and ischemic stroke in patients with atherosclerosis.

Interest in the PLAC ELISA test remains high, but the cardiovascular specialty lab channel has been experiencing significant challenges recently with one of the largest labs Health Diagnostic Laboratory or HDL filing for bankruptcy and other cardio specialty labs struggling to find growth avenues.

It’s also well known that obtaining reimbursement coverage from the commercial payers is a challenge, yet in spite of these challenges, the diaDexus sales team has been able to stabilize losses, add new business and main solid foundation revenue levels and important cash flow for the company.

Looking forward, we have several key initiatives that we’ll be implementing, which we believe will help accelerate the revenue growth. As we move into the second half of 2015, we have separated the sales force to focus on either the commercial lab or hospital channel. The existing sales force which we call the lab team will focus on PLAC ELISA and will continue to emphasis on maintaining and expanding relationships with specialty reference and national labs.

We’ve already began hiring an additional small that focused sales force, the hospital team, which will be solely dedicated to selling PLAC Activity. The PLAC Activity. Can be run on automated lab systems, a significant advantage from PLAC ELISA.

Therefore the PLAC Activity force will focus their efforts on selling this test to the hospital market, a diversified channel which includes Integrated Health Systems, Accountable Care Organizations also known as ACOs, the veteran’s health administration, the Department of Defense, and large and small physician group practices.

It’s currently estimated that over 70% of physicians are now employed by hospitals and our goal is to identify and educate those physicians, so that they can begin to drive demand for PLAC Activity that their hospitals.

The Mayo Clinic and University of Oklahoma collaborations, which Lori mentioned earlier, are the first examples of our refined strategy, which targets the hospital channel. We believe both collaborations demonstrate the significant interest in PLAC Activity from both key opinion leaders and the border scientific and medical community.

There are few other initiatives designed to accelerate sales of our PLAC franchise that I would like to highlight. In June, we successfully launched PLACactivity.com, this website serves as a clinical knowledge portal and features annotated publications, expert commentary, regulatory updates and multimedia platforms like video concert to educate physicians, laboratory professionals and patients about the clinical value of the test.

Later this year, we are looking forward to the publication of the results of the regard study. The validation study which was used to obtain FDA clearance for PLAC Activity. This study looked at nearly 4600 individuals with no prior history of cardiovascular events and demonstrated that a high PLAC Activity versus a low PLAC Activity showed a greater hazard ratio for predicting subsequent coronary heart disease and then nearly all other established cardiovascular risk factors including LDL, HDL, age and hypertension.

These results were statically significant within each gender and race analyzed independently. As many of you know, validating clinical data is the cornerstone of any diagnostic technology and is critical to gaining both critical acceptance, and market uptick for a new test like PLAC Activity. This will be the first publish data for the PLAC franchise in several years and it will be a key component of our sales and education efforts.

So in closing, there are numerous potential customers of the PLAC test and we believe the implementation of two highly focused sales organizations is an important step toward maximizing the value of both the PLAC ELISA and the PLAC Activity test. There is lot of work ahead, but that said, I’m confident that we can achieve commercial success with our PLAC franchise and we look forward to achieving this in the months ahead and the quarters ahead.

So with that, now let me turn it over to Leone Patterson, our Chief Financial Officer.

Leone D. Patterson

Thank you, Hodi. I’ll provide an overview of our year-to-date results. Total revenues or the six months ended June 30, 2015, were$10.1 million compared to $13.1 million for the six months ended June 2014. This decrease is primarily due to lower PLAC ELISA product sales and a decrease in service revenues related to GSK agreement, which completed during the fourth quarter of 2014.

Total product sales for the six months ended June 30, 2015, were $10 million compared to $11.6 million for the six months ended June 30, 2014. This $1.6 million decrease is primarily due to a $2.7 million decrease and PLAC ELISA sales to our specialty lab customers but partially offset by $1.1 million increase in PLAC ELISA sales to our national and regional labs.

Of the $2.0 million decrease in sales to specialty labs, $2 million is due to our largest customer HDL filing for bankruptcy in June 2015. Subsequent to HDL’s bankruptcy filing, they have continued to place orders with the company on a cash-in-advance basis.

Total operating cost and expenses for the six months ended June 30, 2015 were $13.5 million compared to $17.2 million for the six months ended June 30, 2014. This translates to a reduction of approximately 22%, which was primarily due to lower research and development expenses related to the B.R.A.H.M.S. Thermo Fisher license agreement for the company’s three heart failure biomarkers.

In addition, there was reduced personnel expenses due to the first half of 2015 resulting from the restructuring implemented in the fourth quarter of 2014. These decreases were partially offset by $0.9 million of provision for doubtful accounts related to outstanding receivables associated with HDL’s bankruptcy, we do intend to file a claim to recover those receivables but their prediction under the bankruptcy filing this amount has been considered uncollectable for accounting purposes.

The company’s net loss for the six months ended June 30, 2015 was $4.3 million or $1.13 per share compared to a net loss of $4.5 million or $1.24 per share for the six months ended June 30, 2014. Cash and cash equivalents at June 30, 2015 were $12.1 million compared to $14.9 million at December 31, 2014.

As we look forward due to our largest customer HDL filing for bankruptcy and the accompanying uncertainty around their ongoing business operations, we are rescinding our previously stated revenue guidance for 2015 and will not be providing an outlook for 2015 at this time.

As mentioned previously, HDL continues to place orders on a cash advance basis, but going forward there is uncertainty regarding their ability to continue these orders given the bankruptcy filing along with their announced company sale process. However, excluding HDL, we are seeing growth overall in the business year-to-date 2014 to 2015 led by our national and regional labs trending at approximately 76%, we expect that growth to continue as we execute on our strategy and diversify our customer base.

Operating cost and expenses are expected to increase in the second half of 2015, this expected increase is primarily due to ongoing investments in the PLAC business to develop the new hospital channel and drive physician demand for PLAC activity, advancing the development of proADM ELISA towards FDA submission in early 2016 and lastly the development of proADM point-of-care. We will continue to closely manage our expenses as we work towards our goal of significantly reducing our 2015 operating cost versus 2014.

Now I would turn the call back to Lori.

Lori F. Rafield

We are very pleased with the progress we have made during the first half of 2015, we continue to expand the leadership team and adding Hodi to the team this quarter was a critical step in executing our larger commercial strategy aimed at accelerating revenue growth. We are actively engaged in activities designed to maximize our two commercial products PLAC ELISA and PLAC Activity.

We believe there is a significant untapped market opportunity for the PLAC franchise and we are working to accelerate growth of PLAC with a focus on driving activity through new channels of potential customers. On top of the financial foundation that PLAC provides having a high value biomarker in heart failure allows diaDexus to tap into a huge opportunity and potentially help manage patients suffering from acute heart failure episodes.

We are rapidly advancing our lead pipeline asset proADM ELISA and we are on track to submit a 510(K) regulatory filing early in 2016. Assuming FDA clearance we could have our third commercial product next year. Behind proADM ELISA the proADM point-of-care product is completing feasibility.

On the corporate front, we are actively working toward an up listing to the NASDAQ, we believe this will increase visibility and awareness of the company, improve our access to capital and improve liquidity for our shareholders. In closing, we believe our assets have substantial commercial potential that is yet to be unlocked and we see multiple opportunities to drive top line revenue growth.

We realize there is substantial work ahead especially given the challenges in the cardiovascular labs channel. However, with PLAC Activity we now have an opportunity to accelerate growth through the new hospital channel and directly drive demand for this new test. We will continue executing on our strategy of diversifying our customer base and product pipeline and we will strive to maintain the momentum we've created in the last few months.

We look forward to updating you on our progress in future calls and with that we would be happy to take your questions.
