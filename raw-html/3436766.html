This increase was mainly due to higher hog selling price during the second quarter of 2015, and increased retail sales volume of specialty black hog pork products, as well as revenue contributed from newly acquired servo and security and protection business that contributed $0.78 million, or 7.4% of total revenues.

We sold a total of 40,970 regular breeder hogs, regular market hogs and black hogs during the second quarter compared to 40,306 hogs sold for the same period of last year. And average selling price increased 8% to $271 from $251 for the same period of last year.

However, sales of black hogs continue to grew -- grew, increasing 21.5% to $3.34 million with number of black hog sold decreasing slightly to 13,123 hogs and the average selling price of black hogs increasing by 21.5% to $254.

We sold 13,123 black hogs with an average selling price of $254 generating $3.3 million in revenues during the second first quarter of 2015.

We sold 67,155 kilograms of specialty black hog pork products through retail at approximately $5 per kilogram, generating $0.31 million revenues for the second quarter of 2015. This compared to 39,568 kilograms sold at approximately $5 per kilogram and revenues of $0.18 million for the same period of last year.

For the second quarter of 2015, revenues from our servo business was $0.54 million, including $0.38 million from selling electro-hydraulic servo devices and relevant components and $0.18 million from providing maintenance services to customers and accounted for 5% of total revenues for the second quarter of 2015.

For the second quarter of 2015, revenues from our security and protection business was $0.25 million, including $0.19 million from selling optical fiber invasion alarm systems and $0.06 million from selling education devices and providing relevant technical supports and accounted for 2.4% of total revenues for the second quarter of 2015.

For the second quarter of 2015, cost of goods sold increased 9.4% to $8.16 million from $7.45 million from the same period of last year. The increase in overall cost of goods sold was mainly due to newly acquired servo and security and protection businesses, as well as increased sales in retail and hog farming business.

Cost of goods sold related to hog farming, retail, servo and security and protection were $7.59 million, $0.23 million, $0.23 million, and $0.1 million respectively for the second quarter of 2015, compared to $7.28 million, $0.18 million for hog farming and retail respectively for the same period of last year.

For the second quarter of 2015, overall gross profit increased 299.5% to $2.48 million from $0.62 million for the same period of last year. The increase in overall gross profit was mainly due to increases in gross profit from newly acquired servo, security and protection business, as well as an increase in retail and hog farming business.

For the second quarter of 2015, overall gross margin was 23.3% with gross margin for hog farming, retail, servo and security and protection segment was 21%, 25%, 56% and 58% respectively for the second quarter of 2015. This compared to overall gross margin of 7.7% and gross margin for hog farming and retail of 8% and 3% respectively for the same period of last year.

SG&A expenses were $1.54 million for the second quarter of 2015, increasing $0.58 -- $0.59 million from $0.95 million for the same period of last year. The increase was primarily due to the result of our new operating segments, which incurred expenses of $0.14 million for our servo business and $0.21 million for our security and protection business and non-cash stock-based compensation expenses of $0.14 million.

Operating income for the second quarter of 2015 was $0.94 million, compared to operating loss of $0.33 million for the same period of last year. Operating profit margin for the second quarter of 2015 was 8.9% compared to operating loss margin of 4.1% for the same period of last year.

As a result of foregoing, net income for the second quarter of 2015 was $0.92 million, compared to $0.56 million for the same period of last year. After the deduction of non-controlling interests, net income attributable to common shareholders for the second quarter of 2015 was $0.92 million, or $0.03 per diluted share. This compared to net income attributable to common shareholders of $0.56 million, or $0.03 per diluted share, for the same period of last year.

Now shift gears to year-to-date financial results. For the six months ended June 30, 2015, revenue grew 23.8%, to $21.99 million from $17.76 million for the same period of last year. This increase was mainly due to higher hog selling pricing during the second quarter of 2015, and increased retail sales volume of specialty black hog pork products, as well as revenue contributed from newly acquired servo and security and protection business that contributed $2.55 million, or 11.6% of total revenues.

For the six months ended June 30, 2015, revenues from hog farming which include sales of regular breeder hogs, regular market hogs, and black hogs, increased by 8.8%, to $18.64 million for the six months ended June 30, 2015 from $17.13 million for the same period of last year.

We sold a total of 84,765 regular breeder hogs, regular market hogs, and black hogs during the six months ended June 30, 2015, compared to 81,658 hogs sold for the same period of last year. Average selling price for our hog sold increased to $220 for the six months ended June 30, 2015, from $210 for the same period of last year.

For the six months ended June 30, 2015, revenues from regular breeder hogs increased 2.8% to $3.84 million with total numbers of regular breeder hogs sold increasing 2.6% to 14,374 hogs from 14, 007 for the same period of last year, and average selling price increased 0.4% to $267 from $266 for the same period of last year.

For the six months ended June 30, 2015, revenue from regular market hog increased 2.2% to $8.16 million as numbers of regular market hogs sold decreased 1.9% to 41,999 hogs and average selling price of regular market hogs increased 4.3% to $194. However, sales of black hogs continue to grow, increasing 22.6% to $6.64 million with a numbers of black hogs sold increasing 14.2% to 28,392 hogs and average selling price of black hog increasing 7.3% to $234.

For the six months ended June 30, 2015, we generated $7.44 million in revenue through the sale of black hog and black hog specialty pork products compared to $6.05 million for the same period of last year. Revenue from our servo business was $1.75 million, for the six months ended June 30, 2015, including $1.39 million from selling electro-hydraulic servo devices and relevant components and $0.37 million from providing maintenance services to customers, and accounted for 8% of total revenues for the six months ended June 30, 2015.

Revenues from our security and protection business was $0.79 million, for the six months ended June 30, 2015, including $0.37 million from selling optical fiber invasion alarm systems and $0.42 million from selling education devices and providing relevant technical support, and accounted for 3.6% of total revenues for the six months ended June 30, 2015.

Cost of goods sold increased 9.9%, to $17.25 million for the six months ended June 30, 2015 from $15.71 million for the same period of last year. The increase in overall cost of goods sold was mainly due to newly acquired servo and security and protection business, as well as increased sales in retail and hog farming business.

Cost of goods sold related to hog farming, retail, servo, security and protection were $15.25 million, $0.61 million, $1.02 million and $0.37 million, respectively, for the six months ended June 30, 2015, compared to $15.21 million, $0.42 million for hog farming and retail, respectively, for the same period of last year.

Overall gross profit was $4.73 million for the six months ended June 30, 2015, increased 130.5% from $2.05 million for the same period of last year. The increase in overall gross profit was mainly due to increase in gross profit from newly acquired servo, security and protection, as we as increase in retail and hog farming business.

SG&A expenses was $3.87 million for the six months ended June 30, 2015, increasing $1.86 million, or 92.8% from $2.01 million for the same period of last year. The increase was primarily the result of our new operating segments, which incurred expenses of $0.3 million from our servo business and $0.7 million from our security and protection business, our non-cash stock based compensation expenses of $0.74 million.

Operating income for the six months ended June 30, 2015 was $0.86 million, compared to $0.04 million for the same period of last year. Operating profit margin for the six months ended June 30, 2015 was 3.9%, compared to 0.3% for the same period of last year.

Net income for the six months ended June 30, 2015 was $0.71 million compared to $0.83 for the same period of last year. After the deduction of non-controlling interests, net income attributable to common shareholders for the six months ended June 30, 2015 was $0.68 million, or $0.02 per diluted share. This compared to net income attributable to common shareholders of $0.96 million, or $0.06 per diluted share for the same period of last year.

Now for a quick summary of our balance sheet and cash flow. As of June 30, 2015, the Company has cash and cash equivalents of $46.81 million. Working capital as of June 30, 2015 was $53.9 million as compared to $47.52 million at December 31, 2014. Net cash used in financing activities was $3.47 million for the six months ended June 30, 2015, compared to net cash provided by financing activities of $16.33 million for the same period of last year.
