NephroGenex's (NRX) CEO Pierre Legault on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 10:31 AM ET  |  About: NephroGenex (NRX) by: SA Transcripts

NephroGenex, Inc. (NASDAQ:NRX)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015 8:00 AM ET

Executives

John Hamill - Chief Financial Officer

Pierre Legault - Chief Executive Officer

Analysts

Yi Chen - H.C. Wainwright & Co., LLC

Operator

Good morning, ladies and gentlemen. Thank you for standing by. Welcome to the NephroGenex Second Quarter 2015 Earnings Call. At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. Later, we will conduct a question-and-answer session and instructions will follow at that time. As a reminder this conference will be recorded and available for replay.

I would like to introduce you to your host today, John Hamill, Chief Financial Officer. Please go ahead.

John Hamill

Good morning, everyone and thank you for joining the call. With me today is Pierre Legault, our Chief Executive Officer; and Jai Patel, our Chief Medical Officer. We announced financial results for the second quarter ended June 30, 2015 yesterday after the market closed. You can access the press release on the Investor Relations page of our website at www.nephrogenex.com.

Before we review the financial results and business highlights, I will read the company’s Safe Harbor statement. Comments made during this conference call will contain statements that are or may be deemed forward-looking statements within the meaning of the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995. Forward-looking statements include statements regarding NephroGenex, Inc.’s potential income streams, financing position, development and commercialization efforts, timelines, availability of data, and potential efficacy and market potential with respect to products and product candidates of the company and its partners.

These forward-looking statements are subject to risks and uncertainties that could cause actual outcomes and results to differ materially from those in the forward-looking statements. Reference to these risks and uncertainties was made in yesterday’s press release and they are disclosed in more detail in the company’s most recent filings with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission. The forward-looking statements made during this call reflect the company’s current views with respect to future events, and the company does not undertake and specifically disclaims any obligation to update or revise these statements.

All forward-looking statements are expressly qualified in their entirety by this cautionary statement. When evaluating the company’s business and securities, investors should give careful consideration to these risks and uncertainties.

And now, I will hand the call over to Pierre.

Pierre Legault

Thank you, John and good morning everyone. Thank you for joining us today. I am pleased to report that NephroGenex made significant gains in the second quarter greatly increasing the base of enrollment of our pivotal Phase 3 study of oral Pyridorin in diabetic nephropathy. PYR-311 is the first of two double-blind, placebo-controlled studies in our PIONEER program design to evaluate the safety and efficacy of Pyridorin in reducing the rate of renal disease progression in Type 2 diabetic patients.

With more than 120 clinical sites now participating in the U.S., Europe and Asia, we have been able to significantly increase the number of patients who are undergoing screening for randomization.

As a result, we are currently tracking ahead of our plan for full enrollment by mid-2016. As you know, we have a Special Protocol Assessment with the FDA for the PIONEER program, allowing the use of a new endpoint time to reach serum creatinine level of 50% versus baseline, which is expected to reduce trial follow-up time and cost by approximately 50% compared to previous diabetic nephropathy trials. NephroGenex is one of the first companies to use this new endpoint. Which we believe will provide the strategic advantage.

Pyridorin has received fast track designation from the FDA for this indication. The company also adds the support of the European Medicines Agency for the PIONEER study’s end-point and clinical protocol. The positive scientific advice from the EMA indicated that the Phase 3 PIONEER program could be adequate to support a Marketing Authorization Application for full market approval in Europe solidifying our goal, global regulatory strategy, for a treatment that could make a huge difference for millions of patients around the world who suffer from diabetic kidney disease, a chronic and life-threatening disease.

It is estimated that 19 million people are diagnosed with diabetics in the United States alone. It is estimated that approximately one-third of these patient will suffer from diabetic nephropathy, a significant unmet medical need. To this extent, it is important to note that Pyridorin is the only advanced drug candidate for diabetic nephropathy that targets an important underlying cause of the disease. The compound inhibits and scavenges the pathogenic oxidative chemistry that lead to the development and progression of diabetic nephropathy.

Our study is 90% powered to detect the 28% treatment effect. In the earlier Phase 2 studies, Pyridorin has shown a 57% treatment effect in the patient population being studied in PIONEER. Over the past six months, we extended the number of clinical sites for the study and plan to have approximately 150 sites by the end of 2015. The study is worldwide and will take place in the United States, Germany, France, Spain, Hungary, Israel, Hong Kong, Australia, the UK and other countries.

The program for our second product candidate, an intravenous form of Pyridorin to treat hospital-acquired acute kidney injury or AKI has achieved strong results and continues to advance towards an IND filing later this year. At the National Kidney Foundation’s 2015 Spring Clinical Meeting, we reported strong results from animal model of ischemia reperfusion induced acute kidney injury, which emulate the AKI that can occur following cardiopulmonary bypass surgery.

In the studies, Pyridorin substantially reduce the severity of acute kidney injury that emerges in this model, as well as marker of renal tissue damage and fibrosis. Based on these results, we are optimistic that this provides yet another example of the compound’s treatment effect on renal function.

On the corporate front, we continue to strengthen our leadership team with the addition of Dr. Jai Patel as our new Chief Medical officer, bringing with him an impressive track record in clinical research and development. Dr. Patel who previously held several senior management roles at GlaxoSmithKline will help guide the company’s Phase 3 program with oral Pyridorin for diabetic nephropathy and the development of our product candidate for acute kidney injury.

Dr. Wesley Fox has transitioned to the role of Chairman of our scientific advisory board and will also act in an advisory role focusing on strategic projects and investor relations. In July, the company closed a secondary financing, raising net proceed of $7.1 million extending our cash runway into early 2016. As always, we continue to look at option to fund the company’s operation.

With that, I’ll turn now the call over to John to take you through our second quarter financial results.

John Hamill

Thank you, Pierre. Our company’s financial results for the second quarter ended June 30, 2015 were in line with our expectations. Research and development expenses increased to approximately $4.2 million in the quarter from approximately $3.9 million in the second quarter of 2014 due primarily to the initiation of clinical sites and patient recruitment of our Phase 3 Pyridorin program.

General and administrative spending in the second quarter ended June 30, 2015 was approximately $2.0 million versus $1.6 million in the second quarter 2014; due to the timing of expenses, additional payroll and non-cash compensation expenses, and increased cost incurred from operating as a public company.

The net loss in the second quarter 2015 was approximately $6.4 million or $0.72 per common share basic and diluted, as compared to a loss of $5.4 million, or $0.61 per share basic and diluted in the second quarter of 2014. The results included non-cash stock-based compensation charges of approximately $314,000 in the second quarter of 2015, and approximately $440,000 in the second quarter of 2014.

Turning to the balance sheet, total cash, cash equivalents and short-term investments as of the second quarter ended June 30, 2015 were approximately $18.9 million versus approximately $28.7 million at year end 2014.

I will now hand the call back over to Pierre.

Pierre Legault

Thank you, John. As you have heard today, we’re continuing to made solid progress with the late stage development of oral Pyridorin, and we are excited about the level of interest in the trial and steady pace of recruitment. We believe our PIONEER program is in a strong position to succeed based on two very important factors.

First, the trial has incorporated the revised endpoints of a 50% change in serum cystatin [ph] versus the 100% change that was previously required for approval. This endpoint has been agreed with the FDA under the SPA and follows the 2012 FDA-National Kidney Foundation, a special scientific workshop. This will reduce the overall length of the trial.

Second, the Phase 3 study is being conducted in an enriched population of CKD patients with advanced renal disease. This population is expected to have a high event rate and has been selected based on the clinical results of our Phase 2b trial. With these reasons along with the compound’s unique mechanism of action, we are confident our compound has the potential to change the life of millions of diabetic patients around the world. We currently have limited treatment options.

Before I conclude, I want to thank all of our employees for their continued hard work and their enthusiasm. And I reiterate my confidence in our management team’s ability to take full advantage of our solid scientific IP, our clinical and regulatory strategies, and all of the groundwork we laid in the past years to advance our scientific programs and build value for our shareholders over the long-term.

Again, thank you for joining us today, and for your interest and support. We look forward to any comment or question you may have.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

Thank you. Ladies and gentlemen at this time, we will be conducting a question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions] Thank you. Our first question is coming from the line of Yi Chen with HC Wainwright. Please proceed with your question.

Yi Chen

Hi. Congratulations and thank you for taking the question. Can you update us your current estimate of data reports from the PIONEER trial?

Pierre Legault

I’m sorry. Could you repeat your question? You broke up on…

Yi Chen

Can you - I’m sorry. Can you update us with your current estimates for the data report out of the PIONEER trial?

Pierre Legault

Well, at this point, we’re progressing. We expect to have the full recruitment of the 600 patients of the first trial in the first-half of next year. Then, we also expect to have an interim analysis will be done middle of 2016. And then, it will all - we need to - before we can comment really on when we expected to complete the trial, it will depend on how the drug really works. If the drug has the efficacy we saw in the Phase 2b, it’s going to take a bit longer to collect the events, if the trial is slightly below 57%, then it’s going to go faster. We expect the whole trial to take 3 to 3.5 years. So we started the trial in June 2014.

Yi Chen

Okay.

Operator

Thank you. [Operator Instructions] It appears we have no further questions at this time. So I’d like to turn the floor back over to Mr. Legault for any additional concluding comments.

Pierre Legault

So, thank you everyone for joining us on our second quarter earnings call today, and have a very nice day. Thank you.

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, this does conclude today’s teleconference. We thank you for your participation and you may disconnect your lines at this time.
