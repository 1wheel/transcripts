Matthew Rosen

This is an exciting time for our company, as we emerge as a major participant in a rapidly growing and evolving industry. Then I'll review our key strategic goals in more depth, after which we'll open the call to your questions.

To provide some context, over the last two-and-a-half years Fusion has grown from $44 million of revenue to approximately $100 million in revenue run rate today. The portfolio of cloud-centric products we offer today to our existing and prospected customers, include software-as-a-service and infrastructure-as-a-service, as well as the underlying cloud networking component that bind these product offerings together, enabling us to provide best-in-class unified communications, cloud computing, security and cloud storage for every size of business and enterprise customer.

This positions Fusion as the single source for businesses, looking to move into the cloud, of course, shared by a growing number of businesses, both in the U.S. as well as around the world. One of the leading market research companies, Gartner, has estimated this to grow to $244 billion market in 2017.

Our strategy is centered around increasing our customer base through capital efficient growth, primarily through disciplined targeted acquisition, complemented by our partner, direct and inside sales efforts.

The company has demonstrated, through three acquisitions executed in the past two-and-a-half years, it's ability to identify high-quality cloud services businesses that we can acquire at attractive valuations, integrate tightly into our platform and gather synergies both on the revenue and cost side, generally making them accretive on a cash flow basis within six months, but often from the date of acquisition.

We also believe that as we continue to gain scale and add to our already large portfolio of products and services, our organic growth will increase, primarily through cross-selling and up-selling within our growing customer base. I'll speak more about this later on the call, but we are very optimistic about the opportunities our industry and strategy offer and our abilities to take advantage of these opportunities.

Turning now to the company's performance for the second quarter. Our total revenue for the quarter was $25.1 million, an increase of 8% year-over-year.

Fusion engages in two lines of business: our Business Services segment, focusing on cloud-based product offerings; and our Carrier Services segment, which provide interconnect services worldwide between large carriers. The Business Services segment contributed approximately $16 million and the Carrier Services segment about $9 million of the overall $25.1 million in total.

Revenue in the Business Services segment, Fusion's major focus, was up 3% year-over-year. And importantly about 85% of those revenues were contracted monthly recurring revenue or MRR, which we typically book through multi-year contracts.

Revenues from our Carrier Services segment were up 20% from $7 million in the year-ago period. This increase reflects a return to more normalized level of activity for Fusion, following the completion of network upgrades that now allow us to carry more minutes of traffic.

Fusion's adjusted EBITDA for the second quarter was $2.2 million compared to $3 million in the second quarter last year. This reflects the investments we've been making in our sales and marketing and service delivery platform to support our growth plans. We expect this level of spending to grow, offset by cost reductions we're undertaking, which we'll discuss later.

When we look at our business, we focus on several key measures, which we believe are good predictors of future financial performance. These include: the number of customers purchasing Fusion services; the average monthly revenue per customer, referred to in the industry as ARPU; and the churn rate, a rate at which customers terminate services provided by Fusion. You will see these metrics in our quarterly announcements and earnings calls.

We ended the second quarter with approximately 10,820 customers. And we continue to have an average revenue per user, or ARPU, well above the industry average, coming in at near $500 for the quarter.

Our average churn for the second quarter was 2%, reflecting the bankruptcy and subsequent laws of two large customers that together represented over $1 million in annual debt for Fusion. Without these two one-time events, we calculate our churn rate at 1.2%. Again, materially lower than the industry average churn rate.

We continue to see opportunities to improve our performance in several areas, which Don will discuss later in the call, and which we believe will result in significant gains in both revenue and cash flow and add to the value of our underlying business.

With the majority of our new monthly recurring revenue contracted for three years or more, with automatic renewal upon expiration, we see the business is having a high degree of consistency and predictability. As a result of our focus here, we ended the quarter with $217,000 in monthly recurring revenue backlog, representing $2.6 million in total contract value.

Additionally, we signed $5.2 million in total new contract value during the quarter. Our focus has begun to shift from investing in building of a highly scalable network infrastructure, service fee, and product and marketing platform for accelerating the growth in revenues and cash flow.

A key element of our strategy to build the foundation for Fusion's organic growth is to further expand our sales force. Over the last nine months, we've doubled our number of sales professionals from 10 to 20 across our three channel, partner, direct and inside sales.

With the Broadvox migration behind us now, these individuals are fully engaged in their sales effort, and we plan to add several more sales professionals in the coming months. We will do so in a measured fashion, balancing the need to spur our organic growth with incremental overhead we will bear, while they ramp up to full productivity.

Additionally, we've made a number of organizational changes in our marketing group over the past quarter to increase the direct support of our sales effort. We've increased our marketing spend to accelerate results. And among our initiatives to increase organic growth, we've recently launched targeted marketing campaigns to promote brand awareness and increase lead generation, using cutting-edge automation and CRM tools.

Now, I'll turn the call over to Don, to provide some more details on our second quarter performance. Don?

Gordon Hutchins

Thank you, Matt. And let me also welcome everyone who has joined us on our call today. To recap a bit, consolidated revenues in the second quarter of 2015 were $25.1 million or an annual run rate of approximately $100 million.

We have integrated the three acquisitions we made in the past two-and-a-half years, NBS in 2012, Broadvox in 2013 and PingTone last year. About two-thirds of our revenues now come from our higher margin Business Services segment, which is focused on cloud-based solutions and services. This is up from 5% from 2012.

Our consolidated gross margin in the second quarter was 44.9%, flat with the year-ago period. Gross margin in the company's main area of focus, the Business Services segment, improved over 200 basis points year-over-year to 63.6%. While on the Carrier Services segment, gross margins improved by 100 basis points to 11.6% for the second quarter.

We had a consolidated operating loss in the second quarter of $1.7 million compared to an operating loss of $30,000 in the second quarter last year. This was mostly due to investments we've made in expanding our sales and support platform as well as other investments to position the company to scale meaningfully.

Fusion's net loss for the second quarter was $700,000, down from a net loss of approximately $2.5 million in the second quarter last year. This improvement was primarily a result of the $2.5 million non-cash gain due to the change in the fair market value of our derivate liability.

During the second quarter, we continue to move forward with our major initiative to leverage the scale we have built and harvest synergies and efficiencies coming out of our acquisitions. We used some of these savings to invest in the areas that enhance our ability to scale.

We are already beginning to execute on the cost savings, which will include, for example, the consolidation of our data center at 75 Broad Street, New York, into existing locations, which alone we anticipate will save the company over $800,000 per year.

Moreover, now that we have begun to achieve scale, we have been able to negotiate more favorable commercial terms with our underlying suppliers. Looking at all cost reduction opportunities, we expect the majority of the annualized savings to be in place before yearend.

Turning now to our operational performance. We continue to make progress in the second quarter and strengthening our internal sales and support platforms to build Fusion's growth engine. Adding to this, our acquisitions have resulted in the expansion of our geographic footprint by adding eight new data centers, upgrading our network core and adding additional leading-edge service platforms.

On the organic growth side, we are focusing on growing our direct and indirect sales channels, and we are working to further penetrate vertical markets, such as the healthcare and hospitality industries. In the second quarter, we also announced the signing of major customers in the housing, building technology and global agriculture industries.

Now, I'll turn the call back to Matt to discuss our strategic goals for the business.

Matthew Rosen

Thanks, Don. As I mentioned at the beginning of the call, we've made significant progress in transforming Fusion to become a leader in cloud services. We've built a very strong management team, a robust flexible and scalable platform and a unique go-to-market strategy.

As we look ahead, we've identified three main strategic objectives for our business. The first objective is to continue to create significant scale, primarily through acquisition. This has been and will continue to be the main driver of our overall growth strategy, and we'll complement this with organic growth. In our business, scale is paramount, as it allows us to reduce our operating cost of our entire business, while lowering our cost of capital, thereby making future growth opportunities even more economically attractive.

We can either build or buy to achieve scale. While others in our industry have focused primarily on building scale organically, spending up to 50% of revenues to accomplish it, we continue to look at the financial balance between organic growth and growth through acquisitions. We believe that at this point the returns on growing our customers, our revenue and our cash flow, primarily through acquisitions, is significantly more attractive to us then doing so primarily on an organic basis.

We benefit not only from the fragmented competitive landscape and cloud services, but also from the inherent leverage in Fusion's operating platform as well as the experience and track record of our management team to identify, acquire and tightly integrate these businesses successfully. Our approach toward acquisitions is built around a highly disciplined process.

The criteria we use when identifying potential acquisitions is: a cloud services company focusing on business customers; a next-generation network infrastructure, avoiding legacy infrastructure; a low churn rate and high ARPU; a high percentage of recurring revenue; and an integration process, which can be undertaken with low risk and an accretive effect within 12 months.

Our second strategic objective is to optimize our capital structure, which is closely related to our acquisition strategy. At the end of June, Fusion had about $45 million in total debt, carrying a blended interest rate of about 11.2%.

This was a reasonable interest rate, when we made our first cloud services acquisition in 2012. But in recognition of our financial progress and current market conditions, we believe we can do better. We are constantly looking at how we can strengthen our balance sheet. And one of the areas of particular focus is our leverage, and we look at that along two parameters.

First, in looking at the credit picture, which is key in implementing our acquisition strategy, we examine both our total leverage and our cost of capital, recognizing that as our EBITDA grows as it has, we will also be decreasing the level of cash flow required to service or debt.

We have therefore made relevant decisions in terms of our capital structure and our capital deployment plans to support our acquisition strategy, and we are working to lower the interest rate on our debt. We're not prepared to go in further detail of our plans today, but we expect to share more about our progress on this topic later this year.

Finally, our third strategic objective is to continue to leverage our growing scale to drive efficiencies and cost saving, some of which Don described earlier. We're increasing our focus here to identify areas, where we can affect cost saving as well as harvest efficiencies accruing from our acquisitions.

We assign direct responsibility among the management team for each of these cost saving items. We track these as a team on a weekly basis. And as a result, we have the confidence in level and timing of these savings, which is critical to managing our business as efficiently as possible.

So to summarize, we're focusing on carrying forward our acquisition strategy, growing the business accretively, building scale, optimizing the balance sheet and improving our credit stats, and lowering our cost to generate greater cash flows, which we can ultimately return to shareholders and invest in future growth.

We continue to believe that our expanding scale, robust nationwide network with global reach and an end-to-end service, suite of innovative, yet proven cloud solutions, and our positioning as the single source for companies moving to the cloud, give Fusion a unique competitive advantage that will enable us to achieve meaningful value in a large and very dynamic marketplace.
