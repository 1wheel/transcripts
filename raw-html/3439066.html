Philip Sassower

Thank you for joining us to review the financial results of Xplore’s first fiscal quarter ended June 30, 2015. We appreciate your time and interest in our company. With me on the call are Mark Holleran, our President and Chief Operating Officer; and Tom Wilkinson, our Interim Chief Financial Officer.

Before our prepared remarks, I want to mention a few housekeeping items. To being with, let me provide some background on Xplore in case there are any new participants on the call. Based in Austin, Texas and with a presence across the globe, Xplore has been a leader and innovator in the rugged tablet space for almost 20 years. Our customers include household names like American Airlines, AT&T, Verizon, Daimler, Merck, FedEx, Procter & Gamble and Hertz, just to name a few.

These customers have chosen Xplore Solutions to extend the benefits available by deploying computer devices in the field, while minimizing the downtime and loss of productivity that otherwise occurs with non-rugged computers. With the recent acquisition of certain of Motion Computing’s assets, Xplore is now the second largest provider of rugged tablets worldwide. I encourage you to visit both our Xplore branded website, as well as www.motioncomputing.com, our website dedicated to Motion brand products, where you can find detailed information on our company.

Our first fiscal quarter was a historic one. It represents yet another record breaking quarter with revenue of approximately $24 million or 191% above last year. In the first quarter that includes revenues from our expanded offering that now includes products acquired in the Motion Asset deal.

It is interesting to note that revenue from our historical product like represented a significant portion of the overall increase, growing a very healthy 31% year-over-year. It also is an auspicious thought to our expanded operations with a better than expected bottom line performance, which bought for certain onetime costs related to the Motion transaction would have generated net income.

We are extremely proud of this strong performance in our first quarter that includes the Motion assets. As expected, the Motion deal significantly accelerated our revenue growth compared to growing Xplore organically. Our integration efforts are ongoing and we continue to work towards generating growth and synergies in the process.

Before turning the call over to Mark, who will provide an update on our operations, I want to formally welcome Tom Wilkinson as our Interim CFO. As disclosed in our 8-K filing last week, our long term CFO, Mike Rapisand departed Xplore for personal reasons on August 7. Due to concerns for privacy, we are not in a position of make any further comments on this departure, but thank him for his years of service and wish him well in future endeavors.

Mike has been replaced by Tom who has hit the ground running. He completed and signed our Form 10-Q filing. He presented this quarter’s results to our Board of Directors for approval and here he is with us today to review our fantastic performance during the quarter. We are honored to have someone of Tom’s caliber and with his level of experience join our executive management team.

Tom has worked both as a CFO and as an Auditor, including as a partner in charge of Xplore’s audit process for five years while at PMB Helin Donovan. We have had more than a decade long relationship with Tom and he is very familiar with the Xplore financial management process, our team and our overall business. Tom is uniquely positioned to lead Xplore’s finance and accounting function and is one of the leading candidates in our search for a permanent CFO.

Now to discuss our operations and to provide an update on our integration efforts, I turn the call over to Mark Holleran, our very confident President and Chief Operating Officer. Mark.

Mark Holleran

Thank you, Phil and thank you all, to the participants on the call for your interest and support of our company. Let me begin by stating that I am thrilled about the fantastic results achieved during the quarter. While I have always been confident in our ability to make our expanded operations profitable in a timely manner, I must admit that delivering operating income in our first quarter, both acquisitions was beyond my expectations.

The quarter results were aided by a number of factors, including some pent-up demand for our iX104 and C5S5 product lines in the aftermath of our previously disclosed screen issue. The later was resolved during the quarter and both product lines are back into production. In addition to higher revenue, a favorable product mix and the timing of certain cost to goods solid adjustments led to relatively strong gross margins for both our historical and acquired product lines.

Our integration efforts continue at a rapid pace. Under the stewardship and coordination of our management team, all functional areas have made significant strides towards complete integration. Our North American sales structure has been rationalized to effectively and efficiently address opportunities of all sizes, both through our distribution network and for our direct sales.

Our sales directors have been assigned sales engineering and inside sales resources to drive pipeline and revenue growth and together with our partners have been trained on all our products. A similar effort is ongoing with our international sales structure. In early September we will hold an international partner conference in Budapest, where over 50 representatives from our international channel partners will be presented our expanded product line and strategies. A similar event is in the planning stages for our North American partners.

Our marketing efforts has been fully consolidated and is active in generating new leads, in working with engineering and operations to plan and develop further products and to complete the important work of consolidating our online presence with a particular emphasis on search engine optimization.

Our engineering and operations team together with our testing labs have been successfully consolidated in our Wells Branch location, where Xplore has been located for over a decade. Existing and newly hired engineers are now working side by side to accelerate product develop and to address clients request on a timely basis. Executive management together with our sales, marketing, finance and accounting teams have been consolidated in Motion’s historical offices as we negotiate a new lease in a significantly reduced footprint.

The morale of the company is among the highest I have witnessed in my tenure. Our historical employees are thrilled with Xplore’s current position as the second largest rugged Table, PC company. With size comes an improved ability to attract and retain talent and to become a more impactful factor in our space.

Our acquired employees are also excited to part of a more viable and growing enterprise. We have experienced minimal turnover and in certain areas like engineering we have been able to retain everyone. We have initiated a compensation review to ensure that our expanded team is properly compensated, including an attractive equity incentive plan.

It is a great time to be involved with Xplore. In short, while we still have meaningful integration steps ahead, there has been good progress achieved in the short period and remain confident in our ability to complete this integration in a satisfactory and timely manner.

I now turn the call over to Tom, our Interim Chief Financial Officer who will present the financial results for the first quarter. Tom.

Tom Wilkinson

Thank you, Mark and thank you to the participants on this call for your interest in Xplore. I’m very happy to be working with Xplore again as I know the company very well and have the utmost confidence in both the financial accounting team and the quality and accuracy of the work performed. I will now review some of the more significant aspects of the financial results for the first fiscal quarter ended June 30, 2015.

Revenue for the first quarter was approximately $24 million, an increase of approximately $16 million or 191% compared to the prior year period. As mentioned by Phil, this growth resulted both from an increase in year-over-year revenue for Xplore’s historical products and from the inclusion of recently acquired Motion products. Approximately 78% of our revenue was generated in the United States.

Our gross profit percentages for the first quarter was approximately 34% compared to approximately 37% in the prior year period. These decreases in gross profit percentage principally was due to changes in the product mix sold.

Operating expenses increased by approximately 116% for the first quarter over the prior year period. This increase was attributable primarily to the inclusion of Motion operations. The first quarter had operating income of $369,000 compared to an operating loss of $532,000 in the prior year period. This improvement resulted from our increase in revenue and gross profit, offset by the increase in operating expenses.

The first quarter had a net loss of $244,000 compared to a net loss of $551,000 in the prior year period. This improvement in our bottom line resulted largely from the aforementioned operating income that was partially offset by $670,000 in non-recurring costs related to the acquisition of the Motion assets.

Xplore’s financial condition remains very strong with approximately $14 million in positive working capital, including approximately $11 million in cash. At the end of the quarter we have drawn approximately $4 million out of $15 million on our working capital facility and had approximately $18 million in accounts receivable generated from a number of sales towards the end of the quarter.

That concludes my review of the financial results. Back to you Phil.
