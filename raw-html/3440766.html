Fifth Street Asset Management's (FSAM) CEO Leonard Tannenbaum on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 14, 2015 12:12 PM ET  |  About: Fifth Street Asset Management (FSAM) by: SA Transcripts

Fifth Street Asset Management Inc. (NASDAQ:FSAM)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015 10:00 AM ET

Executives

Robyn Friedman - VP, IR

Leonard M. Tannenbaum - Founder, Chairman and CEO

Alexander C. Frank - COO and CFO

Analysts

Kenneth Worthington - JPMorgan

I would now like to introduce your host for today's conference Ms. Robyn Friedman. Ma'am you may begin.

Thank you, Kelly. Good morning, and welcome to Fifth Street Asset Management Inc.'s second quarter 2015 earnings conference call. The format for today's call is as follows. Len will provide introductory remarks, an overview of our results and strategic commentary and Alex will summarize the financials. Then we'll open the line for Q&A. I will now turn the call over to our Chief Executive Officer, Len Tannenbaum.

Leonard M. Tannenbaum

Thank you, Robyn. Before turning to our June quarterly results, I would like to briefly discuss the overall market environment. The landscape surrounding middle market leveraged lending continues to change, as evidenced by the sale of GE Capital’s Sponsor Finance business to the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board. Due to regulatory headwinds we see a continued disintermediation of leveraged lending from the traditional banking sector characterized by declining bank participation and increased participation from non-bank lenders such as Fifth Street.

We believe that the borrowers and sponsors favor a collaborative partnership approach which Fifth Street provides for more flexible financing solutions, large hold sized and diversified product offerings. As a credit focused asset manager we continually put an emphasis on capital preservation and maintain strict underwriting standards when deploying capital into new loans.

Our size, scale and deep sponsor relationships allow us to source over 1,000 middle market loan opportunities per year. But our single most important job is making prudent investments in credits with appropriate risk adjusted returns which is evidenced by selectivity ratio of less than 4%. We continue to position our portfolios defensively opting to focus on senior secured instruments since we believe that the current risk reward trade-off is strongest in first lien and one stop transactions, while also limiting our exposure to cyclical industries including energy, consumer driven businesses and companies with direct retail exposure.

My view on energy is that this is not the time to bottom brush [ph] but we believe it is too early to be adding to our energy exposure across the platform. As of June 30th, approximately 70% of our fee-earning AUM was in first lien debt and only 1.4% of fee-earning AUM was invested in energy. For the quarter ended June 30, 2015 FSAM generated $0.19 of pro forma adjusted net income per share which excludes the results of a majority owned company, MMKT which generated a loss of about one penny per share.

We believe MMKT may add significant value to FSAM in the future as I will describe later on during this call. This quarter, excluding MMKT, our earnings were stable quarter-over-quarter driven by consistent performance at our funds and prudent expense management despite investments in our platform. As we’ve stated on previous conference calls we are in an operating environment where our two business development companies, which contribute a majority of our earnings, have been trading at a discount to book value for an extended period of time.

During the quarter at FSC’s fee-earning AUM decreased due to sale of Healthcare Finance Group a portfolio company of FSC as well as additional repayments and exits that were in excess of origination levels. At FSFR fee-earning AUM rose as management deployed capital into loans with strong risk adjustment returns increasing leverage on its balance sheet and funding a $148 million out of the $300 million of capacity at FSFR Glick JV.

Our CLO business continues to be one that we are actively focused on growing. We remain excited about the product line, as it is a natural expansion of our core middle market lending business. We are currently evaluating options regarding the securitization of Senior Loan Fund II and are in the process of raising our third senior loan fund. Our hedge fund, Fifth Street Opportunities Fund continues to generate solid performance and interest from multiple potential investors, although the sales cycle in converting leads to AUM has been longer than expected.

We are in the process of finalizing a key hire to strengthen the hedge fund team and look forward to providing an update in the near future. Beyond our existing vehicles, we continue to explore other growth initiatives and remain optimistic about the opportunities of the platform that’s developing. We are evaluating organic growth opportunities, including expanding to products that are adjacent to our core strategies, such as middle market SMEs, while continuing to assess acquisition opportunities, including adding teams to grow new product lines. We also anticipate making announcements on this -- on these, both these funds later this year.

One initiative that we've been focused on for last nine months is MMKT, which is a financial technology company that should begin generating revenue during the fall. We are in the process of raising capital from outside institutional investors in the form of convertible note and subsequent to June 30th have closed an initial funding from our lead investor. We are very excited about the partners we are attracting to help MMKT through its next phase of growth and plan to make additional announcements within the next 30 days.

Last quarter we received approval from our Board of Directors for a one year $20 million share buyback. We are pleased to announce that during the quarter utilizing our share repurchase program we purchased approximately $700,000 or 73,000 shares in the open market. Going forward, we plan on -- to continue to repurchase shares under this authorization subject to current market conditions.

This week, our Board of Directors declared a quarterly dividend of $0.17 per share which is consistent with our dividend from last quarter and within our targeted payout range of 80% to 90% on pro forma adjusted net income. The dividend will be payable on October 15, 2015 to shareholders of record on September 30, 2015. We believe that 2015 continues to develop as a transition year for Fifth Street, but from an internal standpoint, we are pleased to see the momentum being generated across multiple fronts.

Over the last 12 months, we've hired a handful of individuals that we believe will be instrumental in expanding the Fifth Street platform and driving future growth. As we've previously stated, it will take time for these individuals to generate revenues and our results bear only the cost of hiring these talented individuals.

Looking ahead, we are very excited about the opportunities that the platform is developing internally and the prospects that are presenting itself in the broader market. I would now like to turn the call over to Alex Frank, our Chief Financial Officer to discuss the financials in more detail.

Alexander C. Frank

Thank you Len. Total revenues increased by 3.3% to $24.2 million for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 as compared to $23.4 million for the quarter ended June 30, 2014. Management fees, which include base management fees and Part I fees were $23.1 million for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 or 95.4% of total revenues. After adjusting for non-recurring and reimbursed items, expenses remained relatively flat as compared to the quarter ended March 31, 2015 but expenses increased for the year-over-year period due to investments in talent infrastructure and growth initiatives.

Fee earning AUM at June 30, 2015 was $4.3 billion which was relatively flat year-over-year, as the decrease in AUM from FSC sale of HFG was offset by growth in AUM from the CLO business and FSFR. Pro forma adjusted net income for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 was $9.3 million or $0.19 per share as compared to $0.19 per share of pro forma adjusted net income per share from March 31, 2015.

Including the results of MMKT, pro forma adjusted net income for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 was $9.1 million or $0.18 per share. We have presented our pro forma adjusted net income excluding the consolidated results of MMKT. Going forward, we plan to continue breaking out the results of MMKT from our core asset management business. Additionally, overtime as MMKT continues to grow, we expect that our level of ownership and control over the activities of MMKT will decrease.

I will now turn it back over to Robyn.
