Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 18, 2015 08:30 AM ET

Nick Tavlarios - President

Good morning, and welcome to the Aegean Marine Petroleum Network Incorporated Second Quarter 2015 Conference Call and Presentation. Nick Tavlarios

Good morning and welcome to Aegean Marine's second quarter 2015 conference call. I am Nick Tavlarios, President of Aegean; and on the call with me today is Peter Georgiopoulos, Chairman of Aegean; and Spyros Gianniotis, CFO of Aegean.

Before we begin our presentation, I would like to note that this conference call will contain forward-looking statements within the meaning of the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995. Words such as expects, anticipates, intends, estimates, or similar expressions are intended to identify these forward-looking statements. These statements are based on Aegean Marine Petroleum Network, Inc.'s current plans and expectations, and involve risks and uncertainties that could cause future activities and the results of operations to be materially different from those set forth in the forward-looking statements.

Important factors that could cause actual results to differ include our future operating or financial results, our ability to manage growth, adverse conditions in the marine field supply industries, and increased levels of competition. For further information, please refer to Aegean Marine Petroleum Network, Inc.'s reports and filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

I would like to begin with an overview of our performance during the second quarter; I will then review developments within our specific business as well as the broader industry that have been drivers in our operational financial results.

Once I’ve walked through high level overview of our performance, I will provide details of our financial results for the second quarter. I will then conclude the call by discussing macro trends within the shipping industry, and the positive trends that will help us to continue to grow our company and our bottom line through the second half of 2015.

During the second quarter, our business faced some unforeseen expenses in two temporary headwinds that impact our financial results. Sustain low oil prices and a disruption in one of our larger markets. Sustain lower pricing environment, load barrier to entry into the market allowing competitors to aggressively offer an incremental sales that will always be beyond their credit capacity. These increased competitiveness have resulted in a tighter margin environment that has made an impact across the industry.

Despite headwinds, Aegean extended its track record of profitability in the quarter, which marks our 18th consecutive quarter of profitability. We have now achieved this goal in 33 of 34 quarters, since becoming a public company. This sustained success is a result of our differentiated strategy that separates ourselves from our peers.

Financial flexibility is a main stay of our success and our strong balance sheet differentiates Aegean from its competition, which I will discuss later on in the call. Looking ahead, we continue to take steps to ensure we are well positioned regardless of the economic environment. Fort the second quarter of 2015, we delivered solid results despite the difficult economic environment achieving total sales volume of 3,150,950 million metric tons; a gross profit of $78.5 million, down approximately 7% over the same period in 2014; adjusted operating income of $14.8 million, 23.3% QoverQ down, but adjusted net income of $7.1 million or $0.15 basic and diluted earnings per share for the second quarter. And despite these challenges we experienced in the quarter, we continue to successfully execute in our strategy focused on successfully leveraging our global network and the supporting growth drivers we establish.

Aegean remains uniquely positioned with a global network covering 31 markets across four regions North America and the Caribbean, Middle East and Mediterranean, Northwest Europe and the Far East. This geographic diversification has allowed us to remain competitive in these large markets. Shipping remained strong throughout each of these regions, particularly in North America and the Caribbean, where we experienced strong growth that bolster our results in the quarter.

In Fujairah, our storage facility continues to perform well. The storage facility is operating at strong efficiency levels and is now at about 86% capacity. This success was driven by our strong marketing strategy remain on track to achieve our stated goal of 100% lease rate by the end of 2015. Fujairah continued to contribute to our EBITDA for the quarter that will meaningfully contribute in the going forward quarters.

In addition, we received an outstanding review from the Maritime and Port Authority Fujairah on the terminals performance, another sign of the facility strength. On the physical supply side, two additional load points will be opened by the end of quarter and current year. We expect our sales volume Fujairah to materially increase. Demand in this market is quite strong.

I also want to provide an update on the market in Greece and its impact on Aegean. From a capital perspective, we are not affected by the current environment in Greece. Our business conducted in US dollars and with the diverse group of international banks. From an operational perspective the Greek market has seen some minor impacts, but it continues to be a good market for the company overall.

As stated earlier, sustained lower oil price have provided the company, or have brought the opportunity for some smaller less flow capital suppliers compete per share in certain market. This was particularly noticeable in Northwest Europe. To increase efficiency in this region, we will be winding down of Portland terminal and this and all operations will now be covered by Northwest Europe office. We expect this adjustment to realize a few million dollars in annual cost savings as a result.

While there were no sales of vessels during the quarter, we will continue to pursue the most efficient use of our capital and improvement of utilization of our remaining fleet, by reduced maintenance CapEx. We're focused on maintaining our competitive advantage in order to capitalize on future opportunities as the market environment improves and continue to look for more efficient and profitable ways to generate growth and expand our geographical footprint.

To that point, we are currently pursuing several avenues to drive sustainable profitability and growth as we continue to execute our strategy. This includes entrance into new markets through low cost start-up opportunities as evidenced by our recent expansions. Each of these initiatives is in line with our strategy focused on being in position of strength as the markets continue to normalize allowing us to respond to increased market demand.

Moving to our capital allocation strategy, we are proud of our ability to generate significant levels of free cash. In the past, we have reinvested in the capital back into our business to enhance operations and created strong platforms for long-term sustainable growth. As our business continues to grow free cash, the board and management will and as we always have continued to assess our capital allocation priorities.

Our focus remains on deploying our cash to drive the most significant stockholder returns. As such we will consider all options to achieve this objective, including ongoing reinvestment, share repurchases, and dividend increases, as well as other opportunities to deliver shareholder value. We also work to further strengthen our balance sheet and ensure that we continue to support our growth plan. We are confident that with this financial strength and by continuing to execute our proven strategy, we will generate value for our shareholders and position our company for continued growth.

I will now provide some additional detail on the financial results for the quarter. As you know since market conditions fluctuate at ports around the world, we provide sequential comparisons in order to discuss our quarterly financial results comparing results for the second quarter 2015 to the first quarter 2015.

For the second quarter, our sales volumes increased by 8.1% to approximately 3.2 million metric tons on a sequential basis, 2.95 million metric tons of which was physical supply with the balance being made up of marketed back to back sales. During the second quarter, the gross spreads per metric ton of marine fuel sold was $22.5, a decrease of 6.6% over Q1 levels. Total gross profit for the second quarter decreased by 2.7% sequentially to $78.5 million. Net income for the second quarter was $7.1 million or $0.15 per basic and diluted share. EBITDA for the second quarter was $24.1 million.

Utilization of our physical supply fleet, which as a reminder does not include Aegean Northwest Europe or US East Coast or West Coast and Gulf operations and our Hamburg business, is measured as volumes related per vessel per day excluded both schedule and non-scheduled, non-operating or off hire days. It was 545 metric tons per day, compared to 530 metric tons per vessel per day in the first quarter 2015.

We generate volumes revenues of $6.1 million in the second quarter. This figure was approximately $2 million lower than our original forecast due to dry docking requirements for chartered vessels. Looking ahead, we expect these voyage revenues to increase. We expect storage levels to increase as we lease greater storage capacity to third parties at our Fujairah facility.

On the expense side, our fuel consumption cost increased approximately $1.7 million to the approximate 11% rise in fuel costs. On slide 10, we present our historical gross profit in the EBITDA margin trends. Our EBITDA margin, which is calculated as EBITDA divided by gross profit, was 30.6% in Q2, a decrease of 11% over the previous quarter.

During the quarter, Aegean reported net cash used in operations of $59.3 million as trade receivables expanded from $363.3 million to $420 million; and inventory grew from $176 million to $206 million through a 8.1% quarter-on-quarter increase in sales volume, and almost 11% increase in fuel prices. As of June 30, 2015, our cash position was $42.4 million and our working capital position was $267.7 million.

Our total debt of $740.6 million can be analyzed as follows. Our fixed asset base of $630.7 million is financed by $211.8 million in debt, $38.8 million of which is classified as current, which implies leverage of 33.6%. Our $528.8 million in working capital debt, $233.4 million long-term, and $295.4 million in short-term both of which combine finance current assets of $750.7 million, resulting in leverage of approximately 70%.

As you know, our current assets are very liquid and our inventories are turned over every 16 and our receivables every 31 days. Our debt can be further analyzed as follows. Our fixed that asset debt of $211.8 million secured by 36 vessels, all built after in 2007. This means that we have $5.9 million of debt per vessel with an average vessel age of seven years. We own an additional 12 vessels that are debt free. All financing is committed in attractive rates. We have set a $73.5 million term loan financing our storage in Fujairah with a book value of over $223 million. All term debt is also committed.

We continue to enhance our considerable financial flexibility, which remains a key differentiator to Aegean. We are also pleased to announce a successful refinancing of our $250 million U.S. facility with the ability to expand it to $300 million. We have a strong balance sheet that can support continued growth over the long term and we appreciate the continued support of these banks and their confidence and Aegean Marine’s business model.

As of June 30, 2015, our fixed asset debt to adjusted EBITDA multiple was approximately 2.1 times, significantly lower than many peers who have consistently over levered in the difficult market. Our financial liquidity which includes more than $1.8 billion of working capital credit facilities including supplier credit. We are in compliance with all loan covenants and have a strong fixed debt to adjusted EBITDA ratio.

I’ll now turn the call over to Peter Georgiopoulos for closing comments.

Peter Georgiopoulos

Our second quarter results demonstrate our ability to deliver returns in a variety of market conditions as a result of our strong global platform. While we faced headwinds during the quarter, we remain well positioned due to our unique and dynamic business model. For the month of July, our sales volume grew approximately 1,050,000 metric tons. Before opening the call to Q&A, I want to outline some of the emerging macro trends in our industry and detail why we believe we are positioned to continue to successfully navigate industry challenges and capitalize on positive shifts in the market landscape.

Market dynamics in the shipping environment continue to improve steadily. The commodity price environment has had an impact on the entire industry as sustainable oil prices have led to increased competition. As we continue to the second half of 2015, we are confident that overall fundamentals in the maritime industry will gradually improve as well. As the regulations in our industry continue to change specifically in fuel content requirements, we remain focused on leveraging our diversified approach to maintain a competitive advantage. With strong cash flow, we are well capitalized with limited debt and vessels that can support multiple fuel grades. As a result, we are not facing any issues under these regulations and are confident that we will be able to gain profitable market share as our competitors exit the market.

Despite challenges, we are entering the second half of 2015 with several growth drivers in place that we expect will allow us to further improve our financial performance. Aegean’s global brand diversified across four regions and 31 markets and world class marine logistics platform position us well for the markets continued improvement. Our strong balance sheet will continue to support our growth plan and our core operations around the world. As a result, we feel well positioned to capitalize on market opportunities.

Looking ahead, we continue to focus on evaluating more efficient and profitable ways to execute our proven strategy. As fundamentals continue to improve, we are poised to benefit from our expanded global market operations, diversifying our business and advancing our position in the global fuel supply market. We remain committed to delivering value to our shareholders enhancing our network by opportunistically expanding our global footprint and ensuring customers are even better equipped to run their business around the world.

With that, I would like to turn the call over to questions. Operator?
