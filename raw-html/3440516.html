Nuo Therapeutics' (NUOT) CEO Dean Tozer on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 14, 2015 11:01 AM ET  |  About: Nuo Therapeutics, Inc. (NUOT) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Lee Roth – Investor Relations

Dean Tozer – Chief Executive Officer

David Jorden – Chief Financial Officer (Acting)

Analysts

David Bautz – Zacks Investment Research

Nuo Therapeutics Inc. (OTCQX:NUOT) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 14, 2015 8:00 AM ET

Operator

Greetings. And welcome to the Nuo Therapeutics Second Quarter 2015 Financial Earnings Results Conference Call. At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. A brief question-and-session will follow the formal presentation. [Operator Instructions] As a reminder, this conference is being recorded.

It is now my pleasure to introduce your host Lee Roth of The Ruth Group. Thank you, sir. You may begin.

Lee Roth

Thanks, Christine and good morning, everyone. This is Lee Roth with The Ruth Group, Investor Relations Advisors to Nuo Therapeutics. Joining me today the second quarter 2015 financial results conference call from Nuo Management are incoming Chief Executive Officer, Dean Tozer; and Acting Chief Financial Officer, David Jorden.

By now you should have received the press release issued yesterday afternoon announcing the company’s financial results for the second quarter of 2015 and providing an important corporate update. If you haven’t received the news release or if you’d like to be added to the company’s distribution list, please call us at Ruth Group in New York at 646-536-7000 and ask for myself or Joseph Green.

Before we begin today's call, I’d like to remind you that comments made during this conference call will contain forward-looking statements that involve risks and uncertainties regarding the operations and future results of Nuo Therapeutics. And that our actual results could differ materially from those projected.

We ask that you review the company’s filings with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, including but without limitation to the company’s Forms 10-K and 10-Q, which identify specific risk factors that may cause actual results or events to differ materially from those described in such forward-looking statements.

Furthermore, the content of this conference call contains time sensitive information accurate only as of the date of this live broadcast, Friday, August 14, 2015. Nuo Therapeutics undertakes no obligation to revise or otherwise update any such statements to reflect events or circumstances after the date of this conference call.

With that, it’s now my pleasure to turn the call over to incoming CEO, Dean Tozer. Dean, go ahead please.

Dean Tozer

Great. Thank you, Lee, and thank you all for joining us this morning. Well a lot happened since our last – our first quarter call three months ago. First of all we announced yesterday, I’ll be assuming the role of CEO, effect from tomorrow, succeeding Marty after eight years of leading the company. Before reviewing the remainder of the strategic realignment plan we announced yesterday, I’d like to take this opportunity to thank you the board for their confidence and for giving me the opportunity to lead Nuo, as Marty’s successor.

I also want to thank Marty for all his important contributions as CEO. I look forward to continuing to work with him in the coming months on an orderly transition on the CEO responsibility. As we announced yesterday, we are continuing the execution of our strategic realignment which will result in a leaner organization that is more intently focused on maximizing the opportunity to exist within the VA segment of the chronic wound care market.

In the ten months, since we launched our seventh [ph] VA, our three person team has accomplished a great deal. On our last call in May, we had two VA facilities purchasing Aurix and six others in late stage product evaluation. I’m pleased to say that as of today we have seven VA hospitals and one airforce base treating patients with Aurix. We also have ninth facility schedule to begin patient trials in Monday. As you can appreciate from these numbers the ratio we experience of converting Aurix 90 [ph] sites into sites that want to evaluate and ultimately use Aurix is incredibly high.

As I’ve consistently said there is a tremendous need within the VA system. Particularly, in spinal cord injury unit for a product that effectively treats pressure ulcers. This feedback following each patient trial has been extremely positive and shows that healthcare providers in the VA are becoming more and more familiar with the healing power of the Aurix in the best possible way by seeing the results firsthand.

When we first decided to pursue the VA, we knew that the length and limited predictability of the sales cycle could prove quite challenging. At the time, we estimated a minimum ramp cycle of roughly six months for each facility that estimated time table is proving to be accurate and I’m please to say that we are beginning to see the rewards of work that was initiated at the beginning of this year. More important than the speed with which we are advanced in our VA efforts is the fact that the success we are achieving validates our strategy of focusing on VA facilities, with dedicated spinal cord injury units. We are leveraging Aurix’s unique ability to heal complex wounds that often result from mobility impairment to gain entry in to the VA. This significant advantage of our other chronic wound treatment is opening eye to other departments as well as clinicians see the results that Aurix can achieve for pressure ulcers in the SCI unit and asking to trial the product in other units for chronic wound such as diabetic foot and venous leg ulcers.

Our team of three VA-focused account [ph] executives has done an excellent job so far, and we believe we have reached point where a larger team can accelerate our penetration at new facilities and increase our footprint in the centers where we are already selling. With this in mind four of our sales representatives who are focused on the hospital outpatient wound care centers are being reassigned to the VA effective immediately, bring our dedicated VA sales team to eight. I’m confident that this new approach will enable us to continue growing Aurix sales in the VA. I also believe our ability to effectively market products within the VA wound care system will make us an attractive partner for companies that maybe searching for means of marketing their products in this channel.

In order to support this reassignment of representatives the Gold Study data collection effort, which is previously lead by our hospital outpatient and commercial team will now become a clinical affairs managed effort. In the six months since we initiated the Gold Study we’ve learned the nuances of data collection maybe navigated more effectively by clinical affairs professional. Today, nine hospital outpatient wound centers are enrolling patients in the Gold Study. These initial types [ph] agreed to participate in the study using pen and paper data collection, while we worked with Net Health to complete integration of new functionality that digitizes the data collection process for this study within their WoundExpert system.

This new functionally will make it much simpler and more streamlined for sites using WoundExpert to identify and track patients in the Gold Study. That integration was recently completed and the system will be launched Monday, August, 17. This past week a number of sites were trained on the new Gold Study functionality in their WoundExpert system and the early reviews are very positive. We believe this will remove many of the barriers, previously responsible for the slower than anticipated enrollment in this study.

We remain committed to bringing new sites on board and enrolling patients in the Gold Study as we continue to believe in the opportunities in the outpatient market. With that said, we have establish clearly defined objective in timelines over the next several months for determining whether Aurix can be economically viable under the current CED program. This includes establishing an economically appropriate reimbursement rate in 2016 and beyond. Simply put, the reimbursement rate for the product has to be on rational basis in order for clinicians operating in the wound care space he’s selected [ph] on a regular basis. We are engaging directly with CMS on this topic as we speak.

In conjunction with these strategic changes we have implemented a workforce reduction that has reduced our full-time employee comp by approximately 30%. This was an incredibly difficult decision but one that was necessary. And it will give us increased flexibility over our cost structure and lowering our long-term cash burn, while maintaining resources we need to continue expanding our VA business. My goal in the near-term is to work closely with David to reduce cash requirements while at the time demonstrate our ability to effectively market Aurix in the VA System.

Finally, we have been in contact with Deerfield and expect promptly engage in substantive discussions with them, aimed at restructuring their investment in Aurix, which totals approximately $35 million. Our goal would be a consensual arrangement that satisfies the needs of both parties.

In summary, I'm pleased with the progress of our VA efforts and confident that the expansion of our VA sales team will allow us to grow in this market. We remain committed to the Gold Study and believe that this new clinical affair [ph] centric approach is the right one while still holding ourselves to some firm time lines which I mentioned previously.

With that I will like to hand the floor over to David for the financial review.

David Jorden

Thanks Dean. Let me first offer my own thanks to Marty, for his commitment to new role over the past eight years. My comments this morning will be brief and I’d direct you to our 10-Q which we filed yesterday afternoon.

Let me also say that our GAAP financial results are obviously important with the SEC registrant and public reporting company. But there are times when the GAAP financials take a reader only so far given some of the arcane accounting rules that have been implemented over the years in terms of derivatives, et cetera. While this is one of those times the reader of our financials and footnote disclosure – does – have a full and complete understanding of our current financial situation.

Total revenues for the second quarter were $2.9 million, compared to $2.3 million in the second quarter last year. The increase was attributable to increased sales and royalties associated with the Angel product line.

Gross margin in the first quarter of 2015 was 18%, flat compared to 18% in the second quarter of 2014.

Total operating expenses for the second quarter were $5.0 million, compared to $10.3 million in the second quarter of 2014. Our second quarter operating expenses last year included a one-time non-cash charge of approximately $4.7 million related to the partial impairment of all these related trademarks and in-process R&D. Further year-over-year decline in operating expenses was related to reductions in both R&D and G&A expenses partially offset by increased sales and marketing expense associated with the development of our commercial organization.

In the second quarter of 2015, we reported a net loss of $0.9 million or $0.01 per share, compared with the net loss of $11.3 million or $0.09 per share in the second quarter of last year. Second quarter 2015 net loss included an unrealized non-cash gain related to the change in fair value of derivative liabilities totaling $4.5 million of the second quarter of last year included in unrealized non-cash loss of $0.9 million related to the change in the same derivative liabilities.

We finished the quarter with cash and cash equivalents totaling approximately $6.9 million compared to approximately $15.9 million as of December 31, 2014. And our press release last time indicated our credit facility agreement Deerfield requires that we maintain cash on deposits of at least $5 million and that we pay Deerfield the accrued interest amount of approximately $2.6 million on October 1, 2015. We currently expect that our cash on deposit balance will fall below the $5 million threshold for September 1 and that will be required to pay the October 1 interest payment in cash, as our ability to make the payment in shares is contingent on a number of conditions that we do not expect we will be able to satisfy. As Dean said earlier, we’ve been in contact with Deerfield and intend to engage in discussions with them to modify the facility with them [ph] in order to hopefully avoid a situation that would result into fault. There is a very limited amount of additional information we would wish to share today, with regards to the pending Deerfield engagement.

I will say, however, that there are three assets at Nuo with demonstrable value [indiscernible]. The Angel asset under license to Aurix and the long-term royalty streams associated with that agreement, obviously Aurix which we have full ownership in control of. And finally, the Aldagen asset, which is consuming only nominal resources of Nuo today but with the NIH-funded Phase II trial in an [indiscernible] remains ongoing and is approximately two-thirds enrolled at the beginning of July with the expectation of full enrollment, will be reached in the first quarter of next year.

With that said, I turn it back over to Dean for your questions.

Dean Tozer

Thanks, David. And as David said, this concludes our prepared remarks. And now we would like to open for some questions. Christine?

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

Thank you. We will now be conducting a question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions] Thank you. Our first question comes from the line of David Bautz with Zacks Investment Research. Please proceed with your question.

David Bautz

Hi, good morning guys.

Dean Tozer

Good morning David.

David Bautz

Thanks for taking my question. First one is, about the reimbursement rate Aurix is already there, but why is that still being discussed at this point.

Dean Tozer

Sure. So, David the way that CMS approaches reimbursement, is it is reviewed every year and so what happened in our situation and happens for everybody, in midyear, July this year, they published what is called a proposed set of reimbursement rules for all products. And there is period of discussion and in the company is typically engaged with CMS to discuss what has been published. And then there is a final set of rules that comes out in November and those rules are applicable for the following year.

So in our situation there were a set of rules that were, the proposed rules that came out in July. They will then publish a set of final rules in November typically is right around Thanks Giving and those rules will become effective January 1 of 2016. So currently we are reimbursed at a national average of $430 per application of Aurix and that is what we are in discussions with CMS regarding whether we can convince them that that should be raised to be more appropriate for Aurix and the effectiveness of the product.

David Bautz

Okay. That makes sense okay. Thanks. Now when we’re looking at the VA, can you give a sense of what the breakdown or coverage of those patients, or is cash and [indiscernible] of the payors?

David Jorden

Yes, so David in the VA there is a system called FSS, which is a Federal Supply System scheduled. In essence it’s like a formulary for the VA. When you are on that system in the VA you have a national – in essence a national coverage across the VA. So we are on that system Aurix is reimbursed in the VA system at $340 price point at average selling price. So we don’t run into in the VA system an issue with what’s called secondary or private insurance. What we are required to do is then on a VA by VA basis, so facility by facility, we then go in and work with their facilities on product adoption.

So it’s not related to pricing or related to coverage. What we work with those facilities on is typically a VA facility wants to trial the product for a period of time on some patients and then once they accept that the product is effective and then want to use it, then we enter into a process of just product review so it’s a very different situation so to speak and that its more about us working with that facility in the clinical sense to gain adoption of the product as a treatment option, not so much from a reimbursement perspective.

David Bautz

Okay. And my last question is with regard to the Gold Study if there is any guidance for interim analysis taking place for that?

Dean Tozer

We’re not providing guidance at this time on this study. Our hope is on a go forward basis we will be able to, but as of today what I want to go back to and remind people is we started this about six months ago Monday our Net Health system will come online, which is the automated data collection for this. And then as David and I have both said we have some very defined guidelines and parameters, process and organization for the balance of this year. To make an assessment on as we’re making the progress that we expect. So my anticipation is by the next call we’ll have a good sense of that and be able to get a little more update.

David Bautz

Okay, great. Well, thanks for taking my questions.

Dean Tozer

Sure, thanks, Dave.

Operator

Thank you. Ladies and gentlemen, we have reached the end of the question-and-answer session. I would now like to turn the floor back over to management for closing comments.

Dean Tozer

Sure, thanks, Christine. So as I’ve said in my remarks, there has been a lot of change here at Nuo over the last number of weeks. I want to remind people of what David alluded to in his comments if you have additional questions please reference the Q [ph] which was filed last night.

And I appreciate everybody support. I’m looking forward to the next period of time and leading Nuo and coming back to you in a number of months and give you an update on our progress.

And with that, I’ll say – good morning I guess. Thanks very much. Thanks, Christine.

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, this does conclude today’s teleconference. You may disconnect your lines at this time. Thank you for your participation and have a wonderful day.
