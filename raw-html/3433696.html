I think it's important to mention that the summer in Canada has been extremely warm. So consequently we may as a result have seen some cost hits going forward. We don’t know, but it would not surprise us.

We have also seen strong growth in Southern Europe and Germany. France is stable but still challenging on smoked products.

So once again a slight improvement year-on-year, but still not satisfactory for Consumer Products profit wise.

We have extended our bank facility from €555 million to €805 at the exactly same terms, which we find attractive covenant, only one covenant and that's the equity ratio. We do not have any earnings requirement.

No attention as related to convertible bond beyond adjustments of the conversion price related to the dividend and no changes related to the high elbow.

So then over to supply and demand Page 23 and supply. Global supply growth in the second quarter was in line with previous guidance. Low growth from Norway and Europe and substantial supply growth from Canada due to recovery year and declining volumes for Chile as expected.

Then over to prices Page 24, prices were down in Europe in the second quarter, but I think it’s very important to bear in mind that Easter this year was in the first quarter and in the second quarter last year. So as we must adjust for that.

In addition we have the Russian ban. We also saw a weakening -- sorry a strengthening on the NOK during the quarter. So adjusted for those effects I think the prices in the second quarter in Europe were quite decent and as already mentioned twice they have the third quarter has started very well in Europe at very attractive prices.

That being said, we normally see seasonality in Europe in the second half and we think we’ll see that this year as well. In Americas poor price development, so not only in the second quarter but also in the first quarter; we also saw that in 2014. So slow demand in the U.S.

Then over to Page 25, and the demand volume distribution for markets stable and good demand in EU adjusted for Easter says, FX and Russia as already mentioned.

Weak demand in the Americas at low prices. China, Hong Kong influenced by lack of large salmon and trading buyers. That being said we see a positive underlying assumptions for Asia and we think that Asia will continue to grow also in the years to come.

Then over to the industry supply outlook on Page 26, we've taken the 2015 guidance somewhat down, since last time we presented our figures i.e. in the first quarter in Europe due to a cold summer.

For the third quarter we think the supply global wise will be a plus minus breakeven or zero, somewhat up in the second quarter, but the supply situation adjusted for seasonality looks good for the second half and for 2016 it’s also positive.

According to Kontali, the analyst agency a 2% growth in 2016 worldwide is likely and that bodes well for the prices going forward, is also a very good sign for getting a continued tight market balance.

Then over to our internal volumes on Page 27, we’re not only taking down the industry supply outlook, our harvest volumes are also down from 440,000 tons to 430,000 tons on an annual basis 2015 of which 9,000 tons are in Norway due to the cold summer.

So we think that what has hit our numbers will also hit the entire industry in Europe something as it would be strange.

Then over to Page 28 and the outlook, to sum up a very favorable supply outlook, going forward a strong future price next month of NOK42 per kilo relatively good underlying demand in European and Asian markets.

Developments in the American market definitely remains a concern. So we must continue to work with the American market through introducing more value added concepts and new products.
