Dynasil Corporation of America's (DYSL) CEO Peter Sulick on Q3 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 12, 2015 7:40 PM ET  |  About: Dynasil Corporation of America (DYSL) by: SA Transcripts

Dynasil Corporation of America (NASDAQ:DYSL)

Q3 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 12, 2015 05:00 PM ET

Executives

Patty Kehe - IR

Peter Sulick - Chairman, CEO and President

Tom Leonard - CFO

Analysts

Joe First - First Associates

Operator

Good day and welcome to the Dynasil Corporation of America Fiscal 2015 Third Quarter Conference Call. At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. [Operator Instructions] After today’s presentation there will be an opportunity to ask questions. [Operator Instructions] Please note this event is being recorded. I would now like to turn the conference call over to Patty Kehe of Dynasil. Ms. Kehe, please go ahead.

Patty Kehe

Thank you, Laura and good afternoon everyone. With me are Peter Sulick, Dynasil’s Chairman, CEO and President and Tom Leonard, Dynasil’s Chief Financial Officer.

Before we begin, please note that various remarks management makes on today’s conference call that are not historical facts, including, but not limited to statements about our expectations, beliefs, plans, designs, objectives, prospects, financial conditions, assumptions and future events or performance, are forward-looking statements under the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995.

Forward-looking statements involve risks and uncertainties. Actual results may differ materially from those indicated by such forward-looking statements, as a result of various important factors, including those discussed in Dynasil's Annual Report on Form 10-K for the fiscal year ended September 30, 2014 filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission. Dynasil's filings can be accessed on the Investor Relations section of the Company's Web site, www.dynasil.com.

In addition, any forward-looking statements represent the Company's views as of today, August 12, 2015. These statements should not be relied upon as representing the Company's views as of any subsequent date. While Dynasil may elect to update forward-looking statements at some point in the future, the Company specifically disclaims any obligation to do so.

Now, let me turn the call over to Peter Sulick.

Peter Sulick

Thanks Patty, good afternoon everyone. We're happy to be joining you today to talk about our financial results for the third quarter of our 2015 fiscal year. Tom will go into detail about our result in a few minutes, but with respect to certain high level financial metrics for the quarter, I’m happy to report Dynasil net income for the quarter of $0.4 million $400,000 or $0.02 per share compared with break even income or $0.00 per share in the quarter ended June 30, 2014.

Optic’s segment revenues for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 increased to $5.6 million, an increase of $0.4 million or 9% compared to the same quarter in 2014. Contract Research segment revenues for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 were $4.6 million a decrease of $0.8 million or 15% compared to the same quarter of the prior year.

As we’ve reported regularly in October 2013, the net assets of the tissue sealant technology previously owned by Dynasil Biomedical were contributed to Xcede Technologies Inc., a joint venture with Mayo Medical Ventures, an investment arm of the Mayo Clinic. Because Dynasil owns 90% of the common stock of Xcede, it is required to be included in our consolidated net income for financial reporting purposes. The quarterly loss for Xcede was approximately $250,000. Without this consolidated loss Dynasil’s net income would have been approximately $650,000 for the quarter.

Our Optimetrics, Hilger and EMF subsidiaries all continued to experience solid revenue growth and I anticipate continued growth in the Optics segment in the fourth quarter. Both Optimetrics and Hilger have large long term customer contracts which are driving this growth while EMF continues to integrate the DichroTec business, acquired in June, 2014.

This acquisition now known as EMF Rochester was running at a loss prior to our acquisition and – on the part of our EMF management team. We are very active in bidding on opportunities for coated optics and other substrates to take advantage of the capacity we now control and are hopeful that some of the good news on that front will come in the near future.

In the interim, we are running a flexed schedule on this plant allocating our workload between our two coating sites continuing to improve the working environment at Rochester and selectively upgrading the hardware where appropriate.

Our contract research segment revenues have been steady for the first three quarters of fiscal 2015 after a contraction which began in last year’s second quarter in response to federal government fiscal constraint on R&D spending, while stabilizing our contract revenue was still 15% below the 2014 third quarter.

Our response over the past year to this contraction has been to prudently manage costs as well as the utilization of our contract backlog which stands at $35 million as of June 30, 2015. We emphasize bidding on non-SBIR research and have been very active in participating in collaborating bidding programs which include both research and acquisition components.

In this age of uncertain government R&D budgets continuing resolutions and lack of predictability when granted projects like the funded we continue to manage R&D reflective of this reality.

I would also like to provide brief updates on certain of the items mentioned in the past. Our Click [ph] production now exclusively handled in RMD and Watertown is reaching a mature stage. We offer numerous configurations and sizes of Click of up to three inches which is being currently incorporated in handheld and pager size radiation detectors.

Hilger is still handling the fabrication and direct customer shipments to OEMs of this synthetic material; however we are considering transferring crystal fabrication to RMD over the next 12 months. The entire Click effort has been a very successful collaboration with U.S. Department of Homeland Security over many years.

From the recognition of DHS over the impending shortage of helium-3 to the funding of the development of a replacement for helium-3 at RMD and now the commercial deployment in the market place for use by first responders we are quite proud of this effort and the contribution it is now making and will continue to make towards keeping us safe from radiation contamination, dirty bombs or worse.

I’m happy to report our quarterly gross margin for the optics segment has improved to 39% from 32.5% in the second quarter, while still below our traditional operating margins this increase is reflective of improved operational cost at Optimetrics while still experiencing above average cost at Hilger servicing one large developmental account.

Our Hilger capital improvements to service this customer have been largely completed which will improve gross margins once steady state production is achieved. We have spent approximately $750,000 this year re-configuring our Marge [ph] U.K. plant including all new fabrication hardware necessary to process synthetic crystals. This included moving an entire fabrication operation to the second floor of the facility along with new saws, vacuum equipment, meteorology lab and cements etcetera. We are also negotiating with our suppliers from volume related cost reductions across all companies, but particularly at Hilger and Optimetrics.

I also anticipate margin improvement at utilization of our EMF Rochester assets improves. Overall, our operating segments reported increased operating profits for the quarter compared to the prior year, however we also consolidated results of our 90% owned joint venture Xcede and while it continues to make progress on its tissue sealant technology also continues to incur approximately $250,000 of research and development expense each quarter included in our results.

I believe the Xcede business funded since 2013 by outside NGO investors and certain members of Dynasil’s executive team and board of directors represents a wonderful opportunity for Dynasil. On August 7, we converted the provision of patent filed last year on the improved Xcede patch to a full utility patent and filed several additional complimentary patents. These filings are a critical step towards protecting the Xcede’s [Indiscernible] property as we continued down the path towards potential commercialization. We remain very committed to the realization of value for this technology as one of the most exciting opportunities within Dynasil.

As mentioned in our prior quarterly call we are actively working on putting in place a management team necessary to advance this technology through clinical development. We will be making an announcement in this area prior to the end of our current quarter. We are also negotiating with several potential commercial partners for product development, clinical and manufacturing support. We hope to have announcement in the near term here as well.

In short, we are putting in place the elements to take this conceptual developmental product conceived by Dr. Dan Ericson at Dynasil Biomedical with early stage funding by Dynasil through regulatory testing and into the market place. And as previously disclosed this product has the potential to service one of the largest addressable markets available to Dynasil.

So, to briefly summarize, we have stabilized our research revenue and are maintaining our research backlog. We have invested in bidding on longer term projects with acquisition opportunities something we have not done in the past to diversify our backlog and revenue stream within our contract research division. We have incurred expense to participate in the potential opportunities but believe the potential long term upsize worth the short term investment.

We have successfully obtained longer term multiyear commitments for products within our commercial businesses as opposed to less predictable job shop revenue. We are benefitting from this in fiscal 2015 but the longer term benefit will come in 2016 and future periods. And we have invested capital dollars selectively to support these revenue opportunities.

We are prudently managing cost across all of our operations. We have remained cash flow positive for the past several years which will enable us to refinance our subordinated debt incurred three years ago, lower our cost of capital and improve our balance sheet. We expect to accomplish this during our current quarter.

Finally we are supporting our exceed initiative and opportunity within the company for an outsized win, while Dynasil can no longer invest directly if these efforts has been supported through outside investment, persistence in recruiting and management team, expertise at the Xcede board level and assistance in strategic direction.

On very important consideration and thinking about Xcede’s recognition at Dynasil can conceptualized this an opportunity and take it to market with limited resources and to a certain amount of organizational group. Hopefully other opportunities are materialized from our research operation which will enable us to replicate this model.

Now let me turn the call over to Tom Leonard, our CFO.

Tom Leonard

Thank you, Peter and good afternoon everyone. Revenues for the third quarter ended June 30, 2015 were $10.2 million up 3% from last quarter's revenue of $9.9 million, but they were $400,000 or 3.8% below the $10.6 million we achieved in the third quarter of fiscal 2014. As Peter explained, the $400,000 decline resulted from an $800,000 drop in the Contract Research revenue, partially offset by a $400,000 increase in revenues from our Optic segment.

Our Contract Research business segment revenues have been steady at $4.6 million in each of the first three quarters of fiscal 2015, while our backlog has increased from $30 million last September, the end of our last fiscal year to $35 million as of June 30, 2015.

We expect Contract Research segment revenues to increase modestly beginning this quarter in response to the increase in backlog. While we are pleased with the contract wins and backlog increase this year, we continue to monitor this business and are prudently managing headcount given the current uncertain market conditions.

Our $400,000, Optic segment revenue increased was a 9% increase over the same quarter last year and a $400,000 increase over the revenues we’ve reported last quarter. As Peter mentioned two of the business units in the Optic segment Hilger and Optometrics, each generated significant business with different large customers and we expect these custom engineered component parts business relationships to continue.

In addition the DichroTec acquisition that we completed in June 2014 contributed incrementally to the Optic segment revenues for the quarter. As Peter discussed, we're continuing to integrate the capacity we acquired with the DichroTec acquisition and to seek additional revenue opportunities to utilize this capacity.

Gross profit for the third quarter increased $300,000 to o $4.2 million or 41.2% from $3.9 million or 36.4% in the same quarter last year. Operating expenses for the third quarter remained level at $3.7 million in the third quarter of 2015 consistent with the prior quarter and the third quarter of last year.

We’ve maintained steady operating expenses compared to last year by reducing operating expenses at the Contract Research segment as revenues began to soften in the third quarter of 2014, while carefully managing increases in expenses in the Optic segment needed to support that segments revenue growth.

We have also carefully managed the Biomedical segments research expenditures at an approximate quarterly expense rate of $250,000 or approximately $1 million on the annual basis.

Interest expense decreased approximately $20,000 to $124,000 for the quarter as a result of lower rates on both of our secured borrowings. The Company had net income of $400,000 for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 compared to net income of $74,000 for the same quarter last year.

As Peter said earlier, these results include losses of $236,000 in 2015 and $173,000 in 2014 from Xcede our Biomedical joint venture that was being funded by outside investors.

I should also mention that Xcede rates in additional $300,000 in convertible from outside investors during the quarter and a further $200,000 in the current quarter.

Turning briefly to the balance sheet, cash at June 30, 2015 was approximately $3.5 million and the Company had approximately $1.7 million of availability under the line of credit with Middlesex Savings Bank.

With that Peter and I would be happy to take your questions, operator?

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

Thank you. [Operator Instructions] And our first question comes from Joe First of First Associates.

Joe First

Hi. Afternoon Peter. Good job on lot of segments – sections of the company there, you make a nice progress. Can you expand a little bit on the Xcede and sort of you can touch on tissues technology, sealant technology and also what is going to take to get to the finish line, because it seems to be in previous conversation it will take lot of money and a lot of time, but can you sort of expand on that?

Peter Sulick

Sure, absolutely. So Joe, we started this project about three or four years ago. And the project, the actual technology has evolved over the last couple of years to where it now is a patch product similar to a band-aid where its appealable kind of peal in place product now as oppose to [Indiscernible] product that required a separate, almost salt shaker like device to active it. So where we are today is a very significantly advanced product over and above what it was about a year ago. So we’ve taken a little bit of additional time to develop it. And the patents that I mentioned during the turmoil part of the presentation, our formalization and filing of the patents around that technology, the initial patent which was filed about year ago.

So we’ve protected lot of the research and we’ve gotten let’s say design trees on the product at this point which is what we’ve accomplished over the past year. So it has been largely a research driven exercise for the past three or four years and is now at the point where it needs to move out of the lab and actually into the clinical development stage which means we have to go through, potentially go through clinical trials of various forms and other large animal trials and biocompatibility stage and things of that nature some which we’ve already done.

So, it’s moving from developmental let’s say conceptual phase into a let’s call it, clinical phase and through the clinical trial phase into a product launch phase. We need a management team to do that. We have started that process and as I mentioned we will have an announcement about that before the end of the quarter. We will also need additional personnel, very specific skill sets to take the product through this next phase and so we will be hiring for that. We will need to get into clinical trials which will involve human clinical studies both offshore and in the United States.

So, we will be going through an FDA process. And in order to do all of that we’re going t need to raise money for this entity and have partners that will assist us along the way. Joe, the funds required to take this product through that process are not going to be insignificant. We believe it could be somewhere between $10 million and $25 million. Some of its going to depend upon who our collaboration partners are and how much they participate in the potential funding and some of it is going to depend upon the exact nature and the route that the clinical trials take to get through FDA approval.

We have a general sense of what that’s going to be but we will have a better sense probably by the time we have the next one of these calls because we will have a team in place that will be able to advise us on that a little bit better.

Our current thinking is that we will be looking to move into the market place to fund that in the first quarter of next year, that we have adequate resources over the next year today to get us through to March of next year, but we will be looking to either do an institutional private equity round or some other type of equity round that will provide at least the initial step towards getting us to this clinical phase.

And it may be entirely possible that we do it in the series of funding, but it’s I think its reached an area of exciting point that we are now in a position to be able to do these things and you know that’s what we are putting a team in place to do over the next six to nine months.

Joe First

And I think from what we’ve heard before to this product has huge potential for significant revenue and a lot of companies out there that are losing a lot of money and continue diluting their stock and developing things aren’t as far long – but we have so, on the one hand we are in a very good position where we are and the other hand we want to try to do something to keep as much as we can to get the expenses out of Dynasil and I know you’ve been working on that for months and months but it just seems like we just never get there, but I can see why I’ve been sort of delaying it because of improving the product before you did it. So I can understand that and we’re making good progress I think. Thank you.

Peter Sulick

Sure.

Operator

[Operator Instructions] And seeing no further questions, I would like to turn the conference back over to Mr. Sulick for any closing remarks.

Peter Sulick

All right, thank you Laura. Well I just would like to say thank you all for participating in our third quarter analyst call and the conference is now concluded. You may all now disconnect.
