YOU On Demand Holdings' (YOD) CEO Weicheng Liu on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 12:04 PM ET  |  About: YOU On Demand Holdings, Inc. (YOD) by: SA Transcripts

YOU On Demand Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ:YOD)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015 8:00 AM ET

Executives

Jason Finkelstein - Director, Strategy and Investor Relations

Shane McMahon - Chairman

Weicheng Liu - Chief Executive Officer

Grace He - Vice President, Finance

Analysts

Jay Srivatsa - Chardan Capital Markets

Operator

Good morning, everyone. And welcome to YOU On Demand’s Second Quarter 2015 Investor Earnings Call. At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. Later, we will conduct a question-and-answer session.

At this time, for opening remarks and introductions, I would like to turn the call over to Jason Finkelstein, Director of Strategy and Investor Relations at YOU On Demand. Please go ahead.

Jason Finkelstein

Thank you, operator, and good morning. Welcome to our second quarter 2015 earnings conference call. Joining me today, I’m pleased to have Shane McMahon, Chairman; Weicheng Liu, CEO, and Grace He, VP of Finance.

We announced our financial results at approximately 7:05 AM Eastern Time. The financial results and the webcast of this call are also available at the company’s corporate website at corporate.yod.com. Once Shane, Weicheng and Grace, complete their prepared remarks, we will open up the lines to analysts’ questions and then continue with the Q&A session with questions received via e-mail, both prior to and during the call.

The webcast of today’s call will also be archived and available in the Webcasts and Events section of the YOD corporate website for a minimum of 30 days. We may make forward-looking statements during this call regarding the company’s future performance. Actual results may differ materially from these statements due to risks and uncertainties related to the business. These risks and uncertainties are detailed from time-to-time in the Management’s Discussion and Analysis section of our corporate filings. Copies of which can be obtained from the SEC or via our website.

All information discussed on this call is as of today, August 13, 2015. And YOU On Demand undertakes no obligation to update any statements or expectations from prior conversations. Regarding today’s agenda, Shane will begin with a quick introduction and Weicheng will discuss trends and our strategy. Finally, Grace will cover our financials.

Shane, please go ahead.

Shane McMahon

Thank you, Jason. I am very pleased to announce another strong quarter of growth, with revenues in Q2 2015 of approximately $1,480,000, YOU On Demand saw year-over-year and quarter-over- quarter growth of 710% and 44%, respectively. The increase in revenue was primarily attributable to the growth in the video streaming business on mobile and Over-The-Top or OTT platforms. 2015 year-to-date revenues now stand at $2,508,000, surpassing total revenues for the entire year ended 2014 by $545,000, or 28%.

The entire team continues to execute on strategy, grow brand awareness and recognition and further develop and enhance the YOU On Demand value proposition. We continue to navigate carefully and within the scope of the Chinese regulatory environment and learned a tremendous amount about consumer video consumption habits. This leaves us well positioned with both our content lineup and wide distribution channels to continue capturing one of the ever growing and massive media and entertainment market in China. Anytime and anywhere started off with simply video distribution called the digital cable but for over a short amount of time it’s morphed into IPTV, mobile internet, smart TVs, railway Wi-Fi and OTT. YOU On Demand is a company very adaptive moving along with constantly changing technology and consumer preferences and that gives us a tremendous opportunity to continue to grow market share.

With that, I’d now like to turn the call over to YOU On Demand CEO Weicheng Liu.

Weicheng Liu

Thank you, Shane and thanks to everyone for joining our call. In China, the media and entertainment industry has seen tremendous growth in recent years, even outperforming the overall economy. Drivers of this growth have come from three areas which continue to expand at a tremendous pace. Disposable income, government subsidies and new media adoption. While the media and the entertainment industry continues to undergo a profound change in both commercial acquisition and technology, the middle class continues to expand and disposable income has grown with it. As a result, millions of people are finding that they have extra money to spend after accounting for the cost of living.

China’s new generation has had more education and more opportunities to work outside the factories than their parents and grandparents did. So unlike in the US where middle aged adults make the most, in China it’s the younger people and the generation and usually most tech savvy who make up the higher income brackets. Not only do they have more money but they are more willing to spend it as they have only known the Chinese economy where standards of living having risen drastically and they want to live in a same manner and have the things that young people do in other markets in the world.

In addition, the proliferation of broadband and mobile internet users has been turbo-charged by massive infrastructure and the backbone investment from Chinese policymakers working on funding ways to diversify China’s economy and shift away from the country’s heavy reliance on exports.

All of this has created a very favorable environment in which YOU On Demand is now capitalizing on with both revenue and the user growth as well as distribution expansion with new and the current partners. As YOU On Demand business grows and evolves, each platform and distribution channel presents its own set of unique challenges, operating and marketing demand at our customer base. Each also have their own set of standards requirements and metrics and as a relatively young company in an evolving industry [indiscernible] to give a specific implication for operational data in the context of how a particular arrangement for deal is going. I understand that this can cause frustration at times, but I am asking our investors to focus on several areas. First, our impressive deal flow and future deal flow. Second, our strong growth on our top line revenues. And third, a continued reduction in operating expenses as it directly relates to a more streamlined operating structure.

Simply put, YOU On Demand is starting to hit its strides. We are seeing encouraging consumer demand. We are restructuring our movie content offering monthly to give consumers the latest titles available as well as a strong selection of library titles. And our Android mobile app has both the strong rankings and reviews on consumer public app sites. We are constantly operating both our service and user interface and working closely with our partners to ensure seamless compatibility with most platform types and because of these strides and overall strengthening of the YOU On Demand brand, our rates have grown beyond our beginnings on digital cable and are now found almost everywhere on mobile devices, IPTV, OTT set top devices, computer trends – commuter trains and smart TVs.

Our most recent announcement and the expanded launch with Dr. Peng Group, this after a brief start and the structure of our partnership is a good example of the direction YOU On Demand is likely to take future distribution deals. As a recap, under expanded collaboration and the special promotion, Dr Peng Group providing 1-year or 2-years of automatically activated and bundled YOU Hollywood service, to both the first 2 million purchasers of Dr. Peng's new Domy Box and the first 200,000 purchasers of the new Domy Smart TV. The fee for the YOU Hollywood service will be built into and included in the sale price of each individual piece of hardware. Upon the sale of a device, YOU On Demand will receive payment from Dr. Peng Group.

We find a deal like this to be very attractive for several reasons. First, subscribers are signing up for long term subscriptions as our service is bundled and add together to the long term of the broadband service provided. Second, YOU On Demand as to reup [ph] that balances off the distribution partners, marketing capabilities and the reach without having to increase our own budget. So in this case, not only is our service being marketed through e-commerce and the social platforms, but on top of that are Dr. Peng Group’s broadband service centers where there is a direct interaction between customers and sales associates. Think of it in terms of the customer walking into a Verizon store to purchase a mobile plan and the hardware or some – or smartphone that go with that, generally speaking the direct to consumer face to face interaction in brick and mortar locations is a very strong sales tool with higher conversion rates, and YOU On Demand gets access to Dr Peng’s sales and service centers in key cities.

Finally, this partnership with Dr. Peng is an expansion of an already existing partnership set on a great working relationship between the two companies. This is important because it allows YOU On Demand to expand with a partner where months of work have already been put into infrastructure and both companies are [indiscernible] of each other’s capabilities and protocols when it comes to delivery of new content.

To close, I am very pleased that YOU On Demand got back on track with quarter-over-quarter sequential growth and investors should expect that trend to continue.

With that, I would like to hand it over to Grace to cover our financials.

Grace He

Good morning everyone. As Shane mentioned earlier in the call, with revenues in second quarter of 2015 of approximately $1,480,000, YOU On Demand saw year-over-year and quarter-over- quarter growth of 710% and 44%, respectively. The increase in revenue was primarily attributable to the growth in the video streaming business on mobile and Over-The-Top platforms.

Gross profit for the second quarter of 2015 was approximately $651,000, as compared to a gross loss of $674,000 during the same period in 2014. The increase in gross profitability was primarily due to growth in revenue while maintaining tight control of costs. YOU On Demand’s cost of revenue is largely comprised of content licensing fees and, to a lesser extent, costs associated with direct delivery of our content services such as content preparation fees and government censorship clearance costs.

Selling, general and administrative expenses for the second quarter of 2015 decreased approximately $612,000, to $1,659,000, as compared to $2,271,000 in second quarter of 2014. The decrease resulted predominantly from resource shifts to China as part of our long-term cost savings and operations enhancement initiatives.

Salaries and personnel costs are the principal components of selling, general and administrative expenses. While we added resources in the area of product development and operations in the second quarter of 2015, salaries and personnel costs decreased 6% as compared to the same period in 2014, due to our cost saving initiatives.

Total operating expenses in the second quarter 2015 decreased 23% to $1,905,000 from $2,486,000 in the same period last year.

Net loss was $1,324,000 for the second quarter of 2015 compared to $1,086,000 in the comparable 2014 period. Net loss for the second quarter of 2014 was primarily affected by non-operating income of $1,502,000 due to changes in fair value of warrant liabilities. We recognize certain warrants as derivative liabilities and re-measure these warrants at the end of every reporting period and upon settlement. The fair value change of these warrants is primarily affected by fluctuation in our closing stock price. Net loss for the second quarter of 2015 was primarily affected by loss from operations of $1,255,000.

Basic and diluted loss per share for the second quarter of 2015 was $0.06 as compared to a $0.05 loss per share in the same period in 2014. For the six months ended June 30, 2015, basic and diluted loss per share was $0.17 as compared to a $1.50 loss per share for the same six-month period in 2014.

As of June 30 2015, the company had cash and cash equivalents of approximately $6.9 million and total current assets of approximately $10.9 million.

More details of YOU On Demand’s second quarter financials can be found in Form 10-Q of our corporate filings, copies of which can be obtained from the SEC or via our website.

This concludes our management team’s prepared remarks. Now I’d like to return the call back to the operator and open up the line for your questions. As a reminder, webcast participants are invited to e-mail us their questions at ir@yod.com now.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

[Operator Instructions] We have a question coming from the line of Jay Srivatsa with Chardan Capital.

Jay Srivatsa

Thanks for taking my questions. Congratulations on the numbers, Shane and Weicheng, good numbers. Let me ask you, in terms of the split in Q2, can you help us understand how it broke out between mobile, cable and OTT?

Weicheng Liu

Thanks Jay for the question. Well in general, we generate revenue roughly – two-thirds of revenue come from the mobile and OTT, the rest of all come from IPTV and cable. We divide it that way because IPTV and cable are considered as closed network where the building and everything is more handled by the operators themselves. So we are a pure content provider, whereas in OTT space we have our own VRF with more control over how the content is consumed and we have back end data to analyze the user behavior. The mobile is even more of a direct to consumer service, so overall about two-thirds come from the OTT and mobile.

Jay Srivatsa

And do you expect the similar split in the second half or is that mix going to change?

Weicheng Liu

I think generally that trend will continue. We might be able to do better in IPTV space but I don’t say it would change the overall kind of split.

Jay Srivatsa

In terms of this engagement, partnership you have with Dr. Peng Group, I think in the press release it talks about roughly 3 million boxes and 500,000 TVs by the end of the year. How much have you already recognized in the first half and how much is yet to be recognized in the second half?

Weicheng Liu

Well, by far we haven’t really recognized much from Dr. Peng only some small numbers because it took long preparation time. We also run two rounds of trials on their broadband network and also for them to launch their service nationwide, it probably covers 20, 30 different cities, to get things going take us for a while. So I think that most revenue will come in the second half of the year, Dr. Peng Group.

Jay Srivatsa

Can you give us some sense on what your revenue contribution is going to be from that business?

Weicheng Liu

From this Dr. Peng Group or –

Jay Srivatsa

Yes.

Weicheng Liu

All right. Well, Dr. Peng Group is one of the major revenue contributor in the second half in the OTT space, I would put in the top three but it’s just hard for me to now give you a specific number but we know for sure they set their own goals. We have to piggyback on that number. But by far it’s hard for me to give you a specific number on what sort of revenue number will come back. But definitely Dr. Peng Group is a major revenue contributor.

Jay Srivatsa

But do you still feel comfortable with the 3 million box number and the 500,000 TV number in terms of just sales for the rest of the year?

Weicheng Liu

All right.

Jay Srivatsa

No, the question is, do you feel comfortable with the numbers that are in the press release which –

Weicheng Liu

Their sales number and also our agreement basically – there is a bundling arrangement in there. So we are closely watching how they do with the sales target they set for themselves.

Jay Srivatsa

Shifting over to your partnership with C Media, I think last quarter there was some discussion over your partnership and how that goes into long haul trains and stuff. Can you give us an update on where things are with that?

Weicheng Liu

Well, first of all, that project is going reasonably well. C Media has deployed more than 100 trains of their Wi-Fi network onboard the long haul trains and has big trains. By the number we received, this is still in the trial stage, we try to collect more user data and analyze it, we already start receiving some money from C Media but in my view this Wi-Fi on the train -- while it’s more like a show room for distribution channel, people take train in the journey of two to three hours, sometimes bit longer, but that is the type of environment where people have this immersive environment, they can enjoy a movie and then when they get off the train, they take our service, go with it. That comes to our focus. Rather to consider that as a major distribution channel for a while, but said all that, the number come back from C Media tells us roughly 50% of the passengers on train well turn on their mobile phone or their tablets to connect to the Wi-Fi, and out of that 50% passengers, 80%, 90% of the people are actually watching a movie, the long form.

Jay Srivatsa

So roughly 100 trains have been deployed thus far and what’s your expectation in the second half?

Weicheng Liu

In the second half, we don’t yet have numbers from C Media side. So we are focused on the improved, enhancing the operation side and collect more user data back and improve its service. The two companies are working closely and I believe we will soon have some more announcement in terms of like how the operation can benefit both companies onboard the train and off-board the train.

Jay Srivatsa

And shifting to the mobile side, related to the handset business, specifically with Huawei, can you give us some sense on where things are with that and any new customers you have signed up for providing that on the mobile platform?

Weicheng Liu

Huawei continues to be one of our key partners, strategic partners. We are working very closely with the senior management of Huawei. At the same time our team, especially our operating team is working very hard with the Huawei to get our app tested with more of their different models of the phones and now get more users involved. Huawei runs their own mobile app store and it’s one of the top two on our list right now in terms of user activities and also the user feedback we collect. That’s something we’re currently focused on and to improve the product in preparation for something more significant.

Jay Srivatsa

And then last question for me, for Grace, can you give us a sense on what the burn was in the quarter and what type of revenue run rate you need to breakeven?

Grace He

Thanks for your question, Jay. Well, right now our quarterly cash burn is approximately 2 million. So you can do the math and right now we are focusing on growing the business, grabbing opportunities as they emerge and we have been carefully managing our costs which is clearly demonstrated in the decreasing operating expenses. Of course we are going to have to put in some investments in resources and in technology but I believe we will be able to achieve our business goals, we are achieving clearly a positive operating cash flows sometime next year.

Operator

Thank you. I would now like to turn the call over to Jason Finkelstein for email questions.

Jason Finkelstein

Thank you, Jay and thank you operator. So let’s move it to some of the questions that have been emailed to us investors both before and during the call and would exclude anything already asked by Mr. Srivatsa. So first, on a macro level, Weicheng, as far as the recent slide in the stock market in China, how is that going to affect consumer disposable income and is YOD sensitive to this?

Weicheng Liu

Well, it’s good question. We do not anticipate any meaningful effect or any effect at all on our business. Mainland China’s box office received nearly 50% in the first half of 2015, about $3.3 billion in ticket sales in the first six months of the year, that’s a growth of 48.9% over the first half of 2014. Imported films accounted for roughly half of that. As far as the recent slide in the Chinese stock markets, according to reports we have read stocks makes up a near 15% of household financial assets. Chinese households prefer to hold the bulk of their wealth in cash and deposits instead of equities. Moreover stock ownership is limited in China, only approximately 9% of urban households own stocks. That figure is almost 50% in the United States. So the Chinese market sort of stock market slump is not likely to derail the economy or consumer spending in China. Specifically for YOD, our consumer base is primarily made up of young adults who are not yet of the age where their focus is on investing in the stock market, instead they are interested in enjoying the lifestyle their disposable income can bring such as in the area of entertainment and technology.

Jason Finkelstein

It is for Grace, obviously there has been a lot of news related to the recent devaluated Chinese currency, what s YOU On Demand sensitivity to all of that?

Grace He

Right now, it’s a little too early to tell for us. We are operating RMB, we have in US dollar expenses and we do report in US dollars. So the devaluation will probably have some effect on our financial reporting. However we are careful to manage our RMB cash reserve, so our impact is not expected to be significant at this point.

Jason Finkelstein

Okay. Has there been any progress on hiring or naming a new CFO?

Weicheng Liu

We expect to announce the hiring of CFO later this year as we continue to interview candidates.

Jason Finkelstein

For Weicheng, what are the plans for enhancing shareholder value going forward, is the stock not moving that much as of late?

Weicheng Liu

We plan to do this by solidly executing our business plan and realizing our long term strategy. We pledge to improve our investor communication and information sharing, including speaking to institution of funds and analyze and engaging with investors at conferences. Our long term strategy, we have talked about this previously is basically focused on four components. The first is to build our business vertically by focusing on movie and premium content, it’s not just for movie, we have been the best movies, best of pure content to consumer households, we build our product vertically, meaning, anything movie related, upstream or downstream, we are focusing in – and sort of provide the users something beyond just watching that movie itself. I think the way it addresses that mobile content, that’s number one.

And the number two, we focus on expanding our existing distribution channels and by working with the most significant, and most influential partners in the consumer space who can help us to reach consumers, with this, we do business, so we do not burn too much money on marketing and the bandwidth as many other video companies, as you see they tend to generate some sort of a larger revenue numbers, that also burn a lot of money, we don’t. Number two, I need to check – but anyway I think we could have more discussion about the long term strategy pieces but for now – and I think the best we could bring back to the investors, I know investors are concerned about sort of how to improve the investor value proposition, we definitely hear that message, we are working on it, we will improve our investor communications and also – but more importantly, is to focus on executing our business to continue to demonstrate to the market the solid numbers in both top line and hopefully improve the bottom line as well.

Jason Finkelstein

Next question. As far as Future TV and the Samsung Smart TV deal, how far reaching is that, is the YOU On Demand service available for potential purchase on everything Samsung TVs sold in China?

Weicheng Liu

We are available on all the Samsung Smart TVs which are partnered with Future TV. As far as I can recall, we are talking about a list of five or six top models, each selling in probably – has to be significant numbers. We don’t yet have a specific number but we know those are the top numbers. We are one of the primary premier paid services being offered along with other contents including some free contents. Based on our current cooperation with Future TV, we expect the subscriber number to show a steady growth with both Samsung Smart TV and some other devices will be announced soon.

Jason Finkelstein

And then as far as personnel costs, is there any plan or need to hire additional headcount in the US or in China?

Grace He

Well, Jason, our headcount has been increasing in the area of product development operations in China in proportion with the growth of our business. Due to the streamlined operations having the team based here in Beijing, we have been able to manage the costs and expenses and bringing that under control. So as business grows, we should be expected to see that – we will need additional resources.

End of Q&A

Jason Finkelstein

That is all the questions we have for today. And thank you again to our investors for joining us. On that we look forward to updating you on YOU On Demand’s ongoing progress when we host our 2015 Q3 call. Have a great day everyone.

Operator

Thank you. Today’s conference has concluded. Thank you for your participation and you may now disconnect your lines at this time.
