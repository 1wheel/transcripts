Tabcorp Holdings' (TABCF) CEO David Attenborough on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 2:36 AM ET  |  About: TABCORP Holdings Limited (TABCF) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

David Attenborough - CEO

Damien Johnston - CFO

Analysts

Matthew Ryan - JPMorgan

Mark Wilson - Deutsche Bank Australia

Sacha Krien - CLSA

Larry Gandler - Credit Suisse

Adam Alexander - Goldman Sachs JBWere

Rohan Sundram - Citigroup

Brian Han - Morningstar

David Rosenbloom - Perennial Investors

Tabcorp Holdings Limited (OTCPK:TABCF) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 12, 2015 8:00 PM ET

David Attenborough

Good morning and thank you for joining us for Tabcorp results presentation for the 2015 fifteen financial year. I'm David Attenborough and I'm the CEO of Tabcorp and I'm joined on this call Damien Johnston our CFO I intend to speak for about twenty minutes and I'll take you through the presentation launch with ASX today and then we will open up the call to questions. However before I get into the detail of the presentation let me provide a brief overview, the results we present today reflect the success of our multichannel strategy and the benefits of our ongoing focus on products brands and customers. A strong wagering a media performance underpin the group result. We’re competing very well in what is a highly competitive market and the benefits of our ongoing focus on products, brands and customers a strong wagering of media performance underpin the group result. We're competing very well in what is a highly competitive market.

Customers are responding to our wagering offer and our results are demonstrating the value of integrating our unique retail digital and media assets. The full year financial performances of keno and gaming services were relatively flat but strategically both businesses have been well positioned and resourced for growth going forward. We're pleased to have delivered on our objective of increasing returns to shareholders. And finally I'd like to update you on the Statement of Claim launched by AUSTRAC with the Federal Court. This statement includes matters which we have been discussing with AUSTRAC over an extended period. We have a program of works which has been underway for some time and this program has been expanded and accelerated. We are committed to achieving the highest levels of regulatory compliance across the business as a priority and as it's before the courts you will appreciate that my ability to comment and answer questions about the matter is restricted.

If I can now take you to slide two which provides a more detailed overview of the year, statutory impact was AUD334.5 reflecting strong operational performance and some nonrecurring income tax benefits excluding these significant items, impact from continuing operations was up 14.7%. The Board has announced a full year ordinary dividend of AUD0.20 per share up from AUD0.16 in the PCP. Our full year dividend accounts for 93% of impact before significant items. In addition our target payout ratio for FY ‘16 will increase to 90% of impact before the amortization of the Victorian Wagering License of AUD35 million per annum.

On progressing our strategic agenda, we finalized the acquisition of act ACTTAB in October -2014 and integration is on track to be completed in FY ‘16. We also secured New South Wales thoroughbred [ph] media rights in early 2015 and we were very pleased to announce last week the addition of the Victorian thoroughbred media rights ensuring that our customers will not only be able to watch every Australian race of every code on Sky in retail and the home but also for the first time enjoy the same complete service through Tab's digital channels. This is a very positive outcome for Tabcorp racing our venue partners and our customers. Slide three outlines the group P&L results which are broken down in more detail on slide 4, slide 4 provides an overview of the results for each business and there are just three items I would like to call out. Firstly, wagering the media revenue growth with 6.9% and EBITDA was up 7.2% despite AUD5.8 million in one off acquisition and integration costs for ACTTAB. Secondly the increased expenditure levels in keno reflect the step up of investment to set it up for growth in FY ‘16. And thirdly TGS is moving from being a Victorian only business to one which is building a presence in New South Wales and we are pleased to have signed a number of new venues in that market. We therefore expect expenses and DNA to increase significantly in this business in FY ‘16.

However we are focused on managing our expenses within the framework of 20% to 21% OpEx to revenue ratio. If not for the ACCTAB one-offs we would have been at 21% for the group in FY ‘15. Maintaining this expense discipline remains a priority going forward.

I would now like to take you through more detail on the performance of each of our businesses starting with the wagering and media KPI's on slide 5. And these have good results, they demonstrate that we have been getting it right in the way we've been engaging customers, managing risk and bringing new products to market. We of course also benefited from 8.5 months revenue from ACTTAB. Total racing revenue growth was 5.1% with the growth in Fixed Odds reflecting both changes in customer preferences and a further expansion in yield. The soccer World Cup impacted the growth rate of both Tab Sorts and Luxbet in each half as it was in the second half of last year and in the first half of this year.

The underlying Tab Sports business is performing well on the back of the expanded product range available to customers. Media revenue continued its recent growth trends driven by increased international vision and new comingling arrangements such as the one named [ph] place with Hong Kong. Once again our performance demonstrates our significant contributions to racing with returns to racing from Tabcorp up AUD40 million on last year to more than AUD770 million.

If I can now take you to slide 6 which provides an overview of initiatives we are taking to maintain our leadership in wagering. Future retail is a program which largely brings together recognizes two things. Firstly we have very powerful retail assets which give us a competitive advantage. And secondly the consumer shift to digital is undoubtedly continuing. Our Future retailer pilot sites go live this half. We continue to execute well in the digital environment with a smooth transition to a our new Tab website one of the highlights. It's an area where we continue and will continue to invest in our capabilities and we plan to employ 40 additional digital specialists this year. We've been very pleased with the performance of the cash out product across all distribution channels which we launched today. We understand we are the only wagering operator in the world that enables cash out across retail and digital channels as well as driving new sales cash out is changing the customer conversation and we have seen high levels of engagement.

While it's a small investment and very much in its infancy, our involvement in eSports through Unikrn demonstrates that we are prepared to think differently about the products we offer as well as well as ways to reach a new segment of customers. We've also waited are focused on upweighted our focus on data and analytics under a new Chief Marketing Officer to drive improved customer experience and engagement. In relation to key metrics around customer engagement, the numbers are pleasing with our total Tab active customer base up 12% and more than 300,000 Tab rewards loyalty program members.

Finally on the slide meter is a key enabler of wagering activity and we are relaunching our Sky thoroughbred central channel on the 17th of August, new wagering graphics and triggers, new sets, virtual display of in-studio graphics, giant touch screens and a fresh lineup of talent as some of the new enhancements. It's on the base tier of the Foxtel platform and that gives it reach to 2.6 million homes across the country. And if I can take you to slide 7 and gaming services, during the year we focused on the rollout in EGM loyalty system and now have the system implemented to 88AUD to the Victorian network. We also extended a number of venues on five year contracts and that means we now have 84% of the base contracted in 2022. This is a vote of confidence in the TGS business model and the value of the offering to our venue partners. We remain focused on improving the performance of our existing partners whilst also moving forward with our expansion plans into New South Wales.

The New South Wales business contributed its first full year in FY ‘15 through a 120 machines. But the focus of the TGS team was to build credibility in New South Wales and give the market confidence that our model can create value for licensed venues. Pleasingly our first two New South Wales venues have been performing very well and that's resulted in further sign ups and growing momentum in this state. We now have seven New South Wales venues in our portfolio and have around 600 machines under contract with a growing number of interested venues in the pipeline.

Moving on to slide 8 now, we're stepping up our focus on driving venue performance, recognizing success in this area will drive further sign ups. With 9300 EGMs under contract we have very strong relationships with the key gaming machine suppliers. This enables us to secure first to market games for our venues. We are adding the resources and capability to drive a superior TGS customer experience and to support a planned growth of the TGS business. This will result in additional OpEx investment of around AUD5 million per annum from FY ‘16. TGS is well placed going forward to deliver sustainable long term performance for our venue partners and our shareholders.

Slide 9 outlines the results for the keno business where we saw good customer activity in New South Wales and Victoria with Queensland more subdued at an aggregate level turnover was up 2.7%. The revenue performance varied across the states with the abnormal run of jackpots we've seen during the year impacting performance in Victoria and Queensland. In fact the Victorian business achieved double digit turnover growth yet it delivered revenue growth of just 1.5%. As I mentioned earlier we have been investing in brand, product and digital capability and we will continue to do so in FY ‘16. Pleasingly, we are already seeing signs that the recent transformation of the keno brand and customer experience is gaining traction.

On Slide 10, I can provide a bit more color around this transformation. The relaunch of the brand and customer experience has been about broadening the game's appeal across different demographics and making keno more contemporary. It's had early success with good performances in Victoria and Sydney where the new brand has gone live so far. The rollout is continuing into Queensland and regional New South Wales later this month. The pooling of jackpots between New South Wales and Victoria has improved the product offering and Queensland is planned to follow once regulatory approvals are in place. Keno is also starting to change the conversation with its customers. While it has traditionally been a purely retail game we are developing an in-venue mobile offering that will allow a keno customer to have a digital account for the first time. Again this is subject to the necessary regulatory approvals.

As with wagering, we believe digital and retail integration is an important part way for growth for Keno. We continue to take a disciplined approach to Cap Ex and slide 11 outlines some of the key projects in each business. Capital expenditure for FY ‘16 is expected to be up to a AUD160 million and that includes additional CapEx for the TGS expansion. The DNA expense for FY ‘16 is expected to be between AUD180 million to a AUD185. Slide 12, highlights our key capital features and shows we continue to have well diversified sources of funding and I'd like to call out the recently refinanced 400 million facility which extended the maturity profile of our debt facilities, the next refinancing activity is due in 2017. At year-end our gross debt to EBITDA ratio stood at 2.1 times down from 2.3 times at December 2014. Our balance sheet is in a strong position and that's allowed us to increase the target dividend payout ratio in FY ‘16.

So if I can summarize by taking you to slide 13, Tabcorp is performing well and is set out to continue to deliver growing returns for shareholders. Strategically, we are in a better position than when we started FY ‘15. As a result of completing the acquisition of ACTTAB and securing the New South Wales and Victorian thoroughbred media rights. In the context of a our focus on shareholder returns, the full year dividend was AUD0.20 per share fully franked and up 25% on last year. A special dividend of AUD0.30 per share was also paid to eligible shareholders earlier this year and our performance and confidence in the business has enabled us to increase our target dividend payout ratio for FY ‘16 again reflecting our intention to deliver attractive shareholder returns. We've kicked off FY ‘16 with a clear set of priorities and we have backing ourselves to grow. We plan to employ 100 additional full time staff in FY ‘16 with all almost all of these in customer experience in digital roles.

Importantly the additional headcount will be achieved within our ongoing framework around disciplined expense management.

On the specific priorities in the year ahead, in wagering and media these include the relaunch of Sky Thoroughbred Central, future retail roll-out and further product innovation. We remain alive to the opportunities that can leverage the powerful assets that we have built up over the years; our industry relationships, our capability, our technology, our reputation and the liquidity of our betting pools.

For gaming services we're focused on improving the performance of our venue partners and further expansion, particularly in New South Wales. For Keno the priority is to advance the brand and product transformation, link Queensland into jackpot pooling and implement the digital strategy. We will address the matters raised by AUSTRAC as a priority. We are committed to achieving the highest standards of compliance across the business. This is a business that is well positioned to continue to drive future performance, maintain expense discipline and deliver on our target of 14% ROIC by FY '17.

Thank you. I will now open up the lines for questions.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

[Operator Instructions]. Our first question comes from the line of Matthew Ryan from JPMorgan. Your line is open. Please go ahead.

Matthew Ryan

I just had a question about retail turnover within your Wagering division. It grew by about 1% in the second half which is slightly above the first half. I just wanted to know how you see the consumer environment out there and perhaps looking forward, do you think that that sort of migration away from retail towards perhaps online or call centers is coming to an end? You know, does retail take a more important view for growth going forward?

David Attenborough

There are two things that have helped us with retail. One is the fact that new product launches like cash out have really upped the actual conversation and the engagement of customers within retail. It's a very social environment and that certainly helped drive the performance this year. It also benefits from the acquisition of ACTTAB which comes through in the numbers and that has also helped and reflects the growth in the retail. It's important to remember that the way we approach our customers is a multi-channel approach and a lot of what we're doing is actually integrating digital in retail and we expect the retail social environment to be a strong part of our offering going into the future.

Matthew Ryan

Okay, talking about cash out, you know, one of the other products that's rolling around is more of a click-to-call sort of online betting service. So I just wanted to sort of hear your views on what you think of the product and whether it's worth pursuing.

David Attenborough

So first of all click-to-call, we do not believe it's in line with the approval under the Interactive Gambling Act so we are not offering that product. With respect to live betting we've seen very good growth in live betting across our retail network and we continue to offer it as a product that is popular with our customers.

Operator

Our next question comes from the line of Mark Wilson from Deutsche Bank. The line is open. Please go ahead.

Mark Wilson

Thanks very much David. Firstly on the CapEx side, with the guidance there of AUD160 million, how much of that is earmarked for TGS expansion?

Damien Johnston

Mark, Damien Johnston answering that question. Look, we're still operating to an underlying level of CapEx around sort of AUD130 million, AUD140 million so subject to sign-ups and contracts we'd expect to see some growth above that level driven by TGS expansion.

Mark Wilson

And just on the sign-ups in New South Wales, can you just give us some sense of timing as to when those venues impacted through the division and the more recent sign-ups, when they took place?

David Attenborough

We would expect the sign-ups that you're seeing there -- it does take some months to obviously bring them to market so we would expect them to be billing in the first half and in general you are looking at sort of between three to five months from signature to bringing them to market depending on the complexity of the upgrade. Because when we sign these contracts it includes -- and we work with the venues -- a complete upgrade of the venues which also has ongoing benefits to our Keno and wagering businesses that take place in those venues.

Operator

Our next question comes from the line of Sacha Krien from CLSA. The line is open. Please go ahead.

Sacha Krien

Just wondering if you can talk a little bit about the impact on wagering turnover of the blackout and perhaps give an indication of whether you're still seeing any lingering effects from that. It looks to me like the retail turnover number was reasonably soft in the fourth quarter, minus 1.4%. Was that a factor in terms of that number or is that really just the ACT impact starting to roll off?

David Attenborough

So the impact of the blackout was fairly minimal. It impacted. It wasn't something we would have liked to have seen and certainly was not a great customer experience so we're really pleased to have now come to agreement and have got it back on Sky. But we did see some shift of sales across to other products and generally overall the impact was minimal.

Sacha Krien

In terms of ratings for your Sky Network, have they recovered since the content's come back on?

David Attenborough

It's too early really to--

Damien Johnston

Last weekend was the first week.

David Attenborough

I mean it was last weekend. But we're certainly seeing and I think we saw Racing Victoria come out with some comments. There was, you know, very strong interest and involvement in the Flemington meeting on the Saturday. It was really good to get it back up onto Sky for that meeting and it's just good that we can now offer our customers also the integrated vision across our TAB digital platforms. So we now have, you know, every race from every code on Sky and not only in the home and in retail but across our TAB digital platforms so it's a good outcome.

Sacha Krien

And just one more question on wagering win rates. It looks to me like excluding Luxbet your win rate was about 17.4% in the fourth quarter which is the highest I can see going back in time. I mean what's driving that increase in win rate for you guys? Is it price or is it management of the book?

Damien Johnston

So it really has seen a continuation of the trend of increasing yields across racing and sports so we've seen both of that this year. Some of the drivers for those increased yields go to multi-products. David spoke about cash out and how that's assisting betting on multi-leg products in particular. So as those multi-products become a high proportion of turnover that also helps drive those yields up. So it's essentially I think risk management, the way we price and also customer preference as they move to the high yielding products.

David Attenborough

It was also worth mentioning that in June there was some very high sports yields based on results as well. So, you know, we are also subject to the ebb and flow of sports results that do affect yields.

Operator

We'll move on to our next question from the line of Larry Gandler from Credit Suisse. Your line is open. Please go ahead.

Larry Gandler

Continuing the discussion on wagering, the totes seemed to improve in the second half in terms of a moderated decline. I'm just wondering David, what is supporting that business. Where is the customer interest developing in tote betting?

David Attenborough

Larry, a large part of that difference is ACTTAB coming in to the numbers so you are seeing the effect of ACTTAB which is a strongly pari-mutuel business. They haven't developed a particularly large fixed-odds business. So that's obviously coming through the numbers. We continue to drive the pari-mutuel business. Obviously the increasing comingling with overseas operators is important in helping to drive those pools and make them more attractive. So it's a combination of factors but I think the largest one would have been the ACTTAB business coming into the mix.

Larry Gandler

In terms of exotic bets in the tote, are they still growing or is that also now perhaps moderating?

Damien Johnston

The mix hasn't changed a lot in the last 12 months Larry. So when you think about some of the newer pari-mutuel products like Early Quaddie, etcetera, they're now cycling so the mix is I think not a material change over the course of the 12 months.

Operator

Our next question comes from the line of Adam Alexander from Goldman Sachs. The line is open. Please go ahead.

Adam Alexander

A question on costs, I thought there was very good managing of costs in the Wagering segment particularly in light of the current level of ad spend in the industry. I was wondering David or Damien; can you give us some color around where your spending increase is on marketing and maybe where you're getting the offsetting cost savings?

David Attenborough

We are actually increasing our spend on marketing and in those numbers we actually have put more money into marketing quite significantly. But we are focused very much on leveraging our own assets. The Sky channels and the media network, retail. A lot of money is going into direct-to-customer offers around free bets and specials around different events. We're seeing those as being very effective and not only retaining but also re-engaging customers and that's been a lot of our focus. Regarding what I would call the normal media landscape, we do take a very disciplined approach to the way we look at different media assets and that will continue. You know, we do not chase assets that won't give a return on investment.

Adam Alexander

Do you think with the new sort of free-to-air coverage in Victoria that you'll need to allocate more spend to that channel?

David Attenborough

So if I put that into perspective for you, about a year before this channel came into formation there used to be TBN and TBN would be out there with New South Wales and Victorian product on it with a full suite of corporate bookmaker advertising. This is nothing new. Channel 78 will be out there. It will have some corporate advertising on it no doubt but we will continue to advertise as we consider appropriate. We may or may not do some advertising on that channel and that will be a decision made by our marketing team from time to time. It is just another medium where racing has got eyeballs watching it.

Adam Alexander

Just on Keno, I just note there the marketing campaigns launched a month ago for the rebranding. Have you got any feedback from customers? Is there any initial sort of early response to that?

David Attenborough

Yes, we tried to give you a flavor of that in my notes which is essentially it's been well-received. The initial sort of traction in Victoria and Sydney, it's certainly created a lot of conversations, lively engagement. We're seeing some really good early signs and we're continuing the rollout now across regional New South Wales and into Queensland and that certainly reflects what we're seeing so we are accelerating the rollout.

Operator

We'll now move onto our next question from the line of Rohan Sundram from Citi. Your line is open. Please go ahead.

Rohan Sundram

I just had the one question on the competitive wagering environment. No surprises to hear that but David I was just wondering if you have a view whether you're gaining share or maintaining share in this competitive environment. We're seeing one new entrant grow pretty strongly. Another seems to be struggling with a rebranding and migration, just wanted to get your thoughts on that.

David Attenborough

It's an interesting question because lot of the turnovers that we see in the NT are difficult to know whether they're local or actually reaching out to the Asian market because we know some of the operators there offer a wider sort of reach. So what we tend to do as a business is less focus on trying to understand the turnover market share but we focus very much on driving revenue growth, driving customer engagement. I believe that we are achieving what I would call the right growth in the right areas in a sustainable way.

It's very difficult for me to understand whether we have grown market share. I certainly see a lot of bookmakers out there struggling to make any money. I think we've seen Bet365 come with another big loss and I think Crown announced today a north of AUD20 million loss. It's a tough market for them out there and we intend to make sure that it's a highly competitive market.

Operator

Our next question comes from the line of Brian Han from Morningstar. The line is open, please go ahead.

Brian Han

For Keno it sounds like you're willing to sacrifice short-term margins to really drive that top line so my question is what do you think is the sustainable EBIT margin for that business?

Damien Johnston

What we've guided to in the presentation is that we are increasing our investment in Keno both in and across product, brand and also in capability. So you'd expect to see a level of expense growth that's similar to what we saw this year. In terms of revenue growth, the business has been relatively flat for the last couple of years and of course with those investments we're making, we're optimistic that we're going to see a return to top line growth.

So I think rather than predict about what the EBIT margins would look like, I think I'll say at this stage is we're looking forward to an FY '16 where these investments will start to deliver results and we'll see how the top line goes over the next 12 months and have a look at the results. But we're feeling pretty excited and optimistic about the prospects of that business looking into next year.

Operator

[Operator Instructions]. We have one question remaining in the queue from the line of David Rosenbloom with Perennial. The line is open, please go ahead.

David Rosenbloom

Just a quick question, you talk about ROIC of 14% for fiscal 2017. What was it this year?

Damien Johnston

David, it's 13.4% this year. That includes the one-off expenses that David's mentioned for the ACTTAB acquisition. If we just look at those it would be about 13.6% this year.

David Rosenbloom

So 13.6% and your definition of that is?

Damien Johnston

It's essentially net debt and shareholders' equity so it's like the assets that are employed in the business. So in other words, it's a measure that's not impacted by capital structure.

Operator

It appears there are no further questions at this time. Mr. Attenborough, please continue.

David Attenborough

Well thank you very much and I'll close the call off. Thank you.
