Opower's (OPWR) CEO Dan Yates on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 11, 2015 10:26 PM ET  |  About: OPOWER, Inc. (OPWR) by: SA Transcripts

Opower, Inc. (NYSE:OPWR)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 11, 2015 17:00 ET

Executives

Charlie Mayer - Director, Operations & IR

Dan Yates - CEO & Co-Founder

Thomas Kramer - CFO

Analysts

Richard Davis - Canaccord Genuity

Monika Garg - Pacific Crest Securities

Matt Pfau - William Blair & company

Stan Zlotsky - Morgan Stanley

Robert Breza - Wunderlich Securities

Operator

Good afternoon. I would like to welcome everyone to the Opower Second Quarter 2015 Earnings Call. [Operator Instructions]. I'll now turn the call over to Opower.

Charlie Mayer

Welcome to Opower's second quarter 2015 earnings call for the period ended June 30, 2015. I am Charlie Mayer, Director of Operations and Investor Relations. With us from management are Dan Yates, Chief Executive Officer and Cofounder and Thomas Kramer, Chief Financial Officer. We have issued the earnings press release which is hosted at Investor.Opower.com. This call is being recorded and will be posted on that website.

During the call, we will make forward-looking statements pursuant to the Safe Harbor provisions of the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995 regarding trends, strategies and anticipated performance of the Business. These forward-looking statements are based on management's current views and expectations as of today and are therefore subject to various risks and uncertainties. Actual results may differ materially. Please refer to the risk factors listed in our most recent 10-Q filed with the SEC on May 14, 2015.

During the call, may we may offer incremental metrics to provide greater insight into the dynamics of our Business. These details may be one-time in nature and we may or may not provide updates in future. We'll also refer to certain non-GAAP financial measures in an effort to provide additional information to investors. A reconciliation schedule of non-GAAP to GAAP measures is provided in our press release.

Now let me hand it over to our CEO, Dan Yates.

Dan Yates

Thanks, Charlie. It was another strong quarter for Opower. Q2 results beat our expectations for revenue and the bottom line. Revenue was $35.8 million, slightly ahead of our guidance and an increase of 15% over Q2 2014. Adjusted EBITDA loss for the quarter was $3.5 million, well ahead of our expectations. Our plan for 2015 is on track. We are deepening our strategic partnerships as we signed larger and longer contracts. For the first half of the year, our weighted average contract length moved above 36 months as we signed 11 utilities to new contracts of three or more years.

This compares favorably to 2014 when we signed 15 such contracts for the entire year. As a result, our backlog which includes all contracted revenue, whether billed or unbilled, now exceeds $340 million, up from $244 million at the end of 2014. As the leader in cloud software for the utility industry, we are working to digitize every aspect of the utility customer experience. Within the context of that larger focus, our three goals for 2015 remain clear, first, continue to build our core businesses of energy efficiency and customer care; second, deliver client success with industry-leading support and professional services; and third, complete the rollout of our software platform, Opower 6. We are making progress in all of these areas which we believe will support accelerating revenue growth in 2016 as we expand with existing clients, add new clients and realize the benefits of our technology investments.

I will begin our Q2 update with the announcement of a major new contract. Opower has signed a large U.S. utility to a contract worth more than $50 million over 6 years. This contract includes territory-wide deployment of our digital engagement solution plus a large energy efficiency program. We are not naming the client at their request.

The six-year contract term, compared to our historical weighted average of 26 months, is a clear signal of our successful transition from point solution to strategic platform vendor. We saw this dynamic in Q1 as we signed long contracts with PG&E and SMUD. We see it again in Q2 with this contract. Despite the large size of these deals, it is important to note that there is still plenty of room to expand with these clients. In each case there is significant potential for Behavioral Demand Response, Billing Suite, now renamed Bill Advisor and of course more efficiency.

I'm also excited to announce that we've signed a new international client with a deep implementation of Opower's digital engagement product. This contract is worth approximately $5 million over five years. This new client operates in a competitive retail environment. They engaged Opower to enhance their brand identity and to build a digital experience for their customers. Opower will deliver digital engagement and components of our Bill Advisor product. These tools will work together to engage customers as part of a comprehensive digital strategy.

This contract is a good example of how our Customer Care offerings can help us win business in competitive markets, especially outside the U.S., where efficiency may be less of a buying motivation. In Q2, we completed numerous energy efficiency expansions and renewals with Consumers Energy in Michigan, Columbia Gas of Ohio, New Jersey Natural Gas and EDF in France, among others. One expansion with Hawaii Energy is especially notable. Hawaii is a unique and challenging market. Massive residential rooftop solar penetration has challenged the state's energy sector and nearly all attention recently has been focused on this disruptive technology.

In that context, it might seem that new energy efficiency products would be a tough sell, but it's been the opposite. Hawaii Energy is eager to help energy consumers keep their bills down and is looking for ways to raise awareness while complying with mandated efficiency requirements. Under a new and expanded contract, more than 60% of Hawaii's households will get Opower efficiency and all 380,000 households will get Digital Engagement.

We also expanded or renewed several Demand Response clients. The largest of these is Baltimore Gas and Electric which has expanded our offering to serve all of its 1.1 million residential customers. BGE has enjoyed three successful peak events so far this summer. We launched Demand Response in two large existing clients, also and at Hydro Ottawa which we welcome as a new client.

Turning to our second goal, of client success with the industry-leading support of Professional Services, in Q2 we marked a client success milestone with E.ON UK, one of our strategic international clients. More than 1 million E.ON UK households, approximately 20% of their customers, signed up for E.ON's energy saving toolkit. The toolkit which is E.ON's branding for a set of features from Opower's Digital Engagement product, helps customers understand their usage and reduce consumption with personalized insights and tips. Reaching this level of penetration with opt-in interactive features may be an industry first and certainly has been well-received at E.ON.

Equally exciting, we are now serving E.ON with Professional Services building bespoke highly-valued features on the flexible web and outbound frameworks of Opower 6. In Q2, we sold and launched a set of digital communications designed to engage and retain E.ON customers as they move homes. Moving is the leading moment when E.ON loses customers. Opower's capabilities to inexpensively and rapidly develop custom segmented triggered data-rich communications and web interfaces met their requirements.

Which brings me to our third goal, completing the rollout of Opower 6. It is the leading customer engagement platform in the industry and the only one developed specifically for the needs of utilities and their customers. This platform enables rapid development of new products and expanded professional services for our clients. As a reminder, the key components of the platform are our big data analytics engine, our personalization layer with segmentation and targeting and our delivery engines for an outbound communications, web and mobile.

Last month we launched the latest version of our web and mobile delivery engine named NextWeb. This is a JavaScript framework that enables rapid development of web features backed by our data rich platform through standalone pages, embeddable widgets and APIs. Before NextWeb, our digital engagement offering was a set of webpages that utilities integrated into their website.

NextWeb turns that on its head. Now our digital engagement offering is an extensible platform in which utilities can choose to build their entire web experience weaving our powerful segmentation, targeting, data and analytics into every portion of their website. We've see the capabilities of NextWeb, combined with the flexibility of our Opower 6 platform, as a key selling point for Opower. This is especially true in markets that are not focused on efficiency.

Finally, as will be noted in an 8-K filed today, Harry Weller has decided to resign from our board. Harry has been a valued presence and trusted advisor to the Opower team. Harry is a general partner at venture capital firm NEA, Opower's largest shareholder. Jon Sakoda, also from NEA, remains on Opower's board and will take over Harry's responsibilities on the audit committee. Harry, thank you. We appreciate your many years of service to the company.

As I conclude, this was a strong quarter for Opower. Our newest large contract, combined with the steady drumbeat of expansions and renewals across our client portfolio, positions us to accelerate growth in 2016.

I'll turn it over now to our CFO, Thomas Kramer.

Thomas Kramer

Thank you Dan. Revenue for the second quarter was $35.8 million, of which 92% was recurring. International revenue was 8%, compared to 14% in Q2 2014. We ended Q2 with 588 employees globally. EBITDA loss of $3.5 million was well ahead of our guidance which was a loss between $5.4 million and $4.8 million. This demonstrates the operating leverage we are beginning to see in our Business. Our outperformance resulted from a combination of higher revenue and lower than anticipated expenses, approximately $600,000 of which we expect to incur in the back half of the year.

The large new contract Dan mentioned is an essential component of our reacceleration strategy. This contract was anticipated and is already reflected in our 2015 guidance and longer-term target for 2016. Dan talked about our NextWeb platform migration. While certainly exciting to our clients for its advanced technology, from my vantage point as CFO I am most excited by what NextWeb will do for Opower's top and bottom lines.

While NextWeb will not necessarily produce direct incremental revenue, it does support the topline by enabling our professional services work, bolstering our digital engagement offering and more deeply embedding Opower technology in our clients' businesses. On the bottom line, NextWeb introduces efficiencies across our technology stack. We expect increased operating leverage in 2016 as revenue ramps while R&D expense remains relatively constant. This is due to the increased productivity of our engineers building on top of this advanced platform.

Turning to our full P&L, I will discuss expenses on a non-GAAP basis. This excludes second quarter stock-based compensation expenses of $6 million. Non-GAAP cost of revenue for the second quarter was $13.4 million. Non-GAAP gross margin was 62.7%, compared to 67.2% for the prior-year period. Non-GAAP gross margin for our subscription business was 70.1%, compared to 73.1% for Q2 last year. Our target operating model indicates total gross margins in the high-60s to low-70s.

As discussed in the past, we expect gross margin to vary throughout 2015 and 2016 as we deploy large contracts. To give investors a better visibility on how services impact our business, we are now disaggregating gross margin for services and subscription businesses. We have published an historical breakout in an 8-K issued today.

Services in Q2 accounted for 10% of revenue. We expect services to account for no more than 15% of revenue over the next few years. It is important to note that gross margin for our services business can be highly variable on a quarterly basis. Non-GAAP operating expenses in the second quarter were $28.6 million, up 8% from the same period last year. Expenses were driven by headcounts. Non-GAAP operating loss in the second quarter was $6.1 million, compared to a loss of $5.4 million for the prior-year period. Adjusted EBITDA in the second quarter was a loss of $3.5 million, compared to a loss of $3.7 million for the prior-year period.

In Q2 we had $51.1 million of weighted average shares outstanding. Our second quarter non-GAAP net loss was $6 million or a loss of $0.12 per share. This compares to a non-GAAP net loss of $5.5 million or a loss of $0.12 per share in Q2 2014. As of June 30, cash and cash equivalents on the balance sheet was $120.5 million. Deferred revenue was $61.9 million, up $3.8 million sequentially from Q1, though not quite to the level we anticipated. This again is a result of individual contract billing schedules differing from the average based on client needs.

As we have discussed on previous calls, the idiosyncrasies of billing schedules on our large contracts makes deferred revenue a poor indicator of both future revenue and booking strength. In fact, as Dan mentioned, our backlog at midyear was over $340 million. This is a record high for Opower and a strong indicator of our success in the first half of 2015. As a reminder, backlog counts for all contracted but unrecognized revenue. Backlog increased substantially faster than our revenue growth rate. This sets a solid revenue foundation for 2016 and future years. As reminder, we will typically report this method on an annual basis.

For the third quarter, we anticipate revenue of $37.9 million to $38.5 million. At the midpoint, this will be 13% growth compared to Q3 2014. We anticipate Q3 adjusted EBITDA loss between $4.6 million and $4 million. For the third quarter, we anticipate non-GAAP net loss between $7.6 million and $7 million. That would be a loss between $0.15 and $0.13 per share based on an estimated 51.5 million shares outstanding. Our adjusted EBITDA guidance excludes approximately $7.2 million in stock-based compensation expenses and $2.9 million in depreciation and amortization.

Our revenue guidance for the full year remains unchanged with revenue expected between $144 million and $149 million. Our performance in the first half of the year increases our confidence that we can hit the middle of this range with some off-site. The middle of the range represents 14% growth compared to the full-year 2014. We expect to accelerate toward 20% growth in 2016 based on our client wins and strong pipeline. Robust deal activity and bookings in the first half of the year have tracked closely to our plan. This does not change our outlook for 2015 or 2016, but it does increase our confidence in hitting earlier stated guidance and longer-term growth rates.

We are improving our outlook on profitability for 2015 and now expect full-year adjusted EBITDA loss of between $20 million and $16 million. This excludes approximately $26.5 million of stock-based compensation expense and $11.4 million of depreciation and amortization. This results in a forecasted non-GAAP loss per share of $0.62 to $0.53 for the full-year, based on the expected 51.5 million weighted average shares outstanding. Looking ahead to 2016, we expect increased operating leverage due to our NextWeb platform migration, as revenue accelerates while R&D expense remains relatively constant. We remain on track to reach profitability in 2017. In summary, our progress to date gives us continued confidence that we will deliver on our 2015 guidance and accelerate toward 20% growth in 2016 and beyond.

We look forward to your questions.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

[Operator Instructions]. And your first question comes from the line of Richard Davis with Canaccord. Your line is open.

Richard Davis

Two quick questions. It's showing up in your numbers, but could you remind us again where you stand in terms of your view on sales productivity metrics? And then the second question would be, if you could talk about the potential impact, minor or not, with regard to Supreme Court case regarding FERC's 745 and beyond and things like that, because I know I will get questions on that. Thanks.

Dan Yates

On the sales productivity, we're feeling really good about new reps producing. As a matter of fact, that big deal that we announced in this quarter was led by a rep that we hired in the last 18 months and is doing outstanding work. We're seeing strong performance from both new and tenured reps. As a reminder, a lot of the reps that we hired were hired in late 2014. They are not yet fully productive and we will be seeing that productivity get to where we want it to be near the end of this year. And a lot of the reps, about half of the reps that we hired in our big surge last year, were hired overseas and there the quotas are lower because they are mostly selling new pilots into new countries. So we're feeling good about how things are going. And it's pretty much on track.

With respect to the FERC 745 Supreme Court ruling, I'll be honest with you, we haven't even been following it because it's not really impacting our business. The stuff that we sell, the demand response, is all through utilities and won't really be meaningfully touched by those decisions. Great, that's what I've been saying, so it's good to be reminded. Sure thing.

Operator

And our next question comes from the line of Monika Garg with Pacific Crest. Your line is open.

Monika Garg

Could you update us where are the end points for all the existing solutions?

Thomas Kramer

Right now, the number of endpoints per solution hasn't really changed from where it has been over the last quarter, given that a lot of the sales that we made have been into existing clients. However, in terms of endpoints, this is a number that we think fluctuates within a year and it's one that we update on the Q4 call which will be at the beginning of 2016. But to remind you all, the energy efficiency still has a 15 million plus endpoints and the digital engagement has more than 50 million end points with demand response and bill advice are having just over a million endpoints each.

Monika Garg

Can you talk about any big renewals coming this year? And how to think about any growth expected on those renewals?

Dan Yates

What was the last thing you said Monika? Any renewals expected this year and what?

Monika Garg

And should we think about 20% growth from these renewals?

Dan Yates

You mean, could we expect contract expansions of 20%?

Monika Garg

Yes.

Dan Yates

I would say each renewal is, when we are talking about big renewals there aren't that many of them and there is going to be a wide range. This big deal that we just announced, it's about 80% incremental and 20% renewal. On the other hand, the big deal we announced last quarter with PG&E was about the opposite, 80% renewal, 20% expansion. So it'll vary. When we look forward in the next 12 months, the biggest customer of ours that is up for renewal is Exelon and we are feeling in very good stead with that customer, excited to renew them and in all likelihood expand them.

Monika Garg

Just the last one for me. I think in April you had announced a partnership with Oracle. Could you maybe provide more details on the same? And are you working with any customer together with Oracle?

Dan Yates

The partnership is great. We have a number of shared customers and we're marketing together with them. We had a very successful co-marketing event in late Q2 where we were both able to generate a lot of new business opportunities and there was really positive response from the market in seeing the two companies together. I can personally speak to recently beginning to start exploring more deeply collaborations with Oracle at Exelon and I believe that it's happening with some of our other customers, as well.

Operator

And your next question comes from the line of Matt Pfau with William Blair. Your line is open.

Matt Pfau

First, I was wondering, Dan, if you could provide us an update on the demand response pilots that you have been running this summer, what you have seen from them so far?

Dan Yates

We've had an awesome BDR summer so far. We have 1.5 million homes on our behavioral demand response product this summer. Both were gas/electric, it was full territory wide, all 1.1 million households. And we've got, I believe, eight customers total now live on BDR which is a doubling from last year.

And it's going great. We have had nearly a dozen events across the various utilities, several of them have been on the same simultaneous, so on the same day, so we have put our system to the test and it's performing really well. And then the validated results come later in the summer. The most validated ones come through third-party validation, but we have actually built some automated analytics that are basically as good as that and they're showing results to be as strong as last summer where we really outstanding results in the 5% to 7% savings range. It is all systems go on BDR.

Matt Pfau

And wondering if you could comment a bit on the billing solution both from a pipeline perspective? And then Puget Sound was supposed to be the first customer rolled out on it. So where do they stand on their rollout?

Dan Yates

So everything is green on Billing Suite or Bill Advisor rather. We have a number of important launch dates with Puget Sound this quarter and everything is on track. That will include ebilled capabilities for them, as well as the customer service interface and that is all looking very good and the client is happy so far. And I am excited to see results start to roll. And the pipeline is also really good. We expect to see some deals close in the back half of this year. As a reminder, we launched this product at our customer conference in February and our typical deal cycle is about 9 months to 12 months sale cycle. Closing deal for the back half of the year is something that the earlier side of where we expected them to come in and they are coming in. So it is looking really good.

Matt Pfau

Got it. And then one last one for me. Maybe could you comment, if this Clean Power Plan were to get pushed through, what sort of potential impact would you foresee that having on your business?

Dan Yates

Yes, it's a very exciting development. The Clean Power Plan is going to be a long-term driver of our domestic energy efficiency business. As I'm sure you know, it sets CO2 pollution targets and it sets them for each state. And then states now, they get to decide how to meet these targets and thanks to some good work done by our regulatory team, our behavioral efficiency and behavioral demand response programs are explicitly considered; they are approved compliance resources. And as is the case with Opower, as always the case, they will be among the most cost-effective options.

It's going to be a huge expansion of clean power investment across the country. The estimate is something on the order of over 300 terawatt hours of energy savings by 2030 which to put into context is about 40 times the amount of savings we have generated since we founded the company, so it would be a huge market expansion. The one dampening fact is that it's not next year. State compliance is - full compliance is required in 2022 and some early incentives will be going live in 2020. So this thing we are going to see unfolding over the next couple years and we expect to be getting deals in that timeframe.

Operator

And your next question comes from the line of Stan Zlotsky with Morgan Stanley. Your line is open.

Stan Zlotsky

I wanted to dig into the backlog number for a second. It's great to hear that it is growing faster than revenue, but you also have lengthening of contract terms at the same time. If you were to adjust your bookings that are hitting your backlog number, on an annualized basis, to make it apples to apples, how is that number growing? Just to give us a qualitative or maybe quantitative number.

Thomas Kramer

We don't actually guide to that metric. The reason for the metric I used, the reason for us disclosing our backlog at this point, is because it was materially impacted by two recent very large deals and we wanted to give people more information around those.

Dan Yates

And I think the importance here Stan, there's two things to consider. One is contemplating our acceleration towards 20% growth next year. And the other one is reflecting on the fact that the biggest negative impact to our business in the last year was two international cancellations. And increasing our contract length with these deals as we are securing our largest customers, was not only good as we described expansion renewals, but now massively longer ones, obviously, is a clear vector for high renewal rate on a go-forward basis. What's exciting for us about this is that these deals are positioning us, continuing to solidify our confidence around this acceleration next year and creating a lot of leverage in our sales force who has spent a lot of time renewing customers this year to spend a lot less time in the next couple years.

Stan Zlotsky

And then just on the big deal that you guys signed, the $50 million deal, within that customer, how much more room is there for existing, for expansion of other Opower products?

Dan Yates

Actually there is a lot of room and it's one of the exciting things. This contract covers only one of multiple states that this customer operates in and there's still room to sell more efficiency within the state where we have done the expansion as well as in other states and there's a lot more room to sell customer care.

Stan Zlotsky

Okay and just a quick housekeeping question. What was the ending customer count?

Thomas Kramer

We provide the ending customer count on an annual basis. But what I will say that's relevant to this is we have added a bunch of new customers this quarter which we are excited about. We named a couple of them, Hydro Ottawa which incidentally is our first customers to be a new customer signing up on demand response. And another one that we were not able to name that we mentioned, another international customer. The other thing I'd say is, so far everything we are seeing in 2015 has us returning to our historical high levels of revenue retention which have been 95% plus.

Operator

[Operator Instructions]. And your next question comes from the line of Robert Breza with Wunderlich Securities. Your line is open.

Robert Breza

Maybe Dan, just quickly, as you step back and listening to the commentary on the call here tonight, obviously congratulations first on the large win, as well as the international win. When you step back and look at the pipeline and the sales productivity you are seeing and people you have hired, how should we think about the deal pipeline international versus U.S.? And where do you see the acceleration versus long-term growth?

Dan Yates

We are seeing good pipeline now building, as we have all year now, in both internationally and domestically. Our international business is hovering around 10% this year and-- we are not expecting it to eclipse domestic growth next year, we're seeing a lot of rapid uptake of these new products and continued expansions of existing products domestically. From a percent allocation basis, we are expecting a similar ratio, around 10% international next year. And then that, again, is driven by a combination of pipeline that is rich with all three, new sales, cross sells and expansions.

Operator

There are no further questions. I'll return the call now to the presenter.

Dan Yates

I thank you very much. I appreciate the questions, this was a great quarter for us. We are thrilled to be able to bring more good news about some of these big deals that we are rolling out and as we continue to cement our customer base. And we are looking forward to a positive back half of the year and a strong 2016. Thanks.

Thomas Kramer

Good night.

Operator

This concludes today's conference call. You may now disconnect.
