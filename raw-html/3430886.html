Noble Roman's (NROM) CEO Paul Mobley on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 11, 2015 10:22 PM ET  |  About: Noble Roman's, Inc. (NROM) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Brett Maas - Hayden IR

Paul Mobley - Executive Chairman, CFO

Analysts

Howard Halpern - Taglich Brothers

John Rolfe - Argand Capital

Harry Venezia - Healthcare Capital Advisors

Noble Roman's, Inc. (OTCQB:NROM) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 11, 1969 4:30 PM ET

Operator

Greetings, and welcome to the Noble Roman Second Quarter 2015 Financial Results. At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. A question-and-answer session will follow the formal presentation. [Operator Instructions] As a reminder, this conference is being recorded.

I would now like to turn the conference over to your host, Brett Maas with Hayden IR. Thank you, Mr. Maas, you may begin.

Brett Maas

Thank you and good day. The call today will be hosted by Mr. Paul Mobley, Executive Chairman. Following his discussions, there will be a formal Q&A session open to participants on the call.

Before we get started, I'm going to review the Safe Harbor statement. This conference call may contain forward-looking statements that are made pursuant to the Safe Harbor provisions of the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995.

Paul, the floor is yours.

Paul Mobley

Thanks, Brett. And thanks to all of you for joining us today for our earnings call. Our efforts to build a diversified platform for growth based on three primary venues is progressing well and I'm glad to discuss this progress with you today.

The progress we've seen to-date has given us the confidence to increase our marketing and sales effort with the goal of driving more rapid near term growth. At the end of the second quarter of 2015, the company previously announced that it was carrying out plans to position itself for much more rapid growth. Substantial progress has already been made on executing those plans.

Those plans were to hire a supermarket veteran as Vice President of Supermarket Development, Procurement and Distribution. Additional experience sales person and the engagement of well-established national franchise broker to accelerate our standalone Take-N-Bake venue.

We expect those additions plus the ongoing stocking of additional grocer distributors with Noble Roman's products and access to their many customers for future growth will potentially result in some very exciting and significant growth during the remainder of this year and beyond.

The hiring of the supermarket veteran and experienced sales person were both effective in early July. The engagement of the national franchise broker was effective in late July. These additions are being managed such, that our total expenses will remain relatively stable by eliminating other cost to pay for these additions.

Currently, we are interviewing applicants for additional sales person. We believe, the company has created a diversified platform for sustainable profitable growth to the future. During the second quarter and for the first six months, we saw a notable growth in the grocery store venue validating efforts in this area.

The second quarter, our non-traditional rebounded from the weak weather related performance in the first quarter. The non-traditional venue has proven very predictable overtime and we expect this to continue the rest of this year and beyond delivering a modest revenue growth for the year.

While we have achieved modest growth in recent times, the bad weather quarter aside. It has come largely from existing franchisees and licensees. We think there is a much more opportunity in the well proven venue and intent to focus more internal sales time on it. We are refreshing our look and tweaking our menu right now.

We think this segment has a lot of room for further development. This combined with the expected 15% growth in the standalone Take-N-Bake revenue. The expected 70% growth of the grocery Take-N-Bake venue, 25% growth in upfront fees and 10% growth in restaurant revenue is expected to deliver excellent overall revenue growth for the year of 21% to 22%.

These expectations are based on performance so far. Grocery store revenue growth from new distributions centers in place thus far and additional boost from additional sales capability put in place in the last 30 days to 45 days. We remain confident in all three targeted venues and believe we have created a highly diversified platform for sustainable and profitable growth.

Now for the financial highlights for the second quarter compared to second quarter 2014. Total revenue is $2.15 million compared to $2.09 million. Operating income was $918,000 compared to $855,000. We did have an adjustment for valuation of receivables including the Heyser case of $600,000 compared to none, although we currently believe all of our receivables are collectible.

Net income before taxes was 276 or $0.01 per share compared to $808,000 or $0.03 per share. The company will pay no income taxes approximately in the $22 million net income. Without the adjustment for valuation of receivables income taxes or net income before taxes would have been $876,000 or $0.04 per share.

Net income was $170,000 or $0.01 per share compared to 503 or $0.03 per share. Without adjustment for valuation of receivables net income would have been $540,000 or $0.03 per share. Operating marring was 42.8% of revenue compared to 40.9%, a year ago.

Upfront franchise fees and commissions were $37,000 compared to $116,000. Royalties in fees, less upfront fees were $2 million compared to $1.8 million. Royalties in fees from non-traditional franchises other than grocery stores were $1.25 million compared to $1.19 million.

Royalties in fees from grocery store Take-N-Bake locations were $487,000 compared to $373,000. Royalties in fees from standalone Take-N-Bake locations were $189,000 compared to $221,000. Royalties and fees from traditional locations were $68,000 compared to $74,000. In total, operating expenses plus interest were $1.27 million compared to $1.28 million a year ago.

Now I'll briefly discuss the financial highlights for the six months ended June 30, compared to six months ended June 30, 2014. Total revenue was $4.01 million compared to $4 million. Operating income was $1.58 million compared to $1.63 million. Adjustment for valuation receivables including the Heyser was $600,000. Net income before taxes was $894,000 or $0.04 per share compared to $1.5 million or $0.08 per share [ph].

The company will no income taxes on the next $22 million in income. Without the adjustment for valuation receivables, net income before taxes would have been $1.5 million or $0.07 per share. Net income was 550 or $0.03 per share compared to 942 or $0.05 per share without the adjustment for the receivable valuation. Net income would have been $920,000 or $0.05 per share.

Operating margin was 39.4% compared to 40.8%. Upfront franchise fees and commissions were 103 compared to 166. Royalties and fees less upfront fees were 3.7 million compared to 3.6 million. Royalties and fees from non-traditional franchises other than grocery stores were $2.3 million compared to $2.4 million.

Royalties and fees from grocery store Take-N-Bake locations were $880,000 compared to $717,000. Royalties and fees from standalone Take-N-Bake locations were $425,000 compared to $400,000. Royalties and fees from traditional locations were $134,000 compared to $146,000.

Total operating expenses plus interest were $2.52 million compared to $2.47 million. The company made the decision to create a valuation allowance of 600 against its receivables, even thought, we currently believe that all of our receivables are collectible.

Overall strategy remains to expand revenue from our three targeted growth venues. That being, non-traditional franchises licenses other than grocery stores. Grocery store, Take-N-Bake pizzas and standalone Take-N-Bake franchise locations. We anticipate growth in all three venues in future years, while maintaining relatively stable operating cost. As has been our history for the past several years.

To leverage the results of our research and development advances to add new products and new corporations methods with the goal of making Noble Roman's products more attractive in all three targeted venues. To aggressively communicate and market our advantages to our target markets.

As previously mentioned, at the beginning of third quarter consistent with the above strategy the company added Vice President of Supermarket Development, Procurement and Distribution and additional experience sales person and engaged a well-established national franchise broker. Plus continued with its ongoing stocking of additional grocery distributors with Noble Roman's products and gaining access to the remaining customers.

In addition, till we expect to hire another sales person by the end of August. These actions are very exciting and significant events and our plans to rapidly accelerate revenue growth. Our standalone Take-N-Bake venue is still relatively new and we're always testing new innovations to continually improve sales, reduce cost and make the operation even more appealing.

We have dramatically increased our efforts to add new locations to our standalone Take-N-Bake venue by the recent engagement of the respected national franchise broker. Further enhancements to this venue will be ongoing. Our marketing efforts to attack new franchisees will continue and will intensify. We will maintain the same advertising we have been doing and in leads generated there from will be referred to the franchise broker.

The franchise broker will be also be advertising for potential franchisees. In addition, we've entered into an agreement with Business Alliance Inc., who will be generating leads and referring them to our franchise broker for a follow-up.

Previously announced, new packaging for our grocery Take-N-Bake deli pizza program consist of bake-able treats in aluminum pans with a clear plastic top is proving to be a significant advantage. Consumers bake the pizzas in these pans that have anodized treatment on the bottom design to increase home baking performance making the pizzas crispy on the bottom, but soft and tender in the middle.

The clear plastic top allows entire pizza to be visible and appealing to the grocery shelves. The previously announced new products include gluten free crust, design to appeal to growing venue of the population in three varieties of 14-inch mega top pizzas design for a tremendous value appeal, which are called [indiscernible] pizza, extra cheese, four cheese, double top pepperoni.

These new products now account for approximately 40% of the sales in the grocery stores, where those products have been implemented. The primary growth in the non-traditional franchise license venue has been coming from existing franchisees and exhibiting in various industry trade shows including the National Association of Convenience Stores. Western Petroleum Marketers Association, International Association of Amusement Parks and Attractions. And select state convenience store association shows.

We believe, there is much more opportunity in this well proven venue and we intend to focus more of our internal sales time on it. We are refreshing our look and tweaking our menu right now. We believe this segment has a lot more room to develop.

The progress we've made to-date, and the interest we continue to see from all three targeted venues confirms that our strategy is working to grow revenue while maintaining relatively stable level of expenses, which we expect will result in a meaningful increase in our bottom line as we continue to add more locations.

We are able to maintain relatively stability in operating expenses even though, we have added the additional talent discussed previously. We were able to eliminate more than enough expenses to pay for the Vice President of Supermarket Development Procurement and Distribution. The franchise brokers entirely compensated with commissions to pay for this position, we eliminated fix salary plus commissions.

In addition, I'll add to, that we increased our franchise fee from that venue from $15,000 to $25,000. So after we pay the commission, we'll still have more than $15,000 left. Total operating expenses plus interest for the three month period ended June 30, 2015 were $1.27 million compared to $1.28 million for the comparable period last year.

Total operating expenses plus interest for the six months period ended June 30 were $2.52 million compared to $2.47 million for the comparable period last year. We plan to continue our prudent management of expenses consistent with pursuing a more rapid growth in revenue.

Behind the expansion of our footprint, an increased sales is a significant investment of time and effort in creating a competitive advantage through our products and our systems. The quality of our products created through a simple productions process and service system offered at a reasonable price point, is a strategic strength and key driver of our future growth potential.

Every ingredient and process has been designed to support our diverse marginalized menu offering and deliver superior results. Our ready to use form and Take-N-Bake concepts both deliver products that are great taste in quality consistent and easy to assemble. Our low food cost, minimal labor and speed from raw ingredients to prepared products sets up apart from our competition. Especially in non-traditional franchises and licenses.

We carefully select both our third party manufacturers and distributors for the contract production of our proprietary products and services and source with highly efficient suppliers who help to keep those cost low, compared to our competitors in the non-traditional venue that typically own, operate, distribute systems all within their own corporate structure.

Our leverage in the competitive advantages of our business model, we expect to continue to drive results in a key linchpin and our overall business strategy. Coupled with our strong product in system development, our marketing efforts to communicate those advantages and convey the high quality of our products to perspective franchisees and licensees.

We utilize several techniques to accomplish this and have found that conducting live demonstrations of our systems and products and selected trade shows and food shows across the country is an effective channel for demonstrating advantages that cannot otherwise be difficult potential prospects to visualize.

There is no substitute for tasting our products to truly understand the quality we deliver. We carefully select trade shows based on existing relationships, as well as the potential for fruitful lead generation based on our prior experience. These shows allow us the opportunity to demonstrate the superior quality and taste of our products to broad based prospects at one time.

Our execution is solidly aligned with our business strategy as we remain focused on growing the revenue, while maintaining nearly constant, total expenses to drive margin expansion. We will further refine and enhance our products, packaging and communications to marketing as we move through 2015 and beyond, but our mission and our focus remains the same.

Let me now briefly turn to the capital structure and balance sheet. Current assets totalled $4.8 million and current liabilities totalled $2.3 million as of June 30, compared to current assets of $4.4 million and current liabilities of $2.1 million as of December 31, 2014. Total bank debt was $2.6 million as of June 30 compared to $3.3 million as of December 31.

Total stockholders’ equity as of June 30 was $14.6 million compared to $13.8 million as of December 31. We are excited about and are expressing our expectations for the full year 2015 results, which includes modest growth in revenue from the non-traditional franchise and license other than grocery store and increase in revenue from grocery store Take-N-Bake of 70%, an increase of approximately 15% revenue from standalone Take-N-Bake. A 25% growth in upfront fees and a 10% growth in restaurant revenue.

The above growth rates compared to 2014 would indicate an overall growth in revenue in 2015 of 21% to 22%. We looked to the future, we believe that our largest driver of revenue growth for at least the next two years will be from the grocery Take-N-Bake because of the groundwork we've established in 2014 and during the first half of 2015.

This year, we have concentrated our focus on supermarkets because that is where we could accelerate growth the quickest. That is the venue, where much of the near term growth will come from. For mid and long-term growth, we thank the non-traditional and the standalone venues will play an increasingly larger role in the growth rate.

To summarize, the second quarter, the non-traditional not including grocery store revenues increased by approximately 5% over the same quarter last year. Grocery store Take-N-Bake revenue increased 30.4%. Revenues in the standalone Take-N-Bake was down by approximately 14% and all our total revenue increased by 3%.

Our total expenses plus interest were down slightly in the second quarter versus second quarter 2014.
