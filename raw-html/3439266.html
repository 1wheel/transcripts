Telkonet's (TKOI) CEO Jason Tienor on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Telkonet, Inc. (OTCQB:TKOI) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 13, 2015 4:30 PM ET

Operator

Good afternoon and welcome to Telkonet's Second Quarter Earnings Conference Call. As a reminder, today's conference is being recorded. Before I turn the call over to Jason Tienor, Telkonet's Chief Executive, I would like to read the following statement.

Further information on potential factors that could affect the Company’s financial results can be found in the Company’s registration statement and on its reports on Form 8-K filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission. Telkonet is under no obligation to update items discussed today to reflect subsequent developments.

Now I would like to turn the call over to Jason Tienor, Telkonet’s President and CEO to discuss the results. Mr. Tienor, you may begin.

Jason Tienor

Thank you, operator. And good afternoon everyone, and thank you for joining us. Today we reported record results on almost every major performance indicator from completing a single best revenue quarter in the Company’s history to reporting the second highest gross margin percentage at 60%, we’ve continued to demonstrate Telkonet’s potential for growth and profitability. In addition, EcoSmart sales grew 22% year-over-year, showing the continued potential and strength in innovative Internet of Things platform. While we continued to recognize the greatest strength of sales in the hospitality sector in the second quarter, we saw a record performance in our educational market with the best single quarter performance in the Company’s history and 67% growth over the same period prior year.

Much of this educational growth continues to benefit from the development of our channel strategy which also reported 64% growth over the prior year period. Our entry into the higher educational market exposes Telkonet to more than 4,700 schools within the U.S. With more than 20 million post-secondary students and over 5 billion square feet of floor space, the higher education sector in the United States spends an estimated $14 billion annually on energy costs. Thus, campus living has become a significant concentration for us and relationships such as that with Johnson Controls assist us in maximizing our growth in this market.

Q2’s solid performance is the result of the last few years’ focus on aligning our core strengths of networking, intelligent platforms, and smart devices to capture the significant opportunity in the developing smart grid and Internet of Things space. During this time, we’ve continued to create additional operational efficiencies and architect our organization to accelerate growth and innovation in our platform. Because of these investments, we’re seeing an increased momentum in our business, improvements in our operating results, and evidence that our strategies are working. As I mentioned, our results on both the combined and divisional basis were the best in our history and are setting Telkonet up for a terrific year ahead.

Looking at the year ahead, our priorities for fiscal 2015 continued to be maximizing our top line growth while maintaining profitability, continued expansion of our channel sales strategy, expanding our target markets into the fast growing international space, and positioning ourselves for the enormous growth of consumer markets. I’ll begin by reviewing how we’re performing against our first priority; maximize top line growth while maintaining profitability. Having demonstrated 84% sequential and 9% year-over-year revenue growth, we’ve shown through the second quarter performance that we’re able to rapidly ramp sales and deliver on our pipeline opportunity. Also while successfully ramping to record revenue and delivering a strong top line performance, we were able to report the best operating income and EBITDA in the Company’s history with each resulting in 100% and 79% year-over-year growth respectively while also resulting in record gross margin performance at 60%.

In terms of maintaining profitability, the second quarter’s performance has demonstrated Telkonet’s ability to efficiently deploy capital to meet our strategic growth initiatives and the successful seasonal ramp of our business overall. This attention to operations and understanding of our business, allows us to adjust to the macro trends of our target industries while appropriately forecasting inventory and SG&A requirements. Moving to priority two, continued expansion of our channel sales strategy, the second quarter represented the second highest revenue driven through channel sales in the Company’s history reaching $1.9 million or 40% of total sales and representing 64% growth over prior-year period.

Our continued growth and success in this area are driven by a number of factors, some are discussed before including segmenting our sales team into direct and channel account managers to better service and educate our channel partners, executing on the dedicated marketing calendar to provide news updates, webinars, partner training and certification meetings and more, and updated an improved educational portable for our partners offering training and continuous information sharing and education, invitations to and presentations at our yearly sales submit which recently took place in Milwaukee in July, dedicated personal time with our sales representatives and project development engineers to assist in closing sales opportunities.

A newly renovated product showcase in our Waukesha, Wisconsin headquarters to provide access to our partners and their customers in a full deployment environment. And lastly, shared marketing and trade representation to assist in market penetration and education. As you may have seen through some of the recent press, our channel focus and efforts are paying off as we continue to secure substantial channel agreements with influential partners in our industries. The publicized EcoSmart supply agreements filed earlier this year provides a strategic relationship with one of the largest HVAC providers in the hospitality space. Having combined revenue from sourced or unitary products or approximately $500 million and with the hospitality space purchasing roughly 400,000 units per year from a handful of vendors, our relationship with Trane provides an enormous opportunity for growth.

And since energy is the second highest operating cost component in the lodging industry, and 50% to 80% of these costs are related to HVAC system operation, our EcoSmart platform has demonstrated itself invaluable to Trane’s customers as well. In total, Trane employees more than 29,000 people at 104 manufacturing locations in 28 countries and has annual sales of more than $8 billion. We’re excited to continue to grow this mutually beneficially relationship and share future successes with you. Even more recently, you may have seen Samsung’s partnership announcement with Telkonet, this agreement has been long awaited since our development work with Samsung has taken well over a year. This relationship has two significant components, one a technology partner and the other a platform facilitator. With the release of Samsung’s HMS platform for hospitality, Telkonet is the only energy management platform integrated in North America through extensive product development completed alongside Samsung over the last year and half.

This means that we’ll have EcoSmart components deployed within the HMS platform for the only completely integrated entertainment and controls platform available in the hospitality market today. Secondly, Samsung chose Telkonet due to our national field service team, in-house project management, provisioning and deployment resources and our 24/7 support and monitoring center to the master solutions agent handling all deployments in North America as well. This not only means additional services revenue tied to [Technical Difficulty] deployment but also ongoing recurring revenue tied to licensing and support of the Samsung HSM and EcoCentral platforms. As Samsung is targeting to grow up 10,000 rooms annually for the HMS platform, we are very excited with the possibilities provided by this relationship.

Moving to the third priority for 2015, expansion globally and into consumer markets, these areas are a focus for Telkonet because of the size of both of these new markets are substantial. Starting with the global market, one of the fastest growing areas for hospitality and specifically for intelligent controls in hospitality is outside of the United States. Well, there are more than 50,000 hotels in the U.S. alone offering 5 million rooms; there are 187,000 hotels worldwide with 17.5 million rooms which more than triples our total addressable market or TAM. This is why we’re continuing the development of our worldwide sales channel to reach this untapped global market for growth. Successful international expansions into Brazil for example come as a result of strategic relationship with channel partners like Evolutix.

Together, we’ve been collaborating on the sale and installation of EcoSmart energy management technology in a number of hotel and hospitality properties including the marquee Hilton Barra project in Rio de Janeiro. We’re highly focused on growing these international channel relationships to monetize the global demand for our solutions and explosion of interest in the Internet of Things and intelligent automation. In addition to working with channel partners, we are also continuing to work directly with hospitality franchisors to reach and educate this audience. Success to date includes the establishment of relationships with franchisors such as Marriott and Starwood. We also recently signed an agreement with Hilton Hotels which operates a franchise of more than 4,100 hotels and 680,000 rooms across 91 countries, and we’re now an improved supplier for all Hilton branded properties.

These relationships are invaluable for putting us in front of qualified customers and educating hospitality owners. As it relates to expanding into the consumer market, continued product development and new product releases are essential to executing on this priority. This positions us for growth in our current domestic and growing international markets as well. New project releases in the quarter include the recent release of our EcoTouch wireless touch screen thermostat. This generated an enormous amount of interest at the recent HITEC hospitality industry trade show to the point that it was called the best device seen on the floor as well as highlighted in several post show editorials. With the continued development of our EcoSmart suite of products, our next releases are a next generation EcoSense wireless sensor, EcoContact wireless door and window contact, and finally and possibly most important our EcoConnect wireless gateway, which positions us well for the small retail and consumer markets.

In addition, our customer focus EcoMobile web application allowing device control from a user’s own mobile device definitely sets us apart from any other commercial IOT platform available to our target markets. These continued product and platform developments are not only extending our lead in innovation but also enabling us to fulfill demand for our broader market. Existing opportunities through partner relationships continue to pull our products to our development roadmap and enable us to meet the needs of our customer while maximizing the strength of our portfolio. Evidently, we’ve continued the successful execution of our strategy for 2015 with profitable top line growth, expansion in our partner channels and finally expansion of our target market into the rapidly growing international space and forthcoming consumer markets. Our current results and outlook point to a possible growth year for Telkonet and our current strategy and pipeline has generated enormous opportunity for the company moving forward.

One last item that should be mentioned is the completion of the annual shareholder meeting and the vote of approval of a potential reverse split of Telkonet stock. The vote was overwhelmingly passed and provides the Company the opportunity to move forward should the board choose to do so under the appropriate conditions. We truly appreciate everyone’s participation in the proxy and approval.

Now, I’d like to hand the call over to our CFO, Gene Mushrush for a review of the second quarter financials. Gene?

Gene Mushrush

Thank you, Jason. Ladies and gentlemen good afternoon and thank you for joining us. Today I will be summarizing our 2015 second quarter and year-to-date financial results. For the quarter ended June 30, 2015, Telkonet reported record revenues of $4.8 million, a 9% increase compared to $4.4 million for the same period prior year. Largely behind the 19% growth in Eco store activity, non-recurring revenues reached $3.7 million, a 9% increase compared to $3.4 million in the prior year period.

During the quarter, we began and/or completed a number of EcoSmart hospitality projects in Central and South America, a geographical focus for us since customers can maximize their return on investment due to climate and corresponding energy costs.

Recurring revenues consisting primarily of EcoCare and EcoStream support services grew 9% compared to the same period prior year while remaining at 21% of total revenues for the quarter. Gross profits for the quarter were $2.8 million, up 34% from $2.1 million last year. Gross margins were 60%, up 11% when compared to the same period prior year. In addition to increased revenues, lower support, inventory and installation costs assisted with the gross profit improvement.

Operating expenses for the quarter were $2.2 million compared to $1.8 million for the same period in 2014. The increases were attributable to a non-recurring accrual release, sales and marketing efforts, human capital investments and expenses related to the product launch of the EcoTouch Thermostat.

We recorded both operating and net incomes of approximately $590,000 and $525,000 respectively for the quarter ended June 30, 2015 compared to operating and net incomes of approximately $280,000 and $220,000 respectively at June 30, 2014.

Adjusted earnings, before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization or EBITDA, a non-GAAP measure improved to approximately $660,000 for the quarter ended June 30 compared to approximately $350,000 for the same period prior year. Total year-to-date revenues of $7.3 million represented a 5% increase compared to prior year. Similar to the aforementioned quarterly summary, EcoSmart revenues were the primary driver of these results. Recurring revenues accounted for approximately 28% of total revenues consistent with last year. EcoCare introduced in the latter half of 2013 continues to gain traction amongst our customers delivering approximately 225% growth year-over-year.

Gross profits for the year were $4.1 million, up 30% from $3.1 million last year. Gross margins were 56%, up 11% when compared to last year. In addition to the increased revenues, lower support, inventory and outside service costs assisted with the gross profit improvement.

Year-to-date operating expenses were $4.2 million compared to $3.6 million for the same period in 2014. Increases were attributable to non-recurring accrual release of the company expenses, sales and marketing efforts, human capital investments and expenses related to the product launch of the EcoTouch Thermostat.

We recorded both year-to-date operating and net losses of approximately $80,000 and $220,000 respectively compared to operating and net losses of approximately $440,000 and $560,000 respectively at June 30, 2014. Year-to-date EBITDA was approximately $60,000 compared to a negative $295,000 for the same period prior year.

We reported approximately $860,000 in cash and equivalents at June 30, 2015 compared to $1.1 million at this time last year; combining a light first quarter, a back-loaded second quarter and increased operating expenses, this decrease is reasonable. Cash used in operations during the first six months was approximately $510,000 compared to cash provided by operations of approximately $460,000 in the prior year period.

The significant rise in AR is purely timing related and given that our DSO was averaging 35 days, there is no cause for concern. We reported a working capital deficit measured as current assets plus current liabilities of approximately $485,000 at June 30, 2015, an improvement of approximately $640,000 when compared to a deficit of $1.1 million over the same six-month period last year.

In mid-November 2009, the company executed a Series A preferred stock transaction. If certain criteria existed as defined per the agreement, the holders have the option beginning of the fifth anniversary date and continuing for a period of 180 days thereafter to request in writing a redemption of all outstanding Series A shares. In turn, the company was then required to pay the holders $5,000 per share, plus any accrued, but unpaid dividends.

The redemption option expired in mid-May 2015 with no request for such redemption. As a result, $1.3 million was reclassified from temporary equity, which was defined as redeemable preferred stock to permanent equity in the company’s condensed consolidated balance sheet at June 30, 2015. The company will continue to accrue dividends at a rate of 8% until either the company declares and pays a dividend or the holder converts their preferred stock to common.

In conclusion, the second quarter was one for the record books as we established records in both revenues and EBITDA. Recalling our first quarter, it was a substantial rebound, the one that was anticipated when considering backlog and seasonal tailwinds. With a well-received showing at this year’s HiTec Show, the Samsung entering relationships and the launch of the EcoTouch Thermostat, we are positioned for short and long-term success.

Once again, thank you for your interest, and to our shareholders specifically, thank you for your continued support. I will now turn the call back to Telkonet's President and Chief Executive Officer, Jason Tienor.

Jason Tienor

Thank you, Gene. With that I'd like to hand the call over to our operator to answer any questions that you may have. Operator?

The second application is direct to our customers, allowing their engineers or housekeeping staff in order to using their own devices, see the occupancy of guest rooms, see where guest rooms need maintenance, see levels of savings and reporting services directly from their own handheld using the mobile application and the final application was an EcoMobile that we are working on and look to be releasing soon is a customer facing application that will allow our hotel guests use of their own device with our application to control all of the controls within a hospitality room from the thermostat to the wall outlet to the light switch and that’s a new application for every hotel, but the same application will be able to be used through any hotel where our products are deployed.

All four of those legs of the EcoSmart platform come together to create efficiencies such as reducing energy costs, reducing labor costs, reducing maintenance costs, increasing comfort for the hotel here as well as their guests, increasing amounts of the operation of a building and the analytics that are provided through it and increasing the productivity of staff as well.
