Hello everyone and thank you for joining us today to discuss our second quarter 2015 results. During the second quarter, we've made meaningful continued progress despite the difficult market conditions. Our revenue growth and RevPAR have been under pressure reflecting challenges in the surrounding environment.

However, we continue to take important steps to invest resources in areas what we see long term promise. A key sample of this relates to our digital capabilities. We continue to enhance our abilities in the area as evidenced by the fact that mobile app and WeChat user levels reached new highs. Also, our mid-scale business is continuing a strong trajectory and has been encouraging performance.

Let me highlight a few takeaways regarding to the overall performance during the quarter. Revenue was slightly lower than the previous provided guidance, reflecting what the entire lodging industry in China is going through.

Same hotel performance was negatively impacted by the difficult market conditions as we expected. However, we have achieved a good progress in building out our mid-scale offerings. Yitel hotels continue to perform well and the newly launched Homeinn Plus, as we see very positive initial market feedback.

Our membership net number is now standing at 38.6 million, almost double from a year ago. We continue to control cost and improve operating efficiencies in order to help us protect our margin and the bottom line.

Turning to the operations side, group occupancy rate decreased 3.4 percentage points for the second quarter from same period a year ago, while ADR decreased by 0.6%, resulting in a year-over-year decrease of 4.2% in RevPAR, mainly due to the ongoing difficult market conditions during the quarter. Sequentially, RevPAR increased by 13.3%, mainly due to seasonality. As a reminder, the first quarter is normally the lowest period in the year for both our companies and overall hotel industry in China.

This overall result indicates that our business remains under pressure as the external conditions continue to be quite challenging. Still, we are going to -- we are doing everything we can to effectively navigate through the tough climate.

As of June 30, 2015 there were 2,103 mature hotels, or hotels in operation for at least 18 months. These hotels' occupancy rate decreased year-over-year from 88.1% to 84.7% and ADR decreased slightly year-over-year from RMB164 to RMB163, resulting in a decrease in RevPAR by 4.8% from RMB145 to RMB138 during the quarter.

However, during these difficult times for the industry we are keen to further refine our product offerings and improve customer service through internal programs. Moreover, we continue with our cost efficiencies initiatives with our mature hotels base in order to ensure a thorough foundation for our further business development.

Turning to the franchise portion of our business, we have stable performance over the second quarter and we're focused on transforming our Company into a predominantly franchised-and-managed hotel business. As of end of the second quarter, franchised-and-managed hotels represented 66.7% of our total hotels in operation, up from 62.2% in the same period a year ago and 65.7% as of end of last quarter.

In the second quarter of 2015, we opened 126 new hotels and 14 new leased-and-operated hotels and 112 franchised-and-managed hotels. The addition of the varied pipeline for franchised-and-managed hotels [indiscernible].

As of the end of the second quarter, we had 349 hotel projects in the development pipeline, which included 184 hotels contracted or under construction and another 154 [ph] under due diligence. 92% of these projects are for franchise-and-managed hotels demonstrating continuous interest from the franchise partners. This continues to be a strong platform for further business development.

During the quarter, we used innovative approach to recruit new members through the Alipay, China's leading third-party online payment platform launched by Alibaba and other channels, besides cooperate with existing online platform partners, we collaborate with Alibaba's online travel units, by placing our first flagship store on it during the quarter.

As of June 30, our frequent guest program reached a record high of 38.6 million unique non-corporate members, almost double from 19.7 million at same period of last year. The unique non-corporate members under the program contribute more than 63% of room nights sold during the quarter.

Next, I'd like to give you an update of our progress of our digital capabilities and expanding mobile presence and capabilities. Since the launch of our mobile app in October 2013, more than 4.2 million users have downloaded the app as of June 30. This is significantly up from 3.3 million as of March 31 this year.

As a proportion of our total room booking, bookings over mobile apps account for 27% of total bookings in the second quarter. We have also seen exceptional progress on the WeChat platform.

During the quarter, WeChat binding increased 26.6% to 2.4 million. Bookings on WeChat accounted for 2% of total room bookings.

Along with the launching of Homeinn Plus, we initiated the smart hotels campaign to provide a smartphone connected appliance system in the guest room for our customers.

Environment friendly customers are rewarded with Homeinn points. We will continue to build out our digital capabilities in order to meet the increasing customer demand for online solutions and optimize customers' experience. More importantly, we consider these as essential to cultivating our future digital customer base.

Looking at ongoing expansion of our brand portfolio, we launched a new mid-scale brand Homeinn Plus in April this year as a complement to our existing brand portfolio and to better capture opportunities in the mid-scale market where we're seeing emerging growth.

As of the end of the second quarter, we opened a total of nine Homeinn Plus hotels, with a robust pipeline underway, we are fully committed to accelerating the development of Homeinn Plus and we are also keen to further develop and expand our Yitel brand.

We predict that this mid-scale segment will be an important part of our business in the future and are therefore putting in place a strong foundation now.

In conclusion, while overall external conditions remain challenging, and this continues to have a dampening effect on our results. We continue to develop product offering, optimize the customer experience and further expand and engage with our customer base.

As we look ahead into the second half of 2015, the external condition will remain quite challenging and we are not counting on a market rebound in the remainder of the year. We therefore will continue to focus on the area that will help position us well and keep our overall business healthy.

In particular, we will further diversify our product offerings, a key example of these continue to study the development of our mid-scale business through the Homeinn Plus in detail. We will develop unique way to engage customers via traditional and digital marketing channels.

Our success with the app so far is a great start and we will continue to build on this, and in the meantime, we will pay heightened attention to the cost control and drive further efficiencies in our operations. We believe this is the right strategy that will not only help us to weather this industry storm, but more importantly ensure that we will be well positioned to capture growth when the [indiscernible] falls back.

With respect to revenue outlook, we expect total revenues for the Company in the third quarter of 2015 to be in the range of RMB 1.83 billion to RMB1.8 billion.

For the full year, given the first half year performance and the ongoing softer-than-expected market conditions, the Company now expects total revenues for full year to be in the range of RMB6.55 billion to RMB6.65 billion, below the initial guidance we provided at the beginning of the year.
