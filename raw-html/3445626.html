Trevali Mining's (TREVF) CEO Mark Cruise on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 17, 2015 2:22 PM ET  |  About: Trevali Mining Corp. (TREVF) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Steve Stakiw - IR

Mark Cruise - CEO

Anna Ladd - CFO

Analysts

Peter Campbell - Mackie Research

Trevali Mining Corporation (OTCQX:TREVF) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 17, 2015 10:30 AM ET

Operator

Good morning, ladies and gentlemen, and welcome to the Trevali Mining Corporation Q2 Financial Results Conference Call. At this time, all the lines are in a listen-only mode. Following the presentation, we will conduct a question-and-answer session with instructions provided. I would like to remind everyone that this conference call is being recorded.

I would now like to turn the meeting over to Steve Stakiw, Vice President of Investor Relations and Corporate Communications. Please go ahead.

Steve Stakiw

Thank you, operator. Good morning everyone and welcome to Trevali Mining's Second Quarter 2015 financial results conference call. Trevali's Q2 financial results were issued on Friday and are available both on our website and on SEDAR.

Additionally, a corresponding news release was also issued with our financial results to review the main points of our second quarter performance, particularly ongoing operations and production at our Santander Zinc-Lead-Silver Mine in Peru. Our presenter today is Dr. Mark Cruise, Trevali's President and CEO. Accompanying Mark for the question and answer portion of this call is Anna Ladd, Trevali's Chief Financial Officer.

I will now turn the call over to Mark Cruise.

Mark Cruise

Thanks Steve and welcome everyone to Trevali's second quarter 2015 financial results conference call. Reading in review of our Q2 financial performance the company posted its EBITDA of 7.2 million for the second quarter and 5.4 million in operations income from our Santander mine, that’s close to over $30 million in concentrated sales revenue. Net income was 0.2 million for the quarter, really breaking it down at the detail just over 190,000 tons of mineralize materials processed through the Santander mill in Q2. Underground production for the quarter was approximately 191,000 tons and it was really sourced primarily from the Magistral North-Rosa and Magistral South zone.

During the second quarter Magistral central was in development cycle and scheduled production from that zone will start to increase and have more impact throughout the remainder of that 2015. Production results for the quarter were 13.7 million payable pounds of Zinc, 8.7 million payable pounds of Lead and 191,000 payable ounces of silver approximately. The mills does continue to perform above design recoveries, Q2 recoveries averaging 90% per zinc, 88% for lead and 78% for silver and we're using average head grades were little bit shy of 4.3% per zinc, 2.47% for lead and 1.85 ounces per tons silver. And that resulted in the production of 14,700 tons of zinc concentrate, averaging 50% zinc and a little bit over 7,000 tons of lead silver concentrate averaging 59% lead and approximately 39.5 ounces per ton silver. And sales during the quarter were approximately 13.2 million pounds of zinc and 8.7 million pounds of lead and 190,879 ounces of that silver. Average realized prices for the quarter were U.S. $0.95 per pound zinc, U.S. $0.82 per pound lead and U.S. $16.33 per ounces silver and cash cost broke down to approximately U.S. $45 per ton and that’s broadly in line with our guidance for 2015.

The Santander is currently in steady state at 2,000 ton-per-day nameplate production. And we do continue to tweak and try and improve operational efficiencies, and we did commence a pretty modest 6,000 meter predominantly underground drilled program during second quarter and that will ongoing throughout the remainder of the year. [indiscernible] to convert inferred tons to a higher confidence category to get them into our mine plan and also obviously contained in our results and hope this continues to expand the newly discovered Rosa and Fatima zone, and as we say them deeper as development progresses.

Really at this point in time our production guidance for 2015 remains unchanged at approximately 48 million pounds to 50 million pounds of payable zinc in cons grading 50% zinc, 23 million pounds to 25 million pounds of payable lead in cons grading 55% to 57% lead and similar between 850,000 ounces and 950,000 ounces of payable silver and reporting to the lead concentrates. Really our 2015 site cash cost and guidance were between $48 to $51 per ton milled and certainly we should be able to achieve that based on, we're half way through the year at this point in time.

Moving North to New Brunswick and commissioning of our 3,000 ton per day Caribou mill commenced during the quarter and progress data is in line with our expectations. And really based on our experience from our Santander operations we've adopted a phase commissioning plan and we have initially been using lower grade feed material and just as we familiarize ourselves with the both the grind and recovery characteristics and re-agents of the mineral feed. And all critical mill circuits, really in detail, Crushing, Gridding zinc-lead floatation filter and various labs and load out facilities have either been fully replaced, refurbished or repaired and are fully available.

And my primary focus really in the month of June was on the zinc circuit and followed in July by the lead circuit with the silver reporting to lead effectively, and really once we are on the kingside [ph] of zinc and lead circuits we'll focus on the copper circuits, in due course effectively later on this year. And we have been using quite a lot of external specialists and both from South African and in addition with our partners Glencore and they're actually providing support as well to try and get us up and running, hopefully a little bit faster, little bit sooner, but we'll see how that goes. And really we will continue to put out monthly updates on Caribou mine and mill as we continue to progress going forward. And so that's essentially where we are at this point in time. Thanks you.

And operator, and now I'd like to open the call up to questions, if there are any.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

Thank you. We will now take questions from the telephone lines. [Operator Instructions] And the first question is from Peter Campbell from Mackie Research. Please go ahead.

Peter Campbell

My questions this morning primarily relates to Caribou and I have just a couple them. One of them is that you reported that the mine is operating at approximately 1,500 tons per day which is adequate to maintain the stockpile, can we therefore infer that the mill is also operating at approximately 1,500?

Mark Cruise

Mark here, and absolutely, I mean I think the mill really in June and July, we've been kind of running on different grind characteristics, we’ve been ramping it up just try to stress the system effectively. But certainly in June, run rate in the mill in June varied anywhere from effectively 500 tons per day to 1,500 per ton per day. There really was very variable, just as we're getting used to the various feed. Certainly for July we're running between 1,500 to 2,000 tons per day and we'll continue to ramp that up in August. So certainly the concept is we don’t want to ramp it up too fast, until we get the recovery there where they need to be, because we're just putting equity [ph] cash into the tailings base at that point in time. So really underground we're just maintaining that 100,000 tons of course over feed pile and just by the crushers there and really there is no point in putting more on it, but actually you just have capacity at this point in time, so I think at the mill continues to ramp up that will eventually balance out towards the end of the year and everything will be in sync.

Peter Campbell

Okay, so then it's -- in terms of the ramp up to your nameplate capacity, then it seems that it's more about making careful progress at the mill instead of any short of bottlenecks that are developing with the Caribou [ph] mine, is that correct?

Mark Cruise

Absolutely and I mean that's all certainly internally, I mean, I think where we've been -- everyone’s well aware of the metallurgy at Caribou is challenging and its finer grain than certainly in our Peru operation. So that has been and remains the main focus. I mean, if anything underground is ahead of schedule and we do have three years of development in place and we’ve got close to 10 kilometers to 13 kilometers fully refurbished. And so really underground is there and it's just me -- it kind of makes sense to up that underground mining rate, and bringing in extra feed or what have you, just to get it in sync with the mill. So that mill has and continues to be the primary focus.

Peter Campbell

Okay very good and then my final question is that you've reported that the zinc concentrate that you've produced and have been able to sell so far has a grade of just over 50% in zinc, which seems quite good given the fact that you're using very low grade material to commission the mill. What are the expectations for concentrate grades once you've achieved the commercial production?

Mark Cruise

I mean we're pretty much in line, I mean, if you look the Bathurst camp as a whole, typically zinc cons grade anywhere from 50% to 55%, so Brunswick 12, I think was 53% to 55%. And based on what we're seeing, I think 53 is probably doable, so we're effectively there. And you're correct as you up the hedge grade should improve you recoveries and up that as well, but now zinc is there, I mean anything over 50 is definitely sellable, which has been the main focus primary for us is just to prove to ourselves we can produce sellable cons.

Peter Campbell

Sure and then for like lead, what would be your objective for like the lead con?

Mark Cruise

Lead for the Bathurst camp, I mean, it's not the highest quality lead con to be honest. I mean typically anything from kind of high 30s to kind of maybe mid 40s is standard for the camp and averaging I would say 40% to 43% is pretty typical. And I think we're at 39% and change at the moment, effectively 40%. So we're kind of there. So I would anticipate to mine our modest incremental improvement going forward, but really the main focus now again is that we have produced sellable cons is, we need to dial in those recoveries which is what the guys are focusing on now and trying to get them up to where they need to be.

Operator

Thank you. The next question is from Alex Terentiew from Raymond James. Please go ahead.

Alex Terentiew

Just similar the few questions on Caribou here, looks like the mine you guys have spent there a quite bit of your remaining CapEx at Caribou in the quarter, can you give us an estimate of how much CapEx we should expect to be spent at the mine in the second half of this year and a related question here your 2014 PEA call for a nearly 19 million in CapEx or development spending anyways for next year, is that still a number we should be relying on?

Anna Ladd

So, yes to your second part of the question, I think it's a good number for next year. Between now we ended the year, I mean we're pretty much kind of all the CapEx spend on the mill and such is behind us, so I'm going to guess roughly between 6 million to 9 million in that kind of mine development, but that could gear down a bit too because we’re actually ahead of schedule there. So that's roughly the number if I can give you off the top of my head.

Alex Terentiew

And then one more question, again Caribou and I know, you kind of touched on this little bit earlier, but I know the mine is still ramping up here, but can you give us an idea of what recoveries are and operating costs are for the mine so far? I know you said things are tracking in line with your expectations and mills is the focus and I head [ph] you and so forth, but I'm just trying to get a little bit of sense of -- should we still continue to use the PA numbers or how long do you think it may take to get those sort of numbers and just basically what you're seeing so far?

Mark Cruise

I think, like I said June and July have been focused on producing salable cons, so to be honest, recoveries have been all over the place and so, I don't want to be misleading, I think certainly we are comfortable with the PA recovery as they are, so that's about 84% to 85% for zinc and high 60's for lead and about 38% to 40% for silver. And we're comfortable with them and we see no reason why they can't be achieved by end of year and that's what we're focusing on. And of course, because we’re in the commission phase, we don't actually track it on cost per term basis, because -- we just don't track it at this point until we start hitting commercial production effectively. And Anna I don't know, if you want to say some words.

Anna Ladd

So, as we expect the cost to come in at around what we said in the PA, once we hit commercial production.

Operator

Thank you. The next question is from Derek Macpherson from M Partners. Please go ahead.

Derek Macpherson

Just a follow-up on the last question, is the -- just as far as you've mentioned $69 million in capital for the balance of the year at Caribou. What about working capital? I mean obviously we saw a little bit of a draw down or a negative adjustment working capital in the quarter. Do you expect a little bit more to come as you guys ramp up operations there?

Anna Ladd

We're just about there, Derek so probably we’re down to August, September, maybe another month or so and we are actually starting to ship and sell the concentrate, but a bit more in this month, so that should kind of balance out this quarter and into the Q4, nothing really which should be there.

Derek Macpherson

And then, on the -- and just following up on, you talked a little bit of Caribou commercial production when you guys get there, when do you guys -- based on the sort of progress to-date, when do you guys see that happening now?

Mark Cruise

It's certainly going to happen in Q4 of this year, I'm not going to be able pin a date down exactly, but based on what we're seeing now, we see no reason why we shouldn't be declaring commercial by end of year or prior to end of year. That's kind of where we are at this point in time and obviously, it's hard to pin it down, but I think -- we're pleased with the progress we've made on the zinc and lead in Q2 and now we're just really focusing on September, October, getting those recoveries where they need to be and that's pretty much the last missing piece at this point in time and probably more materially for us is the grind in the mill is where it needs to be and it's all about the grind at Caribou. So our SAG and Ball mills are consistently grinding in the kind of 30 to 40 micron range. And the IsaMills which we feel we need -- which is what we need for the -- sorry the lead recoveries are from 17 to 8 microns averaging 12 micron grind size. So certainly that for us internally has been a big breakthrough, that happened during the second quarter and we're very pleased with that and the team has done an excellent job and spent a lot of time focusing on that and so hopefully from hear on in it should be relatively straight forward and just get optimized in the reagent mix, so like I said, just to reiterate we don’t see any issues at this point in time and we should definitely be able to declare a commercial product till end of the year.

Derek Macpherson

And then just, I guess with the grind sizes coming in a little more consistently, than have you guys seen sort of recovery start to steadily improve then? A little less radical or a little more constant?

Mark Cruise

Yes.

Derek Macpherson

I mean, just shifting gears a bit on to Santander, obviously the current resource excludes sort of Rosa and Fatima zones, I mean you have that -- the drill program ongoing and when do you guys expect to update the Santander resource?

Mark Cruise

Yes, certainly the hope is to get it updated for end of year reporting and so it is something we're working on at this point in time and clearly, I mean to be upfront obviously the main focus is Caribou and but never the less that is going to show [ph] in a way in the background and have that two rigs turning at Santander, one on surface, one underground and like I said the hope is to kind of update everything for kind of end of year reporting schedule.

Derek Macpherson

And is that -- I mean obviously the -- this probably is going to focus with where Caribou has been, is that resource sort of the -- going to be the decision driver behind what you guys look at the expansion at Santander or not?

Mark Cruise

No, not at this phase, no absolutely not, I mean this is just with Santander maintenance ongoing I think, obviously contingent on results and we’d need a pretty hefty drill program in 2016. Obviously, that’s contingent and a lot of variables and including commodity prices and obviously Caribou as well. So certainly just so there is no confusion, this won't be a kind of go, no-go decision for any potential expansion at Santander.

Operator

The next question is from Stefan Ioannou from Haywood Securities. Please go ahead.

Stefan Ioannou

Maybe just following up a little bit on the CapEx spending. The 6 million to 9 million going forward -- looking at what you spent in Q2, the sort of $19 million. Can you give a breakdown on sort of what was spend at Caribou versus Santander?

Anna Ladd

Yes at Santander it was approximately 3 million and the balance was Caribou.

Stefan Ioannou

Okay so is it fair to say that Caribou is coming a little bit over budget now? With CapEx point of view.

Anna Ladd

I think it like I mentioned before, we’re ahead on underground, so we're going to be backing that up a little bit, probably changing the spending there for the second half of the year. We did not anticipated it in the PA, but what we have done is brought in quite a lot of experts due to the challenges with the mill. So that has added to our cost a little bit, but making progress anyway in getting us there soon than expected.

Stefan Ioannou

Great. And just as in the cash revision currently, is there much of any flow through dollars left in that current cash position at June 30 or is it all hard dollars?

Anna Ladd

There is probably a few million and we’re fine with spending that.

Stefan Ioannou

Okay and I guess would that be money that’s kind of earmarked then for Stratmat past mile stuff or?

Anna Ladd

Yes.

Stefan Ioannou

Okay what's the timing there in sort of in terms of getting an updated mine plan or idea around half mile Stratmat with or without a mill that a [indiscernible] deal or something.

Mark Cruise

Really we're working on it at the moment, so what we have done in second quarter, we obviously had the Stratmat resource estimate out -- update out there. And based on the recommendations we complete a couple of metallurgical drill test tools. I need to catch up the guys today but certainly -- they should be certainly at the lab at this point in time. So the guys will be doing some initial map work at Stratmat. Certainly Stratmat mineralization is materially courser than anything else in the Bathurst camp. So we'd anticipate positive results from that, but nevertheless we need to wait and see how that goes.

And we're currently putting in a few confirmation holds at half mile, we've got a ridge turning or we're dropping in two or three holes there at half mine. And at the base of that I mean I think the plan will be to have an updated PA out probably late Q4, but probably more realistically into 1Q one of 2016. But that is churning away in the background and we're working with [indiscernible] on those updates.

Stefan Ioannou

And in terms of just I guess talking about planning and stuff, in terms of the pace fill and stuff [ph] at Caribou. How is that going?

Mark Cruise

Certainly, we're looking at obviously various pace fills/engineering fills and work today seemed positive. And the guys are being kind of optimizing the potential mixes just to see where it's going and certainly the 7 and 90 days test results were positive and they're just waiting for the 180 days test results. And really from that we're assuming they’re positive or whatever the result would be that will kind of dictate which way it may make sense to go, whether it's a full paced or it's an engineered fill, where you bring some sand as well to use with it. And that’s kind of ongoing in the background and we just need to wait until the cylinders cure and the guys can do their strength testing on them.

Mark Turner

Sorry guys I tried to withdraw my question back as most of them are around CapEx and working capital at Caribou, had answered. May be just one question in terms of the throughput ramp up at Caribou. I guess the difference between sort of the 1,500 where you may be right now and 3,000 tons per day is still a big step up. What have you seen more recently that you get comfort that you'll be able to continue sort of that volume pull at the grind size that you've been looking for or that you've been achieving right now at the lower rate? Is there any sort of worry there or anything needs to happen to continue to sort of lock step, bring those up?

Mark Cruise

Mark here, no, I think certainly internally and I mean like I said in June and July, obviously we're I guess stress testing the mill and the circuits, so it has been variable throughput, but certainly we have it on short bursts, so in about a week period we have been able to take things up to 2,500-2,800 very easily. And so we don't see any issues the power-drill and the mills and the grinds were maintaining at that higher throughput rate, albeit for short bursts of time. So really then like I said the guys have just backed it up a bit just so we can just focus more on the recoveries.
