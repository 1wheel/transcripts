Actions Semiconductor's (ACTS) CEO Zhenyu Zhou on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 14, 2015 11:22 AM ET  |  About: Actions Semiconductor Co., Ltd. (ACTS) by: SA Transcripts

Actions Semiconductor Co., Ltd. (NASDAQ:ACTS)

Q2 2015 Results Earnings Conference Call

August 14, 2015, 08:00 AM ET

Executives

Elaine Ketchmere - IR

Zhenyu Zhou - CEO

Nigel Liu - CFO

Analysts

Operator

Good day, and welcome to the Actions Semiconductor Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call. Today's conference is being recorded.

Now at this time, I would turn the call over to your host Elaine Ketchmere. Please go ahead.

Elaine Ketchmere

Thank you, operator. Good morning, ladies and gentlemen, and good evening to those of you joining us from Asia. I'd like to welcome all of you to Actions Semiconductor’s earnings conference call for the second quarter of 2015. This call is being broadcast live over the web and could be accessed on the Investor Relations section of Actions website www.actions-semi.com for 90 days.

On the call are Dr. Zhenyu Zhou, Chief Executive Officer; and Nigel Liu, Chief Financial Officer. Before the market opened in the U.S. today, Actions issued a press release discussing the results for its second quarter ended June 30, 2015. The press release is accessible online and on the company's website or you can call Compass Investor Relations at 310-528-3031 and we will email you a copy.

We would like to remind you that during the course of this conference call Actions management team may make projections or other forward-looking statements regarding future events or the future financial performance of the company. We wish to caution you that such statements are simply estimates and actual results or event may differ materially. We refer you to the documents that Actions files from time to time with the SEC, specifically the company's most recently filed Forms F-1, 20-F and 6-K. These documents identify important factors that could cause the actual results to differ materially from those contained in our projections or forward-looking statements.

And now, I'd like to turn the call over to Dr. Zhenyu Zhou.

Zhenyu Zhou

Thank you for participating in Actions earning conference call. We appreciate your continued interest in the company. I will provide an overview of our performance in the second quarter of 2015, along with some operational updates. Later on the call, Nigel Liu, CFO, will discuss financial results for the quarter.

Quarter summary; revenue for the quarter was US$13.7 million, coming in at the high end of our expectations and up 14% from US$12 million in the second quarter of 2014.

Sales of multimedia products performed well, driven by strong demand for our Bluetooth 4.0 solutions for speakers and other Bluetooth enabled audio devices, as well as stronger than anticipated sales of our video boomboxes and other traditional multimedia products.

In our application processor business, conditions in the worldwide tablet market remained challenging, reflecting a mature market fraught with intense competition. Although consumer demand for smart connector device is still strong volumes of our 32-bit and 64-bit smart product SoCs based on 28-nanometer process technology have ramped more slowly than anticipated due to longer design and qualification times for some of these products.

Gross margin was 25.7%, up from 23.5% in the first quarter of 2015, and up significantly from 18.4% in the second quarter of 2014. In the second half of 2014, we wrote-down certain lower margin, slower moving inventory according to our accounting policies, we were able to sell a portion of this inventory and recover some of these expenses in the first half of 2015. We expect to sale through the majority of our existing inventory by year end.

Net loss attributable to Actions was $3.7 million or $0.063 per basic and diluted ADS, compared to net loss attributable to Actions of $3.5 million or $0.05 per basic and diluted shares in the second quarter of 2014.

Looking ahead, we continue to anticipate revenue growth in 2015 but at a slower pace than our previous expectations. Our Bluetooth solutions are rapidly gaining market shares and we are seeking – we are seeing healthy growth involved, led by our single chip 4.0 Bluetooth solutions for speakers and other Bluetooth enabled audio devices.

We remain confident that a strong consumer demand will drive adoption of our latest 32-bit and 64-bit chipsets based on 28-nanometer process technology, along with our open source SoCs and reference design platforms for smart devices.

Now I will provide some details about our multimedia business and application processor business. Multimedia business led by our Bluetooth 4.0 solutions for speakers and other Bluetooth audio and music products, our multimedia business continues to perform well, exceeding expectations for the third consecutive quarter.

We are steadily [ph] gaining market share in a fast growing Bluetooth audio and music market. In addition to single chip Bluetooths 4.0 SoC for speakers we are shipping Bluetooth 4.0 SoCs for some pats, LED lighting and car audio systems. We will further expand our footprint while we begin seeking Bluetooth 4.0 headsets solutions later this month.

Bluetooth technology in consumer electronics has rapidly evolved beyond the streaming audio, music and mobile phone accessories to become standard of technology in many of today's most popular consumer electronics devices.

By 2018, research from IHS projects, Bluetooth enabled consumer electronics shipment will reach 1.4 billion and more than half of all consumer electronic devices shipped to the Bluetooth enabled.

We believe our investment in Bluetooth audio and the video market positions us to capitalize on a much bigger and the emerging market opportunities for an Internet of Things, IoT, and a smart hardware.

Application processor business. During the second quarter of 2015 shipments from our application processor business performed below expectations, consumer demand for tablets, especially Wi-Fi only models is declining more rapidly than anticipated.

The worldwide tablet market experienced another year-over-year decline in shipments and the competition remains fierce. Our current expectation is that tablet SoCs which still account for a largest portion of our application processor business today will continue to face challenging conditions for the balance of this year.

We have been working aggressively to position our application processor products beyond tablet and into new and profitable growth markets, such as OTT set-boxes and other smart connected devices.

During the second quarter, growth in OTT market was hindered by additional policy changes from the PRC government, where as while we see some room for growth in OTT shipments in the second half of this year, the market is still evolving and is somewhat unpredictable.

We are opportunistic about our vast opportunity for other smart devices and smart hardware. We have compelling portfolios of leading edge products targeting these markets, including digital display signage, POS machines, netbooks based on popular OS, smart projectors, such as digital and virtual reality devices among others.

Early this year we shipped a small volumes of our edges [ph] smart products solutions based on 28-nanometer process technology, with a expectation of steep volume progressing [ph] in the second quarter. In reality, volumes were more slowly than anticipated due to longer design and qualification times for certain types of smart products.

Despite the slower than expected volume from that we remain confident that strong consumer demand will drive adoption of our solutions. In particular, we expect our 32-bit quad-core chipset targeting the low to mid tier of the application processor market and our ultra high performance 64-bit quad-core chipset targeting the high end of the market, most based on 28-nanometer technology to be major drivers in our application processor business going forward.

We believe the open source intelligence of our community will play a fundamental role in the development of the IoT market and smart hardware market. To that end, we introduced the ActDuino 32-bit S500 and the ActDuino 64-bit S900 open source SoCs and reference design platforms for Android and Linux.

We are also a founding member of Linaro’s 96Boards initiatives, and our Bubblegum-96 product [ph] it offers developers a very high performance and diversified development platform, as well as the best technology access for the latest 64-bit architecture. Our open platform for the products are generating strong interest from software developers and hard based [ph]

Business update summary. As we enter the second half of the year we continue to anticipate a revenue growth in 2015, supporting with our compelling portfolio of leading edge products targeting gross markets. The fast growth – fast growing Bluetooth and much larger the emerging IoT and smart hardware markets provide attractive opportunities for Actions.

Our Bluetooths 4.0 solutions led by our single chip solution for speakers and other Bluetooth audio and music products will be the driving force in our multimedia business. We expect the tablet market to remain challenging and anticipated limited growth emerging in our OTT set top box market.

We remain confident that strong consumer demand for smart products will drive adoption of our latest 32-bit and 64-bit chipsets based on 28-nanometer process technology along with our open source SoCs and reference design platforms. We will focus on cost control with a goal of reducing research and development expense from 2014s level.

Now I would like to turn the call over to Nigel Liu, our CFO who will review our financial results for the second quarter.

Nigel Liu

Thank you, Dr. Zhou. As a reminder, our financials are reported in accordance with U.S. GAAP. For the second quarter ended June 30, 2015, we recorded revenue of $13.7 million compared to $10.5 million in the first quarter of 2015, and $12 million for the second quarter of 2014.

Our gross margin for the second quarter was 35.7% compared to 23.5% for the prior quarter and 18.4% for the second quarter of 2014. Gross margin in the year record was impact by write-down of slower moving inventory which reduced gross margin by about 7%.

For the second quarter, total stock-based compensation expenses was 34,000 compared to 5,000 in the first quarter of 2015 and 5,000 in the second quarter of 2014. The year-over-year and quarter-over-quarter increases was due to stock options recorded [ph] in May related to employee stock ownership incentive plan of Actions Zhuhai Technology Corporation Limited.

R&D expense was $7.3 million for the second quarter compared to $6.8 million in the first quarter of 2015 and $6.6 in the second quarter of 2014. The year-over-year and quarter-over-quarter increases was mainly due to mask expenses related to our OTT set-top box and the tablet SoC as Zhenyu [ph] mentioned with a full year R&D expenses in 2015 to be below 2014 levels.

G&A expenses were $4.2 million in the second quarter compared to $2 million in the first quarter of 2015 and $2.8 million in the second quarter of 2014. The quarter-over-quarter increase was due to normal seasonal fluctuations.

The year-over-year decrease was related to a write-off of impaired intangible assets and an increase in consultant fees in the year ago period. Sales and marketing expenses was $0.6 million in the second quarter compared to $0.6 million in the first quarter of 2015 and $0.6 million in the second quarter of 2014.

Operating loss was $5.1 million for the second quarter compared to an operating loss of $7 million for the prior quarter and the operating loss of $7.7 million in the second quarter of 2014.

Dividend income was $0.5 million for the second quarter, compared to nil for the prior quarter and $0.4 million for the second quarter of 2014. The quarter-over-quarter increase was due to cash dividends we faced from a cost-method investee company.

Interest income was $1.7 million for the second quarter, compared to $1.6 million in the first quarter of 2015 and $2.8 million in the second quarter of 2014.

The year-over-year decrease was due to lower cash and interest bearing securities following the Dutch auction tender offer in September 2014.

Loss before tax was $3.1 million for the second quarter compared to a loss before tax of $5.5 million in the prior quarter and $4.6 million in the second quarter of 2014.

Income tax expenses was $0.5 million for the second quarter compared to an income tax credit of $0.5 million in the prior quarter and an income tax credit of $0.9 million in the second quarter of 2014.

Net loss attributable to Actions Semiconductor on a U.S. GAAP basis for the second quarter of 2015 was $3.7 million or $0.063 per basic and diluted ADS. This compares to a net loss of $5 million or $0.085 per basic and diluted ADS for the first quarter of 2015 and a net loss of $3.5 million or $0.05 of basic and diluted ADS for the second quarter of 2014.

Now moving to the balance sheet, cash and cash equivalents together with time deposits, trading securities and marketable securities totaled $155.7 million as of June 30, 2015 compared to $152.4 million as of March 31, 2015.

Of the $155.7 million total, $109 million was in cash with a short-term interest period investments together with time deposits that was generally issued by large domestic bank in China for terms no more than three months and it can be redeemed at any time.

$46.6 million was in trading securities and the marketable securities, which were principle of guarantees of pledged investment with high interest rate and the minimal returns of three months, less marketable securities while many issues manage or guaranteed by top ranking state owned financial institutions in China.

Our short-term borrowing totaled $45 million at the end of the second quarter unchanged from March 31, 2015. Accounts receivable was $4.5 million at the end of the second quarter of 2015 compared to $3.4 million at the end of the first quarter of 2015.

Accounts receivables includes amount due from a related target and equity method investees. Inventory was $50.6 billion at the end of the second quarter compared to $50 million at the end of the prior quarter.

We continue to buy back shares, spending approximately $0.9 million during the second quarter. As of June 30, 2015, the company had invest a total of $18.9 million in the program, representing the equivalent of 34.9 million ADS, including 35 million or the equivalent of 10 million ADS as part of our Dutch auction tender offer in September 2014.

And now, turning to our outlook. Based on current market trends, we expect revenue in the third quarter of 2015 to be in the range of $13 million to $14 million. We continue to anticipate revenue growth for the full year 2015, and a lower pace than previously expected due to declining demand in tablet market and longer than anticipated design and qualification times for SoCs targeting other types of smart devices and hardware.

Zhenyu Zhou

Thank you for joining us on today’s earnings call. As disclosed in our earnings press release issued earlier today, we will not be considered - conducting a question-and-answer session in today’s call. However, we continue to welcome written questions and comments from our shareholders. We appreciate your interest in the continued support of Actions.

Question-and-Answer Session

End of Q&A

Ladies and gentlemen, this does conclude your conference for today. We do thank you for your participation.
