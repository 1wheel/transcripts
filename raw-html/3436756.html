RMG Networks Holding's (RMGN) CEO Bob Michelson on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 12:05 PM ET  |  About: RMG Networks (RMGN) by: SA Transcripts

RMG Networks Holding Corporation (NASDAQ:RMGN)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015 9:00 AM ET

Executives

David Roberts - General Counsel

Bob Michelson - President and Chief Executive Officer

Loren Buck - Executive Vice President and Chief Operating Officer

Jana Bell - Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer

Analysts

William Gibson - ROTH Capital Partners

Operator

Good day, ladies and gentlemen, and welcome to RMG Networks’ Second Quarter Earnings Conference Call. At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. Later, we will conduct a question-and-answer session and instructions will follow at that time [Operator Instructions] As a reminder, this conference call is being recorded.

I would now like to introduce your host for today’s conference, Mr. David Roberts, General Counsel. Sir, you may begin.

David Roberts

Thank you, operator. Good morning, everyone, and thank you all for joining RMG Network's second quarter 2015 earnings conference call. Joining me on the call today are Bob Michelson, our Chief Executive Officer; Jana Bell, our Chief Financial Officer; and Loren Buck, our Chief Operating Officer.

RMG Networks issued its second quarter 2015 earnings press release today, which can be found on the company's Investor Relations pages at ir.rmgnetworks.com along with the slides accompanying the remarks during this call. These slides can also be found by registering through the web link. A PDF of these slides has also been made available.

Before we start, I would like to remind everyone that some of the statements that will be made today on the call will be forward-looking. These forward-looking statements are based on the company's current expectations and beliefs concerning the future developments and their potential effects on the company.

All forward-looking statements involve risks and uncertainties that could cause actual results or outcomes to differ materially. Such projections and statements of expectations should be interpreted in conjunction with the risk factors and other disclosures that may affect our results, all of which can be found on the company’s Form 10-K, Form 10-Qs, our Annual Report and other related SEC filings.

Let me also remind everyone that in light of SEC Regulation FD, we have a policy of providing certain financial guidance at each quarterly conference call, unless significant developments warrant a revision during a given quarter. Therefore, it is the general policy of management to refrain from specific earnings model guidance beyond the general guidance given on these conference calls or otherwise provided publicly.

During this conference call, we will also disclose non-GAAP financial measures, including adjusted revenue, adjusted gross margin, cash operating expenses, and adjusted EBITDA. The comparable GAAP financial information, including operating income, GAAP measurement most directly comparable to adjusted EBITDA and reconciliations are provided in the financial tables at the end of the second quarter earnings press release, which I previously referenced and which is available on the Investor Relations section of our website at www.rmgnetworks.com.

At this time, I would like to turn over the call to Bob Michelson, our Chief Executive Officer.

Bob Michelson

Thank you, David, and good morning, everyone. During the second quarter, we made significant progress on a number of initiatives that have advanced our strategy to reinvigorate growth and build towards achieving long-term sustainable profitability. In fact, the second quarter indicated the first tangible top-line results of this progress, and I am excited to discuss them with you.

But first, as this quarter marks my one year anniversary with RMG Networks, I would like to provide you with a recap of where we started and the progress we have made over the past year in executing several strategic initiatives that have laid a stable foundation for our future success.

When I joined RMG Networks in July 2014, I took the first 100 days to listen, learn and ultimately understand the inner workings of the company, which included identifying the key shortcomings affecting us. Following is a list of the five most acute challenges I identified.

One, a bloated cost structure with too many people on staff in a number of underperforming geographic locations. Two, a business segment, our media entity that was operating with thin gross margins and a number of significant long-term unprofitable contracts. Three, a sales organization in the U.S. with continuing underperformance, driven by poor leadership, desperate focus and an ineffective organization structure.

Four, a lack of new product innovation. The company had not innovated its core technologies new or launched any material new product offerings in a number of years. Five, financial dependence on one primary solution segment for our business contact centers, in spite of being the recognized leader in the industry, servicing approximately 70% of the Fortune 100 companies, this segment alone did not present an opportunity large enough to drive RMG’s long-term growth aspirations.

Now a year later, I want to share what we have accomplished to impact these shortcomings.

One, in regards to the bloated cost structure. We have reduced our headcount by 30%. In the first half of 2015, we've reduced our cash operating expenses by over $1 million in the same period last year with plans are underway to reduce it further. We achieved this by being disciplined and prudently taking costs out of all areas of the business. Several of the key areas included, reducing our headcount, reducing our annual marketing spend and taking costs out of underperforming geographical offices, such as India and China. Overall, the actions we have taken not only significantly reduce quarterly operating expenses across the entire organization, it has allowed us to free up resources for better strategic location.

Two, in regards to dealing with our underperforming Airline Media business. After several quarters of intense efforts to improve the gross margins and increase revenues of the Airline Media business, we determined there was not a rapidly foreseeable path to profitability. Accordingly, we made the decision to sell the business. We closed the sale of the Airline Media business on July 1 of this year. This action produced several tangible benefits. One, we eliminate material distraction to our leadership team, and two, we eliminated a drag on cash going out of the company with approximately $6 million in cash losses over the last four quarters attributed to the media business.

Three, in regards to an underperforming U.S. sales organization. Over the past year, we have reorganized our sales force, providing intense product and industry training to our sales teams, restructured our sales rep compensation program and formalized account planning and management processes. Further, we hired Chuck Corbin, as our new Head of North America sales during Q2.

Chuck has brought extensive sales and marketing expertise to RMG along with deep industry expertise. He has only been on board for 90 days, but I'm pleased with his contribution thus far. Chuck has already infused a greater sense of energy, focus, accountability and strategic account planning. Chuck is a terrific new hire. His efforts will produce tangible long-term dividends for RMG.

Four, in regards to a lack of new product innovation. As highlighted in each of my quarterly earnings calls to-date, my top priority was to restore passion and delivery of innovation. Innovation has the necessity in order to keep our solutions on a cutting edge and to expand the depth and breadth of our products and solutions we offer to clients. We have absolutely succeeded in this goal, not only did we launch and complete an initiative producing six new discrete products over six month period, but we also just recently launched the largest new software update that revolutionizes our core operating platform and represents one of the biggest advancements of our software in half a decade. Most importantly however is the fact that we have demonstrated to the industry and our clients that RMG is once again providing compelling reasons to engage or re-engage with the company. I will provide more details on our new core software release later in this discussion.

And five, in regards to financial dependence and one primary solution segment for our business, contact centers. In order to diversify ourselves financially, our next priority was to create a comprehensive technological and sales effort around select solution areas that we believe provide a significant opportunity and align well with our core competencies are providing comprehensive data intensive visualization. As a result, we have enhanced our innovative solutions within the supply chain, internal communications and retail banking areas, and have provided significant sales, marketing and product development to support these solution areas.

Behind these strategic operational initiatives, we have also raised significant capital and eliminated our senior debt facility, strengthening our balance sheet and putting us in a much more financial stable footing. All in all, the progress we have made over the past year has kept us busy and has continued to move us closer to our goal of delivering revenue growth and generating positive adjusted EBITDA over the intermediate and long-term.

The strategic progress we have delivered over the last year notwithstanding, we saw encouraging signs in the second quarter indicating some of the first elements of top-line financial progress toward our goal. Most notable was that the revenue recognized for product sales increased more than 32% on a sequential basis. This increase is a positive indication that our sales execution is beginning to improve.

Maintenance and content services increased almost 4% sequentially, representing the first sequential increase in maintenance revenue in the past two quarters. You may recall that we began experiencing a sequential decline in maintenance revenue last year due to our proactive decision in early 2014 to end-of-life maintenance services in certain products. The sequential increase this quarter is a positive indication that maintenance revenue is stabilizing.

Our professional service revenues were down at disappointing 42% in Q2. However much of the loss was attributable to normally productive Q1 that included a high level of third-party installation services.

Subsequent to quarter-end, we maintained momentum completing several additional key activities. First, we announced the signing of a multi-million dollar opportunity with a large international telecommunication provider, representing one of the largest customer wins in our history. The initial scope is in excess of $1 million, with $4 million of potential revenue upside from future orders under the agreement. We will be providing this customer’s retail locations with interactive and non-interactive video walls, LED displays, interactive Smart Screens that allow customers to learn about product and service options with multi-language support. This opportunity is a perfect example of the type of million-dollar deals we can continue to target.

As I discussed earlier, we also completed the strategic divestiture of the Airline Media Network business, subsequent to quarter-end on July 1. The divestiture of this business reduces cash burn and allows us to direct our full attention in resources to accelerating growth in our enterprise organization.

Also as I just mentioned, we completed the launch of our Six New Products in Six Months initiative, equipping our sales team with value-added solutions that have re-engaged customers and opened new doors for new opportunities.

Our innovation did not stop there. Last December, I challenged our development and technology teams to find ways to revolutionize our core operating platform, so we can provide users greater functionality at a lower cost and enhance the efficiency and usability of our products. I challenged our team to think outside of the box in a collaborative way and gave them a 12-month timeframe to develop and deliver what I hope would be the biggest advancement of our software in several years.

Our team rose to the challenge and delivered results beyond my expectations. In less than six months, they surveyed customer needs, designed and delivered software that includes eight significant advancements and over 100 new features that save company’s time and money with easy-to-use interfaces, enhanced security controls, a 500% increase in scalability, comprehensive monitoring, enriched visualization and the list goes on. This project represents the largest software upgrade our company has made in the last five years.

On July 15, many months earlier than the original plan, we formally launched this new platform to our customers with overwhelmingly positive feedback. Over 80 of our customers have asked us to provide - to formally provide a quote for RMG to provide an upgrade to the newly announced software platform.

As we now look forward, the strategic plans that we completed over the last year have set a strong foundation from which to advance our growth strategy and shift our core focus toward improving our sales execution. We will continue to advance a more focused solution area of sales strategy. Our overall focus will remain on four strategic enterprise areas, where we believe we hold a strong competitive advantage and see significant opportunities; contact center, supply chain, internal communication and retail banking, though we will continue to opportunistically pursue deals in other solution verticals including retail and hospitality.

In the contact center market, where we maintain a leading market position, we see a particularly strong opportunity to rollout our new 2015 software release, given our sizable existing customer base in this vertical. We have launched a comprehensive sales campaign and see a tremendous opportunity to upgrade our existing customers onto our new software and hardware solutions.

We will continue to focus on expanding our presence in our supply chain solution area. We have multiple subject matter experts that provide our sales teams with the knowledge and tools necessary to successfully penetrate this vertical. We are actively marketing in this space with webinars, blogs, tradeshow and strategic partner relationships. Additionally, we have installed several large pilots that are currently underway in Fortune 500 companies. These pilots allow us to demonstrate the value proposition of our solution to customers and represents significant potential future opportunities.

All in all, we are making significant progress in supply chain, in both, the United States and now throughout Europe, and we’re excited for the future opportunity with supply chain.

We will also continue to focus on our third solution area, internal communications. Our internal communication products allow us to target virtually any large company, including existing customers with contact center, supply chain or banking solutions. Similar to supply chain, we have been actively marketing our internal communication solution in trade shows, articles and with strategic partners.

We have a strong pipeline with internal communications and continue to increase the number of customer meetings and proposals on a year-over-year basis. We will also continue to focus on delivering a robust suite of solutions for retail banking industry. We are excited about the progress in this vertical and we have multiple large opportunities in the pipeline with the global financial service institutions.

An additional sales initiative we have begun focusing on, involves the expansion of our third-party sales channel in the United States to value-added alliance relationships. While today, we are mostly a direct sales organization in the United States, we have effectively used relationships - alliance relationships internationally for many years with great success. Internationally, the company drives 80% of its business through alliance partner channels, whereas in the United States, it is less than 10%.

I believe, indirect sales through alliance partners is an area that we can expand upon in United States as well to drive increased sales without the activation of a large increase in fixed sales costs. Executing greater sales efficiency and targeting larger deals will also continue to be a focus of our sales teams. The recently announced multi-million dollar agreement illustrates this shift in mindset we have been encouraging within our sales organization to larger solution sales with a strong value proposition for our customers.

In conclusion, I want to indicate at the end of my one year anniversary at RMG, we are on the exact glide path that I anticipated we would be on.

Our most recent quarter produced a sequential revenue growth, with expected continuing revenue growth for the second half of the year. We have generated great enthusiasm in our most recent release of RMG’s new software platform, producing a multi-million dollar pipeline in less than 30 day since release. We have launched with great receptivity expanded capabilities and marketing to deeply penetrate several new solution verticals, thus augmenting and diversifying from our already industry-leading contact center solutions.

We reinvigorated our sales organization with a terrific new SVP of sales. We disposed our media business, eliminating leadership distraction and any material cash burn. And finally, we cleaned up our balance sheet by converting $15 million in long-term debt into equity and raised $10 million of new cash proceeds.

We are on a plan to move toward long-term profitability. I am very pleased with this progress. However, make no mistake, we are not anywhere near satisfied with our results and growing our revenues while prudently managing our costs, will continue to be my top priority, as we continue to build toward long-term and sustainable profitability.

I'll now turn the call over to Jana for a review of our financials.

Jana Bell

Thank you, Bob, and good morning, everyone. I will be glad to walk through a review of the financial results for the quarter ending June 30, 2015.

First, I would like to note that the 2015 and 2014 results that we are discussing today are being presented on the basis of continuing operations. As you will see in our earnings press release that was issued this morning, the financial results from the Airline Media Networks business have been presented as discontinuing operations, consistent with the presentation in the first quarter, due to the completion of the sale of this business on July 1.

In addition, both the press release and my discussion regarding the results are being presented on a non-GAAP basis. The reason that we are discussing the results in this manner and providing these reconciliation and tables is to better reflect the underlying performance of our core enterprise continuing operations of the business, and as such, we have adjusted items such as the effective purchase accounting, the impact a of large non-reoccurring contract and certain other items as noted in the tables. You can certainly find our GAAP results in the earnings release we filed this morning, as well as in the 10-Q that we’ll be filing shortly.

For the second quarter of 2015, total adjusted revenues from continuing operations, which as a reminder reflect only our enterprise business unit, were $9.3 million, a slight increase from the first quarter of 2015.

Product sales revenue was $4.1 million, an increase of 31.5% from $3.1 million in the first quarter of 2015, driven largely by improved sales execution. Maintenance and content services revenue was $3.8 million, an increase of 3.9% from $3.6 million in the first quarter of 2015. This sequential increase is a positive indication that our maintenance revenue has stabilized from the significant slide we experienced last quarter as a result of the proactive decision we made in early 2014 to end-of-life maintenance services on certain products.

Professional services revenue was $1.5 million, a 41.6% decline from $2.6 million in the first quarter of 2015, resulting primarily from the completion of a large implementation in the first quarter of 2015. Additionally, given an increase in sales support and R&D activities related to larger jobs [ph] as we experienced lower realization rates in the quarter.

Adjusted gross margins from continuing operations was 55.6% in the second quarter of 2015, compared to 57.9% in the first quarter. The decline is primarily result of a shift in sales mix and lower professional services revenue in the quarter.

Adjusted EBITDA from continuing operations in the second quarter of 2015 was negative $2.4 million compared to negative $1.6 million in the first quarter of 2015. The sequential decline in adjusted EBITDA resulted largely from an increase in operating expenses during the quarter. The increase in operating expenses resulted from an increase in professional fees, such as legal, audit, tax and shareholder related expenses related to a number of complex non-reoccurring transactions taken place in the quarter, such as the divestiture of the Airline Media Network business and the conversion of our convertible preferred stock into common shares. Outside of the non-reoccurring events, we continue to be prudent in our decisions around operating expenses. Looking forward, we expect operating expenses to decline in the second half of 2015.

In our calculation of adjusted EBITDA, we exclude depreciation and amortization, stock-based compensation expense and other non-reoccurring charges. Please see the reconciliations of GAAP to non-GAAP results in the appendix of the earnings press release we filed this morning.

For the second quarter of 2015, our GAAP operating loss from continuing operations was $2.4 million, compared to a GAAP operating loss of $10.5 million in the first quarter of 2015.

Turning to our balance sheet and capital structure. RMG Networks ended the second quarter of 2015 with $7.8 million in cash and no debt for capital leases. The company's book value at June 30 was $11.9 million, compared to negative $10.6 million as of March 31, 2015.

Bob, I'll turn the call back over to you.

Bob Michelson

Thanks Jana. We've accomplished quite a bit over the last year. We identified the root causes that were challenging the company and implemented strategies to address these areas one by one. We brought innovation back into the core of the business, enhanced our suite of products and solutions, strengthened our executive management team, installed a new sales leadership team, strengthened our balance sheet and refocused our business on specific vertical markets where we see significant growth opportunities.

With a strong foundation put in place from the completion of these key plans, we are pivoting our focus to improving our sales effectiveness and driving top-line growth. Moreover, having executed on these key initiatives, we have set the stage for a diverse set of revenue generating opportunities globally and across several solution verticals.

As I have said before, we cannot guarantee that all these will be successful, but with several pathways to growth, we enhanced our ability of achieving it. We have a strong pipeline and as we execute on our sales enhancement programs, we are confident in our prospects and believe the effect our work will be evident in improving sequential financial results in the near-term.

I am pleased with how far we have come and encouraged by what lies ahead. I look forward to providing you with additional updates in the coming quarters.

With that, I now like to turn the call over to the operator for Q&A.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

Thank you. [Operator Instructions] Our first question comes from the line of William Gibson with ROTH Capital Partners. Your line is now open.

William Gibson

Good morning. I need a little help. I think you've been booking this - and it’s just an accounting [ph] issue here, loss on the long-term contract, and then there was a reversal this quarter. How should we look at this going forward? Is this something that runs out at the end of this year, does it go long for couple of more years? I apologize for not being to the footnotes to know the answer.

Jana Bell

Good morning. Yes, the long-term contract that we have, we have been making adjustments to that on a quarterly basis. So correctly in first quarter, we did incur a loss on a contract as we adjusted for the reduction in revenue that we expected on that contract. During the current quarter, you'll note that we recorded a gain on that contract, and that the company made a decision to reduce the expenditures associated with that contract over the remaining loss of that contract. So by reducing our projected expenses, we were able to reduce that liability booking that gain. Going forward, I would not expect us to have that volatility or adjustments associated with contract.

William Gibson

Okay, now I understand. So it has to do with reduction of the expenses. Secondly, when in your release on the telecommunications contract, the big award - and I believe I read where most of it would show was profit or gross profit, and I was wondering how that could be with all the video displays and LED displays and all of that. How does that flow through the income statement?

Jana Bell

That's a great question. We are very excited about that contract actually, but due to the way certain components in that contract are structured, we have determined that the appropriate GAAP treatment for certain aspect of the contract would need to be recorded on a net versus growth basis. So it's a pretty complex contract, but we have reviewed it, and so there is just specific elements that we'll need to take as a net revenue versus growth revenue.

William Gibson

Good. And one - this is just - this is more on the request category. Could you file the - and I know you are doing this on a quarterly basis, the third quarter numbers for last year adjusted for the sale? I know you’ve done it for year-end and the first two quarters. If there was a third quarter filing, I didn't see it?

Jana Bell

Sir, you're referring to the third quarter of the prior year?

William Gibson

Correct.

Jana Bell

Yes. So as we put together our third quarter comparisons, we will be putting that together. So we can think about getting that information out, but we will be doing that as part of our preparation for this next quarter.

William Gibson

Yes, I would ideally love to see it in advance of that. And then lastly, I think one of the most positive things I heard and it was on your new platform that you’ve already had over 80 customers ask for quotes on an upgrade. What do you think the sales cycle is on something like this?

Bob Michelson

The sales cycle is actually shorter than trying to engage a new customer. The benefits are pretty compelling. I think on a number of these things, we’ll see conversions in one to 3 months. Today, we actually have a webinar, and last I saw we have 106 people signed up for this webinar. I haven't seen anything, but there is much interest as quickly as this one has provided.

William Gibson

That’s certainly positive. Thanks a million.

Operator

[Operator Instructions] And I am showing no further questions at this time. I'd like to turn the call back to Bob Michelson for closing remarks.

Bob Michelson

Thank you everyone. We appreciate your time and look forward to giving you progress, if not before, at the end of Q3. Thank you.

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, thank you for participating in the today's conference. This does conclude the program, and you may all disconnect. Everyone have a wonderful day.
