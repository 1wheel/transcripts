Chorus Aviation's (CHRVF) CEO Joe Randell on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 12, 2015 2:33 PM ET  |  About: Chorus Aviation, Inc.B (CHRVF) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Nathalie Megann - Director, IR

Joe Randell - President and CEO

Rick Flynn - CFO

Jolene Mahody - COO of Jazz Aviation LP

Analysts

Cameron Doerksen - National Bank Financial

Walter Spracklin - RBC

Kevin Chiang - CIBC

Chorus Aviation, Inc.B (OTC:CHRVF) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 12, 2015 10:30 AM ET

Operator

Good morning. My name is Dan, and I will be your conference operator today. At this time, I would like to welcome everyone to the Chorus Aviation Inc. Second Quarter 2015 Earnings Analysts Conference Call. All lines have been placed on mute to prevent any background noise. After the speakers' remarks, there will be a question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions]

I'll now turn the call over to Nathalie Megann, Director of Investor Relations. Please go ahead.

Nathalie Megann

Thank you, Dan. Hello and thank you for joining us today for our second quarter 2015 conference call and audio webcast. With me today from Chorus are Joe Randell, President and Chief Executive Officer; and Rick Flynn, Chief Financial Officer. From Jazz, we have Jolene Mahody, Chief Operating Officer. We'll start by giving a brief overview of the results, and then go on to questions from the analyst community.

Because some of the discussion in this call may be forward-looking, I'd direct your attention to the caution regarding forward-looking statements and information, which is subject to various risks, uncertainties, and assumptions that are included on Pages 6 of our management discussion and analysis of the results and operations of Chorus Aviation Inc. for the period ended June 30, 2015, the Outlook section and other sections of our MD&A where such statements appear.

In addition, some of the following discussion involves certain non-GAAP measures including references to EBITDA, adjusted EBITDA, and adjusted net income. Please refer to Section 20 of our MD&A for a discussion relating to the use of such non-GAAP measures.

I'll now turn the call over to Joe Randell.

Joe Randell

Thank you, Nathalie, and good morning everyone. Thank you all for joining our second quarter earnings call. I know this is a very busy day for analysts.

We continue to build upon the major achievements we've made so far this year, and we are strengthening our organization further. We've made significant advancements to increase shareholder returns with the achievement of our new long-term commercial agreement with Air Canada, the acquisition of Voyageur Airways, the growth of [ph] our aircraft leasing portfolio, and our cost reduction efforts. Our actions are both strategic and opportunistic, and always with the best interest of our company and shareholders first and foremost.

As announced this morning, we are realigning our C-suite to provide a concentrated dedicated focus required to bolster Chorus' subsidiaries and to better support corporate priorities. These appointments are effective October 1st, and we will maintain the continuity of the Chorus senior executive team, and preserve our collective expertise and knowledge, and provide a seamless transition.

Rick has been appointed Executive Vice President and Chief Corporate Development Officer of Chorus, and is now charged with further diversifying our group of companies through strategic initiatives to increase our revenue base and profitability. Chorus' aircraft leasing arm will be a primary focus at the outset.

The timing for this focus couldn't be better, as we just welcome Steve Hannahs to our Board of Directors. Steve has been involved in aviation and aircraft finance and leasing for over 35 years. He has an impressive track record in aircraft leasing and an extensive network within the industry that will bring significant value to Chorus. We are extremely fortunate to have his high caliber of aviation expertise on our Board.

So this will be Rick's last analyst call as our CFO, but he won't be far away, and will be a great resource for our incoming Executive Vice President, and Chief Financial Officer, Julie Mahody. While Jolene has been responsible for the Jazz operations since 2009, a great portion of her career has involved finance and accounting. Jolene will be focused on working with capital markets and creating additional shareholder value from the Chorus group of companies. So, welcome Jolene, and I guess we'll be hearing from you next quarter.

Jolene Mahody

Thank you, Joe. And yes, I'm looking forward to my new role and to working with capital markets community.

Joe Randell

So Jolene has done a great job managing the Jazz operation. Jazz is the leader in the regional airline industry and has earned a solid reputation for safety, operational performance, and customer service.

I'm also very pleased that Colin Copp is taking up the challenge to make Jazz an even stronger airline as it's new President. Colin has been the Chief Administrative Officer of Jazz since 2009, and has 26 years experience in the industry. He has significant experience in both the operational and administrative side of the business, and has been a major contributor to the transformation of Jazz. His primary focus will be to continue to build upon the progress made in our relationship with Air Canada.

These executive appointments are another important step in becoming a more formidable competitor in the regional aviation sector. Congratulations to all of you.

We continue to make significant progress in a number of fronts in the second quarter; the implementation of our new Capacity Purchase Agreement or CPA with Air Canada is transitioning as planned and delivering financial results consistent with fast performance. We are also very pleased to reach a new collective agreement with Jazz dispatchers for a term of just under 11 years.

Our discussions with our flight attendants and maintenance employees continue, and we are confident that this will result in an outcome that is mutually beneficial, and will make Jazz even a stronger airline. The Jazz team delivered great operational results again last quarter, and captured just under 97% of the maximum available performance incentives under the CPA; great work to the Jazz team.

We are also very pleased to complete the acquisition of Voyageur Airways. Their operations contributed 2.3 million in adjusted EBITDA in the quarter, and we see much potential for future growth. Voyageur was recently the successful bidder for new and expanded air ambulance contract with a long-term customer, and they have many other irons in the fire.

So we are embarking on a new era. We are well positioned to take advantage of new opportunities as we work to increase shareholder returns. So, thank you for your time, and I look forward to speaking with you again soon and during the question period. And now I will turn the call over to Rick to take you through the second quarter results.

Rick Flynn

Thank you, Joe. I'm pleased with our financial performance last quarter, which delivered adjusted net earnings per basic share of $0.20. Here is the breakdown for the second quarter compared to the same period in 2014.

Operating revenue decreased [technical difficulty] by 17.8 million to just over 400 million. Flight revenue including charter revenue decreased by 8.8 million. Under the amended CPA, certain terms have been removed from flight revenue, which accounted for 26.5 million of the reduced revenue. Further more billable block hours accounted for an additional reduction of 1 million. These decreases were offset by a favorable U.S. dollar exchange rate that resulted in an 8.8 million increase in the quarter, and rate increases under the CPA accounted for an increase of approximately 1.7 million. Further, new flight revenue from Voyageur accounted for 8.2 million in the quarter.

Aircraft leasing revenue under the CPA increased by 1.8 million, supported by a favorable U.S. dollar exchange rate. As we modernize our fleet with the addition of more Q400 aircraft, this revenue stream will grow as the fleet transitions.

As Joe mentioned, incentive revenue was 5.6 million, an impressive 96.7% of the maximum available incentive. Pass-through revenue decreased by 12.3 million to a 134.3 million, which included a decrease of 24.8 million primarily related to decreased fuel costs.

Under the amended CPA, certain items are no longer billed, and this accounted for 1.9 million of the reduction. Also under the amended CPA, certain costs have been removed from controllable costs and reclassified as pass-through cost, which affected an increase in pass-through revenue of 12.3 million.

In addition, a favorable U.S. dollar exchange rate partially resulted in a 1.9 million increase. Other revenue increased by 3.5 million primarily related to increased sale of consignment inventory and new revenue from the Voyageur MRO operations.

Operating expenses decreased by 21.6 million to 362 million. The decrease was primarily driven by certain items no longer billed under the new CPA, and accounted for 15.6 million of the reduction.

Operating income was 38 million, an increase of 3.8 million over second quarter 2014. Operating income generated from flying operations and aviation industry services increased by 1.9 million, which includes 600,000 from Voyageur. The remaining net 1.3 million decrease in operating income under operations, excluding Voyageur, was mostly attributable to reduction employee separation programs, more labor maintenance cost being capitalized offset by increased stock-based compensation, increased professional fees related to the Voyageur acquisition, and the absence in this quarter of a compensating mark-up. Operating income driven by aircraft leasing operations under the CPA increased by 1.9 million, primarily driven by a favorable U.S. dollar exchange rate.

Second quarter adjusted EBITDA was 52.1 million, an increase of 1.4 million which resulted from the increase in operating income offset by a quarter-over-quarter net decrease of 2.4 million in depreciation and amortization. Adjusted net income was 23.8 million in the quarter or $0.20 per basic share compared to 22.2 million or $0.18 per basic share in 2014. As we work through the transition period, we will update our guidance. However, now we're able to provide the following.

There is no change to our previous guidance for 2015 billable block hours to range between 368,000 and 375,000 hours. Capital expenditures for 2015, excluding goals for the acquisition of aircraft and the extended service program or ESP under Dash 8-300s are expected to range between 33 million and 40 million. The increase in 2015 reflects higher anticipated major maintenance overhauls and the additional spend for Voyageur.

We are projecting expenditures for aircraft acquisition and the ESP program to range between 200 million and 204 million this year. Planned aircraft acquisitions include five Dash 8-100s, two Dash 8-300s, six new Q400s, and ongoing deposits for the Q400s and the ESPs. Two King Air 200s are also being acquired for the Voyageur air ambulance contract.

Approximately 170 million to a 173 million of the aircraft acquisitions relate to ongoing pre-delivery payments on all 13 Q400s, and the net cost at delivery on the six Q400s scheduled to be delivered before December 31, 2015.

Chorus has committed financing for approximately 146 million related to the 2015 deliveries. As of June 30 of this year, 15.5 million have been paid -- has been paid of the 27.5 million planned in pre-delivery payments for this year on Q400s.

Further, of the approximately 31 million to 33 million remaining in aircraft acquisitions and ESP pre-delivery deposits, Chorus has a non-binding indicative offer or long term financing on the aircraft from a financial institution for approximately 26 million.

Chorus plans to conclude these negotiations in the second half of 2015, and draw on this facility. For further information supporting our projected guidance for the balance of this year, I will refer you to section 8D in the outlook section of our MD&A for the second quarter.

That concludes my commentary. So with that, operator, we can open the call to questions from the analyst community, when you are ready.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

[Operator Instructions] Your first question is from Cameron Doerksen with National Bank Financial. Your line is now open.

Cameron Doerksen

Thanks, good morning.

Joe Randell

Good morning.

Rick Flynn

Good morning.

Cameron Doerksen

I guess I just want to get maybe a better handle on the growth for the Voyageur operation. Just looking at the aircraft acquisitions there, it sounds like there is actually quite a few aircraft coming into that fleet. Are these all for growth, or are they replacing aircrafts that are currently in the fleet, I just wondering if you can maybe talk about what's being added there, and what the growth prospects are for Voyageur?

Rick Flynn

Yes. Hi, Cameron, this is Rick. So with regard to I guess growth to some respect, the two King Air 200s are in support of a newer contract on the air ambulance side. And then with regard to the Dash 8-300s that we just recently acquired in the end of July, I think so subsequent event, well, in the financials. Those are currently, and will continue to operate in the Jazz charter fleet. We had an opportunity to buyout the aircraft. I mean the return is good. The aircrafts are very productive in terms of the charter operations.

So we will actually bring those into the fleet, and they will remain productive. But basically I guess what I am saying is we are harvesting the margin on the leasing side that existed previously with a third-party. And then when you look at the Dash 8-100s, those are coming over from Air Canada capital. Those will be some of the first aircraft to exit the CPA as we transition here. And, yes, they are actively being marketed in the Voyageur network. So we do see some real opportunities there.

Joe Randell

Yes. And just to add that Voyageur has several proposals in the market right now with respect to the potential use of these aircraft.

Cameron Doerksen

Okay. And maybe you can talk a bit, Rick, as I guess especially since you are moving more into this role about the growth of the third-party leasing business. How that's -- the infrastructure that you are creating for that is progressing? I'm just wondering if you could also maybe talk about what additional costs that might be incurred to support that business? I mean, are we going to require additional manpower, sales agents, and things like, that to support of that business?

Rick Flynn

Yes. We're currently in the middle of sizing and planning of all of that. But clearly as we go forward here over the next number of months we'll give you more color on that. Clearly, I'd like to leverage as best I can, what we already have in place. But I think it's fair to assume as well, we are going to bulk up in terms of horsepower and people, et cetera, as we move into space. That's a necessary requirement.

Joe Randell

The leasing business, of course unlike the airline business is not labor intensive. And is very efficient in terms of how you are able to deploy resources in that business.

Cameron Doerksen

Right. And have you studied that business a little bit more in recent months? I mean has your level of confidence grown in your ability to successfully grow the business?

Joe Randell

I think our level of confidence has grown significantly, but we do know that there is a requirement to add expertise in certain areas with respect to background, knowledge, et cetera. And we are in the process of looking at that in finalizing our plan with regard to how we grow further this part of our business.

Cameron Doerksen

Okay. That's actually all I had. Thanks very much.

Operator

[Operator Instructions] Your next question comes from the line of Walter Spracklin with RBC. Your line is now open.

Walter Spracklin

Thanks very much. Good morning everyone.

Joe Randell

Good morning.

Walter Spracklin

My first question I guess is on, Rick, your moved to that Chief Corporate Development Officer role. Obviously, you are lending some pretty interesting financial expertise to that presumably new role. I know you are focusing a lot on leasing part of the business, and expanding that. And obviously that's going to be a very important strategic element of the organization, that source of revenue. Just curious how much of your time will be on that? And are we to also infer based on the description that you provided of that new role that a meaningful portion of your time will be dedicated as well towards looking for new sources of revenue, and new acquisition opportunities?

And should we view this as something shorter term, in other words something could be executed sooner rather than later? Or is this something that is in the background, if an opportunity presents itself you will look at it; in that type of aspect? Or is it something more active that you are looking at in the near term, in terms of M&A?

Rick Flynn

So, all in incremental steps, Walter; so we just completed the Voyageur acquisition, so clearly in terms of some of the CapEx guidance we have given here you can see how we are going to leverage that up as we go forward. So there is also a key role in terms of leveraging up what we currently have as well. And clearly see the opportunities with Voyageur, especially on the front with the fleet transition that's spinning off with Jazz, and capitalizing on that through a vehicle like Voyageur. And even from the point of view an opportunity that came along with regard to the Dash 8-300s that currently reside in the Jazz charter operation under operating leases for the last number of years.

It's very easy to do the return on the investment. In particular, in terms of how those aircraft are being operated and making a return in charter operations, but then kind of parlaying your way into another level of margin generation, probably no different than when you look at the margin we make on the CPA than the margin we make on Q400s that are flowing through the CPA. So there is all those elements. But, yes, clearly -- and I think as well, looking at the board level and bringing on Mr. Hannahs. That certainly adds a whole new strategic element to the development and the execution of the plan beyond all of that, in terms of making sure, we are maximizing what we currently have as well.

Clearly, my group's role would be very much involved in the ongoing development of the ESP program on the 300s. Are there opportunities to leverage that into the 100 fleet, and really spin that out as well under long term leasing arrangements. And, yes, clearly out in the marketplace, looking for the opportunistic plays; we will be there.

Joe Randell

If I could just add to what Rick has said, Walter. As we've gotten our new CPA in place with Air Canada, as we now acquired Voyageur with a significant flying operation component, but also specialty MRO, et cetera, we have done a lot of work over the last few years in the regional aviation sector in totality, including things like the leasing side of business, the parts, the services side of the business, et cetera.

This is a very interesting niche for us where -- that is not very crowded at all from a worldwide perspective. And so we see a lot of opportunities here. And as Rick said, and I said in my comments, we're going to be strategic, but we will also be opportunistic as well. Because it's a very interesting business, and I think you can cover it fairly well. And we also have other things coming our way.

For instance, a very significant number of Dash 8-100s that are coming out of the CPA, and are freed up as assets for us now going forward. And we're going to see quite a number of those even come out as early as this fall, as we bring in the new Q400s. So we really see this as being the area of focus and growth, although we will continue to work closely with Air Canada, and also to grow the Voyager flying operation. And we believe that there will be opportunities there as well. So we'll be working on multi fronts here to grow and diversify the revenue base, which I think will bring value to shareholders.

And by transitioning this business from being a pure CPA type of business as well, I believe -- with Jolene, we'll be working in the markets so that people understand how our business is transforming, and how, in fact, it is going to be and becoming unique within the market.

Walter Spracklin

Okay, thank you very much. That makes sense. I guess my second question here is on your financing strategy. Rick, you mentioned that you are in negotiations right now with a third-party lender in terms of access to capital that way. How much do you think you have in terms of overall debt capacity to finance aircraft acquisition after which point you would have to look at other sources of capital?

Rick Flynn

As far as the covenants, and things like that that we currently have in terms of things, like the EDC financing, we have plenty of headroom there. We're probably only halfway down the dial on that one. And then clearly in terms of -- if the investment, and the level of EBITDA generation its making, if we can hold to a constant ratio I think we have a lot of runway here for sure.

And on the aircraft financing side, it's a very popular sector in the industry right now overall. I mean, these are well-secured assets. We know the assets very well. So we don't run into a problem in our discussions here around the financing, especially on the aircraft side. I'm pretty comfortable. Obviously we'll be working very closely as we bulk up here with opportunities. And we'll have a close finger on the pulse, so to speak.

Walter Spracklin

Okay, and just a final question here I guess, and partially for Jolene, and, Jolene, welcome to the call here, and you, Rick. But in terms of your guidance and disclosure, now that you are moving the organization, as just mentioned, away from a pure CPA, and one toward lease revenue generation, and Voyager. When can we expect, and in what way will we see your disclosure change? And how will you be providing guidance for those items going forward once you have those operations a more part of your ongoing reporting?

Joe Randell

Well, it all comes back to materiality, and how big each sector or segment is. Right now it's all relatively small. Clearly, in terms of giving you color on the leasing revenue that's flowing through the CPA, we will maintain that. And that, in fact, as we know, is scheduled to grow significantly over the next year or so with the addition of those 13 Q400s. So you'll have good color on that.

But, clearly, as we grow different segments in the business here, and they become material in and of their own right, then we will give you the proper guidance.

Walter Spracklin

Okay, great. Thank you very much.

Operator

Your next question comes from the line of Kevin Chiang with CIBC. Your line is now open.

Kevin Chiang

Hi, thanks for taking my question. Just a couple of quick ones from me; as you noted earlier, I think the Dash 8-100 that's coming under the CPA is going to be added to Voyageur. And I'm just wondering given the number of Dash 8-100s expected to exit the CPA -- is there a plan to have all of these, and turning to Voyageur, or could you -- I just see that, and turning to under another banner say, just trying to get a sense what your plans are there.

Joe Randell

Yes. No, we've established on the financing targeting, some of these initial Dash 8-100s for operations outside of North America with Voyageur. But as the other aircrafts that are coming out, we are evaluating various options with respect to those airplanes in terms of leasing to third parties and other opportunities. So no, the answer is that all of these airplanes are not slated go to the Voyageur.

Kevin Chiang

Okay. And when you think of maybe out in the medium to longer term, when you look at your leasing opportunity and the ability to grow that business, and maybe Rick is in position to answer this, now given his new role. When you think of the fleet types that you have today, are there ones that you would like to gain experience in that you think made for a better leasing operation or would you like to stick to the fleet types you have today?

Rick Flynn

I think overall I mean as a regional aircraft operator, regional aircraft captures -- it's a nice umbrella, if you will. Clearly to this point in time we've been I guess Bombardier-centric, but we certainly are open to looking at the regional aircraft space in its entirety, and again being opportunistic and strategic.

Joe Randell

Again, as I was saying earlier, diversifying our revenue base, but diversifying, for instance, leasing operation, the manufacturing base, but sticking to the regional air market is something that I think would strengthen us.

Kevin Chiang

That's it from me. Thank you for the comments.

Operator

[Operator Instructions] As there are no further questions on the line at this time, I will now turn the call back over to the presenters.

Nathalie Megann

Thank you, Dan, and thank you everyone for being with us on the call today, and we look forward to speaking with you again soon.

Operator

This concludes today's conference call. You may now disconnect.
