[Foreign Language]

Ladies and gentlemen, good morning and good evening everyone. I am Zhang Chuanwei, the CEO and the Chairman of the Board of China Ming Yang Wind Power Group. Welcome to join us in the earnings release of the second quarter 2015.

[Foreign Language]

First of all, I would like to talk about the macro environment and the development of wind power industry.

[Foreign Language]

The wind power industry in China maintained a fast growth in the first six months this year leading global wind power market. According to the data on grid connection published by National Energy Administration NEA for the first six months, the incremental grid connected capacity was 9.2 gigawatts with a year-on-year growth of 46%.

By the end of June, the accumulated grid connected capacity has amounted at 105.5 gigawatts with an increasing average hours of utilization compared to that of last year to further solve the problem of wind power utilization and consumption 18 ultra-high voltage transmission lines are to be built from 2016 to 2018 including the [indiscernible] to Shandong UHV line started from our new assembly facilities in Inner Mongolia.

[Foreign Language]

According to China Wind Energy Association, Ming Yang ranks the third among peers in terms of the incremental installed capacity for the first half of the year with a market share stood at 11%, which is 2% higher than the 9% of last year. If we talk about the WTG models 2 megawatts WTG instead of 1.5 megawatts WTGs became the mainstream model for installation for the first half of the year, a multi added capacity of 5.1 gigawatts, which is half of the total installed capacity. The fact that Ming Yang ranks number one in the incremental installed capacity of 2 megawatt WTG and above and takes 16.5% of the 2-megawatt segment shows our strong capabilities in technology and in strategic market positioning.

[Foreign Language]

We foresee two trends in the wind farm construction in come to use. One is to be built in a large-scale wind farms in areas which abundant wind and solar energy resources such as in Inner Mongolia, Xinjiang and transmit power via UHV transmission lines. The second trend is to build distributed wind farms in Middle China and South China where the wind speed is low, but the consumption is high. We fully leveraged our unique strength in cooperating with local governments, capturing the rights to develop local wind resources and building our own assembly facilities.

To better cater to the areas which low wind speed will produce the low wind speed 2.0 megawatts 118 meter WTG and the 3 megawatts WTG with three-blades which creates more value for wind farm owner and deliver a much better return on investment.

[Foreign Language]

Next, I will to take about Ming Yang progress in our business development for the first half of this year.

[Foreign Language]

In terms of new products in the first half of this year, we launched two world leading WTGs. The first one is a new generation of the 2.0 megawatts and 118 meters ultra-low wings speed WTG, this product is already connected to great as started to generate power in May this year. This product has 118 meters of blades, which is the largest blade with such a capacity. [Indiscernible] in five to 6.2 meters per second actual low wind speed areas and few areas with low wind speed in Middle China and South China including [indiscernible] and Shandong provinces.

Comparing to the previous generation this new generation of products generates 10% to 20% more power therefore it is well received by the market. By the end of July we have received orders amounting at more 150 units, 2.0 megawatts WTGs it’s now the main stream product in the market, which echoes the fact that we Ming Yang keeps leading the market with our cutting edge technologies.

[Foreign Language]

This may another new product of ours MY SCD 3.0-120 was launched, this WTG is for areas with low and mid level wind speed and it runs at the largest power onshore globally. This product is especially designed for large onshore wind farms, it is small, light, compact and of higher utilization hours, with the smaller cost of transport and installation this product helps to reduce the owner investment and maintenance cost.

This new SCD 3 megawatt WTG with three-blade is based on well developed transmission chain, control technology and good connection technology, SCD is regarded as more power generation, higher reliability and low cost per kilowatt hour. SCD is bound to be the main strength of WTG for onshore wind farms. In fact we signed a contract with the first wind farm equipped with [550] (ph) megawatt of WTGs which is to start operation by the end of this year in [indiscernible] inner Mongolia.

[Foreign Language]

In terms of our business expansion in west China, I am very glad to announce that we officially have decided to launch our assembly facility construction in Inner Mongolia. We signed a strategic cooperation agreement with [indiscernible] in Inner Mongolia, Ming Yang will invest RMB200 million to build a SCD 3.0 megawatts three blades WTG assembly facility there. The total exploitable wind resources in Inner Mongolia is estimated to be around 380 gigawatts accounting for 60% to 70% of all onshore wind resources in China.

In addition, our facility is exactly located at the start of the [indiscernible] to Shandong UHV transmission line, which is recently approved by the central government. Therefore, power transmission will not be a problem in the future. We already started the construction in July and is expected to start operation in May next year.

Going forward, based on the Inner Mongolia facility, we will give our strength in resources of full play and achieve a coordinated growth along the value chain. Based on our strategic, cooperation with local government we will grow our capacity and apply more valuable resources effectively. It is estimated that we will lock 2 gigawatts wind resources in the coming three years for development.

At the same time, our [indiscernible] facility in Xinjiang province has been growing really fast which enables us to lock 1.5 gigawatts wind resources in [indiscernible] provinces. In the future, we are planning to replicate this model to Qinghai province for more wind resources for innovation and downstream development.

[Foreign Language]

Thinking of downstream businesses in the first half of this year, we accelerated our investment in the development and the construction of our own in-house wind farms. Ming Yang has a total reserve of 2 gigawatts of quality wind resources, among them 230 megawatts is under constructions covering areas such as [indiscernible] a 50 megawatt wind power generation project in Qinghai will be our first downstream project that get connected to the power grid, following which more project had to come [indiscernible] provinces.

In the second half of this year, 100 megawatts of projects will start construction. In terms of project returns, we estimate that the IRRs are beyond 12%. Some projects will be even higher than 15%, which is higher than that of existing wind farm operators, for this downstream projects we are very selective and benefits oriented.

With the benefit oriented the decision making philosophy we will invest in areas and projects with quality resources high on concrete terse better great infrastructure and construction commissions, lower wind curtailments and the more favorable investment policies. We are also innovating to financing, the construction and operation models to deliver both high quality and high returns, which we believe our stronger than our peers.

[Foreign Language]

In terms of overseas business expansion thanks to the One Belt, One Road policy of advocated by the central government Ming Yang made a quite a lot residues [ph] in oversea orders as well. First, our GWPL India is constructing the first wind farm, which will be Ming Yang showcase in India. Ming Yang will supply this HV with WTG of 10.5 megawatts.

In addition, we are providing WTGs for 49.5 megawatt of wind farm in South Pakistan, besides Ming Yang supply equipments this project is developed by a state owned company and financed via state owned banks, which is the showcase model in developing wind resources under One Belt, One Road policy with our customize products we often strike more oversea orders. We are confident that oversea order will take a 5% to 20% in our total orders.

[Foreign Language]

Ming Yang Big Data center was established this month and started operation the newly developed headquarter is centralized control system is capable to collect and transit data covering 500,000 data points and 5,000 WTG units globally.

In addition to upgrade and manage WTGs this system is also able to generate a large quantity of operational data by leveraging new technologies in Big Data and Cloud computing we are able to identify those valuable data which help us to increase WTG reliability and performance and reduce maintenance cost and cost per kilowatt hour through data mining.

[Foreign Language]

Next, I would like to talk about the REnergy as well, REnergy is the largest supplier of three core WTG components and REnergy’s products are famous for their high reliability and stability. Ming Yang acquired 99% of REnergy’s equities in May 20 at RMB925 million this year. We are witnessing more synergies already it is estimated that the reported net profit of REnergy will be more than RMB90 million by the end of 2015 and starting from Q1, 2016 REnergy will contribute approximately 3% margins to Ming Yang Group and we will see increasing net profit as well.

[Foreign Language]

Last but not least I would like to talk about the orders for the first half of the year driven by a more favorable regulatory environment and a better market environment the newly signed order in Q2 was 647.5 gigawatts contributing to a total order backlog of 3.7 gigawatts by the end of Q2. We have additional 3.5 gigawatt orders that won the bidding by haven’t signed a contract yet. So our order backlog is looking pretty good now.

[Foreign Language]

For the first half of the year, the share of 1.5 megawatt WTG in bidding is around 15% while that off 2.0 megawatt WTG is 79% and that of WTG more than 2.0 megawatt is 6%, the orders are located mainly in Inner Mongolia, Guangdong, Shanxi and the [indiscernible] provinces in recent months Ming Yang’s bidding price is around RMB4100 per kilowatt, which shows a steady growth comparing to the price of our peers although the bidding price of our major model MY 2.0 180 meters is in the middle level among our peers, the power output is more than its counterpart product, therefore this model is preferred by the market and we have won 60 megawatt orders already.

For the coming six months, we expect the market demand to keep growing and the total bidding volume across the country will exceed 20 gigawatts.

[Foreign Language]

Next, I would like to leave the floor to our CFO, Ricky Ng to talk about the financial performance of the second quarter 2015.

Ricky Ng

Okay. Thank you, Chairman and good morning and good evening, ladies and gentlemen, this is Ricky and I’m the CFO and I’m glad to share to you the performance of our company for the second quarter.

The revenue in the second quarter of 2015 was RMB1.563 million, which increased by 67% compared with same period last year. The increase was due to a 55% increase a number of [indiscernible] commissioned during the period, which is measured by the power output and actually the selling price increased by 5% on an average basis.

In terms of sales proportion 62% sales goes to the 2.0 megawatt wind turbine and the rest goes to 1.5 megawatt wind turbine. We also see a significant increase in gross profit, gross profit was RMB245.9 million in this quarter, which increased by 99% compared with last quarter - the same period in last year. Gross profit margin in the second quarter 2015 was 15.7% compared to 13.2% in the second quarter of 2014. So that’s a pretty good increase in the gross profit margin, which is mainly due to the increase in average wind turbine selling price by 5.1%.

In this quarter we recorded a provision for doubtful [indiscernible] and other receivable of RMB31 million this is due to our prudent accounting policy of making provisions things on the aging analysis of the receivable. And as a result of that, the profit for the period was RMB68 million for the period compared to RMB11.7 in the second quarter last year. So it also exceeds our guidance of RMB15 million to RMB55 million throughout in last quarter.

And lastly, I would like to share about our earnings guidance for next quarter and we estimate at the net profit for the third quarter 2015 to be in the range of RMB95 million to RMB100 million excluding share based payment expenses and which will represent an increase of 30% compared with same period last year.
