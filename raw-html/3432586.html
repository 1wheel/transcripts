Tahoe Resources' (TAHO) Management on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 12, 2015 11:44 AM ET  |  About: Tahoe Resources Inc. (TAHO) by: SA Transcripts

Tahoe Resources Inc. (NYSE:TAHO)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 12, 2015 10:00 AM ET

Executives

Ira Gostin - VP, IR

Kevin McArthur - Executive Chairman

Ron Clayton - President and COO

Mark Sadler - Vice President and CFO

Edie Hofmeister - VP of Corporate Affairs

Analysts

Chris Thompson - Raymond James

Tony Lesiak - Canaccord Genuity

John Tumazos - John Tumazos Very Independent Research

David Christensen - ASA Ltd

Matthew O’Keefe - Dundee Capital Markets

Operator

Thank you for standing by. This is the conference operator. Welcome to the Tahoe Resources Second Quarter 2015 Conference Call. As a reminder, all participants are in a listen-only mode and the conference is being recorded. [Operator Instructions]

At this time, I would like to turn the conference over to Ira Gostin, Vice President, Investor Relations. Please go ahead.

Ira Gostin

Good morning, everyone, and welcome to the Tahoe Resource earnings conference call for the second quarter of 2015. Management on the call include; Kevin McArthur, Executive Chairman; Ron Clayton, President and Chief Operating Officer; Mark Sadler, Vice President and Chief Financial Officer; Edie Hofmeister, Vice President of Corporate Affairs; and a large contingency of our operating and finance team.

During the call, we will be discussing forward-looking information that involves unique risks concerning the business operations and financial performance and conditions of Tahoe. Use of the words forward-looking statements include, but are not limited to statements with respect to future metal prices, the estimation of mineral resources, the timing and amount of estimated future production, cost of production, capital expenditures and cost and timing of the development of new projects.

Forward-looking statements are subject to known and unknown risks, uncertainties and other factors that may cause the actual results to be materially different from those expressed or implied by such forward-looking statements. Accordingly, you should not place undue reliance on forward-looking statements. Our forward-looking statement, cautionary note, and technical disclosure are on SEDAR as well as our website at tahoresources.com. There is no webcast or slide deck for this quarter’s call.

I will now turn it over to Executive Chair, Kevin McArthur.

Kevin McArthur

Thanks, Ira, and thank you everyone for attending this call. I only have a few remarks and then I will turn to Ron Clayton and Mark Sadler to provide second quarter details. First off, I have assumed the CEO duties since Alex Black’s departure. This is not much of a difference for me because I have been in the office every morning since giving up the CEO title in April. My role has been one of assuring the integration of the two companies was going in the right direction and walking over strategy.

We have a great bench strength here, so as far as I see it, it will be business as usual. Our mines are running well. Shahuindo construction is underway, balance sheet is strong and I’m happy to report that we are one of the few companies not having our dividend. Mark will describe the noise in our 2Q earnings and you will see we have a lot of non-recurring items in the second quarter. Also a note, we paid down $50 million in debt and we ended the quarter with $35 million in remaining debt and $112 million of cash in the bank.

Our company is very healthy with a strong cash flow and a strong balance sheet, but we cannot sit on our hands and not react to reduced revenues in this metal price environment and consequently, we’ve launched a program to simplify our overheads and to reduce G&A. And we at the board are leading that effort with pay cuts and we will be further developing this initiative throughout this quarter.

Our message to shareholders is this. We’ve seen this before and it seems a lot like 1999. We are reacting appropriately to protect our margins and to ensure this company gets through this period in good shape and also ensuring that we will be ready to outperform when the markets turn around. And I'm convinced and this is very similar to when we ran this gold in 1999, convinced, we will one day look back at 2015 and say, what a great time to build a company. That's just the way we get it back then.

So with that, I will turn the call over to Mark Sadler, our Vice President and CFO.

Mark Sadler

Thanks, Kevin. This has been a very busy first quarter for the combined company and integration and the finance function is ongoing and we are of course focused on supporting the operations.

The loss that was reported for the quarter of $9.3 million or $0.04 per share included several non-recurring costs and adjustments associated with the acquisition of Rio Alto as well as other items that were adjusted during the quarter. I'd like to reiterate five impacts on the quarterly results.

First, $5.7 million or $0.03 per share was played in transaction costs upon closing of the transaction with Rio Alto in April. For your information, prior to closing the transaction, Rio Alto also paid substantial transaction costs in March and since they repaid prior to the closing the deal, they were not included in this quarter's results.

Second, approximately 62,000 ounces of gold in process at La Arena are adjusted to fair market or net realizable value on April 1. This effectively increased costs by $9.6 million or $0.04 per share with no margin recognized on those ounces when they were sold. All of the ounces have now been produced and sold and will have no further impact going forward.

Third, although the purchase price allocation has not been finalized, assets acquired in the Rio Alto transaction have been adjusted to fair market value and the write-up will be depreciated going forward. This quarter, the impact was $1.5 million or $0.01 per share.

For concentrate sales initiated prior to 2015, royalties have been accrued at 5% and January royalty payments of 5% was assisted by the Guatemalan government. Ongoing evaluation and interpretation of the new mining law by administrators have indicated that all future royalty payments are expected to be made at 10%, irrespective of the period in which the sale was initiated. Even though the law has been challenged, royalties previously accrued at 5% have been adjusted to 10%. This is a one-time adjustment and the impact to earnings was $7.2 million or $0.03 per share.

And last, as we do every period, provisionally priced metals sold in concentrate was mark-to-market, resulting in a negative $3.4 million or $0.02 per share. The total impact was a negative $27.4 million or $0.12 per share adjustment to the income, resulting in the loss reported for the quarter.

At June 30, there were 3.1 million ounces of silver provisionally priced at $15.56 per ounce and the price of these ounces will continue to be adjusted to market from month to month until settled.

Cash provided by operations, before changes in working capital, was $28 million for the quarter and $76.9 million year-to-date and the cash balance of the company was $112 million at the end of the quarter. As previously stated, we had repaid $50 million in debt and now have closed $150 million revolving credit facility as we intended for this year. The new facility has been established to provide liquidity, to ensure the funds are available in case of unexpected disruption at any of the operations and provide funds to top up capital requirements as the construction continues.

Scotiabank and HSBC are the co-leads on the facility, with Scotiabank acting as the administrative agent. The Royal Bank of Canada, Bank of Montreal and Credit Suisse are all also members of the syndicate.

The facility bears interest on a sliding scale of LIBOR plus between 2.25% to 3.25% dependent upon the company’s consolidated leverage ratio. And standby fees for the undrawn portion of the facility are also based on consolidated leverage and are on a similar sliding scale between 0.5625% and 0.8125%. The term of the facility is three years. This revolver is one of the next steps in our progression to the company and we’re now pleased to have them in place.

With that, I'll now turn the call over to Ron Clayton, President and Chief Operating Officer.

Ron Clayton

Thanks, Mark and I'd like to add my good morning to everybody on the call. It's actually been a really good couple of days here with all of our operating leaders from Peru and Guatemala with us for a Board Meeting and discussions this week.

Overall, the quarter was a good production quarter for us; we pretty much met all of our expectations. La Arena was a real star in the quarter, producing more gold than we had expected and meeting our cost guidance for a very low cost high margin authorization, we expect that to continue throughout the rest of the year. Escobal production was on the lower end of the guidance as we had predicted for the quarter, we expect the first half to be a bit lower production and higher costs due to the expansion activities. In particular, the underground development for the second mining front, accessing the East zone coupled with tying up the infrastructure projects underground and on the surface necessarily took precedence.

We’re on track to meet our guidance of 18 million to 21 million ounces for the year and we expect to be in the upper end of that range by the end of the year. I do want to mention and reiterate something Mark mentioned and that was the one-time non-recurring charge of about $1.60 an ounce that impacted our all-in sustaining costs and total cash costs. As we see production come up later in the year or for the second half of the year, coupled with not having that one-time costs, we would expect these numbers to come well back in line of our guidance. I’m very happy with the cost reductions at Escobal. For this quarter, the unit costs were an additional 10% reduction from the previous quarter and down about 25% from late last year. I think that’s a pretty impressive trend that you can see in the table that we put in the press release.

Significant reduction arrays come all the way across the board, everything from throughput increases to reduction and supplies and lower costs, better purchasing and it’s just a very strong effort across the board for the whole team. We’ve completed another check on the reserves at Escobal and I’m happy to report that the reconciliation of production to reserves continues to be positive for all metals but most importantly and significantly the silver ounces are better than the model predict, tonnes are reconciling right on the model level, so we’re extremely pleased with this.

As Kevin mentioned earlier, Shahuindo is moving forward towards the first quarter of 2016 start-up production, we have all the major permits required for construction and production, the process will continue as construction of Phase 1 advances and we reach the appropriate milestones. Permitting and construction are on track to meet our guidance and goals, and we’re working on a new reserve estimate and long-term planning window and plan to provide that 43-101 update near the end of the fourth quarter.

The integration of Rio Alto into the new Tahoe is progressing well and we’re meeting all of our goals on that front. This was never an easy task as you all know and Mark in a test and did a test earlier in his talk for the accounting and merger activities that always creates some challenges and impacts. We’re comfortable that we have this well under control and for the most part behind us.

With that, I think we’re ready to take some questions.

Chris Thompson

Kevin McArthur

Yeah, well, we’re taking a bit of a step back and making sure that all the people are in the right seats on the bus at this time and refocusing on a lot of our integration efforts. So, we don’t have any current M&A activities going on but I assure you that long-term, we believe that we can -- because of the health of our Company, we can take advantage of M&A at some future point. Looking forward, tip-of-iceberg assets or undervalued assets and this is the good time to be growing a Company because one of those fundamental business tenants as you buy low and sell high and we truly believe that this market is, if not at the low, very close to the low. Of course, we can’t predict how long this will continue, but one thing I can predict is, it is a cycle and we will be seeing the business improving and metal prices improving. So, you can back that we’ll be active in the space at some future point in the Americas, precious metals.

Chris Thompson

Great. Okay guys, thanks again.

Thanks, Chris.
