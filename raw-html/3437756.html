Pointer Telocation's (PNTR) CEO David Mahlab on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 2:42 PM ET  |  About: Pointer Telocation Ltd. (PNTR) by: SA Transcripts

Pointer Telocation Ltd. (NASDAQ:PNTR)

Q2 2015 Results Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015 9:30 a.m. ET

Executives

David Mahlab - President and Chief Executive Officer

Zvi Fried - Chief Financial Officer

Gavriel Frohwein - GK Investor & Public Relations

Analysts

Josh Nichols - B. Riley

Amit Dayal - H.C. Wainwright

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, thank you for standing by. Welcome to Pointer Telocation’s Second Quarter 2015 Results Conference Call. All participants are at present in a listen-only mode. Following management's formal presentation, instructions will be given for the question-and-answer session. As a reminder, this conference is being recorded.

You should have all received by now the company's press release. If you have not received it, please contact Pointer's Investor Relations team at GK Investor Relations or view it in the News section of the company's Web site, www.pointer.com.

I would now like to hand the call over to Mr. Gavriel Frohwein of GK Investor Relations. Mr. Frohwein, would you like to begin please?

Gavriel Frohwein

Thank you, operator. I would like to welcome you all to this conference call and thank Pointer's management for hosting this call. Earlier today, Pointer released its second quarter 2015 results. By now, you should have received a copy of the press release, which is also available on Pointer’s Web site at www.pointer.com.

With us on the call today are Mr. David Mahlab, President and Chief Executive Officer; Mr. Zvi Fried, Chief Financial Officer. David will summarize the key highlights of the quarter, followed by Zvi who will provide a summary of the financials. We will then open the call for question-and-answer session.

Before we start, I'd like to point out that the Safe Harbor published in today's press release also pertains to the contents of this conference call . And with that, I'd like to now introduce Pointer’s CEO, Mr. David Mahlab. David, please go ahead.

David Mahlab

Thank you. Good morning, everyone and thank you for joining us today. Earlier today we reported our second quarter results. We reported an overall revenue level of $25.3 million in the quarter, a decrease of 2% over last year and 1% increase over the prior quarter.

I want to make the point that in local currency, our results look good and revenue growing 9% year-over-year. The major factor affecting the result was a strengthening of the U.S. dollar compared to last year especially against the Brazilian Real, a factor out of our control. The Real has devaluated by 43% from June 30, 2014 to 2015, which has a significant impact on our results as denominated in U.S. dollar. The decrease in the value of the Real is the result of the weak economy environment in Brazil which also led to a slowdown in our growth in that market.

I know that the Real has further declined since June and that will be also likely currency impact on the third quarter. Despite the currency headwind, our MRM segment and in particular the service part of it, is growing well. If you remember, mobile resource management is the business which we are focused on. It's our core business and will become our only business once you complete the spinout of Shagrir, the roadside assistance business.

In exhibit A of the press released we issued today with the results, you can find the pro forma of the mobile resource management business as a standalone business. Our total mobile resource management revenue were $16.4 million, out of which our mobile resource management product revenue were $5.8 million and the mobile resource management service revenue based on SaaS model was $10.6 million.

This service revenue are based on the recurring monthly revenue stream from captive software-as-a-service customer base. In local currency terms, the territory where our subsidiaries operate, we grew our MRS service business by 23%. A solid growth rate which we are very happy with.

Now that we have built and having placed a critical mass and infrastructure, the marginal cost of adding additional new subscriber is minimal. As our business grows on a territorial base, we should be able to consistently improve our operating margins. The MRM product revenues declined by $1 million to $5.8 million in the quarter which as you may remember, in the third quarter of last year we stopped selling to two lower margin customers. Looking ahead, we expect our mobile resource management business and the service [indiscernible] in particular to continue on a long-term growth curve.

We are investing in R&D and our goal is to consistently release additional new mobile resource management product, technologies and services for the Internet of Things and asset trading markets. We are now at a better stage with regard to the new product for that vertical market and we target to launch it commercially in early 2016. We are releasing additional product of fleet management and we hope it will further improve our market position.

We believe the new product will come with right long-term growth in our mobile resource management business and we hope to see the positive contribution in 2016. With regard to the separation of the roadside assistance business, we are working hard to complete the spin-off. It is a complicated legal process and takes time. This [indiscernible] explains for the first half of 2016 and we will keep you updated as we progress.

This process is an important part of our long-term strategy and it will simplify the overall Pointer's business structure making the value inherent in the fast growing business clearer to the investor. We hope that these things will allow us to unlock and increase some additional shareholder value.

With regard to our operation in South Africa, we are pleased with the progress we are making in that market. While still small, we are shifting that business over to our systems and the integration is going well and we believe that in 2016, the revenues from South Africa will become a more significant contributor to our whole operation.

In summary, while we do see some currency headwind in 2015, as well as slower growth in Brazil due to their weak economy, we remain optimistic overall. We expect that our mobile resource management business to continue its strong performance which would become more apparent in 2016. I will now hand over to Zvi Fried, our CFO, to summarize our financial results. Zvi, please go ahead.

Zvi Fried

Thank you, David. In terms of our financial results for the second quarter of 2015, the highlights were as follows. Second quarter revenues were $25.3 million compared to $25.9 million in the second quarter of 2014. This represents a year-over-year decrease of 2.2%. The lower level of revenues were primarily due to the [rising] [ph] of the U.S. dollar versus various local currencies where we have our operations, particularly the Brazilian Real and also the Israel Sheqel as David mentioned.

In addition, our product revenue were impacted by absence of two low margin customers which were included in the second quarter of last year but not this year.

In local currency terms, revenues would have grown overall by 9% with service revenues growing by 16% and product revenues decreasing by 7%. In total, for the MRM business, revenues were $16.4 million, 2% below of the second quarter of last year. In local currency terms, revenue would have grown by 9% and the MRM service specifically grew 23% over [indiscernible] of last year.

Second quarter non-GAAP operating income was $1.9 million compared to $2 million in the second quarter of 2015. Non-GAAP net income in the second quarter was $1.4 million compared with $1.7 million in the second quarter of 2015. Adjusted EBITDA in the second quarter was $2.7 million compared with $3 million in the second quarter of 2014.

We lowered our net debt, debt to bank, at the end of the quarter to $7.6 million compared to $11 million as of December 31, 2014.

We shall now open the call for questions. Operator, please?

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

[Operator Instructions] Your first question is from Josh Nichols with B. Riley. Please go ahead.

Josh Nichols

The company has been able to post pretty strong service revenue growth [indiscernible] product revenue. Is that trend do you think the company will be able to [indiscernible]?

Zvi Fried

Can you just repeat the question, it was a little bit unclear.

Josh Nichols

Yes. Do you expect that service revenue will be able to continue to grow in the MRM division without the associated product revenue growth?

David Mahlab

We expect that the revenue from services will continue to grow. The only market in which we see problems is the Brazilian market. But looking into the second half we are more optimistic due to the project we're looking at. And that of course does not take into account the currency changes that might continue to happen and still going on especially in Brazil.

Josh Nichols

Right. Good. And could you give a little bit more detail, I know you mentioned there is an offering the company [indiscernible] on coming out with in 2016. Any additional value [indiscernible]?

David Mahlab

Basically, the new product that we are talking about is targeting new market segments, so goods in transit. We are getting an excellent feedback from the beta process that we are doing right now. But we are gathering feedback and the goal actually is to release the product on a full commercial base once we implement most of the feedbacks we receive from customers in the beta process.

Josh Nichols

Great. And you mentioned about the second half, Brazil seems to be weak but you had a project coming online. Is it one particular project or an array of different ones?

David Mahlab

We have five projects that we are looking at. Which, any of them will have an impact for growth. But, overall, we are clear to think that we have a good position. And on the other hand, the trends in the overall market are not encouraging. So overall I do believe that we will grow, but less than expected at the beginning of the year.

Josh Nichols

And just hitting on the roadside divestiture. Any chance of sale or is this most likely outcome at this junction?

David Mahlab

We are working towards a spin out by listing. We have some discussion in the [indiscernible] with few buyers but at this stage in that, most probably that we will just [lease] [ph] the company.

Josh Nichols

Thanks. Question, just as you are thinking about Brazil. What percentage of the MRM revenue is derived from Brazil?

David Mahlab

We are not publishing those numbers at this stage but it has an effect.

Operator

The next question is from Amit Dayal with H.C. Wainwright. Please go ahead.

Amit Dayal

It seems Brazil is giving you all sorts of headaches. There is also this news release about tax related notice that you received. Can you give some color on what that is about and how you are looking to resolve that issue?

David Mahlab

Yes. This came from Sao Paulo state guys claiming that our service in the mobile resource is actually classifies us as a telecom service provider since we are providing our services over cellular links. In a way it is quite similar as if you classify even Visa, the payment system, also to become a telecom company because they have a payment machine that works on the cellular link, connects to the web. They have already -- actually the municipality of Sao Paulo is now pursuing additional -- including the five companies at the same time claiming for the same kind of classifications. I would remind everybody that two companies on top of the five, that they are claiming now have already won that process, [indiscernible] won. And already won against the Sao Paulo state. So based on our legal, the best legal advice that we had and based on the fact that already two companies have already won the case, we believe that we have a good case of claim.

We know that those claims and especially the one against [indiscernible] was going for the last, I think 3.5 or 4 years. And during that time we also consulted tax lawyers in Brazil and we got the same kind of advice that we are working properly. And if the authorities will sue us, we have a good case. We try to avoid it based on those two companies that already won. But Sao Paulo state added that they want to continue to pursue against all the other five companies.

Amit Dayal

And are these five companies all in the MRM space?

David Mahlab

Yes. They are basically competitor, direct competitor to us.

Amit Dayal

Okay. And is there a timeline, you said three or four years it took for the other guys to sort of settle this. Is that a similar timeframe you are looking to have?

David Mahlab

First, it's not in our hand, in our control. I want to be more optimistic and I hope that it will be better. But it's time consuming and the main problem is it takes management attention and we need to pay lawyers also for the process.

Amit Dayal

Is that going to potentially impact operating cost? I don’t know how much you are going to incur, is that a significant amount or something that’s manageable?

David Mahlab

Once we win -- assuming that we win, it's very manageable. [indiscernible].

Amit Dayal

Okay. Right. This Shagrir spinoff, this time last year the timeline was first half of 2015. Today you are giving an update this is first half of 2016. What is taking so long? Can you give some color on why this is being pushed out by almost a year now?

David Mahlab

Few reasons but all around the legislation in the local stock exchange and the finance authorities in Israel. I think that we learn over time how to [incur] [ph] the tax authority to verify that it not has become a major tax impact on us. And also we have tested all the expectation with the local stock exchange regulation. So I believe that we will be on time. Now that’s correct that we are delayed.

Amit Dayal

We have been modeling the transition out of the Shagrir business but I guess we should continue keeping Shagrir revenues and earnings as a part of the overall story going into 2016 as well. Is that a fair positioning?

David Mahlab

Our target actually is that -- probably it will be done based on the 2016 number, the spinoff.

Amit Dayal

Okay. All right. I will follow up with my other questions offline. Thank you so much.

Operator

[Operator Instructions] There are no further questions at this time. Mr. Mahlab, would you like to make your concluding statement?

David Mahlab

On behalf of the management I would like to thank you for your continued interest and long-term support for the business. I will be in New York and in San Francisco in September meeting investors while presenting at both Rodman and [indiscernible] conference. If you wish to meet me, please contact my investor relations team or speak with the conference organizers. And for the rest of you, I look forward to speaking with you and updating you again on the next quarter. Have a great day.

Operator

Thank you. This concludes the Pointer Telocation second quarter 2015 results conference call. Thank you for your participation. You may go ahead and disconnect.
