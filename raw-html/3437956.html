SilverCrest Mines (SVLC) on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 3:41 PM ET  |  About: SilverCrest Mines, Inc. (SVLC) by: SA Transcripts

SilverCrest Mines, Inc. (NYSEMKT:SVLC)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 12, 2015 01:00 PM ET

Executives

Michael Rapsch - VP, Corporate Communications

Eric Fier - COO

Analysts

Chris Thompson - Raymond James

Operator

Fm. My name is Chris and I’ll be your conference operator today. At this time I would like to welcome everyone to SilverCrest Second Quarter Financial Conference Call. All lines have been placed on mute to prevent any background noise. After the speakers’ remarks, there will be a question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions] Thank you.

At this time, I would like to turn the conference over to Michael Rapsch, Vice President, Corporate Communications. Mr. Rapsch, you may begin your conference.

Michael Rapsch

Thank you, Chris and good morning everyone and welcome to SilverCrest 2015 second quarter financials conference call. On the call today are Scott Drever, our Chief Executive Officer; Eric Fier, Chief Operating Officer and Tom Keating, Vice President, Finance.

But before we get started, I’m required to remind you that certain statements on this call will contain forward-looking information within the meaning of Canadian securities law. At this point I will refer you to the detailed forward-looking statement disclaimer contained in our quarterly financial statement and the MD&A. Those kind of statements regarding the company's anticipated performance and development in the future including gold and silver production cost and revenue forecast, rates and recovery forecast. For additional information in terms of operational update please refer to some of our most recent news releases stated May 07th and July 13th.

And with that I would like to turn the call over to SilverCrest COO, Eric Fier. Eric?

Eric Fier

Very thanks, Mike. Welcome everybody to the Q2 2015 financial conference call. As usual I will begin with giving a brief review of the quarterly financial performance including some operational updates and then we’ll jump into the question-and-answer period.

As our second quarter was another successful quarter for the company notwithstanding the lower first metal and price environment that we’re in. The Santa Elena mine delivered record production again in Q2 resulting from robust financial economics including generating positive cash flow and net earnings. Our cash cost numbers for Q2 [are] our corporate market guidance of $9 to $10 per silver equivalent ounce. This is a big achievement not only for SilverCrest but in our peer group as we continue to move forward and be one of the lowest cost producers in the market.

Let's have a quick look at the Q2 financial highlights. Hopefully everybody has had a chance to look at the news release and I'll just take a snapshot of what’s in that news release and discuss that.

Our cash flow from operations was just over $8 million or $0.07 per share that’s basic and diluted. That was an increase of 163% I'm comparing that to the Q2 2014.

Cash operating costs were at $8.05, an increase of 13%, off of the Q2 2014. Keep in mind during 2014 first half there we were still doing the open pit and we were doing heap leaching, and now we’re underground in mill processing.

The increasing cash cost compared to 2014 basically a direct result of that just going from an open pit heap leach to a underground norm scenario and also the product personally offset by weaker Mexican peso.

All in sustaining cash cost for silver equivalent announce sold was $10.97 a decrease of 7%. Just a reminder our newly revised 2015 cash cost is $9 to $10 per silver equivalent ounce sold. I'll have to point out the sold most of our peer groups used a produced number there, we used our sold number for our cash cost and our all in sustaining capital guidance is $12 to $13 per silver equivalent ounce sold again and those are all revised numbers now as of today.

Revenue increased 169% to 20.07 million. The metal sales for the quarter were 515,070 ounces of silver and 11,567 ounces a gold; this is an increase of 216%, and 144% respectively for the gold. Realized average spot price declined from $19.67 to $16.54 that’s a 16% decline for silver and from 1,296 to 1,202 a 7% decrease for gold. Net earnings increased to 194% to $3.86 million or $0.03 per share basic and diluted, adjusted earnings increased 361% to $4.5 million or $0.04 per share basic and diluted. Cash and cash equivalents total right now at the end of June 30, 2015 were at $36.4 million and that was compared to 40.1 million in last year this time.

Working capital at the end of Q2 $51 million compared to 44.5 at the end of Q2 in 2014. Metal inventory includes at the end of the quarter 247,841 ounces of silver and just over 5,000 ounces of gold which is a pretty good amount in inventory that we’re carrying quite a bit of that right now. Still have the Scotia line of credit or credit facility, we’ve drawn 15 million now that’s unchanged and there is still 5 million that’s available. For an operational update, we continue to optimize the performance in Santa Elena and continue to beat our targets as budgeted as of the beginning of the year in January 2015.

The current average of mining rate is 1,200 tons per day and milling rates averaging 1,000 tons per day as a nominal design -- sorry, 3,000 tons per day as a nominal design and we’re currently running a blend of 60-40 that’s from the leach pads and the underground and that’s as planned and as budgeted.

Most notable operational improvement towards the increase in silver recoveries from H1. We started out the year in January at about 61% to 62% silver and now we’re running at about 85%, this is pretty impressive, our budget was for 70% or well above that. And this is a direct result of optimizing the mill bringing in and injecting oxygen and lead nitrate into the processing tanks.

There is some cost to doing that, but it's far outweighed by the amount of ounces that we continue to produce is still at about for every dollar of oxygen and lead that we put into the processing, we’re still getting about $5 to $6 back net and silver recovery. That’s also increased a little bit of the gold recovery which we’re running at about 93% versus a budget of 92%.

On merger and acquisitions everybody should be well aware as announced July 27th that the company entered a definitive agreement with First Majestic, whereby First Majestic will acquire all issued and outstanding shares of ifor consideration of 0.2769 common shares of First Majestic plus a nominal amount of cash and there is also a New SilverCrest that’s [spin-co] that's coming out of that transaction.

The transaction excluding the [spin-co] equates to about 37% premium of the SilverCrest share price as of the date of the arrangement which is at 21.4% ownership in First Majestic by the SilverCrest shareholders.

In addition to the ownership the SilverCrest shareholders will benefit from First Majestic liquidity in the marketplace diversity provided by six operating mines and the management’s long history of successful operations in Mexico. There is a meeting of SilverCrest shareholders that has been called for as of September 25th to seek approval for the transaction with the closing expected in early October.

I like to turn it back to the operator for any additional comments and going to our question-and-answer period.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

Thank you. Ladies and gentlemen, we will now begin the question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions] Your first question comes from Chris Thompson, Raymond James. Chris Please go ahead.

Chris Thompson

Just wanted to just focus in very quickly on the underground that at Santa Elena, could you just comment a little bit more on the great reconciliation there and where the terms are coming from? I mean I know that you are adjusting your mine plant to take in some of the high grade I guess in time [indiscernible] are we seeing that at the moment at all?

Eric Fier

We’re currently mining from stopes 9 through 11 which is up on the 625 level in the mine, and we’re basically -- although the production from the mine is from those three stopes 9 is almost completed and being backfilled. There is about 15% to 20% that's coming out of [indiscernible] and that’s off of the 550 and the 655 level. We've shutdown the 625 and 675 level right now because we’re doing some drilling to look at for the extension of that. So I'd say 80% to 85% of our tonnage is coming out of stopes 9, 10 and 11 of that level and then the rest is coming from [indiscernible]. The grade reconciliation we’re still working on, I'm seeing numbers still 10% to 20% better about the same volumes as we had expected.

Chris Thompson

Excellent and could you just give us an idea very quickly on the cost there, your underground mining cost?

Eric Fier

We’re running pretty steady now around $30 per ton, that’s excluding the sustained capital and the processing is about 25 I think we’re used to that number and the market is too even though we have additional cost with oxygen and with lead nitrate, we seem to offset those with system optimization in the middle and our G&A is around $4 to $5 per ton.

Chris Thompson

Finally Eric if you would just -- we noticed that’s that you are a little behind I guess on your sustaining capital, do you see this sort of catching up in the back half of the year or is it's more a reflection of the pace side and maybe sort of the cut back from that sort of capital?

Eric Fier

We’re still budgeted for about $4 million left to spend this year on the capital side. Where that money is basically going is we’re back to driving the ramp, we’re currently on the ramp elevations around the 540. We’re doing some of the routing right now for control of water, but water volumes are still the same as they have been for the last three years, they are just coming in certain directions and we’re trying to control it a little bit better. We’re also going to be drilling some holes off of the ramp and doing some pumping below the 540. So ramp cost and more development as we continue -- if you remember we’re doing the [indiscernible] and we started on the 575 to 600 levels and we’re working our way up so we’re developing as we’re going up. So you will see development on the 625 and 655 elevations.

Operator

[Operator Instructions]. There are no questions at this time. Please proceed.

Eric Fier

Well that’s great; we did a great job this time. Just a final note to everybody. I just wanted to say thank you very much as we do the transition with First Majestic. We think First Majestic is an excellent company to work with and move forward and look at our position here as an investment for our shareholders in First Majestic. We believe in the management there, we believe in the projects and just watch for that high beta, the liquidity in silver as we get that wave moving forward [indiscernible]. I think most of us have drank the Kool-Aid and said that we believe in silver price and gold price going back up, that this is only a cycle. And I want to thank all of our shareholders for their great support over the last 11 years as we developed the SilverCrest story and I'm looking forward working with those same shareholders when we bring out the New SilverCrest after the close of this deal. Thank you very much.

Operator

Thank you. Ladies and gentlemen, this concludes your conference call for today. We thank you for participating and ask that you please disconnect your lines.
