KongZhong (KZ) Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 18, 2015 10:45 AM ET  |  About: KongZhong Corporation (KZ) by: SA Transcripts

KongZhong Corporation (NASDAQ:KZ)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 17, 2015 07:30 PM ET

Executives

Jay Chang - Director and CFO

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, thank you for standing by and welcome to the Second Quarter 2015 ZhongKong Corporation’s Earnings Conference Call. At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. Please note that today's conference call is being recorded.

I would now like to turn the call over to Mr. Jay Chang, Chief Financial Officer of the company. Thank you, Mr. Chang, please go ahead.

Jay Chang

Thank you, operator. This conference call may contain forward-looking statements. Although such statements are based on our own information and information from sources we believe to be reliable, you should not place undue reliance on them. For additional discussions of risks and uncertainties relating to forward-looking statements and other factors, please see the documents we file from time-to-time with the Securities and Exchange Commission. We assume no obligation to update any forward-looking statements which apply only as of the date of this conference call. Thank you for your interest in KongZhong.

Total revenues for the second quarter of 2015 were $47.55 million. Total gross loss was 15.29 million. The total gross profit excluding the effect of impairment loss on the tangible assets related to the full carrying value of our licensing agreements related to Guild Wars 2 would have been $19.92 million, within our prior guidance range of 19 million to 20 million.

GAAP net loss was US$30.40 million, with diluted net loss per ADS of $0.65, although this included the same 35.21 million impairment loss on our intangible assets. Non-GAAP net income was US$5.62 million, with Non-GAAP diluted net income per ADS of $0.12, exceeding our prior guidance level of around US$5 million.

At the end of June, the Company had $200 million in cash and cash equivalents, term deposits, held-to-maturity securities, available-for-sale securities and restricted cash or equivalent to $4.29 per ADS in cash and cash equivalents with a net of a $50 million short-term bank loan which was utilized for deficits of last year's dividend payment.

Turning up to each different line, Internet Game revenues were US$24.46 million in the second quarter of 2015, a decline of 7.2% from the first quarter. Although World of Tanks continues to demonstrate stable performance, user activity and purchases in Guild Wars 2 and World of Warplanes continued to moderate during the period. We expect performance and player purchases in Guild Wars 2 to remain depressed until the release of the upcoming expansion.

For the second quarter of 2015, Mainland China online game operations achieved average monthly active users or MAUs of 1.8 million users and aggregated monthly paying accounts of 327,000 with a monthly average revenue per user or ARPU of RMB 152. Total mobile game revenues were US$9.43 million, a 10.7% decrease from the first quarter. The decline in mobile game revenues was due to a decline in revenues from legacy self-developed and licensed games on Chinese smartphone game platforms, as well as lower than expected performance of a new game launch at the end of the second quarter.

WVAS revenues were US$13.66 million, a 0.5% increase from the first quarter and a 9.7% decrease from the same period last year. Total gross loss for the second quarter was US$15.39 million, and this included impairment loss on intangible assets. During the period the Company recognized US$35.2 million impairment loss, which is equal to the full carrying amount of intangible assets related to our licensing arrangement for Guild Wars 2. However, the Company continues to operate Guild Wars 2 and intends to release the upcoming expansion pack in China in the near future.

Other than the effect of impairment on intangible assets, gross profit would have been US$19.92 million, a 4.2% increase from the first quarter and a 18.7% decrease from the same period last year. Excluding the effect of impairment on intangible assets, total gross margin would have been 42% in the second quarter. Total operating expenses in the second quarter was US$17.41 million compared to US$16.19 million in the first quarter, a slight increase related to the smartphone game launch at the end of 2Q.

Now, turning to our business outlook, for the third quarter ended September 30, 2015, we expect total revenues for the third quarter to be within the range of US$40 million to US$41 million. We expect total gross profit to be within the range of US$18 million to US$19 million. And we expect U.S. GAAP net income to be US$4 million and Non-GAAP net income is expected to be around US$5 million.

Our third quarter 2015 revenue guidance reflects stable quarter-over-quarter performance in our Internet games business, but a decline in our mobile game business due to a delay of new mobile game launches and a decline in our WVAS business due to impact from mobile operator policies. Due to the pause in new mobile game launches, we expect sales and marketing expenses in 3Q to moderate compared to 2Q levels.

Thank you for your interest in KongZhong, operator?

Operator

Thank you, ladies and gentlemen that does conclude our conference for today. We thank you all for your participation. You may now disconnect.
