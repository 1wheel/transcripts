Hooper Holmes' (HH) CEO Henry Dubois on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 10:52 AM ET  |  About: Hooper Holmes, Inc (HH) by: SA Transcripts

Hooper Holmes, Inc. (NYSEMKT:HH)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015, 08:30 AM ET

Executives

Stan Berger - Investor Relations, SM Berger & Co., Inc.

Henry Dubois - President and Chief Executive Officer

Tom Collins - Senor Vice President and Chief Financial Officer

Analysts

William Sutherland - Emerging Growth Equities

Tom Horan - Acorn Capital

Operator

Good day, and welcome to the Hooper Holmes 2015 Second Quarter Conference Call. Today's conference is being recorded. At this time, I would like to turn the conference over to Stan Berger of S.M. Berger. Please go ahead, sir.

Stan Berger

Thank you, Tiffany. On behalf of the management of Hooper Holmes, we are extremely pleased that you have taken the time to participate in our conference call, and thank you for joining us to discuss the Company's financial results for the second quarter of 2015.

Before I introduce management, I would like to remind everyone that this presentation contains forward-looking statements as such term is defined in the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995. Concerning the Company's plans, objectives, goals, strategies, future events or performances, which are not statements of historical facts and can be identified by words such as except, continue, should, may, will, project, anticipate, believe, plan, goal, and similar references to future periods. The forward-looking statements contained in this presentation reflect our current beliefs and expectations. Actual results or performances may differ materially from what is expressed in the forward-looking statements. Among the important factors that could cause actual results to differ materially from those expressed in or implied by the forward-looking statements contained in this presentation or our ability to realize the expected benefits from the acquisition of Accountable Health Solutions and our strategic alliance with Clinical Reference Laboratory; Our ability to successfully implement our business strategy and integrate Accountable Health Solutions business with ours; our ability to retain and grow our customer base; our ability to recognize operational efficiencies and reduce costs; uncertainty as to our working capital requirements over the next 12 to 24 months; our ability to maintain compliance with the financial covenants contained in our credit facility; the rate of growth in the Health and Wellness market; and such other factors as discussed in Part 1, Item 1A Risk Factors and Part 2, Item 7 Management's Discussion and Analysis of financial conditions and results of operations of our Annual Report on Form 10-K for the year ended December 31, 2014.

The Company undertakes no obligation to update or release any revisions to these forward-looking statements to reflect events or circumstances or to reflect the occurrence of unanticipated events after the date of this presentation, except as required by law. This presentation contains information from third-party sources including data from studies conducted by others and market data, and industry forecast from industry publications.

Although the Company believes that such information is reliable, the Company has not independently verified any of this information and the Company does not guarantee the accuracy or completeness of this information.

By now, you should have received a copy of the new release which was issued this morning and is available on our website. You should also have received a copy of the slides accompanying management's presentation this morning which is also available on the website.

Participating in the call today are Henry Dubois, Hooper Holmes' President and Chief Executive Officer; and Tom Collins, Chief Financial Officer.

At this time, I will turn the call over to Henry. Henry?

Henry Dubois

Thanks, Stan, and thank all of you for joining us this morning to discuss second quarter 2015 performance and for giving us the opportunity to reengage you on our strategy and progress.

Our acquisition of Accountable Health Solutions is now behind us. It has diversified our revenues and expanded our product offerings. We believe it is a key to profitability and growth. Before we get into our second quarter numbers and our strategy discussion, I’d like to comment that we too are disappointed in our recent stock performance and believe our outlook is not properly reflected in our market cap.

We know we have to deliver revenue growth and profitability and we believe we have the right business model and strategy to do that. During the second quarter, we focused on integrating the acquisition and preparing for our seasonally strong second half. Now that much of that work has been completed, we know we must improve awareness of where our company is going. We will be actively discussing our business model and strategy with investors and analysts in a series of upcoming meetings and sharing it with you this morning.

Please turn to the Slide 3 in today’s presentation. Up until now, Hooper has been focused solely on the left-hand column, Screenings and Data Collection. Hooper now has an attractive end-to-end health and wellness offerings that provides value to both employers and participants.

Through our proprietary products and services, which can be tailored to meet our customer needs, Hooper can now deliver quality screening and data collection services for corporate wellness and clinical research, personalized health coaching and education programs that help individuals set health improvement goals and build plans and skills to achieve these goals and engagement programs that keep participants focused on goals all year long.

There are important synergies between these expanded services and our biometric screenings. Our health professionals who perform biometric screenings are the face of each client’s wellness program and are on to a strategic advantage able to engage participants in additional services for us or our channel partners.

And with the 99% satisfaction rating from our screening participants, we believe our in-house screening capabilities create a successful opening act for our client’s wellness programs. The suite of products and services you see here now entrenches the health risks and poor behaviors that contribute to 15 medical conditions accounts for 80% of the total cost of product illnesses worldwide.

Screening and data collection is the first step in health improvement. Our screening results and other data educate and then engage participants in taking charge of their health.

Throughout the process, we are able to aggregate screening and assessment data to provide useful reports and insights that can lead to further population health improvement. We will be discussing more about our strategy in the growth expansion of our products in just a few minutes.

I would now like to turn to the second quarter 2015. We are pleased to report that both revenues and units increased during what has been historically a seasonally slow quarter. We cite new more customers and completed much of the integration of an acquisition that on a pro forma basis as 50% to our annual revenue.

We also remain on track to deliver positive operating cash flow for the full year of 2015 excluding cost associated with the acquisition.

Before we get into our detailed financials, as you know, from our press release this morning, Tom Collins has decided to leave the company. I’d like to thank Tom for his hard work and the contributions he has made in helping us reposition the company. I have enjoyed working with him over the last couple of years and I am sorry to see him go.

Today, we also announced the appointment of Steven Balthazor as Interim CFO. I have worked with Steven for a number of years. For the last two years, he has supported our most important strategic transactions including our acquisition of AHS. He brings a strong financial background and has a deep understanding of our operations.

Steven will join me in our meetings with investors next month and I will call to introducing him to many of you personally.

Let me turn it over to Tom to review our second quarter numbers. Tom?

Tom Collins

Thanks, Henry and good morning everyone. As Henry mentioned, I’ve decided to pursue an opportunity as the Chief Operating Officer of a private company here in the Kansas City area. This will allow me to expand my career on more operational items. It was a tough decision. I want you to know that I strongly believe in our management team and what we have accomplished and our strategy and our future opportunity. My departure is not motivated in anyway by issues regarding financial disclosures, accounting or legal matters. I am looking forward to supporting Steven during his smooth transition and to supporting Hooper as a shareholder.

With that, let me get into the numbers. As you can see on slide four, for the three-month period ended June 30, 2015, consolidated revenues were $7.7 million, representing an increase of 15% compared to $6.7 million during the same period in 2014. Our second quarter 2015 revenue includes approximately two months of revenue from our AHS acquisition for $2.7 million. We completed 109,000 biometric screenings during the second quarter of 2015 representing an increase of 5.8% from the second quarter of 2014. In our 2015 first quarter conference call, we mentioned a large clinical research study that was ending, that would create a role in the gap that we needed to fill, even as we continue to work with our clients on their bid for a potential expansion.

As you can see from the chart on the right, that clinical study contributed 16,000 screening units in the second quarter of 2014. I am pleased to share with you that we have filled this unit gap and delivered growth in the second quarter of 2015.

Let me also point out that if you adjust for that study, and look at the base of our screening business, our biometric screening units increased 20% year-over-year. We believe this reinforces our potential to grow market share. The average pricing for biometric screening has remained stable. We manage pricing in a range that varies based on group size, volume, promote locations, special protocols, and so on. With AHS revenues including coaching and portal revenue, there is no longer a direct correlation between screening units and revenue.

Turning to Slide 5, selling, general and administrative expense for the three months period ended June 30, 2015 totaled $3.8 million. Excluding AHS integration cost, SG&A decreased 13% compared to the prior year period. Income from continuing operations was in line with Q2 2014’s performance excluding one-time cost and interest expense related to the AHS acquisition.

Gross margins decreased in the second quarter 2015 due to operating investments in advanced of our fall volume including training for our new mobile applications and additional field and event management invest. I’d like to underscore that we expect the addition of new value-added services from AHS and increase screening volumes in the second half to increase our gross margin.

The company incurred acquisition-related cost of $1.6 million for the three months period ended June 30, 2015, of which $300,000 was integration-related, $600,000 were one-time transaction fees, $150,000 was cash interest expense related to our SWK term loan and Aries line of credit and $550,000 were non-cash expenses associated with debt features related to the acquisition.

Excluding integration costs, our SG&A run rate is on track with Q4 2014 results. As we have discussed, prior to the transaction, our annual SG&A run rate was approximately $10 million to $12 million. With the addition of AHS, we believe the run rate will be closer to $14 million to $16 million per year, an improvement on a percentage basis when compared to with our revenue. This supports our objective to continually manage down SG&A expense as a percent of revenues.

In terms of our balance sheet, our cash balance on June 30, 2015 was $1.2 million, with $800,000 in borrowings, outstanding against our revolving credit facility. On August 10, 2015, we finalized an amendment to our existing line of credit to add AHS’s receivables to our borrowing base. This effectively added $1 million to our borrowing base.

As we have discussed, our availability under the Aries facility can be as high as $7 million based on our receivable balance. With the acquisition of AHS, availability on our existing credit line of Aries and the approaching busy season, we believe we are prepared to strengthen our financial position in the coming quarters and excluding one-time transaction and transition costs, deliver positive operating cash flow for the full year 2015.

With that, I’ll turn the call back to Henry.

Henry Dubois

Thanks, Tom. One of the many facts we have confirmed as we have been integrating AHS is a robust demand for health and wellness services. Mark Hay Studies and Research suggests that corporate wellness programs deliver a positive return for every dollar invested. As you can see on Slide 6, health and wellness programs provide value among employers, a major reason is cost control. For example, as you can see on the right-side of this slide, a study reported on Health Affairs Journal demonstrates that a dollar spent on wellness encourages mental cost by more than $3 for every dollar spent.

In an AHS customer study showed an average $3.42 return. Both studies also showed additional cost savings associated with reduced absenteeism. In addition, the latest survey on wellness programs from Fidelity Investments and the National Business Group on Health suggests that employers will spend almost $700 per employee on wellness states and centers in 2015, up from about $600 in 2014 and $400 five years ago.

Wellness programs are also popular among employees. At January 2015, Health Mine Survey of employees was a company sponsored health plan, indicated that 70% of the people wanted health in their employer in health management. More than 75% believed incentives would motivate them to take action to improve their health and Hooper is positioned to deliver.

Turning to Slide 7, we believe we have significant growth opportunities ahead of us, starting with the three pillars of screening and data collection, education and engagement that comprise our integrated offering. We are not the only company offering health and wellness products, but we are one of the only few that offer extensive improvement products and services in all three areas. For screenings, our focus on secure, accurate data and ability to deliver quality screenings in every zip code is a key competitive advantage.

On the education front, we offer a highly flexible wellness forum and incentive administration platform that allows us to tailor our clients’ programs to meet their needs. We also have robust target investing capabilities that allows to personalize the member experience along with solutions to reach those that may not have technology within their reach.

Lastly, everything we do is focused on awareness and engagement, starting with our biometric screenings. Our fully integrated platform allows to appoint members in the right direction and to the right programs from the start and our health coaches do a superb job of engaging members deem to be at risk helping them set personalized goals and motivate behavior change which leads to better health and overall well-being.

All this results in more participation, high participant satisfaction, greater employee engagements, and better health outcomes.

As I said in the outset, we know we need to deliver revenue growth and profitability and that’s what I want to discuss on these next two slides. As you can see on Slide 8, we have created a growth-oriented business model and we are executing against this plan.

Let me start with our revenues. We have the largest sales pipeline we have ever had and with the acquisition of AHS, we have almost doubled our sales and account management teams. As a result, over the last few months, we have added six new channel partners. These new wins have an estimated annual revenue impact of $2 million and we are actively working to grow these revenues, these revenue bases by providing high participant satisfaction and expanding our service scope.

We have also added eight new direct customers. These are typically free of contracts which give us a steady book of business over time. And we have added two new customers for clinical research support. In addition, we continue to work with our large customer, whose study we have discussed is lying down on their bid for a potential extension to that study.

The University of Michigan Health and Retirement Study is a particularly exciting new win for us. If you refer to the press release issued on August 6, you will see that this is a large clinical study over a three year period. With our added focus on clinical research support and our new contracts at the University of Michigan, the Cleveland Heart Lab and others, we are well positioned to increase our client base.

We do not expect to realize the full revenue impact of these wins in 2015 but we will see a full year of activity in 2016 and beyond. And we continue to focus on selling our services through all three of these channels. We believe the momentum we are building in new sales demonstrates that customers value the services we offer and that we are gaining traction in the market.

Next, I’d like to talk about how our business model scales. We believe the combination of our screening capabilities and the assets brought to us by AHS improve our long-term ability to deliver consistent value and profitability. In the last 90 days, we have reaffirmed our belief and the potential scalability of our combined operations.

Slide 9 is a general guide to give you an idea of what our fully integrated company could deliver. This slide shows various revenue levels, which you can think of the stepping stones to building a $100 million business over the next three to four years.

From left to right, you can see that as revenue increases, we expect both cost of sales and SG&A to decrease as a percent of revenue improving our bottom-line. Our cost of sales has turned fixed cost components and our structure should not require significant admission cost as revenue grows.

We believe our combined capabilities position us to improve financial results quicker in a more consistent manner. Let me make it clear that this chart is for illustrative purposes only and it’s not guidance. Let me also make it clear that we believe our service scope, market opportunity and execution of our strategy will significantly enhance shareholder value.

Turning to Slide 10, our most important near-term goal is to finish integrating AHS while not missing a beat in satisfying customers. We are pleased with our progress and we are on schedule to be fully integrated by the end of the year. The work we have done in the first half of 2015 has positioned us for our strong second half which has historically delivered 60% of our annual revenue. We remained focus on delivering positive operating cash flows for the full year 2015, excluding one-time transaction and integration costs. I am also pleased to report that our biometric screenings and how they fit into wellness programs will be featured on a national morning talk show, the balancing act on the Lifetime channel on August the 25.

To conclude our prepared remarks, while we believe our platform and opportunities are not currently reflected in the company’s stock price, we have a powerful and exciting value proposition and a significant market opportunity. Beginning next month, we are looking forward to meeting with investors and analysts to share our story and we believe now it’s the time for investors to take a fresh look at our combined capabilities.

And now Tom and I will be pleased to answer your questions.

Thank you. [Operator Instructions] We’ll go first to Bill Sutherland with Emerging Growth Equities

Thanks, good morning, Henry. I missed a little bit of the call. I apologize. Could you just – I know you had one major customer that disengaged and you quote that in slide. How is return than otherwise churn than that?

But we had great quality service with them. They were quite happy and we are working to see if we can get that project extended. But churn in general is fairly low. We typically have continued – keep repeat business, it’s fairly insignificant.

William Sutherland

Yes, I mean, we are looking at, we sell it through those three channels. We sell through our channel partners and support that primarily on biometric screening. So that’s been a significant portion of the business and we continue to support them and that we consider them very strong partners. And we’ll continue to add partners there. We also sell direct to customers for any portion of our suite of products typically starting with biometric screenings and then adding the wellness programs on top of that. And then, separately from that, we will continue to use our health professional network to support the clinical research markets.

We have channel sales force that is focused on working with channel partners and they can upsell the AHS services as required as requested, but they are completely focused on the channel partners and we have a separate sales force focused on the direct customers who should make sure that we manage that channel conflict.

William Sutherland

Thank you, Bill.

Tom Collins

Sure, Tom. Thanks for the question. Actually, with the acquisition of AHS and also the winding down of that clinical research study, it spreads our revenue more across our customer base and we can create less of a concentration issue.

Which is one of the things when we look at the acquisition we like that one of the fact that they have more – the direct customers that we are able to kind of alleviate that concentration a little bit.
