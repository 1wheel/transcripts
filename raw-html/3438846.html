Thank you, Whitney. Good afternoon, everyone. And thanks for joining us on today’s call. It’s now over the year since Maheesh, my Co-Founder and I return to CafePress. We are delivering on our commitment to restore and stabilize profitability through focus, improved operation, smarter marketing and better product mix. Our focus on doing fewer things but doing them better is working.

We continue to stabilize and simplify operations in Q2. After discontinuing roughly 10% of the underperforming products last year, we took a bolder step this quarter and cut out more than 200 additional product types leaving us with the 400 best performing products in our portfolio.

These reductions allowed us to streamline the website experience for our customers, simplify operations and improve our gross margins. We have seen improve customer satisfaction scores, faster production and shipping turnaround, lower scrap rates and improving labor efficiency.

One remaining area that does not align with our future plan is the white label photo and fulfillment business we acquired as a part of EZ Prints in 2012. While we acquired useful relationships and technology from EZ Prints, the remaining business is deemed to be non-strategic and has a lower average order size and margin profile than the rest of CafePress. We intend to dispose this business entering advance negotiation to do so in the near-term.

The annual revenue associated with EZ Prints was about $20 million in 2014 and it did not contribute meaningfully to EBITDA. The planned divestiture of EZ Prints, along with our previous divestitures has significantly simplified the business and gives us the ability to focus on reenergizing the core.

The most significant changes to performance can be attribute to our product mix, pricing and promotion. But we are also making operational improvements. Earlier this quarter we announced cost control initiatives, including transitioning auditor firms and right-sizing our facilities and leases.

In terms of facilities, we plan to eliminate the Atlanta plant as a part of the EZ Prints divestiture, reduce the office space in California by approximately 50% and rescale our headquarters and manufacturing in Kentucky.

With the reestablishment of positive EBITDA, and a far more straightforward and healthy core business, we can now concentrate on stabilizing and growing the topline, optimizing the customer experience and energizing our customer relationships.

I would like to thank Doug Leone, Michael Dearing and Diane Irvine for their years of service, dedication and advice on the board of CafePress. At the same time, we're excited to have Ken McBride, Tony Allen and Nick Swinmurn on Board.

Ken is the CEO of Stamps.com. He played a crucial role in the company's turnaround and has since lead it through steady improvements to impressive success. Tony is the CFO of Sypris Solutions and brings extensive financial experience and expertise to our Board. Finally, Nick, the founder of Zappos, is a true pioneer in consumer e-commerce and brings valuable consumer centric experience and creativity to the company. We welcome the additional operational, financial and marketing council.

We believe that making strategic investments will kickstart an upward spiral restoring growth.

For the rest of 2015, we anticipate making steady improvements to our customer experience. Q4 will be here soon though and along with it, our seasonal website lockdown. So, while we will be very busy working on growth-oriented plans and projects, we expect 2015 website update and financial improvements to remain modest. We will be focused on traffic and site conversion gains, expanding relationships with our consumer channel partners and investing in our CRM database to improve lifetime value.

We are working on customer segmentation and targeting that will align with our new branding positioning and marketing campaigns that we expect to roll out in early 2016. We believe there are ample opportunities for improvement and look forward to developing and strengthening all aspects of customer acquisitions, retention and growth.

CafePress has a unique position in the industry and a solid value proposition. We’ve been aggressive and proactive, implementing significant changes in a short period of time. We are confident that our acute focus and diligent work revitalizing CafePress will create substantial opportunities for our company, customers and shareholders.

In the second quarter, we repurchased close to 0.5 million shares. Garett Jackson

Thank you, Fred. I will now review our financial results for the second quarter in more detail. All comparisons will be year-over-year unless otherwise noted. As Fred mentioned, we've given thoughtful considerations to the future of the white label fulfillment and photo printing business we acquired with EZ Prints in 2012.

Those products made up approximately 15% of our 2014 revenue. They had a smaller AOS and do not contribute significantly to the bottom line. After careful evaluations including tax and book value implications, we determined that divesting the business is the best course of action. We expect to conclude any transaction in the next quarter, but we do not anticipate the transaction will unlock significant value on disposal.

As such, we’ve take an impairment charge on the assets based upon the current deal terms. Request for EZ Prints, our results for EZ Prints are reported in discontinued operations for all periods presented. Results from continuing operations include CafePress.com and the remaining portions of our B2B business, which had previously been called CafePress Services. For future quarters, we're renaming CafePress Services as retail partner channels, which consist primarily of our offsite fees in our larger corporate shops.

Let’s turn to our second quarter results. CafePress reported net revenues for the second quarter of $21.8 million, a year-over-year decline of 25%. Revenue from CafePress.com was approximately $18.4 million or 85% of our total net revenue, representing a decline of approximately 30% year-over-year. The topline declines are short-term results of our stabilization strategy as we concentrated on unlocking the most profitable revenue streams and eliminating product and marketing channels with the lowest ROI.

CafePress Services had approximately $3.4 million in revenue or 50% of total net revenue and grew approximately 15% over the same period last year. The improving results were driven by fees from our retail partner channel. Our second quarter adjusted EBITDA was positive $0.7 million compared to a loss of $1.8 million a year ago. Adjusted EBITDA was 3.3% of revenue, a significant improvement from the negative 6.3% a year ago.

This marks the first quarter we have achieved positive adjusted EBITDA outside of our traditional peak season in over two years and it’s the second quarter in a row, we’ve made substantial improvements in profitability on a lower revenue base, both on sequential and year-over-year basis. We're excited to realize the positive impact that our focused efforts have had on our profitability and margins.

I will now review some key financial metrics. I encourage you to consult the tables accompanying our press release for full details. On a non-GAAP basis, our second quarter net loss from continuing operations was $0.5 million or $0.03 per diluted share, compared to a net loss of $2.4 million or $0.14 last year.

Gross margin on a non-GAAP basis was 41.1%, an improvement of over 500 basis points compared to the same period last year. The improvement was driven by lower production costs during the quarter, specifically materials and shipping. The removal of the white label phone fulfillment business from EZ Prints is another towards focusing on a higher margin core business.

On last quarter’s call, we discussed contribution margin. This non-GAAP metric consist of gross profit less variable sales and marketing expenses. We’ve chosen to break contribution margin out in our financial metrics, since we believe is an important indicator of progress in the business. We're pleased to report that we are making significant improvements both sequentially and year-over-year.

Contribution margin for Q2 2015 was $6.4 million or 29% of revenue, an improvement of 1,300 basis points over the previous year and an improvement of 700 basis points over Q1 2015. We believe these results reflect the collective efforts to maximizing marketing efficiency and improve our production processes. We will continue to drive toward a contribution margin that we believe will allow us to scale profitably.

For the quarter, our non-GAAP operating expenses totaled $9.8 million, a reduction of over $4 million versus last year. We continue to be mindful of expenses and are making progress toward rightsizing our cost structure with both short and longer-term initiatives.

Our sales and marketing expense on a non-GAAP basis was approximately $4.1 million, or 19% of revenue. This represents more than 600 basis point reduction over the last year, reflecting the continued impact of our gains in marketing efficiency and lower customer service costs. As Fred mentioned, we anticipate a modest reinvestment in sales and marketing to support our new branding strategy and ramp our marketing campaigns to stabilize the topline.

Our technology and development expense on a non-GAAP basis was approximately $2.8 million, or 13% of revenue this quarter, representing a $0.7 million reduction versus last year. This decrease is driven by cost savings in our web hosting services and realigning our technology teams. We anticipate this line will also increase as we start to staff and invest in our optimization strategies.

General and administrative expenses on a non-GAAP basis totaled approximately $2.9 million, or 13% of revenue this quarter. Expenses within G&A were down approximately $0.4 million, compared to last year, mainly attributable to reduction in legal costs.

On a GAAP basis, we posted a net loss from continuing operations of $1.1 million, or $0.06 per fully diluted share. That compares to a GAAP net loss of $4.2 million, or $0.24 per fully diluted share in Q2 2014. Our net loss for the quarter included restructuring charge of $0.5 million. The restructuring charges were predominantly associated with the termination of our California office lease and related employee severances.

As of June 30, 2015 we had approximately 400 employees nationally, including approximately 60 employees who were part of the discontinued EZ Prints business.

Within discontinued operations, we posted a GAAP net loss of $7.7 million, or $0.44 per fully diluted share. That compares to a GAAP net loss of $0.1 million, or $0.01 per fully diluted share in Q2 2014. Results from discontinued operations included an impairment charge of approximately $7.3 million from our planned divestiture of the EZ Prints business. We do not anticipate a gain on this divestiture.

Turning to our balance sheet and cash flows, we ended the quarter with $42.7 million in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments. We significantly improved our free cash flow, which we defined as adjusted EBITDA less CapEx over last year. We had a $0.2 million outflow in the second quarter, compared to $3.3 million outflow for the same period last year.

Our capital expenditures for Q2 totaled $0.9 million, compared to $1.5 million in Q2 2014, driven primarily by lower levels of investment in our planned infrastructure. Our basic and fully diluted weighted average shares outstanding were 17.5 million. As of June 30, 2015, we purchased just under 0.5 million shares of our common stock at a market value of approximately $2.3 million under our share repurchase plan.

To summarize, our results this quarter demonstrate the material progress we're making right-sizing CafePress to build the sustainable, profitable company. We believe our strategic divestitures and simplification measures leave us well-positioned for the future. We're pleased to point to our financial progress as we complete the stabilization phase of our strategy, specifically reduced operating expenses, coupled with improved margins, profitability and capital structure.

Higher margins and more sustainable overall business comes at the cost of lower revenue and volume in the short-term. However, we believe that this is the prudent path that we must take to return CafePress to growth and profitability. We are understanding our customer better and we explore additional growth channels. We intend to invest more heavily into building our brand and enhancing the customer experience.

Operationally, the remainder of 2015 will be used to further refine our infrastructure and continue to execute on right-sizing the company, which may result in additional one-time charges and some higher capital outlays. There are still many challenges we face, but we believe our cash position and improved profitability allows us the opportunity to invest in these areas to provide returns in the future.

I would like to thank the CafePress team for all of their hard work and dedication. We appreciate the support of our investors as we continue to diligently execute our plan to restore the health of the business in a sustainable way that we believe will drive value for our stakeholders.

With that, I will open the call to questions. Operator?
