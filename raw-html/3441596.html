Adaptive Medias' (ADTM) CEO John Strong on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 14, 2015 4:38 PM ET  |  About: Adaptive Medias, Inc. (ADTM) by: SA Transcripts

Executives

Omar Akram - Chairman of the Board

John Strong - Interim CEO

Analysts

Scott White - White Capital

Mark Cowen - Analyst

Adaptive Medias, Inc (OTCQB:ADTM) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 14, 2015 1:00 PM ET

Operator

Welcome to the Adaptive Media Second Quarter 2015 Financial Results Conference Call. My name is Heidi and I will be facilitating the audio portion of today's interactive broadcast. [Operator Instructions]. At this time, I would like to turn the show over to Omar Akram.

Omar Akram

Good afternoon and welcome to Adaptive Medias, Inc’s second quarter 2015 earnings call. As a reminder, this conference is being recorded. My name is Omar Akram and I am Adaptive Medias’ Chairman of the Board. Also with me today is John Strong, our recently named Interim CEO. If you do not have a copy of the press release, it’s available on the Adaptive Medias website at www.adapativem.com or you may call our Investor Relations firm at 818-280-6800 to receive it via e-mail.

Before we begin, I would like to take this opportunity to remind you that during the course of the call, management may make forward-looking statements which are subject to various risks and uncertainties. Actual results and the timing of certain events may differ materially from the results or timing predicted or implied by such forward-looking statements. And the reported results should not be considered as an indication of future performance. Further information regarding factors that could affect the company’s financial results is included in filings it makes with the Securities and Exchange Commission from time-to-time, including the section entitled Risk Factors in the Company’s SEC filings.

Also, I would like to remind you that during the course of this conference call, we will discuss the non-GAAP measures in talking about the Company’s performance.

During the second quarter of Adaptive Media achieved second quarter revenues of 1.1 million meeting our previously announced guidance. Our revenues for the quarter were large flat compared to our first quarter revenues of approximately 1.17 million. Revenues held steady during the quarter despite considering the slower online advertising spend in the first half of the year and also the spike in significant changes in Adaptive Media's sales force as we transition away our focus from our traditional low margin advertising and marketplace revenue to the higher margin and higher growth opportunities in hand with a launch of our mobile technology platform.

We expect our margins on our platform to well exceed 60% compared to the 20% to 30% margins from our traditional marketplace revenues. In the second quarter adjusted EBITDA which includes stock compensation expenses equal 1.8 million compared with 1.9 million in the first quarter of 2015. The improvement reflects lower sales and marketing and R&D expenses partially offset by higher legal and professional cost.

At the end of the second quarter we were pleased to announce the appointment of John B. Strong as our Interim CEO and today we're also excited to report that John has agreed to join the Board of Directors. John has over 30 years of senior level managerial, financial and investment experience with technology companies many of which have experienced dramatic growth and success. We're looking forward to leveraging this type of industry experience and having John lead Adaptive Media's emergence as one of the premier ad tech companies in the industry.

I will now hand the call over to John who will provide an update on our progress since our last investor conference call.

John Strong

Thank you for the introduction, Omar. I want to let everybody know I'm really excited to join Adaptive Media as both the Interim CEO and as a Board Member. There is no doubt there are a number of positive attributes at Adaptive Media in particular our proprietary technology and history of consistent revenue growth that helped me decide that this is where I want to focus all of my energy going forward.

Demand for online video content is one of the fastest growing spaces in technology today. I want to share with you a couple of recent statistics from Cisco's Visual Networking Index that was published recently this summer, by 2019 it would take an individual over 5 million years to watch the amount of video that will cross Global IP networks every month. Every second nearly a million minutes of video will cross these networks by 2019, globally mobile data traffic will increase 10 times between last year and 2019. Mobile data traffic will grow at a compound average growth rate of 57% between last year and 2019 reaching an astounding 24.2 exabytes per month. Global mobile data traffic will grow three times faster than fixed IP traffic as well through 2019, global mobile data traffic was 4% of total traffic last year and will be 14% of total traffic by 2019. And as we've discussed before, advertisers are embracing online video campaigns not only for its growing viewership but also because digital video advertising can be targeted to very specific users and track at a detailed level.

We believe Adaptive Media is uniquely positioned to help publishers and marketers navigate the changing world of digital media in content monetization across video and mobile to deliver these ads today's technology has to be able to accept multiple file types, it needs to work on multiple browsers within various operating systems and on various devices and screens all with various internet speeds. These complexities are really challenging and enormous.

One particular issue for advertisers is whether or not their ads whether they're video or static are viewable and we believe that unseen ads are a technology issue that we can address with our end to end platform. Adaptive Media's value proposition is offering all of these services on a single software as a service platform, Adaptive's comprehensive platform eliminates the usual administrative and technical hurdles of stitching together these multiple service providers for video hosting, video sharing across multiple viewing platforms, content management and acquisition and advertising services. Another issue for advertisers is that the measurement we've commented in the past that as online content and advertising continues to grow, the challenge is turning it into reliable results for each of the customer set segments, basically website publishers, content producers and advertisers. In the world of internet advertising the metrics are not quite as clear. Viewers often have a choice of which advertising they watch or click on and unlike traditional media audience demographics are much more varied making it more difficult to predict the return on advertising spending.

The Media Rating Council and the Interactive Ad Bureau has suggested measurement standards in some agencies and advertisers have taken it further by establishing their own standards. The ultimate goal here is having advertising and video content that consumers want to watch according to an article on media post, the problem for publishers is the lack of tools to address these standards. Again we believe that our end to end mobile video platform offers publishers the tools they need to meet these rising standards. Our technology platform, the media graph provides four major benefits to publishers and marketers who have historically faced the challenges of industry fragmentation and setting up, scaling and optimizing their video campaigns. These are easy content ingestion in content management, less reliance on multiple vendors, robust ad serving with programmatic and real time data functionality and prospering [ph] capabilities.

We believe our technology platform the Media Graph is the nexus of digital advertising and content monetization for the video and mobile sectors. This combined with our new Advanced Video Player which we have optimized for enhanced mobile first user experience should allow us to continue to capitalize on the exhilarating trend we see for increased video viewership by users on their smartphones and tablets devices Media Graph empowers publishers to efficiently and effectively manage video monetization with a self-service user interface programmatic offering, real time reporting and in a capable of operating at a massive scale. We believe our technology is not only unique but defensible. It has been built from the ground up with specific focus on integrating all of the various components of the digital ecosystem.

This barrier to entry is where the market has continued to produce fractional players versus a more comprehensive offering which we believe we have. Adaptive Media services are designed to work for a wide range of clients at different stages in their business developments. Clients can opt to use as many or as few services as they want. Our technology allows clients to customize our content and its business strategy is content and advertiser agnostic meaning that customers can choose to use their own content and avatars or pools or opt in to the advanced or the Adaptive Media stack. As I noted at the beginning of the call Adaptive Media has achieved our second quarter revenue is at $1.1 million steady with revenues of $1.17 million in the first quarter. It's important to note though that we achieved sales despite a significant transition in our sales and marketing efforts as we shift away from advertising sales to technology platform sales.

We're seeing increased spending by existing customers and an increase in the number of active customers adopting this solution. We believe that these trends reflect growing appreciation for our technology's efficacy, simplicity and the flexibility of the platform. Our gross profit for the first quarter amounted to $97,000 or 2.5% of the revenues compared to $197,000 or 17% in the first quarter of 2015. The sequential decrease in these gross profit margins in 2015 reflects a slowdown in low margin ad sales and the company's transition to emphasize ourselves with the high margin technology platform. Operating expenses for the second quarter increased to $3.6 million from $3.37 million in the first quarter. The increase stemmed primarily from higher legal fees and stock compensation expenses but was offset by lower sales and marketing expenses, notably general and administrative expenses remained under control, falling slightly to $1.26 million in the second quarter compared with $1.35 million in the first quarter ended March 31.

Sales and marketing expenses fell sharply in the quarter as we retooled our sales force and marketing efforts to focus on the Media Graph platform. Sales and marketing expenses totaled only $29,172 in the second quarter. Our research and development costs also dropped sequentially to $17,471 compared to $1057 in the first quarter.

We view continued investment in technology as a critical element in maintaining our leadership position in industry and as such we expect R&D expenses to increase in absolute dollars in future periods, however with a launch of our technology platform behind us the rate of increase is expected to slow going forward. General and administrative expenses declined to $1.263 million from $1.347 million in the first quarter of 2015. On a sequential basis general and administrative expenses were relatively stable in 2015 and we expect to keep our general and administrative cost footprint to a minimum requirement, in the first quarter GAAP EBITDA equaled $2.9 million compared with $2.5 million in the first quarter of 2015.

Adjusted EBITDA excluding stock compensation was a loss of $1.7 million in the second quarter compared with a loss of $1.9 million in the first quarter of 2015. Adjusted EBITDA continued to improve on a sequential basis reflecting the company's move from the development stage to commercialization of the Media Graph platform.

Loss from operations in the second quarter was approximately $3.6 million compared to a loss of approximately $3.17 million in the first quarter of 2015. The net loss for this period is approximately 3.7 million or $0.24 per basic diluted share compared to a net loss of $3.19 million or $0.23 per basic diluted share for the first quarter of 2015. Finally I want to broadly address some of our goals over the next two quarters and these goals remain unchanged from last quarter.

Our primary goal for 2015 is to grow our technology user base our business development efforts are focused on three customer categories publishers and content owners that have video content and need an easy efficient centralized technology platform solution for video content management, delivery, syndication and monetization. Second white label partner's, aggregators that provide desktop or display oriented advertising services to hundreds or thousands of websites and that need to provide a technology platform with video solutions or mobile solutions to supplement the services they currently provide to their website owners.

Thirdly syndication partners that have branded video content, these are video sponsored by product by brand advertisers that need a player and platform to syndicate the content and reach additional audiences. Our second quarter goal is to drive advertising demand or fulfillment through our platform while we don't require our customers to buy their advertising through our platform, we believe that we'll be able to demonstrate strong returns on advertising spend relative to its competition which will migrate more of their advertising dollars to our in-house platform.

Finally we'll continue to make improvements to our technology platform. Our that development will focus on two goals increasing performance for our publishing partners through better efficiency, higher fill rates, better cost per thousand impressions. Secondly creating a collaborative solution with the agencies and advertisers that could include planning, content monetization and strategic partnership opportunities. Both of these efforts will have the overarching goal is increasing our customer satisfaction and thus increased retention.

In summary we now have a scalable and tested technology platform [indiscernible] to build upon. Over the next several quarters you will see us make significant steps to increase our publisher base through our sales and marketing efforts and we look forward to providing investors with regular progress updates. We believe Adaptive Media today is stronger, nimbler and well-positioned for accelerated financial growth into 2015 and beyond. Demand for our advertising technology solutions has never been greater in our company's history as reflected by or eight consecutive quarters of growth. We believe 2015 will be a breakthrough year for Adaptive Media, our clients, our employees and our shareholders.

I will now open up the line for questions. Operator if you can assist?

[Operator Instructions]. You have few questions over the phone. Would you like to take those first?

John Strong

Yes.

Operator

Your first question comes from the line of John Harole [ph].

Unidentified Analyst

And taking a look at the current company's cash position it looks like this quarter is nothing short of an absolute disaster and if you're going to have to raise money to cover the cost of operations for the next two quarters you're looking at 60 million shares of $0.10 or quadruple what the current outstanding share count is now.

I mean with gross profit of $27,000 or 1.1 million in sales, isn't this the final nail in the coffin or what am I missing?

John Strong

We really don't believe that we're going to have trouble raising additional capital over the near term. We've always been able to in the past because we do show this growth and we have a good technology platform for people. As I go out on the road and explain to people the efficacy of the platform, we believe that the investor, public is going to understand that and we just don't believe we're going to have a hard time raising the money that we need to continue this.

Unidentified Analyst

Yes but you guys keep talking about raising money year after year or quarter after quarter yet your losses just went up and now your R&D expenses are going to go up. I mean it looks like there's no profit in sight for years if even there, I just don't get it. What's to be so excited about?

John Strong

We don't believe that, we obviously don't believe that, we believe that we're going to achieve breakeven and probability during 2016, we clearly see the growth in the very high margin Media Graph platform and I think that when investors see that growth and understand it better they're going to believe it too.

Unidentified Analyst

How can you break even in 2016 when you're losing 3.5 million a quarter?

John Strong

Well we're not going to have those kind of losses going forward.

Operator

Your next question comes from the line of Bill Robert from Spring Street Capital [ph]. Your line is open.

Unidentified Analyst

You guys keep talking about the transition of your business model to the higher margin Media Graph business, can you spend a couple of minutes and explain to me the specifics of Media Graph and how it competes with some of the big platforms out there such as [indiscernible].

John Strong

The Media Graph is a full stack advertising platform, so it's going to allow the users to do multiples of monetizing their content. The margins on this platform are as high as 80% where our marketplace business you know the advertising arbitrage business is roughly around 20% to 30% so it's two to three times as much margin, there's a lot more opportunities to monetize the content and video, it's focused video you know from the ground up. This player was built specifically for the new mobile video landscape which there are very few of these players out there that can say that most of them have been just kind of stitched together by necessity and this is a ground up solution built specifically for the needs of our clients and we think they're going to really, really be excited about using it. And so far that is exactly what we're seeing with the people that are beginning to on board on to the platform.

Operator

Your next question comes from the line of Scott White from White Capital.

Scott White

I have a couple questions also. First, I do feel that there is some real excitement about this Media Graph going forward. I know that you touched on this a little bit but basically what I'm hearing is there's an understanding that it's 65% margins at a lower burn rate and this is what you guys hope to achieve profitability sooner rather than later. Is this an accurate statement?

John Strong

Yes, and the margins they can be as high as 80% on this platform. So we have a lot of leeway in how we can approach the solutions for our customers as we on-board them and the solution itself. When you about two to three times the margins as our marketplace business I mean if the dollar start adding up an awful lot faster and R&D expenses are not going to be increasing dramatically going forward, we've already developed the product. It doesn't need a lot of spending left on it what we had to focus on is our sales efforts on-boarding new clients and users and that is really our primary focus at this point for the next couple of quarters. I think you'll see some dramatic improvement in the company going forward because of those efforts.

Scott White

Okay. Well I understand but can you give me an idea if the growth rate will continue after you reach profitability I mean what can we expect going forward? And if so will you be providing some type of accordance, some type of revenue guidance for the third quarter?

John Strong

I can't say that I can provide you with that guidance today as you know I'm relatively new in my position you know as you know. But we do expect the growth to continue, it's not a matter of we're just going to grow for a quarter or two and level out. This is going to be an ongoing growth story, there are many, many opportunities to onboard clients to this platform. It's a solution that is needed widely in the ad space and we believe that we're going to have as much of an opportunity to onboard clients as anyone in the industry.

Operator

Your next question comes from the line of [indiscernible]. Your line is open.

Unidentified Analyst

I just wanted to hear your growth strategy for Adaptive going forward and when and how you expect to achieve breakeven?

John Strong

The growth strategy as I’ve outlined is that we’re transitioning out into the Media Graph platform. So those platform or that platform has very, very high margins in comparison to the marketplace platform that we've traditionally been involved with that has as I said the 20% to 25% margins and the Media Graph. You know when we get up to as much as 80% margins on there. It's a dramatic difference and the opportunity is a larger opportunity, we just have to focus our sales team into onboarding new clients. We have a very, very large list of clients, we’re on-board clients today. The platform is nimble, it's easy to use. It's well received by the people that do use it and we have very, very high hopes for it going forward.

Unidentified Analyst

Can you elaborate a little bit on when and how you expect to achieve break even at all?

John Strong

Well we expect to achieve the breakeven because -- we’ve dramatically reduced our burn rate monthly for one. Our expenses going forward are as low as they have been in recent memory and the details that the sales that we should begin onboarding now with the Media Graph platform already double and triple the margins. So you know we see a pretty clear path, we believe going forward to having a breakeven for the company to be in a breakeven situation at the end of the year, or around the first quarter.

Operator

Your next question comes from the line of Mark Cowen. Your line is open.

Mark Cowen

I had a few questions, the first one surrounds your need to raise capital and where you think you might get it from. So the question really is how much money do you think you need? Are you willing to dilute the stock at this present price? And can you comment on the current status of your relationship with the previous gentleman who had been funding the company?

John Strong

As far as the company's relationship with that -- I guess you're probably referring to [indiscernible] is no longer with the company and you know obviously, we wish him luck in the future and other than that I really can't comment on further issues that we hope to resolve with him soon. As far as [indiscernible] I’ve spoken with him personally a couple of different occasions and I intend to see him personally in the very near future. So other than that I can't really say what kind of relationship I'm going to have with him except that I expected to be very productive and very positive. The conversation I've had with him thus far was positive. I don't expect that to be any different when I see him in person.

Okay. And then can you comment about raising capital at this present price which is I think close to 70% to 80% lower than the last price when capital was raised?

John Strong

Yes we believe the stock is very undervalued I mean I honestly believe that. I don't believe that these levels will remain for long as the story is told and people understand the story of the company. You know I think the stock is a great thing right now. I'm a stockholder myself and you know we're going to raise money but we're going to do it in a responsible manner. We've always been able to raise money when we needed it because the company story is a positive story. And that's what our intention is.

Unidentified Analyst

I would beg the question that if you are going to raise capital and dilute it heavily at this price that you allow people who have previously funded your company be able to be included in whatever private placements that you consider.

John Strong

I would encourage you to contact our IR firm and so that we're able to contact you and we're happy to -- then we’re happy to be able to talk with you about any of those issues going forward as we do contemplate raising money.

Unidentified Analyst

Okay and is there a price level that is your drop dead price level that you're willing to raise capital at? I mean if you're $0.20, is it $0.40, $0.60?

John Strong

We really haven't discussed that fully because you know it's not an issue that we're working on today. It's an issue for the next few weeks and I personally believe that's when we will address the price of the stock and at what levels we would be willing to raise money or debt financing.

Unidentified Analyst

Okay. And then with respect to board representation, there are presently both Omar and yourself on the board, word been pretty thin for quite some time. And discussion with respect to getting broader board representation.

John Strong

Well I'm very new on the board as you know one day, obviously we would be very, very open to discussions with industry leaders to join the leadership of the company. It's never something that we would ever preclude, but I think right now as far as my own opinion is I believe we have our work cut out for us over the next quarter or two to show the kind of growth and positive nature of the company's business going forward so that when it's time to have those discussions with candidates that might help us move forward into the next couple of years that we can attract the very best candidates because it is important to us.

Unidentified Analyst

And my last question is you made reference to the attrition of your lower margin business, focus on the higher margin business. But yet in this quarter is that transition took place? Your margins were actually lower. Now I know there's a lot of moving parts in the quarter but if you can you quantify or identify with those moving parts that are onetime in nature to be able to allow us to try to figure out moving forward what your margins should be based on your new model?

John Strong

Well on the new model we already know that the margins are going to be between 60% and 80%, so that’s how we price the model. We're not giving up on the marketplace business it's just not the focus. We believe that as we provide that high margin solution to our onboarding clients that when they come aboard they also will have some of the lower margin business that will come to us itself along with the video player business and so that's how we're approaching that. We're not focusing the sales team on the 20% margin business, we're focusing them on the 60% and 80% margin business. There will be some peripheral gains in the marketplace as onboard those other clients. The vagaries of the difference between you know margins a couple of points here and there, I can't speak to him exactly as I've only been here for a couple of weeks but I'll try to find some answers for that and get them back to you if you give me a day or two.

Unidentified Analyst

Absolutely, there is no rush. And then I guess sticking with that, is have you lost any major clients in your high margin business as this transition of management and your model and sales people have changed?

John Strong

Well in the high margin business we're just onboarding the first client, we're onboarding new clients. So [indiscernible] not that I'm aware of, everybody is new to this point and we intend to retain them as we move forward.
