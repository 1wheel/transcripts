Innocoll's (INNL) CEO Anthony Zook on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 14, 2015 11:43 AM ET  |  About: Innocoll (INNL) by: SA Transcripts

Start Time: 08:30

End Time: 08:56

Innocoll (NASDAQ:INNL)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 14, 2015, 08:30 AM ET

Executives

Anthony Zook - CEO

Gordon Dunn - CFO

James Tursi - CMO

Michael Myers - Head of Portfolio Operations

Robert Flamm - SVP, Russo Partners, IR

Analysts

Andrew Ang - Stifel Nicolaus

Jason Butler - JMP Securities

Operator

Greetings, and welcome to the Innocoll Second Quarter Financial Results Conference Call. At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. A question-and-answer session will follow the formal presentation. [Operator Instructions]. As a reminder, this conference is being recorded.

It is now my pleasure to introduce your host, Mr. Robert Flamm, Senior Vice President with Russo Partners. Thank you. Sir, you may begin.

Robert Flamm

Thank you, Donna. Good morning and welcome to our quarterly conference call. During today's call, members of our senior management team will review Innocoll's financial performance and business highlights for the second quarter ended June 30, 2015.

Before turning the call over to senior management, I would like to remind everyone that during this conference call any forward-looking statements made by management are intended to fall within the Safe Harbor provisions of the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995 and Section 21E of the Securities and Exchange Act of 1934 as amended. As set forth in our press release, forward-looking statements involve risks and uncertainties that may affect the company's business and prospects including those discussed in our SEC filings, which includes among other things our registration statement in Form F1 available from the SEC or our Web site. All of our projections and other forward-looking statements represent our judgment as of today, August 14, 2015 and Innocoll does not take any responsibility to update such information.

With us today are Tony Zook, Chief Executive Officer; Gordon Dunn, Chief Financial Officer; Dr. James Tursi, Chief Medical Officer; and Dr. Michael Myers, Head of Portfolio Operations.

At this time, I would like to turn over the call to Tony Zook. Tony?

Anthony Zook

Thank you, Robert, and welcome everyone. Just joining Innocoll last December, the team and I have articulated our core vision and strategy and we’ve been successfully executing against a set of key objectives in order to drive future success.

Our vision is to positively impact patient health and deliver sustainable growth by improving upon today’s medications for physicians and the patients they treat. This vision comes to life in the form of our late-stage product candidates; XaraColl, Cogenzia and CollaGUARD.

XaraColl is our implantable, bioresorbable collagen matrix in development with the aid to provide up to 72 hours of post-surgical pain relief as well as potentially reduce opioid consumption through the controlled delivery of bupivacaine.

Cogenzia is our topically applied collagen matrix designed to deliver gentamicin to treat diabetic foot infections. Cogenzia could potentially improve clinical cure rates when combined with systemic antibiotic treatment in wound care. This may lead to increased healing success and potentially a reduction in amputations.

CollaGUARD is our transparent bioresorbable collagen film in development to provide surgeons with an adhesive barrier with a potentially differentiating profile relative to ease-of-use in different types of surgical procedures.

In order to realize the promise of these brands for physicians and their patients, we’re focused on the effective delivery of our strategy, which consist of three distinct phases. Phase 1 requires the timely execution of our late-stage portfolio leading to successful commercialization.

Following initial market entry of our current portfolio, Phase 2 of our strategy is to leverage our leadership position in collagen-based drug delivery to support and grow XaraColl, Cogenzia and CollaGUARD in new indication areas and geographies.

Long term, we see additional opportunities to expand our product portfolio and create a platform for sustained growth. As this was quite an eventful quarter for Innocoll, let me now take a few minutes to briefly update you on the status of Phase 1, the timely delivery of our late-stage portfolio.

In keeping with the news flow in the quarter, I’ll start with Cogenzia. I hope you’ll agree that we’ve made considerable progress. As many of you know, both our U.S. and European focused Phase 3 trials COACT-1 and COACT-2 have been initiated and we’re actively recruiting and dosing patients. Although the trial is in the early stages, we anticipate completing enrollment before the end of the first quarter of 2016 and reporting top line results in the third quarter of 2015.

Of course, the big news for Cogenzia this quarter was the FDA designation of Cogenzia as a Qualified Infectious Disease Product or QIDP. Along with his update, James will give you a bit more background on this important designation by FDA.

However, I think it’s extremely important to acknowledge our QIDP designation is limited to products that could have a meaningful impact on the lives of patients, and confers on to Cogenzia benefits including a potentially accelerated regulatory review process.

An accelerated PDUFA date could translate into a U.S. launch as early as mid-2017, which is extremely exciting for us and more importantly for the patients who could benefit with such an innovative new medicine.

Now let’s spend a few moments on XaraColl where we’re also excited to share the significant progress made in our development program during the quarter. In early April, we announced top line results for the XaraColl PK study, which supported the use of the 300-milligram dose of bupivacaine for our Phase 3 trials.

With completion of the PK study and feedback from the end of Phase 2 meeting, we submitted our Phase 3 plan to the FDA. As we shared with you during our last call, it was our intention to gain FDA agreement and move forward with a single 300-milligram dose with the two Phase 3 trials, targeting patient enrollment as early as this quarter.

James will go into a bit more detail in just a moment, but I’m pleased to inform you that we’ve received FDA feedback and will be moving forward with the XaraColl program later this month.

As part of their feedback, FDA agreed with our recommendation to use a single 300-milligram dose of bupivacaine. In keeping with the development of other pain products, the FDA did request a primary endpoint of the two studies be Summed Pain Intensity Difference rather than the Silverman method composite endpoint previously communicated following the end of Phase 2 meeting.

While we believe that the Silverman method does give an integrated view of both pain intensity and opioid consumption, we had always intended to capture some pain intensity data separately as we knew the market and physicians would want to see the data expressed this way.

We believe that this modification of the clinical study endpoint does not impact the timing of or our confidence in the XaraColl Phase 3 program. As previously communicated, we anticipate completing enrollment before the end of the first quarter of 2016 and anticipating recording top line data from both studies during the second quarter of 2016.

Because of the rapid progress we’re making with our lead candidates, we have also been focusing on making the transition from a development stage to a commercial stage product company. Part of that process is recruiting executives who have extensive experience with commercial stage companies. My joining the company was part of that process as was the addition of James Tursi as CMO.

Early last month, we announced the appointment of Pepe Carmona as Chief Financial Officer effective September 1. Pepe has managed the financial operations of divisions within Novartis and Proctor and Gamble and is looking forward to meeting many of you when he joins the team in September.

Gordon Dunn will continue as CFO until September 1 and then will continue to contribute to our future success as Vice President and Finance Director of our European operations. Let me take this moment to thank Gordon for his outstanding contribution to the success of Innocoll to-date, including in particular assisting us in executing our successful IPO.

By the continuation of this transition, yesterday we announced that Rich Fante previously the U.S. President of AstraZeneca has joined us as Chief Commercial Officer and Head of Business Development. His addition to the team is quite timely particularly since the QIDP designation for Cogenzia may potentially accelerate our time to product commercialization in the U.S. market.

Rich is an expert in building first-class commercial organizations and strategies. A few in our industry can match Rich in commercial and business development capabilities and deliverables as he has personally led as well as managed the various teams responsible for some of the largest brands in our industry.

On a personal note, I can probably say that I’ve had the great fortune of leading and being associated with some of the best people and teams in our industry over my 30 years. I can truly say that with the addition of James, Pepe and Rich to our current team, I believe we now have the leadership framework in place to drive our future success as we transition to a commercial stage company.

With that, I’d like to turn the call over to James Tursi to review our clinical programs. James?

James Tursi

Thank you, Tony, and good morning, everyone. I will first provide an update on our progress with Cogenzia for the adjuvant treatment of diabetic foot infections. I’ll then follow this with an update on the status of XaraColl for postoperative pain relief and then finish with an update on our thoughts regarding CollaGUARD, our adhesion preventive barrier.

The Cogenzia Phase 3 studies known as COACT-1 and COACT-2 are underway worldwide. The studies are evaluating the topical use of the Cogenzia gentamicin sponge for the treatment of diabetic foot infection when used as an adjunctive systemic antibiotic in wound care.

The Cogenzia Phase 3 program was reviewed by FDA as part of a special protocol assessment or SPA and the primary endpoint is clinical cured infection evaluated after up to 28 days of topical gentamicin therapy. We will also be testing for pathogen eradication, reduction in the amputation and time to ulcer closure as secondary endpoint.

We anticipate completion of patient enrollment before the end of the first quarter of 2016 and expect top line results for both studies thereafter in the third quarter of 2016. Importantly, we received QIDP designation for Cogenzia for the treatment of moderate to severe diabetic foot infection.

QIDP stands for Qualified Infectious Disease Product, and this designation is granted under the GAIN Act or Generating Antibiotic Incentives Now Act, an Act intended to promote the development of antibacterial and antifungal drugs. QIDP designation allows the drug manufacturer to take advantage of certain incentives to help bring antimicrobials to market.

Antibiotics that have a QIDP designation receive upon request priority review typically shaving four months of review time as well as Fast Track designation, which enables additional consultation between FDA and antibiotics sponsors. QIDP designated antibiotics can receive an additional five years of marketing exclusivity in addition to the existing three-year exclusivity period at the time of approval.

The XaraColl program for the treatment of postoperative pain continues to move forward. As Tony mentioned, we sought feedback from FDA and this was received late last month. The FDA feedback supported the use of the 300-milligram dose as the single active dose in the Phase 3 study. This dose will be compared to a placebo sponge for use as postoperative analgesia following open hernioplasty with mesh.

As part of the feedback, the FDA also recommended we test the 24 hours Summed Pain Intensity Difference in line with the clinical development of other pain products as our primary endpoint as opposed to the composite endpoint of Summed Pain Intensity and total opioid use using the Silverman method.

Though this recommendation differs from the end of Phase 2 meeting feedback, we intend to change our primary endpoint to 24 hours Summed Pain Intensity Difference of XaraColl compared to a placebo sponge.

Now if you’ll recall, the XaraColl Phase 2 program tested both 100 milligram and 200 milligram doses of XaraColl in the treatment of pain following open hernioplasty with mesh. Now while the 24-hour Summed Pain Intensity was not the primary endpoint of either study, it was evaluated following completion of the study.

The endpoint was successful in one of the small Phase 2 studies, the study evaluating the 100 milligram dose. And when pulling data from both studies, the 24-hour Summed Pain Intensity was significantly improved compared to that of the placebo sponge. The Phase 3 studies will include approximately 300 subjects each and will be conducted in parallel in the U.S.

So we’ve increased the size of the studies slightly from our original plan to ensure the safety database is adequate for submission as FDA has reaffirmed their request that we submit a safety database with 500 subjects. This change in study size will just help us to ensure we meet this criterion without difficulty.

While the FDA feedback required changes in the study protocol, we still expect to initiate the Phase 3 study before the end of the third quarter 2015, and we anticipate enrollment will be completed before the end of the first quarter 2016 with top line data in the second quarter.

As Tony mentioned, Phase 1 of our corporate strategy was to execute upon our clinical plans in a timely fashion. We’ve been focused on this endeavor. And now that our Phase 3 programs are moving forward for both Cogenzia and XaraColl, we will direct more attention to CollaGUARD.

CollaGUARD is our collagen anti-adhesion barrier intended to prevent postoperative adhesions. We’ve initiated work to design the clinical program that we believe will enable potential approval in the United States. This program will be designed to test CollaGUARD against a controlled procedure in which no adhesion barrier is used.

We intend to begin interactions with the FDA in the fourth quarter of 2015 with a goal of starting clinical work in the U.S. in early 2016. We plan to provide you with additional details on our CollaGUARD program before the end of this year.

Thank you for your attention. And with that, I’ll turn it over to Gordon to review our financial performance for the quarter. Gordon?

Gordon Dunn

Thank you, James. Our financial statements for the quarter and the first half of the year reflect our focus on executing on our clinical development plans for Cogenzia, XaraColl and CollaGUARD.

R&D spending have significantly increased versus a year ago of €4.4 million in the second quarter of 2015 from €0.5 million in the second quarter of 2014, primarily driven by the initiation of our two Phase 3 clinical trials for Cogenzia and the preparations for initiating our two Phase 3 clinical trials for XaraColl. We expect that R&D spending will continue to increase as our Cogenzia Phase 3 trial continue to enroll patients and we start enrolling the XaraColl Phase 3 trial this quarter.

General and administrative expenses were another driver of the expense line increase. G&A expenses, excluding stock-based compensation, were €3.0 million for the second quarter of 2015 as compared to €1.6 million for the second quarter of 2014, primarily due to expenses associated with becoming a public company, continued investments in our infrastructure to support our increased clinical activity as well as initial preparations for commercialization of our products. The future dollars expense line with be the continued implementation of our commercial plans for Cogenzia and XaraColl.

As of June 30, 2015, cash and cash equivalents totaled €46.7 million or $52.2 million as compared to €45.6 million or $55.4 million as of December 31, 2014. Tax as of June 30, 2015 included approximately 16 million net proceeds from our follow-on offering in April. In addition, we have credit facilities available up to €25 million from the European Investment Bank facility, which we signed in March but have not yet drawn.

Without resort to additional financing options available to us, existing cash and cash available through the EIB facility is expected to be sufficient to fund the company's operations and manufacturing expansion through the first half of 2016. Other parts of our financial statements including non-cash charges for stock-based compensation and warrant liability are included in the press release and our Form 6-K already filed with the SEC. We’ll be happy to answer questions about any of our financials in the Q&A.

Thank you. I’ll now turn the call back to Tony.

Anthony Zook

Thanks Gordon. So as you’ve heard, we continue to focus on our strategy and execute on our key objectives. I’d like to now open the call to your questions. Donna?

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

Thank you. The floor is now open for questions. [Operator Instructions]. Our first question is coming from Annabel Samimy with Stifel. Please proceed with your question.

Andrew Ang

Yes. This is Andrew in for Annabel. I just had a couple of questions. First, with the funds currently in hand and seeing that you’re sufficiently funded through the first half of 2016, do you foresee prioritizing which programs to pursue first? And if so, what programs would you be prioritizing to see through first?

And my second question is, I understand you already have begun the high level of recruiting for your commercial strategy. What sort of pre-commercial activities will you be engaging in? Are you going to be seeking partnerships for these programs once they hit the market, or any color on that would be great? Thank you.

Anthony Zook

Thanks, Andrew. It’s Tony. I’ll take a shot and then if James or Gordon wants to chime in. I think relative to the priority, Andrew, we’ve always been very, very clear on what they were going to be. When we started through the course of the year, we said our number one, two, three and four priorities were getting both Cogenzia and XaraColl through the Phase 3 process and running those programs in parallel. And that continues to be among our highest priorities.

And so those from a clinical perspective has always been job number one. Likewise, running in parallel was investing in our manufacturing capacity in Saal, which was another reason we were pursuing the debt facility with EIB. And so those three big deliverables remain, I think, among our highest priorities. And as James said, we are now beginning to put a little bit more color and feel behind CollaGUARD, which we plan to share those plans with you later in the year. And so those have been and continue to be our highest priorities.

As you noted, with the addition of Rich, we are beginning to put a stronger focus point on our commercialization strategy and plan. In the short term, Andrew, what we are doing is spending a lot of time and energy working through the development of our brand plans, and as you might envision that entails a tremendous amount of work on brand transitioning, brand strategy, resource commitments and allocations, targeting from a customer perspective. We are beginning to assess pricing in the marketplace, et cetera.

So there’s a tremendous amount of work that goes well into the early stage of the commercialization long before we have to make any final decisions relative to commercialization on ourselves or if we were to entertain partnerships. We plan to share our thoughts in more detail with you by the end of the year. But that’s the work that’s ongoing and why we need someone of Rich’s caliber to join the team. I hope that helps.

Andrew Ang

Yes, definitely. Thank you so much for that.

Operator

Thank you. [Operator Instructions]. Our next question is coming from Jason Butler of JMP Securities. Please proceed with your question.

Jason Butler

Hi. Thanks for taking the questions. I guess I just wanted to follow up on James’ comments around the change in the primary endpoint for XaraColl. I guess could you just expand a little more thinking about the Phase 2 hernioplasty results that you have and the data for the 100 milligram versus the 200 milligram, just if you could give us a little more color on why you’re so comfortable that the risk profile is not changing now that you’re not using the Silverman method? Thanks.

Anthony Zook

Okay, sure, Jason. I’ll turn it to James for that.

James Tursi

Jason, thank you for the question. So as we design the Phase 3 program, part of that design process was going through the various endpoints from the Phase 2. So, this endpoint is not a new endpoint to us. It’s not an endpoint that we’ll say was not a potentially expected endpoint when it came to these studies. The Silverman method gave you a report of the potential benefit of combining both opioid use as well as the Summed Pain Intensity.

The way that we’ve designed the study now is essentially we’ve split those two. We still have the Summed Pain Intensity as the primary and we have as a key secondary endpoint that total opioid use. So from a commercialization standpoint, it puts us in a – we think potentially a more competitive position because now we’ve got endpoints that are literally more comparable to what’s been done out there and it also gives us that opportunity to address the opioid use as partner key secondary endpoint.

Jason Butler

Okay, great. That’s helpful. And then just a follow up, a broad competitive question. Can you just talk about how you see the use of bupivacaine versus the monotherapy and in combination with anti-inflammatories and how you think how the market is broken out from that perspective? And then again, how you broadly see the market growing with multiple competitors in this space?

Anthony Zook

Well, Jason, I’ll be happy to take a first crack at it and Michael, if you want to weigh in, by all means. My own personal view is as you know, I think Pacira has done a really nice job in laying the foundation for the necessity of having longer-acting bupivacaine products out there and now he’s engaging for patients going through various types of surgeries. So I think they laid an excellent foundation. And even with their very strong capabilities, we’re still seeing penetration rates in the very low single digits.

And so we think with us coming into the marketplace, we’ll see expansion and growth of the usage of bupivacaine and we believe efficacy ultimately is what’s going to be the determining factor in this marketplace. Fixed combinations or single entity agents, at the end of the day it’s going to be which company can step forward and show that in fact we are extending the pain management relief beyond the 24 hours.

And so I personally believe that we’re going to see increased utilization within this space. I think it will be driven mostly by the single agents, but fixed dose combination may have a place. It certainly represents a higher hurdle through the eyes of FDA. But at the end, single of fixed dose combination is going to come down to who’s been able to extend the pain management effect longer than the 24 hours that I think has been established with Pacira.

Michael Myers

Hi, Jason. Just to add to that, I think Tony’s exactly right. It does come down to the strength of your technology and if you’re technology platform can deliver the full dose without the need to add an additional agent or increase the size of the dose, then I think you can enter the program with a high degree of confidence. And that’s where we find ourselves. We feel very well positioned moving forward from here.

Jason Butler

Very helpful, thank you, and congratulations on all the progress.

Anthony Zook

Thanks, Jason.

Operator

[Operator Instructions]. Mr. Zook, at this time I’d like to turn the floor back over to you for any additional or closing comments.

Anthony Zook

Thank you very much, Donna. In summary, let me again say it’s been a productive first half of the year for us at Innocoll. We secured the necessary financial headroom through a combination of debt financing and an equity capital raise to execute on our late-stage program.

We’ve advanced Cogenzia and very soon XaraColl into Phase 3 testing against the aggressive timelines we anticipated. In parallel, we have initiated our manufacturing expansion plans in Saal and everything is progressing to plan. We’ve made some additions to our leadership team to prepare for the commercialization of our portfolio.

Again, I’d like to thank everyone for joining us on our second quarter financial and operating results conference call. Have a good remainder of your day, everyone.

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, thank you for your participation. This concludes today’s teleconference. You may disconnect your lines at this time and have a wonderful day.
