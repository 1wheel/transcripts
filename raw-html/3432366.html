China New Borun's (BORN) CEO Jinmaio Wang on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 12, 2015 10:53 AM ET  |  About: China New Borun Corporation (BORN) by: SA Transcripts

China New Borun Corporation (NYSE:BORN)

Q2 2015 Results Earnings Conference Call

August 12, 2015, 08:00 AM ET

Executives

Jinmaio Wang - Chairman, President, CEO

Yuanqin Chen - CFO

Ann Yu - Chief Strategy Officer

Analysts

Sarah Dai - Hillhouse Capital

Hover Chen - Caledonia Investments

Operator

Good day, everyone and welcome to the Second Quarter 2015 Earnings Conference Call for China New Borun Corporation. Today's conference is being recorded.

Before we get started, I am going to review the Safe Harbor statement regarding today's conference call. Please note that the discussion today will contain certain forward-looking statements made under the Safe Harbor Provisions of the U.S. Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995. These forward-looking statements are subject to risks and uncertainties that may cause actual results to differ materially from our current expectations.

To understand the factors that could cause results to materially differ from those in the forward-looking statements, please refer to our Annual Report on Form 20-F filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission on April 22, 2015. The Company does not assume any obligation to update any forward-looking statements except as required under applicable law.

At this time, I would like to turn the call over to Mr. Terence Chen, Chief Financial Officer of China New Borun Corporation, for opening remarks and introductions. Please go ahead, sir.

Yuanqin Chen

Thank you, operator. Thank you, everyone for joining us for China New Borun's second quarter 2015 earnings conference call.

Joining me today from Management are Jinmaio Wang, Borun's Chairman and Chief Executive Officer and Ann Yu, the Company’s Chief Strategy Officer. For today's call Mr. Wang will provide a quick review of our business and industry fundamentals. Following that, Ann will offer more details on our operational developments and updates. After Ann, I will discuss our second quarter 2015 financial details.

I would like to note that on this call today, all of our financial results will be referred to in Chinese RMB unless otherwise noted.

At this point, I would like to introduce Mr. Wang, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of China New Borun. Mr. Wang will speak in Mandarin and I will translate. Mr. Wang?

Jinmaio Wang

Hello everyone and thank you for joining our second quarter 2015 earnings conference call. We are ecstatic in being able to deliver strong financial results for the second quarter of 2015 that significantly exceeded our topline expectations. Driven by improved demand from baijiu industry, we were able to increase our sales volume of edible alcohol by more than 11% year-over-year while maintaining a stable average selling price.

Second quarter total revenue grew 7.7% year-over-year while gross margin expanded more than 200 basis points to 12.2%. As a result, net income grew 81.7% year-over-year to RMB38.1 million in the second quarter of 2015. It is encouraging to see the by the baijiu industry is showing visible signs of turnaround having gone through a severe strategy due to the effect of anticorruption campaign that began to sound tough.

According to industry statistics total baijiu output rose in first half of 2015 expanded by nearly 100% basis points, grew to approximately 3.5% driven primarily by mass consumption. It is worth noting that Heilongjiang Province where our packing facility is located led to the consumption growth of baijiu. Output in Heilongjiang in the first half of 2015 grew by nearly 18% which is far above the national growth rates of 3.5% during the same period.

This mass consumption growth driver is more sustainable as [indiscernible] baijiu products are now more affordable to ordinary consumers, while at the same time one more baijiu brands have adopted China’s booming e-commerce in largely distribution and enabling greater consumer access.

In addition we have said last year in the second quarter of 2015 the top brands have posted their price lows and hosted the practice of cutting ex-factory price rationally. They currently are more focused on offering better product mix which is creating innovative marketing and encouraging supply chain efficiency. Accordingly, the pricing of baijiu products has stabilized while volume for mix quick [ph] baijiu experience growth and volume for high grade baijiu is also getting up.

Another positive change is the ongoing structure adjustment in the baijiu industry that should continue to lead industry to the more healthy, rational and sustainable equilibrium. As we have discussed in our previous call, we are observing three major structure adjustments in baijiu industry. They are first, the industry is consolidating the small and inefficient producers are exiting the market.

Second, large scale manufactures means relatively strong brands and better operating efficiency are gaining market share by reducing prices for premium products or introducing lower priced products. And third, mergers and acquisitions are accelerating as quality spread makes us with strong brands and cash positions buy out local brands to quickly expand into the local market.

Reflecting the positive trend in the baijiu industry, demand for baijiu alcohol is improving, in addition the edible alcohol industry in similarity. Ongoing structure change that should also lead industry to a more healthy rationale and sustainable equilibrium. The edible alcohol industry is also consolidating as small and inefficient producers are exiting the market.

As the number of edible alcohol producers significantly contracted, we have seen a much improved competitive landscape, where we are able to maintain stable pricing while extending shipment volumes which ultimately leads to strong revenue and the profit growth. We believe this improved competitive landscape and the stable pricing for edible alcohol will likely sustain for the remainder of 2015.

Furthermore as we had already proactively upgraded our production facilities and effected partial maintenance during the first quarter of 2015 and as such we are able to shorten our annual maintenance period in the third quarter which should help drive higher revenue and profits for second half of 2015.

In summary, as one of the most efficient edible alcohol producer with significant cost advantage, we feel confident about our solid market position and given the improved industry fundamentals, we anticipate greater profits for full year 2015. At this point, I will turn the call over to Ann Yu for more details on operational developments and update.

Ann Yu

Thank you, Mr. Wang and Terence. Good day everyone and appreciating you taking the time to join us on today’s call. We are very pleased to report strong second quarter driven by the improving demand and stable average selling price for edible alcohol. The quarterly [ph] our capacity utilization reached 96% during the quarter, the highest level in the most recent two years. Our proactive action maintenance period in the first quarter will also allow us to have a shorter summer annual maintenance this year as Mr. Wang and Terence have mentioned earlier. We anticipate this year summer maintenance period will be only two weeks compared with four weeks in 2014.

As such, we anticipate the capacity utilization of edible alcohol for the upcoming third quarter will remain quite healthy. Reflecting the improved industry fundamentals and market environment, the average selling price of edible alcohol was stable in a range of RMB5100 to RMB5150 per ton during the quarter. Furthermore, as we continue to benefit from our strong corn sourcing capability, our cost of corn during the quarter decreased by 4.8% to approximately RMB1870 per ton from RMB1970 RMB per ton in the second quarter of 2014.

The improved cost of corn and higher capacity utilization combined to drive our growth margin expansion by 210 basis points to 12.2% comparably 10.1% in the second quarter of 2014. In light of better than expected demand from the baijiu industry, we have enlarged our core, our quality process during the second quarter in order to ensure adequate inventory and profit in price for our upcoming production needs.

During the second quarter of 2015 we could purchased approximately an additional 100,000 tons of corn from granaries. On top of the 350,000 tons of corn we could purchase in the three weeks quarters. With our provinces [ph] cost advantage we remain very optimistic with our profitability in 2015.

In addition as we mentioned in our last call, authorities in Heilongjiang Province including the Finance Bureau, Food Bureau and Agriculture Development Bank of China, this April generally released guidance for subsidies to corn processing entities with over 100,000 tons of corn capacities. Although the final subsidy is not yet confirmed given that Borun is one of the largest corn processing companies in Heilongjiang Province we participated in the designated fund and we believe that we may be entitled to a fixed government bond which will further boost our profitability in 2015.

Meanwhile we also estimate the average selling price for our byproduct, have also stabilized. The price of DDGS Feed which is nearly double-digit decline in the prior quarter due to China Government's recent move to aid import restriction on corn stabilized in the second quarter. Although the average selling price level of our byproducts are still much lower than those of the same period in last year we believe that they will not trend down as they did in the past few months.

Now, let me provide you with a quick view of our CPE business. As we mentioned in our last call, in light of the strong market acceptance of our CPE product, we have moved to double our CPE production capacity to 25,000 tons. During this phase 2 for CPE our top priority was to install the new production lines while it is effecting [indiscernible] phase 1 production lines.

And in this second quarter we completed both missions and to our pleasant surprise, the progress of our CPE business is once again ahead of trend and we were able to reach a better utilization of 91%. With the expanded production capacity, total sales for CPE grew 56.5% year-over-year to RMB34.8 million. We have elected to report that at this revenue level the CPE business is profitable and as the CPE market is not still very recommended, we believe that we can continue to further expand our production capacity to gain greater market share.

Before I hand the call over to our CFO to discuss financial details, I would like to quickly highlight our solid financial position and capital projection. As you may remember, in January and April 2013 we issued a corporate bond totaling RMB5000 million, the year-year fixed rate bonds included a right to for the bond holder to sell back in whole or in part at face value to Borun at the of the second year during the life of the bond. Given our sustained positive net income and solid operating cash flows, during the second quarter of 2015 we put - tied a portion of the corporate bond with the principles totaling RMB140 million.

We feel very comfortable that our modern adequate tax position will not only have a very low working capital and purchase requirement, but also are ready to further enhance our capital structure. Meanwhile, we continue to benefit from our strong local presence and solid relationship with local banks as we also received a short-term bank borrowing of RMB29 million during the quarter.

It is worth nothing against the interest rate of the newly secured short-term borrowing is now relatively cheaper as the People's Bank of China has lowered its benchmark rate and reserved ratios on several occasions in 2014 and 2015. As a reference, the current short-term basic interest rate guidance for the People's Bank of China is 4.6% and even with a premium of 20% to 30%, the effective interest cost of short-term bank borrowings is much lower than that interest rate of 9.1% for our corporate bonds. We plan to further take advantage of cheaper financial resources to optimize our current capital structure and properly tie this long-term bond at the maturity date in early 2016. And that was my part.

Now, let me turn the call over to Terence Chen, our CFO for a view of the financial performance in the second quarter of 2015.

Ann Yu

Thanks Ann. Now I’d like to share with you additional details about our financial performances. For the second quarter of 2015, our total net revenue increased by 7.7% year-over-year to RMB720.6 million, which was mainly driven by strong sales of Borun increase in edible alcohol.

Now let us look into the revenue breakdown during this quarter. Revenues from edible alcohol increased by 11.2% to RMB473.8 million with the sales volume increased by 11.2% to approximately 92,900 tons while ASP unchanged at RMB5102 per ton compared with the same period of last year.

Revenue from DDGS Feed increased slightly to RMB159.9 million, with sales volume increased by 11.1% year-over-year to approximately 82,400 tons and ASP decreased by 10% year-over-year to RMB1940 per ton.

Revenue from liquid carbon dioxide decreased by 53.8% to RMB5.2 million. Sales volume increased by 6.4% year-over-year to approximately 34,800 tons while ASP decreased by 56.5% year-over-year to RMB151 per ton.

Revenue from crude corn oil decreased by 3.3% to RMB46.9 million with sales volume increased by 7.2% year-over-year to approximately 7,200 tons, and ASP decreased by 9.8% year-over-year to RMB6,483 per ton.

Revenues from CPE increased by 63.2% to RMB34.8 million with sales volume increased by 66.5% to approximately 4,100 tons and ASP decreased by 2% to RMB8,547. Our gross profit increased by 29.8% to RMB87.7 million from RMB67.5 million during in the prior year period. Gross margin expanded to 12.2% compared to 10.1% in the second quarter of 2014. The increase of gross margin was primarily attributable to the drop of 4.8% in average comp off partly offset by decrease in average selling price of by-products. Due to the higher gross margin, income from operations increased by 30.8% to RMB72.3 million in the second quarter of 2015, from RMB55.3 million in the same period of 2014.

G&A increased to RMB13.5 million from RMB10.7 million in the same period of last year, while selling expenses, as a percentage of total revenue increased to 0.26% in the second quarter of 2015 compared with 0.23% a year ago.

Interest expense was RMB24 million in the second quarter of 2015, decreased from RMB26.7 million in the same period of 2014 and RMB26 million in the prior quarter. Exceeding [ph] in the interest expense from last quarter was primarily attributable to the repayment of our corporate bonds during the previous quarters.

Income tax expenses in the second quarter of 2015 were RMB12.7 million representing an effective tax rate of 25%. Our net income increased by 81.7% year-over-year to RMB38.1 million from RMB21 million a year ago. Diluted earnings per share and per ADS were RMB1.48 in the second quarter of 2015. The company had 25,700,000 weighted average basic and diluted shares outstanding during the quarter ended June 30, 2015.

On the balance sheet side our cash and bank deposits balance decreased to RMB516.1 million during the quarter from RMB830.4 million as of December 31, 2014. The decrease in our cash in the bank deposits were mainly due to the corn purchase or cash outflow of approximately RMB260 million.

As Ann discussed earlier, we anticipate the recovery of baijiu industry will continue for the remainder of 2015 and accordingly, we have strategically increased our corn purchase volume in the second quarter of 2015. At June 30, 2015 our inventory balance increased to RMB737.6 million and net trade accounts receivable balance totaled RMB476.5 million.

That wraps up the financial review and let me mention our business outlook. For the third quarter of 2015, we believe that demand for edible alcohol will remain solid driven by a turnaround in baijiu industry. At the same time we plan to conduct a short-term maintenance period this summer. Accordingly, we currently estimate revenue for the third quarter of 2015, will be in the range of RMB630 million to RMB670 million, an increase of approximately 19.1% to 26.6% over the same quarter of 2014.

This forecast reflects our current and preliminary estimates of markets and operating conditions and customer demands, which are all subject to change.

This concludes our prepared remarks. Operator, we will open the call up for questions.

Operator

Thank you. We will now begin the question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions] The first question comes from Sarah Dai from Hillhouse Capital. Please ask your question.

Sarah Dai

Hi, thank you for taking my question and congratulations for the great quarter. I have some questions regarding edible alcohol and baijiu industry. So how do you see the pricing trend for edible alcohol for the second half of this year?

Yuanqin Chen

Thank you for your question Sarah. I believe that for the remainder of the year 2015 the pricing for edible alcohol will remain stable.

Sarah Dai

Okay and you also mentioned the sales volume of edible alcohol is quite good, especially in Heilongjiang Province and so how much of your sales to that market is Heilongjiang and do you see opportunities to further expand your volume or your market share in this province?

Yuanqin Chen

Yes, as you know, our subsidiary Daqing Borun is located in Heilongjiang, so lot of our key customers are also in the Heilongjiang Province, so sales of edible alcohol to the Heilongjiang accounts for more than 30% of our total revenue for edible alcohol. And considering the shipment cost, we will definitely not shift the edible alcohol from Shandong and to sell edible alcohol in the Heilongjiang Province. So I do not see any opportunities to specifically, significantly to expand our volumes or market share in the Heilongjiang Province.

Sarah Dai

Okay. I have some followup on the corn price. So, you mentioned also you had purchased another is that 100,000 tons in the second quarter, so what is the average cost compared with the ones you brought in the first quarter which I think is supposed to be the harvest season?

Yuanqin Chen

Yes, we have increased our corn purchase in the second quarter to meet the production needs in the third quarter and average corn cost is a little bit higher, less than 1% higher than the ones we bought in the first quarter.

Sarah Dai

Okay, good. So how long do you anticipate this additional purchase to support your expanding production volume base second half?

Yuanqin Chen

I suppose those inventories, I suppose the corn we have stored now can meet the production needs to late October or early November.

Sarah Dai

So when does the next harvest season start from, so around November, is that right?

Yuanqin Chen

Yes, that's right.

Sarah Dai

Okay, okay. Great, thank you. That's all my questions.

Yuanqin Chen

Thank you, Sarah.

Operator

The next question comes from Hover Chen from Caledonia Investments. Please ask your question.

Hover Chen

Yes, hi, thank you for taking my questions and congratulations on a great quarter. And it looks like from your guidance 2015 looks like it's going to be a strong growth over 2014, so again congratulations.

My question is really centered around a couple of items. One is the new CPE business, it looks like even though you just built a new line, but it was ramping up nicely in the quarter and I think you mentioned the success rate - utilization was 91% in the quarter. What do you think the maximum utilization will be on a sustainable basis for the CPE business?

Ann Yu

Thank you Hover, for your question. Yes, we are very happy to see the great feedback from our CPE customers. Our CPE products are well accepted and yields in many different interest fees. As the whole CPE market is very huge and still fragmented, we are very optimistic to continue this high utilization rate at or above 90%.

Hover Chen

Okay, so were you or are you considering to further expand the capacity of the CPE product fits, you are ready to release – utilization?

Ann Yu

We have just to set free that board with CPE capacity in last quarter and got very positive feedback from the markets, but we are now still evaluating the huge market, the potential customers and profitability. So it is still too early to talk about the further expansion because we still need more time to evaluate it.

Hover Chen

Okay, understood. All right, so looks like the core business is rebounding nicely and the new business also did well so it sounds good. Another question regarding the capital structure. So you mentioned that you are preparing for the bond repayment earlier 2016 and the question is given the fairly large amount, will you have enough cash to support your daily operation and also your corn feed project should you choose to pay off the bond in early 2016?

Yuanqin Chen

I will answer your question Hover. I'm definitely sure that we are having this cash provision to pay the corporate bonds earlier in 2016 since we have more than RMB600 million cash on hand and also we have already purchased our corns for the production needs until November. So it is more than, we stored more than RMB600 million corn. So this corn will be turned into the cash production in foreign corns [ph]. So I think we have enough cash to support our daily operations and also the next harvest season's corns are purchased and also we can prepay corporate bonds.

Hover Chen

Okay, that's good to know. Just quickly, you mentioned also the interest rate of short term borrowing now is lower than your fixed term on the bond. Is there any opportunity for you to perhaps consider getting additional short term borrowings to pay back some of this long-term bond and/or finance your daily operations?

Yuanqin Chen

Yes, of course, after we have repaid the long-term corporate bonds we will consider to increase some short term loans to supply or to support our daily operations.

Hover Chen

Okay, great. Well it sounds like everything is moving forward quite well, so congratulations and I look forward to hearing from you again next quarter.

Yuanqin Chen

Yes, thank you, Hover.

Operator

There are no further questions at this time. I would now like to hand the conference over to your speaker, Mr. Terence Chen for closing remarks. Please go ahead sir.

Yuanqin Chen

Thank you, operator. Once again on behalf of the entire China New Borun Management Team, we would like to thank you for your interest and participation in this call. Thank you for joining us today and this concludes our second quarter 2015 earnings conference call. Thank you, again.

Operator

This concludes our conference for today. Thank you for participation, good bye.
