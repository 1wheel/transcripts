Cosi's (COSI) CEO R. J. Dourney on Q2 2015 Results - Earnings Call Transcript

Aug. 13, 2015 8:21 PM ET  |  About: Cosi, Inc. (COSI) by: SA Transcripts

Cosi, Inc. (NASDAQ:COSI)

Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call

August 13, 2015 05:00 PM ET

Executives

R. J. Dourney - President & CEO

Miguel Rossy-Donovan - CFO

Analysts

Robert Routh - FBN Securities

William Meyers - Miller Asset Management

James Kahn - Oppenheimer

Jeffrey Matthews - RAM Partners

Operator

Good day, ladies and gentlemen, and welcome to the Second Quarter 2015 Così, Inc. Earnings Conference Call. My name is Janice and I will be your operator for today. At this time, all participants are in a listen-only mode. Later, we will conduct a question-and-answer session. [Operator instructions] As a reminder, this conference is being recorded for replay purposes.

I will now turn the conference over to Mr. R. J. Dourney, CEO. Please proceed, sir.

R. J. Dourney

Janice, thank you. Good afternoon everyone. I will just start out with this. I have been looking forward to this call for the last three months. The turnaround strategy at Così is on course. Part of the key to this process has been the refresh initiative and I am going to go into detail on this a little later on in the call, but we are meeting or beating our expectations.

Second point I want to make starting out is that our franchise system is growing and I can't wait to get into the details of what that looks like with you. And if I share anything with you this afternoon that should get you as excited as we are, it is that we are cash flow positive at the unit level. So this afternoon Miguel Rossy and I are going to go through a number of details, we are going to go through key indicators on the business.

We are going to provide very clear direction for you regarding our growth, our progress and the steady-state improvement of the health of our business. And finally, before I turn it over to Miguel, I am very pleased to again share with you that the Hearthstone transaction is complete. What that’s all about is continuing to leverage the asset that we have here in Boston that we are adding very profitable restaurants to our portfolio.

So, Miguel, I will turn it over to you.

Miguel Rossy-Donovan

Thank you, R. J. and good afternoon everyone. I have had the pleasure of working side-by-side with R. J. and executing a disciplined path towards profitability over the last three months that I have been with Così and I’ve also had the opportunity to work closely with our managers and our operators out in the field and I can tell you this. They are absolutely clear on the connection between serving our guests great food which is something we are all very proud of with the reality of operating and growing an economic model that works. I am excited to engage with you all in today's call and let's get into it.

Our second quarter earnings release was issued today at 4:00 PM and is available in the Investor Information section of our website at www.getcosi.com. During the call, we will be referencing supplemental materials, which are also available in the Investor Information section of our website. If you have not already done so, please refer to these materials at this time. As we always do, we’ll address our regulatory housekeeping matters before we begin.

During our introductory comments and in responses to your questions, certain items may be discussed which are not based on historical facts. Any such items, including expected results and any details related to expected performance should be considered forward-looking statements within the meaning of the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995.

All such forward-looking statements involve risks and uncertainties that could cause our future performance and financial results to differ materially, and therefore, you should not place undue reliance on these forward-looking statements. We refer all of you to our filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission for a more detailed discussion of the risks and uncertainties that may have a direct bearing on our operating results, our performance and our financial condition.

Okay, so let’s dive into the results. We reported a net loss of $3.9 million or $0.17 per basic and diluted share in the second quarter ended June 29, 2015 versus a net loss of $4.8 million or $0.27 per share in the same quarter last year. Total revenues for the second quarter, including franchise fees and royalties, increased by $3.8 million to $24.5 million compared with $20.7 million in the same quarter last year.

Restaurant net sales for the quarter increased by $4 million to $24 million, compared with $20 million in the same quarter last year. This increase in restaurant net sales was the result of a decrease of approximately $1.7 million related to the restaurants closed during and subsequent to the second quarter of fiscal 2014, offset by an increase in both our comparable restaurants base of 2.1% amounting to approximately $0.7 million, as well as an additional $5.3 million in our non-comp restaurants which include the acquired Hearthstone restaurants.

The increase in comparable restaurant net sales was the result of 1.1% increase in traffic and a 1% increase in average check. The contribution of franchise fees and royalties in the second quarter of 2014 decreased by $241,000 when compared with the same quarter last year due to the net decrease in franchise units due the acquisition, offset by the opening of one franchise unit and increased sales in the existing franchise units.

At the end of the second quarter 2015, there were 110 Così restaurants of which 32 were operated by franchisees and 78 were company owned compared with 114 Così restaurants at the end of last year's second quarter of which 48 were operated by franchisees and 66 were company owned.

Moving on to restaurant costs, the cost of foods and beverage for the second quarter as a percentage of net restaurants sales were 27.1%, a 150 basis point increase versus the same quarter last year. This increase versus the same quarter last year was primarily driven by higher quality product offerings as well as new menu items.

Labor and related benefits expenses as a percentage of net restaurant sales were 35.7%, a 140 basis point decrease versus the same quarter last year. This decrease is the result of labor efficiencies generated by our operational initiatives, as well as a decrease in the cost of medical insurance related benefits.

Other operating expenses as a percentage of net restaurant sales were 15.6%, a 170 basis point increase when compared with the second quarter of 2014. This increase was mostly driven by equipment and supplies related to our coffee initiative, ongoing repairs and maintenance and credit card fees. Our occupancy costs decreased in the second quarter by 150 basis points when compared to the same quarter last year.

General and administrative expenses decreased by approximately $0.5 million to $3.2 million in the second quarter of 2015 versus the same quarter last year. This decrease was due primarily to the move of the support center from Deerfield, Illinois in the same quarter last year, partially offset by executive stock compensation awards in 2015’s second quarter.

Total restaurant cash flow for 2015 second quarter was a gain of $393,000, compared to $375,000 gain for the same quarter last year. Cash and cash equivalents were approximately $13.9 million as of June 29, 2015. Capital expenditures for the second quarter were approximately $0.8 million versus $0.1 million in the same quarter last year.

This increase was driven by $0.4 million related to equipment repairs and maintenance at the unit level, $0.3 million related to refresh projects, and $0.1 million in the final expenses related to the net POS rollout in the first quarter. On slide 4 of the supplemental schedules, we are providing a reconciliation of the non-GAAP measures to reported quarterly results and we have evaluated there are no subsequent events occurring after June 29, 2015 through today that require recognition or disclosure.

With that, passing it back to R.J.

R. J. Dourney

Miguel thank you. So let’s go ahead and get into the details of the business and state of the business if you will. Regarding our Company-owned restaurants, there are strong key indicators showing the health of the business. Key strategic initiative this year has been our continued focus on our refresh process. Now here’s what’s critical for me to communicate this afternoon. We expected to see a 10% lift in comp store sales as a result of the refreshes and we have. Now, we expect and we need to see a 40% flow through of those incremental sales and we’re seeing that flow through. And finally, we needed to be able to refresh a unit for between 20,000 and 150,000 and we’re right on target. Now, the runway has been short that we’ve looked at but these are the key indicators that we have to focus in on, on the health of the business.

So we’ve now refreshed six units, we have seven that are currently in process, but we have another 10 to 20 that will be done by year’s end. All 55 are scheduled to complete will be done by first quarter of ’16. Now this continues to be a disciplined process. I think it’s important that we remember what we’ve accomplished since March of ’14. At the end of the day, we’re a restaurant company, we had to put it on the plate, we’ve cleaned up our ingredients and we have passionately been focused on a spectacular menu, clean salad dressings, locally sourced ingredients, and an absolutely outstanding coffee has been introduced.

We completed a deep analysis of our business last year and we evaluated our portfolio. And painfully, we shuttered ten locations that should not have been part of our portfolio. We deployed a very robust point of sale system that is a game changer for our business. This team has become very strong and it’s growing stronger by the month. At the end of the day, this is a turnaround and what we’re focused on is a disciplined process to move this business forward. What we should all see are the results that we are achieving and continue to achieve and its constant state of improvement. Couple of minutes ago, Miguel walked us through the improvements we saw from first quarter in our operating margins; cost of goods improvement of 110 basis points, labor reduction of 550 basis points, and occupancy of 340 basis points. Collectively, this continues to push us towards a very compelling economic model.

I’m going to shift gears and I’m going to talk about the franchise system. Last year, we had the positive impact at Hearthstone as being part of the franchise system when we looked at comps. Now because of the definition of comparative store sales, Hearthstone is no longer seen in comps as part of franchise nor is it part of corporate, not until we thought we’ve hit that 15 month point. We should all be looking at the steady improvement we’re seeing in comp sales on the franchise business. Now, we’ve been very candid about the impact of three specific units on the franchise side, these units on a small base of 32 restaurants had caused the comps to skew; now we’ve put a full quarter of press against taking corrective actions with those restaurants. If we pull back for a second and we remove these three concerned units from our franchise comps, the year-to-date comps would go on our franchise business from minus 3 to plus 1.2. That’s an enormous shift.

Next topic, let’s talk about growth. Our franchise system is growing, we have opened three of the six scheduled franchise units as of July 1 and the remaining three are under construction. The three important facts that I should focus on regarding the growth of our franchise system. The first is that we are engaging with the right franchise partners, partners who are as passionate and committed to great operations being profitable as we are. The second is mission critical and it is our focus on making sure that our existing franchisees are profitable. And the third is that we have to make money. As a former franchisee, nothing is more the case than when a franchisee has confidence in their franchise or they built, if you know that you can replicate a compelling economic model you build.

Next subject I’d like to get into is our cash burn. The rate of our cash burn continues to improve. This is a steady improvement for us towards being profitable. As said before, we are taking a disciplined approach regarding our business and we are cash flow positive at the unit level. So here are the headlines. We’re growing, our unit count is growing, our franchise system is growing, there is work that has to be done, we understand that but our revenues are growing. We have enough cash on the balance sheet to do what we have planned. And does it mean that we may not raise capital further down the road but if the near-term is measured by the next year, we’ve got it covered. Now having taken the corrective action that we need, we’ve built a strong team and we’re moving this forward.

Late last year, I shared with everyone that by September of this year, we would start talking about where could this go. I will give you two headlines, the first is in 2016, our franchise growth will accelerate and we’ll get into double digits and the second is, come September we’re going to start talking about how big this could become.

So with that, operator I’m going to turn it over to you and open it up to questions.

Question-and-Answer Session

Operator

[Operator Instructions] And our first question comes from Robert Routh with FBN Securities. Please proceed.

Robert Routh

Couple of quick ones, obviously you guys seemed to be moving at very conservative accounting with the way you do things, and expensing things as you incur, and I’m just curious as to what the rebuild and the refresh is, how much of that are you expensing, how much are you’re capitalizing, you’ve seen that what they’re doing make sense and it’s been very conservative, but you could in theory capitalize on those expenses and if you have done that the reported earnings would probably be a little bit better than what we’re seeing, I’m just curious if you could walk us through the process of how you handle those cash costs as you go through this process.

Miguel Rossy-Donovan

Sure. Thank you. This is Miguel. We are currently capitalizing all of the expenses incurred on behalf of the refresh project. We have a capital budget that we have very carefully and discipline allocated to each of the restaurants and we are not expensing any of those costs as we would your regular repairs and maintenance in all of the other restaurants.

Robert Routh

Okay. And then kind of seeing where your share price is now and given the fully diluted share count of 57 million [ph], correct me if I’m wrong, it would seem if you take that at the share price, less the net cash, less the cash value of the NOL, your business is almost really nothing, can you kind of go over what your current NOL is for purposes and kind of the net cash and share count to get into, it seems like stock seems to be trading like a warrant.

Miguel Rossy-Donovan

Rob, I’ll take the NOL piece. So as you and I’ve discussed in the past, the bad news is Cosi has lost a lot of money through the years. The good news is we get to leverage that NOL. We currently have BDO doing the stress tests on that. We’ll report within the next 60 days what the total dollar is available to us and when we get in to Q3 reporting, we will start talking about how we’ll leverage that.

Robert Routh

Okay. And the current share count, if you include the most recent project you did?

Miguel Rossy-Donovan

Current share count. Rob, before we wrap up, we’ll get you the exact number.

Robert Routh

Great. Thank you very much.

Miguel Rossy-Donovan

Thanks, Rob.

Operator

Our next question comes from Spencer [indiscernible]. Please proceed.

Unidentified Analyst

Hey guys. Good progress in the quarter. Two questions really. One is during the quarter, you made a press release that referenced three underperforming stores. Can you tell us where those stores are located and what you’re doing to change the outcome?

And the second question actually is kind of a surprise as a result of this call, you indicate you have $13.9 million on the balance sheet and you’re doing a store refresh, but the other stores will probably cost you $1.5 million at the top. So what is the use of proceeds of the $13.9 million over the next 12 months? Thanks.

R. J. Dourney

Yes. Spencer, thank you. So yeah, a couple of things. So yes, we’ve given direction to those three restaurants on the franchise side when extracted as I mentioned radically change what the comps look like and we’ve put a full court press against taking corrective action with those three restaurants. Here is what I’d like to focus on.

If a franchisee doesn’t know how to be profitable, we’re going to do everything we can to help them. If they don’t understand why, we’re going to explain it to them, but at the end of the day, this is in multiple choice and in the franchise world, I think we need to be a best in class franchise order, but then I think that allows us to demand that our franchisee is the best in class.

So we’re working very, very closely with those three franchisees and look if they don’t want to or if they can’t take corrective actions, there are steps that we are taking to change that. I’ve not given the locations nor do I intend to, but hopefully that gives you some guidance.

Regarding the use of capital for now, we’re going to say that the capital is being reserved for current business and we’ll talk about how we might use the proceeds down the road. Thanks, Spencer.

Unidentified Analyst

Great, thanks.

Operator

[Operator Instructions] Our next question comes from William Meyers with Miller Asset Management. Please proceed.

William Meyers

Hi. I wanted to ask about your comment about franchise expansion in 2016, what’s the basis of your confidence and do you see that as coming from people who are already franchising with you or is that going to be new owners?

R. J. Dourney

Yeah. William, thank you. So the north of 10 openings that I mentioned are only commitments that we have, concrete commitments we have from existing franchisees. We are in dialog with prospective franchisees in addition to that, but I’m not willing to commit to anything just yet.

William Meyers

Okay. But you sound confident in your statement.

R. J. Dourney

I am.

William Meyers

Okay. Great. That’s all for me. Thanks

Operator

Our next question comes from James Kahn of Oppenheimer. Please proceed.

James Kahn

Hi, R.J. Good quarter. Thanks. So first question is do you anticipate coming back having to raise money in the next year as things stand now?

R. J. Dourney

No.

James Kahn

Okay.

R. J. Dourney

Short answer, no. But Jim, you and I’ve talked about this before, Miguel and the team and I are always going to do which is right for the business, but that’s not the plan.

James Kahn

Right. But I guess to preserve the NOLs, and the 382 rule, you might have to do a rights offering I suppose to keep rather than, I think I’ve said before, I think the rights offering is the preferred way to go, because then instead of a private placement, you don’t want to dilute existing shareholders and you want everybody to get a fair share of participating?

R. J. Dourney

Well said, Jim. And as I sizable shareholder, I couldn’t agree more.

James Kahn

So my other questions have been answered. But my last observation is admittedly a bit quirky and whimsical, but there was a Seeking Alpha piece that was quite negative and in the commentary below, the 17-year old daughter of the Costa Rican franchisee said, no, you don’t understand, Cosi is great and she wrote this eloquent response. Now, the idea would be get her on CNBC, it would be a slam dunk story, everyone would love it. Her defending Cosi, the 17-year old girl whose mother is the franchisee and it would be a great publicity for your restaurants.

R. J. Dourney

Jim, thank you for the idea. I’ll tell you that our franchisee in Costa Rica and that’s Christina is the mother of the young lady that sent that response, what a great kid. She’s a nice young girl. The franchise partnership there, they are passionate, they are driven, they’re successful, they love the brand and they’re doing a spectacular job of growing their business.

James Kahn

Right. My idea sounds like a joke, but actually CNBC loves it when there is a young kid who is entrepreneurial and who believes in capitalism and in the fact that it’s from Central America is even better?

R. J. Dourney

Right. We’ll give it some thought, Jim. Thank you.

Operator

[Operator Instructions] Our next question comes from Jeffrey Matthews with RAM Partners. Please proceed.

Jeffrey Matthews

Three quick questions if you don’t mind. Number one, the point of sales system sounds like it’s everything that it was advertised to be, I know the old one was very quirky. Is it fully rolled out and fully operational?

R. J. Dourney

It is Jeff, it came in below cost estimates, it’s fully rolled out throughout our system and we’re really happy.

Jeffrey Matthews

Okay. Secondly, your labor cost control looks terrific. Will you be able to keep that going in light of the pressures on wages, both just from supply and demand and service labor as well as manual wage loss?

R. J. Dourney

Jeff, here is the reality as you know. Everybody in our business has experienced the pressures coming from minimum wage and what’s on the horizon. We take that in to our operating assumptions, we know what the impact could be and we have mechanisms in place to deal with it going forward.

Jeffrey Matthews

Okay. And my third question is more forward-looking, the three franchise units that have opened, I guess those are the first under your leadership and I’m wondering what you’ve seen from them so far, what you’ve learned and how they’re doing?

R. J. Dourney

I think the two pieces that I would share with you that are of consequence, one, organizationally, it’s a very big deal within the four walls of this office that we obsess over our franchisees coming out of the gates healthy. [indiscernible] a former franchises, but I think everybody on this team knows that if you want a franchisee to build ten restaurants, the first one they build has got to make a lot of money. They have to be able to open it for at or less than they thought, it has to generate more in revenues than they anticipated and has to generate more -- a higher four wall contribution. So we’re pretty focused on making sure that’s the case.

The second thing I would share with you is the franchisees that we’re growing with, I’m very proud of them. I mean it when I tell you, I’m humbled by them. They are driven, they are passionate, they’re good at what they do, they love the brand and I couldn’t be more thankful for who they are. So thanks, Jeff.

Operator

Our next question comes from Chris Stewart [ph], a private investor. Please proceed.

Unidentified Analyst

Hey, guys. Just wondering if you can give me a little bit more color on labor costs, occupancy costs going forward, keeping hear a lot about what will you be doing moving forward to kind of keep this a little bit under where they’re now, I know you just talked about labor, but maybe you could touch on – maybe some leisure and any potential renegotiation there at all?

Miguel Rossy-Donovan

Yes, this is Miguel. Absolutely, one of the things that we are not letting upon is on the management of commodities, both in terms of continuing to negotiate our prices down and find savings that way as well as cost avoidance and making sure that, as we think about our menus and our key mix and our growth, that we avoid any costs by locking in prices on the front end.

In terms of labor besides what R. J. just talked about, to me it’s all in the execution and the planning of the weekly schedules of the partners that serve our guests and I can tell you three things. Our partners have an incredible – technology that is incredible that allows them to actually generate those schedules in a way that provides the most efficient and productivity way of labor schedules. Number two, it allows for very robust recording to allow them to make real-time adjustments with the ebbs and flows of the business. And number three, we have incredible operators that through the layers of management pay attention to it and we’ve seen that provide incredible lift this past quarter. And I am very, very comfortable with the path that we are going forward in terms of labor management as we face even pressures from minimum wage et cetera

R. J. Dourney

Miguel, if I can add one piece on the cost of goods side. Chris, we had talked earlier this year, late last year about the importance of us putting it on the plate and we knew that we were going to have to increase our cost of goods as a percentage in order to do the right thing. That work is done. Now we get, as Miguel was speaking to, he and his team working with the operations team are doing a spectacular job of gently moving us towards a very profitable model, so that we can take full advantage of moving the business forward on the comp side with leverage every penny of sales that we can.

Miguel Rossy-Donovan

Absolutely.

R. J. Dourney

Thanks, Chris.

Unidentified Analyst

Okay, thanks so much.

R. J. Dourney

Thank you.

Operator

And we now have a follow-up question from Robert Routh. Please proceed, sir.

Robert Routh

Yeah, thanks for taking, just one quick follow-up. Given how well it looks the outlook is going to be for this company where everybody seems to think you can do it, you’ve been going into 2016 and given that you have issued some equity [indiscernible] where the market price is now, what is the price that the company would actually consider repurchasing shares really putting into place a buyback authorization so in the event you saw some major shareholders wanting to get out or whatever to stabilize the price and the company would be willing to buy back the stock, reduce the share count and create leverage to equity returns when you are EBITDA positive.

So I think that might be a good signal to the market as well as show that everyone including people there believe in what we’re hearing, we obviously do by owning the stock and then when it does happen, getting back to the share price would be that much more dramatic due to the lower share count, I’m just curious what is your thoughts on that?

R. J. Dourney

Rob, it’s one of those -- we heard you; we’ll take under advisement and leave it at that.

Robert Routh

Okay, thank you very much.

Miguel Rossy-Donovan

Rob, I also wanted to follow up with you on the share count question, as of 06/29, we had 48,104,677, sorry about that.

Operator

And we have no further questions; I would now turn the call back over to management for any closing remarks.

R. J. Dourney

Thank you, operator. I’d like to think that what we just all experienced over the last 40 minutes was encouragement, was the passion of this team and we see this business moving forward. We know we have work to do, we know exactly what needs to happen and I’m very excited about the team that we have assembled to get it accomplished. I’m very thankful to have the partners in this that I have from the franchisees to people like Miguel and the shareholders that are involved with this and as we look down the road, if I was fired up 17 months ago, I’m even more excited today. Thank you everybody.

Miguel Rossy-Donovan

Thank you everybody.

Operator

This concludes today’s conference, you may now disconnect. Have a great day everyone.
