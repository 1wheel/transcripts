Black Diamond Group Limited (OTC:BDIMF) Q2 2015 Earnings Conference Call August 13, 2015 1:00 PM ET

Tom McMillan

Good morning. My name is Tom McMillan and I'm the General Manager of Corporate Communications and Investor Relations for Black Diamond Group Limited. At this time, I would like to welcome participants to Black Diamond’s Results Conference Call for the Second Quarter of 2015 with President, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer, Trevor Haynes and Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer, Mike Lambert.

We’re also joined today by Troy Cleland, Executive Vice President and Chief Operating Officer of Black Diamond Structures; Steve Stein, President and Chief Operating Officer of Black Diamond Logistics; Harry Klukas, Executive Vice President, International; and Toby Labrie, Vice President of Finance.

After our formal remarks, there will be a question-and-answer session. [Operator Instructions] Please note, that while talking about our results and answering questions, we may make forward-looking statements. These statements are subject to known and unknown risks, and future results may differ materially.

We will also be discussing non-GAAP financial measures in today's call, including adjusting EBITDA, funds available for dividends and pay-out ratio.

For more information about these topics, please review the sections in Black Diamond’s second quarter 2015 Management’s Discussion & Analysis and titled Forward-Looking Statements, Risks and Uncertainties and Non-GAAP Financial Measures. This quarter's MD&A, news release and unaudited financial statements can be found on our website at www.blackdiamondgroup.com as well as at SEDAR website.

Dollar amounts discussed in today's call are expressed in Canadian dollars and are generally rounded. I will now turn the call over to Trevor Haynes to review the quarter. Trevor?

Trevor Haynes

Thanks, Tom. Before getting started with the review of the second quarter, I’d like to address some of the concerns that we’re seeing in the marketplace this morning. While the environment in Western Canada is clearly difficult for all industry participants, our platform of remote workforce and specialty rental businesses is performing relatively well. Later in the call, I will talk about how our platform has been designed to weather these conditions.

We continue to have a strong balance sheet. We’re generating free cash flow and selectively investing growth capital where it is supported by long-term contracts. Our clients and assets are diversified across a number of industries and geographies and we’re continuing to see activity on a number of fronts. We are actively picking up market share and our rental fleet, which is relatively new provides us with tremendous operating leverage for when the economy and our economic environment improves.

As for comments on the quarter, weak pricing across almost all commodity classes is negatively impacting asset utilization in Western Canada and Australia. As a result, revenue is down 20% year-over-year. Last week, we announced a renewal of a contract in Alberta's oil sands expected to generate CAD26 million in revenue over two years. We also signed a contract in June in our BOXX Modular division for space rental assets worth CAD5.9 million in revenue over two years. This is in addition to the 1,244 bed camp in Dawson Creek, the second phase of which went on rent in April. These are certainly challenging times for our industry, yet, we continue to see a base level of activity for Black Diamond.

EBITDA declined 33% to CAD23.3 million, reflecting the lower utilization rates across our business platform. We reported a net loss for the quarter of CAD800,000. The loss for the quarter was triggered by the 20% increase in the statutory corporate tax rate in Alberta and its impact on the Company’s annual effective tax rate at a time when the economic environment is driving down revenues. Without this increase in Alberta income tax, the Company would have reported a net profit of CAD2.6 million for the quarter.

Our capital plan for the full-year of 2015 remains at CAD50 million, of which CAD36 million was spent at the end of the quarter and CAD3.9 million committed. We expect that operating levels will continue to be weak compared to historic norms until a catalyst event occurs. Catalyst for higher utilization could include a Canadian liquefied natural gas project, achieving a positive final investment decision, a recovery in oil and gas or mine mineral pricing or any other event that might trigger increased utilization or additional capital expenditures by customers in our end markets.

I’ll ask Mike Lambert to go into more detail about the financial results across our divisions for the quarter. Mike?

Mike Lambert

Thanks, Trevor. On a consolidated basis, EBITDA for the quarter was down 33% or CAD11.6 million from a year ago. As you can see from the chart on slide four, much of this was related to our Structures business unit, which continues to be impacted by lower utilization as a result of a significant decline in oil prices. Our Logistics division was down 11% or about CAD600,000 due to lower occupancy levels, partially offset by higher adjusted EBITDA margin, driven by revenue mix.

Energy Services EBITDA was down year-over-year as low oil prices continue to result in lower rates and lower utilization. International results were also lower year-over-year due to ongoing headwinds in commodity prices that Australia’s reserve sector is currently facing. Administrative costs were down slightly year-over-year due to lower bonus and commission costs offset by severance.

I’ll now review the key financial metrics on the next slide. As Trevor pointed out, we reported a net loss of CAD800,000 for the quarter. The loss for the quarter was triggered by 20% increase in the statutory corporate tax rate in Alberta, increasing the Company’s annual effective tax rate, which when applied to the Company’s deferred tax accounts led to a CAD3.4 million impact to our bottom line this quarter. Unfortunately, this came at a time when the economic environment is driving down revenue. Without this increased Alberta income tax rate, the Company would have recorded a net profit of CAD2.6 million for the quarter.

Funds available for dividends decreased by 39% due primarily to decreased activity levels. This combined with the dividend increase we made in August 2014 meant that our payout ratio was 44% for the quarter compared with 26% a year ago. Based upon the current run rate of Black Diamond business, current contract coverage and diverse range of marketable assets, we expect to be able to meet all of our financial obligations while continuing to pay a dividend.

While the net debt to EBITDA ratio crept up to 1.59 times, primarily as a result of the second tranche of CapEx for the Dawson Creek’s 1,244 bed camp, our balance sheet remains strong.

I’ll now turn the call back over to Trevor.

It’s important to note that the contracted future revenue on slide six does not include the SAGD renewal, which we expect will generate CAD26 million in revenue over two years. Given some of the talk of pricing concessions and rate degradation in our industry, I think it’s important to remind our listeners today about how our platform is different from many of our competitors. Many of our analysts expressed concern about rate degradation in the contract renewal we just announced. Part of the advantage and also performing catering and other services that we know where and how we can work with our service providers’ rate concessions to achieve savings on behalf of our customers without having to significantly compromise Black Diamond’s margins.

Slide seven shows where Black Diamond fits in a value chain we own in specialty rental assets. We outsourced all the services around these assets and can therefore achieve downstream concessions that we can pass along as part of our value proposition to our customers. This is a way in which we are different from our competitors and again speaks to how we have positioned Black Diamond to weather the cyclical nature of the industries we operate in. We also recently saw a report suggesting that should LNG go ahead all the assets for the upstream, midstream and downstream would have to be new builds. This is simply wrong. More than two-thirds of the worker accommodation associated with these LNG projects is serviceable with existing industry assets.

Given the current business environment, we are aggressively addressing operating costs and overhead, outsourcing functions like construction services and certain areas of our logistics business is aligned with our core business model and expected to have a positive impact on our direct costs. In addition, by amending the covenants of our revolving credit facility, we have further enhanced Black Diamond’s financial flexibility in the event of prolonged downturn in commodity pricing.

In short, we are being very proactive about enhancing Black Diamond’s operating and financial flexibility. As a result, we’re well positioned to weather this environment with a diverse asset base, exposure to regions that continue to experience ongoing activity and stable loading in our large managed cash. A good example of our diverse asset base is Black Diamond’s BOXX Modular workspace rental platform, which is currently generating approximately CAD7 million per quarter in EBITDA across United States and Canada. This platform is not only geographically diverse but also services a wide range of industries in addition to oil and gas, including everything from construction, education, government and even banking.

The Company is sound, and within our current run rate, we expect to be able to meet all of our financial obligations while continuing to pay a dividend.
