During the second quarter of 2015, we exceeded our midpoint net sales and same-store sales guidance, and achieved our midpoint guidance for EBITDA. Our second quarter own-brand sales were 25.6% of total sales, up 140 basis point improvement over last year's second quarter. We have recently added many organic and natural own-brand products to our offering and we now carry over 7,800 own-brand products.

The increase primarily reflects the benefit of new and acquired stores in Illinois, partially offset by a 6.4% decrease in same-store sales.

The decrease in same-store sales was due to a 7.3% decrease in the number of customer transactions, partially offset by a 0.9% increase in the average transaction size. Adjusted for the effect of the Easter holiday calendar shift, same-store sales declined 4.9% compared to a decline of 7.7% in the first quarter of 2015. As Bob noted earlier, same-stores sales in Illinois were negatively impacted by the reopening of a significant number of former Dominick’s stores that were initially closed in early 2014 and are now operated by competitors.

The impact related to competitive store openings in the Illinois market was approximately 320 basis points for the quarter. In addition, same-store sales in Illinois were negatively impacted by the cannibalization effect of new and acquired store openings. The effect on several existing stores due to new and acquired store cannibalization was approximately 240 basis points. We will cycle most of these effects by year end.

During the second quarter of 2015, there were three competitive openings in our Illinois market. We expect the total of eight new competitive openings to impact Mariano's in 2015. Year-to-date, there have been six competitive openings and we expect another two openings during the second half of the year.

Gross profit for the second quarter of 2015 increased 5.9% to $263.7 million from $249 million in the same period last year. Despite competitive pressures, gross profit as a percentage of net sales was 26.4% for both the second quarter of 2015 and 2014.

Operating and administrative expenses for the second quarter of 2015 increased to $252.1 million from $239.1 million in the same period last year. The increase is due to labor and occupancy costs related to our new stores. Operating and administrative expenses as a percentage of net sales were 25.3% in the second quarter of 2015 compared to 25.4% in the same period last year.

For the second quarter of 2015, net loss from continuing operations was $1.4 million or $0.03 diluted net loss per common share, compared to a net loss from continuing operations of $5 million or $0.10 diluted net loss per common share for the second quarter of 2014. Adjusted net loss from continuing operations for the second quarter of 2015 was $1.1 million or $0.02 adjusted diluted net loss per common share, compared to adjusted net loss from continuing operations of $1.2 million or $0.03 adjusted diluted net loss per common share for the second quarter of 2014.

Adjusted EBITDA from continuing operations for the second quarter of 2015 was $30.1 million compared to $28.2 million in the second quarter of 2014. The increase primarily reflects the maturing store base in the online market and reduced acquired store startup cost, partially offset by the impact of the Easter holiday calendar shift.

When we look at cost data, inflation was between 1.5% and 2% for the second quarter but decelerated as the quarter progressed. During the quarter we experienced inflationary trends in certain commodities, such as beef and eggs, but we experienced deflation in produce, milk and pork products. We expect the deflationary trend for produce to continue into the third quarter and beef has decreased from its historic highs in the second quarter. We estimate that the deflationary pressures will have an additional 40 to 50 basis points negative impact on same-store sales in the third quarter compared to the second quarter.

Capital expenditures for the second quarter of 2015 were $18.8 million compared to $26.1 million in the second quarter of 2014. The company's effective tax rate from continuing operations for the quarter was a tax benefit of 39.5%.

Our total debt at the end of the second quarter of 2015 was $647 million versus $657 million at the end of the first quarter of 2015. The increase relates primarily to payments made on our revolving credit facility.

As of July 4, 2015, we had $39.9 million in cash and cash equivalents and $166 million of availability in our asset base revolving credit facility.

In terms of guidance, we expect third quarter consolidated net sales to be in the range of $975 million to $985 million, with same-store sales in the range of negative 2.25% to negative 3.25%. For the full year we expect 2015 consolidated sales to be in the range of $3.95 billion to $3.99 billion, with same-store sales in the range of negative 2.25% to negative 3.25%.

Adjusted EBITDA from continuing operations for the third quarter is expected to be in the range of $23 million to $28 million, and adjusted EBITDA for 2015 is expected to be in the range of $115 million to $125 million.

For the third quarter, we expect our adjusted net loss per diluted share from continuing operations to be in the range of $0.06 to an adjusted net loss of $0.12, and for fiscal 2015 to be in the range of a loss of $0.06 to $0.17 per share.

Our guidance for total interest expense is $13.75 million to $14.25 million for the third quarter and $55 million to $56 million for 2015. Our income tax rate for the third quarter and full year is expected to be approximately 40%. Capital expenditures for the third quarter are expected to be $20 million to $25 million, and for the full year, $68 million to $73 million.
